Luke 6:37 is part of Jesus' Sermon on the Plain, and it emphasizes the importance of not judging or condemning others in a condemnatory way. Understanding the difference between discernment and the appetite to condemn requires looking at the underlying principles behind Jesus' teaching.

### Discernment vs. Condemnation

1. **Discernment**:
   - **Purpose**: Discernment involves distinguishing between right and wrong, good and evil, to make wise and informed decisions. It is an essential part of Christian discernment, allowing one to live a life that aligns with God's will.
   - **Attitude**: Discernment should be practiced with humility and a recognition of one's own fallibility. It involves seeking truth and understanding rather than simply passing judgment.
   - **Biblical Support**: Scripture encourages believers to "test everything; hold fast what is good" (1 Thessalonians 5:21) and to "examine yourselves" (2 Corinthians 13:5). These verses illustrate that discernment is a thoughtful, reflective process.

2. **Appetite to Condemn**:
   - **Purpose**: Condemnation is often driven by a desire to elevate oneself above others, to maintain a sense of moral superiority, or to express feelings of moral outrage. It can be more about expressing judgment than seeking righteousness or reconciliation.
   - **Attitude**: Condemnation is often harsh, unforgiving, and lacking in grace. It can lead to division and conflict rather than understanding and healing.
   - **Biblical Support**: Jesus frequently warned against the dangers of hypocrisy and self-righteousness, as seen in passages like Matthew 7:1-5, where he cautions against judging others when we are equally flawed.

### Moral Judgments and the Sermon on the Plain

- **Moral Judgments Never Matter**: The statement "Judge not, and ye shall not be judged" (Luke 6:37) does not imply that moral judgments are irrelevant. Instead, it suggests a transformation in how judgments are made. Jesus is cautioning against rash, hypocritical, and self-righteous judgments.
  
- **Application**: The focus should be on humility and forgiveness. By forgiving others, believers open themselves to receive forgiveness from God. This teaching encourages a focus on mercy and grace rather than strict adherence to judgment.

### Conclusion

The key distinction between discernment and condemnation lies in the motivation and outcome of the judgment. Discernment seeks understanding and alignment with God's will, characterized by humility and truth-seeking. In contrast, condemnation often involves a harsh, unforgiving attitude that seeks to elevate oneself at the expense of others. Luke 6:37 encourages believers to practice discernment with compassion, forgiveness, and humility, reflecting the heart of God's kingdom.
