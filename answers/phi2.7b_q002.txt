When we request for a clean heart, rather than just a pardon, we are asking for an inner change, a transformation from the negative emotions that may be preventing us from experiencing God's grace and love. A pardon is more about forgiveness and is something that may be given without a change in perception or attitude, while a clean heart represents a genuine change.

In the context of Psalm 51, the text is asking for God's help in renewing one's right spirit and bringing back the joy of salvation. It is asking for a new heart that is free from the negative emotions such as selfishness, pride, and hate. Essentially, a clean heart represents a new beginning, a fresh start, and an opportunity to experience God's love and mercy.

We can observe that the verse emphasizes the importance of inner change. It is asking God to remove the veil of selfish thoughts and guide us towards a more loving and humble perspective. A pardon may bring relief, but without an attempt to change, guilt or negative feelings will likely resurface. In contrast, the request for a clean heart suggests that by adopting a new attitude, we will be able to experience a deeper connection with God and receive His grace.

In summary, asking for a clean heart means asking for a genuine transformation from within, while a pardon is more about forgiveness. A clean heart represents a fresh start, a new beginning, and an opportunity to experience God's love, while a pardon is more about getting rid of guilt and negativity.
