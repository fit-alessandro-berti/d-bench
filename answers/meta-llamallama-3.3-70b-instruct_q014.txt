This text suggests that treasure reveals the heart. The phrase "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" implies that the location of one's treasure is indicative of the orientation of their heart. In other words, the things that one values and invests in (their treasure) reflect their true priorities, passions, and loves.

Here, possession, attention, and love become tied together in that:

1. **Possession**: What one possesses or accumulates (treasure) reflects their values and priorities.
2. **Attention**: The things one attends to, focuses on, and prioritizes (their treasure) capture their attention and receives their mental and emotional investment.
3. **Love**: The heart, which is often associated with emotions, affections, and love, is drawn to the things one treasures. Where one's treasure is, their heart will follow, implying that their love and affection are also directed towards those things.

In this sense, treasure can be seen as a magnet that attracts the heart, revealing what one truly loves and values. The text is not necessarily saying that treasure trains the heart, but rather that the heart is naturally drawn to the things that one treasures.

It's worth noting, however, that this text can also be interpreted as a warning: if one's treasure is earthly and fleeting, their heart will be similarly earthly and fleeting. In contrast, if one's treasure is heavenly and eternal, their heart will be oriented towards eternal and spiritual things.
