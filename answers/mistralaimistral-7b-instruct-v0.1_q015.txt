 The image in Matthew 7:3-5 is not a physical image but a metaphorical one. The sharpness of the image comes from the clarity and forcefulness of Jesus' words in pointing out the hypocrisy of those who criticize others for their faults while overlooking their own.

The passage does not forbid moral correction altogether but rather criticizes hypocritical motivation for doing so. Jesus is emphasizing the importance of self-examination and humility before attempting to correct others. In other words, one should first address their own faults before trying to help others with theirs. This approach not only makes the correction more effective but also avoids the appearance of hypocrisy.

Therefore, the passage encourages moral correction but warns against the dangers of hypocrisy and self-righteousness. It calls for a humble and introspective attitude towards both ourselves and others.
