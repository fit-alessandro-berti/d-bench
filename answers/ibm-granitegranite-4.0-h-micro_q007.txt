The passage from Luke 6:37 presents a profound teaching from Jesus Christ, emphasizing the importance of forgiveness, compassion, and the avoidance of judgment. To understand the difference between this teaching and the idea that moral judgments never matter, we need to delve into the concepts of discernment and the appetite to condemn.

### Discernment vs. Appetite to Condemn

**Discernment** refers to the ability to judge well, to perceive and understand the true nature of things. In the context of Luke 6:37, discernment is about recognizing the intentions, motives, and actions of others without imposing personal judgments or condemning them. It involves a compassionate and empathetic understanding of human behavior, recognizing that everyone struggles with their own imperfections. This form of discernment is rooted in love and forgiveness, as it seeks to uplift rather than tear down.

**Appetite to Condemn**, on the other hand, is the inclination to judge, criticize, or condemn others for their perceived shortcomings. This appetite often stems from a place of judgmental attitudes, pride, or a desire to feel superior. It is driven by a lack of empathy and understanding, leading to a harmful cycle of negativity and division. The appetite to condemn is contrary to the teachings of Jesus, who emphasized love, forgiveness, and the importance of treating others as we would like to be treated.

### The Difference Between the Two

1. **Purpose and Intent**:
   - **Discernment**: The purpose of discernment is to foster understanding, empathy, and compassion. It seeks to identify the underlying reasons behind actions and to respond with kindness and forgiveness.
   - **Appetite to Condemn**: The purpose of the appetite to condemn is to assert superiority or to justify personal grievances. It often stems from a desire to control or manipulate others, rather than to help or uplift them.

2. **Outcome**:
   - **Discernment**: The outcome of discernment is healing and reconciliation. By choosing to forgive and not judge, individuals create an environment of peace and mutual respect. This leads to personal growth and strengthens relationships.
   - **Appetite to Condemn**: The outcome of the appetite to condemn is often conflict, resentment, and isolation. It perpetuates a cycle of negativity, damaging relationships and hindering personal and communal growth.

3. **Spiritual Implications**:
   - **Discernment**: In the spiritual sense, discernment aligns with the teachings of Jesus, who emphasized love, forgiveness, and the importance of treating others as we would like to be treated. It reflects a deeper understanding of the divine principle of mercy and grace.
   - **Appetite to Condemn**: This attitude contradicts the core teachings of Jesus. It reflects a lack of spiritual maturity and a failure to embody the virtues of love and compassion that Jesus exemplified.

### Practical Application

In practical terms, discernment involves actively choosing to respond to others with empathy and forgiveness. It means recognizing that everyone is on their own journey, facing their own challenges, and that judgment is not our place. Instead, we can offer support, understanding, and grace, mirroring the love of Christ.

Conversely, the appetite to condemn involves recognizing and challenging our own tendencies toward judgment. It requires self-reflection and a commitment to change, striving to replace critical thoughts with compassionate actions. This transformation involves cultivating a mindset of humility and recognizing that we too are imperfect and in need of forgiveness.

### Conclusion

Luke 6:37 teaches us that discernment and the appetite to condemn are fundamentally different. Discernment is a virtue that fosters love, forgiveness, and understanding, while the appetite to condemn is a vice that leads to judgment, conflict, and division. By embracing discernment and rejecting the appetite to condemn, we align ourselves with the teachings of Jesus and contribute to a more compassionate and harmonious world. This distinction is crucial for personal growth and for building a society grounded in love and mutual respect.
