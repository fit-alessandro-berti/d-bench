• Way and thoughts are two levels of repentance that together mean a total change of direction.

  - Way points to outward life: conduct, habits, and the visible pattern of actions (“turning your life around”).
  - Thoughts points to the inner source of life: intentions, motives, loyalties, imaginations, the way the person
    evaluates life.

  So Isaiah is saying repentance is not only stopping bad actions, but also changing the mental and moral posture that
  produces them. In biblical terms, this is not legalistic self-correction so much as a change of center—from self to
  God.

  The implication is:

  1. Mercy is available to anyone.
  2. But mercy is not a cosmetic repair of symptoms; it is a God-given reorientation of the whole person.
  3. Because God judges both hidden motives and visible deeds, true return (shub, “return”/“turn back”) includes both
     external obedience and inward renewal.
