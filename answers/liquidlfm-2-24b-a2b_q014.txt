Great question! Let’s unpack **Matthew 6:19–21** carefully.

### The Passage

> “Lay not up for yourselves treasures upon earth… But lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven… For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.”

---

## 1. **Does this text say treasure reveals the heart, or that treasure trains the heart?**

**It suggests both:**  
- **Revelation:** The passage implies that what we store up (treasure) reflects what our heart truly values—what we "lay up" reveals our deepest priorities.
- **Training/Training the Heart:** By choosing to store heavenly treasures rather than earthly ones, we are actively shaping and training our hearts toward God-centered values.

**Key point:**  
Jesus isn’t just saying, “Here’s what you already have in your heart”—he’s calling us to *actively* choose what we value, which then shapes our affections and priorities.

---

## 2. **How do possession, attention, and love become tied together here?**

Let’s look at each term:

### **Possession**
- “Lay up for yourselves treasures” refers to what we possess, accumulate, or invest our resources in.
- In context, these are not just material goods but also time, energy, and focus.

### **Attention**
- Wherever you direct your attention and effort—where you “lay up”—that’s where your heart will be.
- If you focus on accumulating wealth or things on earth, your heart follows suit.
- If you focus on storing up spiritual treasures, your heart is trained to seek God.

### **Love**
- Jesus says, “For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.”
- This echoes the biblical principle (e.g., Matthew 6:21) that your heart’s orientation is determined by what you cherish most.
- So, your *attention* (what you focus on) becomes an expression of your *love* (what you value).

---

## **Summary Table**

| Term        | Connection                                                                                 |
|-------------|-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| Possession  | What you accumulate/store (materials, time, energy) reflects your heart’s desires.       |
| Attention   | Where you put your focus determines what your heart is drawn to.                          |
| Love         | Your heart loves what you treasure; thus, your choices reveal and train your heart.      |

---

## **Conclusion**

**The passage teaches that your choices about what you treasure (possession), where you direct your attention, and what you love are all deeply intertwined.**  
- **Possession** is a visible sign of what you truly value.
- **Attention** is the active process of focusing on those possessions.
- **Love** is the underlying motivation—what you love is what you will ultimately treasure.

**In short:**  
Where you lay up your treasures (possessions), where you give your attention, and where your heart finds its love—all are connected. Train your heart by choosing wisely what you treasure.
