Amos 5:21–24 is one of the most scathing indictments of religious hypocrisy in Scripture, and its sharp rejection of worship is not arbitrary—it exposes a deep moral and spiritual fraud. The passage doesn’t reject worship *in itself* but rather the **hypocrisy of performing religious rituals while failing to live justly and righteously in society**. Here’s why the rejection is so severe and what kind of fraud is being exposed:

### **1. Worship Without Justice is an Abomination**
God doesn’t reject worship because it’s inherently bad, but because **Israel’s worship was divorced from justice**. The people were meticulously following religious rituals (feasts, offerings, songs) while **oppressing the poor, perverting justice, and exploiting the vulnerable** (Amos 2:6–8; 5:10–12). Their worship was a **performative act**—a way to appease God while maintaining a corrupt social order.

- **Burnt offerings, meat offerings, and peace offerings** were commanded in the Law (Lev. 1–7), but God says He **will not accept them** because they were offered by people who **cheated the poor in court, took bribes, and lived in luxury while others starved** (Amos 5:11–12).
- **Songs and music** (viols) were part of Israel’s worship (Ps. 150), but God says He **will not hear them** because their lives were out of tune with His justice.

This is the **moral fraud**: **Religious performance without ethical obedience is not worship—it’s idolatry disguised as piety.**

### **2. The Fraud of "Balanced" Worship**
The passage rejects the idea that worship can be **balanced** with injustice. Some might argue:
*"Well, we do our religious duties, but we also have to take care of business in the world."*
But God says **no**—worship that doesn’t produce justice is **an abomination**, not just "unbalanced."

- **Isaiah 1:11–17** echoes this: *"Bring no more vain offerings… your hands are full of blood. Wash yourselves; make yourselves clean; remove the evil of your deeds from before my eyes."*
- **Micah 6:6–8** asks: *"With what shall I come before the Lord?"* and answers: **Not with more sacrifices, but with justice, mercy, and humility.**
- **Jesus’ rebuke in Matthew 23:23–24** targets the same hypocrisy: *"You tithe mint and dill and cumin, and have neglected the weightier matters of the law: justice and mercy and faithfulness."*

### **3. The Call for Radical Justice Instead of Ritual**
Instead of worship, God demands:
- **Judgment (mishpat) running down like waters** – Not just occasional justice, but a **flood of righteousness** that cleanses society.
- **Righteousness (tsedeq) as a mighty stream** – Not just personal piety, but **systemic justice** that transforms society.

This is **not a rejection of worship** but a call to **worship that transforms life**. True worship **flows into justice**; false worship **drowns in hypocrisy**.

### **Conclusion: The Fraud Exposed**
The fraud being exposed is **religious legalism masquerading as devotion**. Israel thought they could **buy God’s favor with sacrifices while exploiting the weak**, but God says:
- **Your worship stinks** (v. 21).
- **Your offerings are vomit to Me** (Isa. 1:13).
- **Your songs are noise** (v. 23).
- **I want justice, not empty ritual** (v. 24).

This is a warning to all who **use religion to justify injustice**—God doesn’t want **more worship** until **worship produces justice**. True faith **cannot be separated from righteous living**.
