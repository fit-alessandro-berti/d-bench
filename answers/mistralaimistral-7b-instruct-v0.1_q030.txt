 The portrait of love in 1 Corinthians 13:4-7 emphasizes the absence of certain negative qualities, such as pride, envy, irritability, and suspicion, to highlight the strength and beauty of love. The reason for this emphasis on negations is to contrast the true nature of love with the human tendency towards selfishness and pride.

By negating these negative qualities, the text proposes a vision of strength that is humble, patient, and selfless. Love is described as suffering long, kind, not envious or proud, not easily provoked, thinking no evil, and rejoicing in the truth. This strength is not about dominating or controlling others, but about enduring, believing, hoping, and bearing all things.

The vision of strength proposed in this text is one that is grounded in compassion, empathy, and selflessness. Love is not weak, but rather, it is the most powerful force in the world, able to overcome even the greatest of challenges. By negating the negative qualities that often hinder our ability to love, we can cultivate a deeper and more authentic expression of love in our lives.
