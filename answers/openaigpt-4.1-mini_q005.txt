Enemy-love, as taught in Matthew 5:44–45, is challenging to take seriously for several intertwined reasons, but ultimately it reveals a deeper form of moral strength rather than mere sentimentality or strategy alone.

### Why is enemy-love difficult to take seriously?

1. **Counterintuitive and Radical Nature:**
   - Loving one's enemies goes against natural human instincts. When faced with hate or harm, the common response is self-defense, avoidance, or retaliation. Enemy-love demands a reversal of these natural inclinations.
   - It seems idealistic or “soft” in a world that often values toughness or justice through punishment and retribution.

2. **Sentimental Misunderstanding:**
   - Some interpret enemy-love as mere warm feelings or naivety. When reduced to sentimentality, it appears unrealistic or easily dismissed, as it doesn’t address the real pain and conflict involved.

3. **Strategic Skepticism:**
   - From a pragmatic angle, people question the usefulness of enemy-love. Is it merely a strategy to gain moral high ground or social peace? Does it risk enabling abuse or injustice by encouraging passivity?
   - For some, enemy-love seems risky in a competitive or hostile environment, making it hard to endorse as practical behavior.

### But enemy-love as a deeper form of moral strength:

- **Radical Moral Power:**  
  Jesus calls to a higher ethic that transcends natural human responses, embodying forgiveness, empathy, and restoration. Loving enemies is not weakness but a courageous refusal to escalate cycles of hatred and violence. It demonstrates emotional and spiritual resilience.

- **Reflection of Divine Character:**  
  The passage highlights that God “maketh his sun to rise on the evil and on the good, and sendeth rain on the just and on the unjust.” Loving enemies imitates God’s impartial and generous love—it’s a way of aligning one’s heart with divine mercy and justice.

- **Transformative Potential:**  
  Love directed at enemies doesn’t just protect oneself; it has the power to transform relationships, disarm hostility, and open paths toward reconciliation and peace. This kind of love breaks the perpetuation of enmity, offering hope beyond mere power struggles.

### Summary:
Enemy-love is difficult to embrace fully because it is radical and against instinctual human responses, and it can be misunderstood as sentimental or merely strategic. However, at its core, it is a profound moral strength—an expression of courageous, divine-like love that seeks restoration rather than revenge, ultimately revealing the maturity and character of those who practice it.
