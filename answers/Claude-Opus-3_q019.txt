This passage from the Book of James exposes the inner contradiction and moral inconsistency in how people often use their speech. James points out the hypocrisy of using the same mouth both to bless and praise God while also cursing and speaking evil against other human beings who are made in God's image.

Speech reveals moral disorder so quickly for several reasons:

1. The tongue is difficult to tame. James 3:8 says "no human being can tame the tongue. It is a restless evil, full of deadly poison." Our words often slip out before we have a chance to catch them, revealing the sinful inclinations of our hearts.

2. Words overflow from the heart. Jesus taught that "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks" (Matthew 12:34). The inconsistency between blessing God and cursing others reveals a divided heart that is not fully submitted to God. 

3. Speech has power to direct and influence. James 3 also compares the tongue to a small rudder that steers a large ship and a little fire that sets a great forest ablaze. Morally disordered speech can quickly cause great damage.

4. Humans are made in God's image. Cursing or slandering people is an affront to God himself, since all people bear God's divine imprint. To curse those made in God's likeness while blessing God is blatantly two-faced.

5. Righteousness should be consistent. As Christians, our lives and speech should consistently reflect the holiness, love and goodness of the God we worship. Using the same mouth for both good and evil exposes moral compromise and lack of integrity.

In summary, this proverb warns that disordered, untamable speech that inconsistently both blesses and curses is a sure sign of a divided spiritual state. The call is to purify our hearts so that our words may consistently honor God and edify others made in his image. What comes out of our mouths quickly and clearly reveals the true state of our inner being.
