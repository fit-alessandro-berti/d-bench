This is an excellent question that cuts to the heart (pun intended) of how biblical ethics actually work. The text in Matthew 6:19–21 does both, but in a specific sequence. It is primarily a *diagnostic* statement that reveals the heart, but it is placed within a *formational* command that trains the heart.

Let's break down your three parts: reveal vs. train, and then the triad of possession, attention, and love.

### 1. Does the text say treasure **reveals** the heart, or **trains** it?

The crucial line is verse 21: **“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”**

The grammar here is descriptive, not imperative. It’s a statement of fact, not a command. Jesus is saying: *This is how it works.* The verb “will be” (*estai*) indicates a certainty, a natural consequence. It is not “Lay up treasure in heaven so that your heart *might* follow.” It is: “Your heart *will* follow.”

Therefore, the primary function of this verse is **diagnostic and revelatory**.
- **It reveals:** Your heart is where your functional treasure is. Your heart *is already there*, attached to what you value. If you look at what you actually spend your money, time, energy, and worry on, you will see a perfect map of your heart’s true loves. The treasure doesn’t create the heart; it exposes it.

However, the *surrounding command* (verses 19–20) is **formational and training**.
- Jesus commands you to *deliberately lay up treasure in heaven*. This is an act of the will. You are to intentionally invest your resources (money, time, talent, prayer, acts of service) in things that last eternally.
- By doing this, you are *training* your heart. You are operating on the principle of verse 21. You know your heart will follow your treasure, so you choose where to put the treasure. You are practicing a kind of spiritual discipline: “Obediently place your treasure here, and trust that your heart will catch up.”

**Conclusion:** The text uses **revelation** (v.21) as the *reason* for the **training** (v.19-20). It reveals that your heart is not a static thing; it follows your attention and investment. Therefore, you can *train* your heart by redirecting your investments. The relationship is dynamic, not static.

### 2. How do possession, attention, and love become tied together?

Jesus brilliantly ties these three concepts into a single psychological and spiritual process. Here is the chain:

1.  **Possession (Treasure):** In the ancient world, “treasure” wasn’t just gold. It was your storehouse of value: grain, fine clothing, land, money. Today, it’s your 401(k), your house, your career, your reputation, your social media following, or your family’s legacy. It is what you functionally **own** or **prioritize**. Possession creates a *stake*. You are invested in it.

2.  **Attention (Where your heart is):** The phrase “there will your heart be also” is not about a spiritual, ethereal location. In biblical Hebrew psychology, the “heart” (*leb*) is the center of the **will, mind, and emotion**. It is your *command center*. It is what you think about, worry about, plan for, and protect. Therefore, “where your heart is” means **where your focused attention lives.** You cannot pay attention to what you do not value, and you automatically pay attention to what you do value. Your treasure occupies your mental and emotional space.

3.  **Love (The Heart’s Orientation):** The “heart” is the seat of love, loyalty, and worship. What you treasure, you ultimately **love**. This is the deepest layer. Possession leads to attention, and sustained attention generates deep emotional attachment. You care for what you own. You defend what you attend to. You become like what you worship.

**The Mechanism in the Verse:**

- **On Earth (False Love):** You lay up treasure on earth. This treasure is fragile (moth, rust, thieves). Therefore, your **attention** is constantly anxious, consumed with protecting and maintaining it. Your **love** becomes attached to fleeting, decaying things. Your heart is enslaved to its own anxiety.
- **In Heaven (True Love):** You lay up treasure in heaven (e.g., character, relationships, forgiveness, service, knowledge of God, eternal rewards). This treasure is secure. Therefore, your **attention** is freed from anxiety and can rest in God. Your **love** is attached to what is eternal and good. Your heart is set free.

**In summary:**

- **Revelation:** The verse shows you a mirror. Look at your checkbook and your calendar (possession and attention). That is a perfect printout of your heart’s loves.
- **Training:** The command gives you a strategy. You cannot simply “will” yourself to love God more. But you can *will* yourself to invest your money and time in his kingdom. You can give to the poor, forgive an enemy, serve at church, or pray. As you do this, your attention shifts. Your heart, by God’s grace, follows. You do not manufacture love; you *create the conditions* for it to grow by placing your treasure where love will naturally take root.

The text, therefore, is not just a moral lesson about generosity. It is a profound insight into the mechanics of the human soul: **you become what you behold, and you behold what you possess.**
