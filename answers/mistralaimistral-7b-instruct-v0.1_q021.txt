 Pride is linked to not just guilt but also blindness and collapse because of the negative consequences it brings. Pride can make a person blind to their own faults and the reality of situations around them. They may become so focused on their own achievements or beliefs that they fail to consider other perspectives or the potential consequences of their actions. This can lead to a fall or collapse, as they may make poor decisions or fail to adapt to changing circumstances.

The contrast with "the lowly" suggests that humility and a lowly spirit are moral virtues that can help prevent the negative consequences of pride. By being humble and open to learning from others, a person can gain valuable insights and perspectives that they may have missed otherwise. Additionally, being humble and compassionate towards those who are less fortunate can foster a sense of connection and community, which can provide support and stability during difficult times. Overall, the proverb suggests that humility and a lowly spirit are important moral qualities that can help prevent the destructive consequences of pride.
