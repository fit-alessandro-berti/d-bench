This passage operates as a **law of spiritual alignment** rather than a simple either/or. It suggests both revelation and training, but with a distinctly **formative trajectory** built into its grammar and context. Here’s a breakdown:

### 1. Reveals or Trains?
The Greek structure leans toward **training through alignment**, while acknowledging a revelatory reality:
- `ὅπου γὰρ ἔστιν ὁ θησαυρὸς ὑμῶν, ἐκεῖ ἔσται καὶ ἡ καρδία ὑμῶν`  
  *(“For where your treasure **is**, there **will be** also your heart.”)*
- The present tense (`ἔστιν`, “is”) paired with the future tense (`ἔσται`, “will be”) indicates that **current investments determine future orientation**. Your heart doesn’t merely reflect a static state; it is drawn, shaped, and settled by where you deliberately store your treasure.
- **Reveals**: Yes. What you accumulate, protect, and plan around exposes what you already value. A heart fixated on security, status, or comfort will naturally hoard earthly treasure.
- **Trains**: More strongly. The future tense implies causation. By repeatedly investing in heavenly realities (generosity, worship, truth, eternal mission), you rewire your affections. The heart follows gravity; treasure is the mass that creates it.
- **Theological nuance**: Jesus isn’t describing two separate mechanisms. He’s stating a unified principle: **investment shapes affection**. The “for” (`γὰρ`) explains why the preceding commands matter: you must choose your treasury carefully, because your heart will inevitably migrate there.

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together
In biblical anthropology, these three are not separate categories but a dynamic pipeline of human orientation:

| Concept | Biblical Meaning in Context | How It Connects |
|---------|----------------------------|-----------------|
| **Possession/Treasure** (`θησαυρός`) | Not merely what you own, but what you *invest in, protect, and accumulate* (time, money, energy, relationships, spiritual practices). Earthly vs. heavenly treasures represent competing economies of value. | Acts as the **anchor**. What you store demands maintenance, defense, and planning. You cannot hoard something without allocating cognitive and emotional resources to it. |
| **Attention** | Where your focus goes, what you anticipate, grieve, celebrate, and strategize around. Attention is the currency of mental life. | Is the **conduit**. You guard what you care about; you notice what you’ve invested in. Attention naturally flows toward your treasures because they require stewardship and carry emotional weight. |
| **Love/Heart** (`καρδία`) | In Hebrew/Greek thought, the heart is not the seat of emotion alone, but the **control center of will, understanding, and covenantal devotion**. It’s where ultimate loyalty resides. | Is the **destination**. Sustained attention to a treasure cultivates affection, and affection hardens into love. Love, in Scripture, is always directional: it commits the whole person to an object. |

**The chain implied in v.21:**
`Investment (Treasure) → Demands Attention → Cultivates Affection → Settles the Heart (Love)`

Jesus compresses this entire psychological and spiritual reality into one sentence. The inverse is also true: where your heart is already captive, you will unconsciously store treasure there. But the verse’s future tense emphasizes **agency**: you can retrain your heart by deliberately relocating your treasure.

### Contextual & Theological Anchors
- **Sovereignty of the heart**: Deuteronomy 6:5 and Jeremiah 17:9 frame the heart as the battleground of loyalty. Jesus assumes it’s malleable but not neutral; it must be captured by something.
- **Anti-idolatry**: 1 Kings 18:21 exposes the impossibility of half-hearted devotion. Earthly treasure isn’t condemned as inherently evil, but as a rival that demands heart-loyalty it cannot sustain.
- **Economy of grace**: Heavenly “treasure” isn’t a spiritual hoard but eternal value accrued through kingdom-aligned living (generosity in Matt 6:2-4, faithfulness to God’s righteousness, love for enemies, etc.). It’s secure because it’s rooted in God’s faithfulness, not human control.

### In Summary
Matthew 6:21 teaches that **treasure both reveals and trains the heart**, but with a forward-looking, formative emphasis: your deliberate investments will inevitably draw and settle your deepest affections. Possession captures attention, attention cultivates love, and love constitutes the heart. Jesus isn’t merely diagnosing human psychology; he’s issuing a strategy: *choose your treasury wisely, because your heart will follow*.
