In **Matthew 6:1**, the danger is **not public visibility by itself**, but **doing good in order to be seen**.

Jesus says:

> “Take heed that ye do not your alms before men, **to be seen of them**...”

That phrase gives the key. The moral problem is **the motive**. Public goodness becomes dangerous when:

- the act is aimed at **human applause**
- charity becomes a form of **self-display**
- the giver seeks **reputation** more than righteousness

In other words, the issue is not that others happen to see the good deed, but that the deed is performed **for the sake of being seen**.

### Why this is morally dangerous
Because it corrupts the act from the inside:

- **Charity turns into vanity**
- **Worship turns into performance**
- **Service to God turns into self-promotion**

The act may still look good outwardly, but inwardly its reward has already been chosen: **human approval instead of God’s approval**.

### Visibility itself is not always condemned
Elsewhere, Jesus says:

> “Let your light so shine before men…” (Matthew 5:16)

So Jesus is not banning all visible goodness. The difference is:

- **Matthew 5:16**: let others see your good works **so that God is glorified**
- **Matthew 6:1**: do not do good works **so that you are glorified**

### Short answer
The verse teaches that **public goodness is morally dangerous when it feeds the ego**.  
The real problem is **not being seen**, but **wanting to be seen for one’s own honor**.
