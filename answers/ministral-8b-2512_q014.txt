This passage from **Matthew 6:19–21** is a profound meditation on the relationship between **treasure, the heart, and human priorities**. While it doesn’t explicitly use the language of "training" or "revealing," the text does suggest a **dynamic interplay** between what we treasure and what shapes our heart—implying that **possession, attention, and love are deeply interconnected** in how they orient our lives. Here’s how the passage works:

### **1. Treasure Reveals the Heart (Indirectly)**
The text doesn’t say, *"Where your heart is, there your treasure will be,"* but rather:
> *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."*

This is a **functional truth**: **What we treasure (possessions, values, priorities) shapes where our heart is invested.** In other words:
- If your treasure is **earthly wealth**, your heart will be **attached to security, status, or material comforts**.
- If your treasure is **heavenly (righteousness, love, eternal values)**, your heart will be **aligned with God’s kingdom**.

This isn’t just about **discovery** (as if the heart is passive) but about **direction**—what we treasure **pulls** the heart in its direction. So in that sense, **treasure does "reveal" the heart** by showing where it is already going.

### **2. Treasure Trains the Heart (Implied by Contrast)**
The passage contrasts **earthly treasure** (perishable, insecure) with **heavenly treasure** (eternal, secure). This suggests:
- **Earthly treasure** **distorts** the heart by making it **self-centered, anxious, and materialistic** (cf. **1 Timothy 6:10**—*"the love of money is a root of all kinds of evil"*).
- **Heavenly treasure** **transforms** the heart by **reorienting it toward God, others, and eternity** (cf. **Colossians 3:2**—*"Set your minds on things above"*).

So while the text doesn’t use the word *"train,"* it **implies a redirection of the heart**—what we treasure **shapes** (for better or worse) what we love and pursue.

### **3. How Possession, Attention, and Love Are Tied Together**
The passage weaves these three ideas together:

- **Possession (What we "lay up")**
  - Earthly possessions are **fleeting, corruptible, and insecure** (moth, rust, theft).
  - Heavenly possessions (righteousness, faith, good deeds) are **eternal and secure**.
  - **What we "possess" (invest in) determines what we "hold onto" with our hearts.**

- **Attention (Where our focus lies)**
  - If we **fix our eyes on earthly things**, our minds become **preoccupied with security, status, and survival**.
  - If we **fix our eyes on heaven**, our minds become **aligned with God’s priorities** (justice, mercy, love).
  - **Attention follows treasure**—what we treasure **captures our focus**.

- **Love (What we truly desire)**
  - Earthly treasure **distorts love** into **self-serving, greedy, or possessive** (e.g., hoarding, envy, fear of loss).
  - Heavenly treasure **expands love** into **generous, selfless, and eternal** (e.g., giving, sacrifice, hope).
  - **Love follows treasure**—what we treasure **determines what (or whom) we love**.

### **Key Takeaway: A Feedback Loop**
The passage describes a **circular dynamic**:
1. **What we treasure** (our priorities) → **shapes our heart** (our desires, fears, loves).
2. **Our heart’s orientation** → **determines what we treasure** (we seek what our heart already craves).
3. **This cycle either elevates or corrupts us**—earthly treasure leads to **self-destruction**, while heavenly treasure leads to **life in God**.

### **Practical Implications**
- **If your treasure is money, status, or comfort**, your heart will be **anxious, competitive, and insecure**.
- **If your treasure is God, people, and eternity**, your heart will be **at peace, generous, and hopeful**.
- **The solution?** **Reorient your treasure**—not by force, but by **renewing your mind** (Romans 12:2) to see **heavenly realities** as more valuable than earthly ones.

### **Conclusion**
The text doesn’t neatly separate *"revealing"* and *"training"*—it shows that **treasure and the heart are in a **dynamic, mutually reinforcing relationship**. **What we treasure doesn’t just reflect the heart; it reshapes it.** And since **possession, attention, and love are all tied to what we treasure**, the choice of where we invest our hearts is the most **practical and profound** decision we make.
