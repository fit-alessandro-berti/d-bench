Matthew 6:19–21 does not merely state that *treasure reveals* the heart — though that is true — but more profoundly, it teaches that **treasure trains the heart**. The relationship between treasure, attention, and love is dynamic and formative, not merely diagnostic.

---

### 1. **Does it say treasure reveals the heart, or that treasure trains the heart?**

**Answer: It says both — but primarily, it says treasure *trains* the heart.**

- **Reveals?** Yes. The phrase “*For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also*” (*gar hoi thēsaurou sou ekei estai kai hē kardia sou*) implies that the location of one’s treasure discloses the orientation of the heart. If someone hoards earthly wealth, their heart is already oriented there. So, treasure *reveals* where the heart already is.
  
- **Trains?** More deeply, the text assumes that *choosing* where to store treasure actively *shapes* the heart’s orientation. Jesus is not just diagnosing a condition — He is issuing a command: “Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven.” He assumes that where you *put* your effort, energy, and resources, your heart will follow and be formed. This is formative, not merely reflective.

> In other words: **Your heart doesn’t just follow your treasure — your treasure molds your heart.**

This is the essence of spiritual discipline. What we consistently treasure — what we guard, pursue, invest in — becomes the object of our affection, our attention, and our devotion. Jesus is giving a spiritual law of formation: *Practice shapes posture.*

---

### 2. **How do possession, attention, and love become tied together here?**

These three are inextricably interwoven in Jesus’ teaching:

#### ➤ **Possession ⇄ Attention**
Jesus contrasts earthly treasures (which can be lost to moth, rust, thieves) with heavenly ones (which are secure). The very act of *possessing* earthly wealth requires constant attention: guarding it, worrying about it, managing it, lamenting its loss. This is why He says thieves “break through and steal” — the more you possess, the more vulnerable and anxious you become. Possession demands vigilance — which means attention is locked onto the treasure.

> *You don’t just have possessions; possessions have you.*

#### ➤ **Attention ⇄ Love (Heart)**
In biblical language, “heart” (*kardia*) is not merely emotion — it is the center of one’s will, moral orientation, and love. Where attention is consistently given, love grows. Jesus assumes that attention is the fuel of love. If your eyes are fixed on riches, your love will gravitate toward them. If your attention is set on God’s kingdom (cf. Matt. 6:33), your love will follow.

This is why Jesus teaches elsewhere:  
> “For where your eye is, your whole body will be full of light.” (Matt. 6:22)  
The eye = the focus of attention; the heart = the moral result.

#### ➤ **Love ⇄ Possession**
Love determines what we treasure. We love what we value most — and then we invest in it. But here, the flow is bidirectional: love shapes treasure, and treasure shapes love. This creates a feedback loop:

- You love wealth → you treasure it → you invest in it → your attention is consumed by it → your heart becomes attached to it → your love deepens for it → you treasure it even more…

Or the reverse:
- You love God’s kingdom → you treasure heavenly rewards → your attention turns to grace, justice, mercy → your heart is shaped by divine values → your love grows for righteousness.

---

### **The Deeper Theological Point**

Jesus isn’t just warning against greed. He’s exposing a spiritual anthropology: **the human heart is not neutral. It is magnetized by what it dwells on.** The heart does not stay still — it is constantly being drawn, molded, and oriented by its objects of devotion.

> **Treasure is the visible signature of the heart — but also the invisible sculptor of it.**

This is why Jesus commands: *"Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven."*

He does not say, “Try to love God more.” He says: *Change where you store your treasure.*  
Because when you invest in the eternal — in acts of mercy, in sacrificial giving, in eternal values — **your heart learns to love what is eternal.**

---

### Summary

- ❖ **Treasure reveals the heart** — yes, as a reflection of current orientation.
- ✅ **Treasure trains the heart** — and this is the *main point*. Jesus gives a directive to form the heart by redirecting investment.
- 🔗 **Possession demands attention** → **Attention feeds love** → **Love determines what is treasured** → and the cycle continues.

Thus, Jesus offers not a warning, but a spiritual strategy: *Change your investment to change your affections.*  
Your heart will follow your treasure — so choose your treasure wisely.

> *Where your money goes, your attention follows. Where your attention dwells, your heart loves. Where your heart loves, your life is shaped.*  
> — *That is the kingdom math of Matthew 6:21.*
