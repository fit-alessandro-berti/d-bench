This passage from Matthew 6 actually suggests **both**—it reveals the heart and trains it—but its primary emphasis is on how treasure *trains* or *directs* the heart. 

Here is a breakdown of how this works, and how possession, attention, and love are inextricably tied together in Jesus’ teaching.

### 1. Reveals vs. Trains the Heart
We often read verse 21—"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"—as a diagnostic tool. We think: *If you want to know what someone loves, look at their bank statement.* In this sense, treasure **reveals** the heart. 

However, in the context of the preceding commands ("Lay not up..." "But lay up..."), Jesus is giving a strategy for spiritual formation. He is saying that the act of storing up treasure actually pulls the heart in a specific direction. In this sense, treasure **trains** the heart. 

The causal arrow is primarily pointing from the treasure to the heart, not the other way around. We usually assume that we must first feel love for something before we give our money or time to it. Jesus reverses this. He teaches that our affections follow our investments. If you invest your resources in earthly things, your heart will become earthly. If you invest your resources in heavenly things, your heart will become heavenly. By commanding us to lay up treasure in heaven, Jesus is giving us a practical mechanism to reorient our inner lives.

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Tie Together
In this text, Jesus establishes a profound psychological and spiritual chain reaction: **Possession dictates attention, and attention solidifies love.**

*   **Possession (Treasure):** Treasure is what we secure, protect, and accumulate. It represents our resources—our money, time, and energy. Jesus sets up a binary: earthly treasure (which is vulnerable to moth, rust, and thieves) and heavenly treasure (which is eternally secure). 
*   **Attention (The Mind/Focus):** What you possess dictates what you must pay attention to. If you lay up earthly treasures, your attention will be consumed by anxiety over their fragility. You have to worry about moths eating your garments, rust corroding your metals, and thieves breaking into your house to steal your wealth. Your mind becomes tethered to the earth because your security is tied to the earth. Conversely, if your treasure is secure in heaven, your mind is freed from the anxiety of loss.
*   **Love (The Heart):** In biblical anthropology, the "heart" is not just the seat of emotions; it is the center of the will, intellect, and ultimate allegiance. As our attention is continually drawn to the things we possess and try to protect, our heart becomes attached to them. We eventually love what we spend our time thinking about and protecting. 

### The Summary of the Mechanism
Jesus is exposing a fundamental law of human nature: **We become attached to what we invest in.** 

If a man buys a vintage car, he will spend hours cleaning it, researching it, and worrying about it. Because he possesses it, it demands his attention; because it demands his attention, his heart becomes tethered to the garage. 

Jesus is warning that if we invest our lives in earthly possessions, our hearts will be dragged down to the level of the earth—bound by anxiety, materialism, and the temporary. But if we "lay up treasures in heaven"—through acts of charity, obedience, love, and prioritizing the Kingdom—our hearts will be lifted upward. Our attention will be fixed on eternal realities, and our love will be rightly ordered toward God.
