Repentance involves both "way" (outward conduct) and "thoughts" (inward imagination) because true change and transformation must encompass both our outward actions and our internal attitudes. It implies that repentance is not just about superficial changes in behavior, but also about a fundamental transformation of the heart and mind.

The fact that God promises mercy and abundant pardon to those who forsake their wicked ways and unrighteous thoughts suggests that there is always a possibility for redemption and forgiveness, no matter how far we may have strayed. It emphasizes the depth of God's grace and His willingness to forgive and restore those who sincerely repent and turn back to Him.
