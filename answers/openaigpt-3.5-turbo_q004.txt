The trust being asked for in these verses is a deep and genuine trust in the Lord with all one's heart. It is a trust that goes beyond mere surface-level belief or blind obedience. 

Humble self-distrust involves recognizing our own limitations and acknowledging that our own understanding may be flawed or incomplete. It is a recognition that we are not always capable of fully comprehending or navigating the complexities of life on our own.

On the other hand, anti-intellectualism rejects the value of intellect or reasoning altogether, dismissing critical thinking and understanding in favor of unquestioning faith. This mindset may lead to a blind and rigid adherence to beliefs without ever questioning or seeking deeper understanding.

In these verses, the emphasis is on trusting in the Lord wholeheartedly while also acknowledging the limits of our own understanding. It encourages us to lean on God for guidance and wisdom in all aspects of our lives, rather than relying solely on our own limited perspectives.
