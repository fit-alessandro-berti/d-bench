This is a penetrating question that gets to the very heart of James’s argument about the tongue. The passage exposes a profound **inner contradiction between worship and relationship**, specifically a fracture in the logic of God’s image.

Here is the breakdown of that contradiction and why speech reveals it so quickly.

### 1. The Inner Contradiction: The Logic of the Image

The contradiction James exposes is not just that we say *nice* things and *mean* things. It is that we perform two acts which logically and theologically **cancel each other out**.

- **Act A:** We **bless** God, the Father. This is an act of supreme reverence, recognizing His holiness, goodness, and authority.
- **Act B:** We **curse** men, "which are made after the similitude [image] of God."

The contradiction lies in the **object of the curse**. To curse a human being is not simply a social faux pas; it is an attack on the very thing for which you just praised God. You are blessing the Artist and then defacing His artwork. You are honoring the King and then slandering His ambassador.

**Theological Contradiction:** You cannot consistently claim to love and bless God while hating and cursing the one who bears His very likeness (the *imago Dei*). To curse the image is to implicitly insult the original. It is an indirect blasphemy. This is the "inner contradiction" James is exposing: **a heart that tries to be loyal to God while being disloyal to God's representatives.** It is a spiritual double-mindedness made audible.

### 2. Why Speech Reveals Moral Disorder So Quickly

Speech is the most powerful and immediate evidence of internal disorder because of three characteristics James emphasizes earlier in the chapter (v. 3-8):

**A. The Tongue is the "Unruly Evil" (v. 8)**
Unlike other sins (e.g., theft, adultery) which require a degree of planning, opportunity, and privacy, speech is **spontaneous and constant**. It is the one "member" of the body that is always "on." You can stop your hand from stealing, but you cannot easily stop your mouth from speaking. Therefore, the tongue is the first part of us to "leak" what is truly inside. A moral disorder in the heart (pride, anger, contempt) will find its way out verbally before it manifests in a concrete action.

**B. The Tongue is the Guide of the Whole Body (v. 3-4)**
James calls the tongue a "bit" in a horse's mouth and a "rudder" on a ship. Speech is not just *a* part of the body; it **steers** the rest of you. A small falsehood, a tiny curse, or a quick sarcastic insult can set the entire course of a relationship, a day, or a life on fire. Speech reveals disorder instantly because it is the **control center** for the subsequent actions. If the rudder is broken (speaking cursing), you know the ship (your life) is off course.

**C. The Tongue is the Direct Channel from the Heart**
This is the core of Jesus's teaching (Matthew 12:34 – "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh"). The tongue is not an independent actor. It is a **thermometer** that reads the temperature of the soul. When the blessing of God and the cursing of a neighbor come from the same mouth, it doesn't mean the mouth is confused. It means the **heart is divided**.

The mouth reveals the moral disorder of a **double-minded heart**—a heart that loves God in one moment and hates His image in the next. You cannot fake it for long. One harsh word reveals more about your spiritual condition than a thousand hymns sung on Sunday.

### Conclusion

The inner contradiction is a **theological inconsistency**: loving the Creator while hating His creation. Speech reveals this disorder so quickly because the tongue is the most unruly, most influential, and most revealing member of the body. It is the **seismograph of the soul**. A single curse reveals a profound earthquake of moral and spiritual disorder below the surface. James’s point is that this "ought not so to be" because it proves that our worship is hollow and our love is conditional.
