This passage from Matthew 6:19–21 primarily suggests that treasure reveals the heart. The text implies that where one chooses to store their treasures reveals what they truly value and where their priorities lie. If someone stores treasures on earth, it indicates a focus on material and temporary concerns. Conversely, if they store treasures in heaven, it suggests a focus on spiritual and eternal values. 

However, the passage also hints at the idea of treasure training the heart. By choosing where to store one's treasures, one can influence the direction of their heart and priorities. If someone intentionally chooses to focus on heavenly treasures, this decision can gradually align their desires and affections with spiritual concerns.

In terms of how possession, attention, and love become tied together, this connection can be understood as follows: 

- **Possession:** Where you choose to invest your resources indicates what you possess, not just materially but also in terms of values and priorities.
- **Attention:** What you invest in captures your attention. By focusing on heavenly treasures, you train your attention on things of eternal significance.
- **Love:** Ultimately, where your attention is directed often reflects what you love. Thus, your investment in heavenly treasures signifies a love for spiritual matters, influencing where your heart resides.

In summary, the text indicates that the location of one's treasure reveals the state of their heart but also suggests that mindful investment in heavenly treasures can guide and shape one's heart over time.
