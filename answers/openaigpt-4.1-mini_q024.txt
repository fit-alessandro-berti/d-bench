The passage from Ezekiel 36:26–27 highlights a profound transformation that God promises to bring about in His people: giving them a "new heart" and a "new spirit." This is contrasted with the current condition of having a "stony heart," which is hard and unyielding.

### Difference Between External Compliance and Receiving a “New Heart”

- **External Compliance:**  
  This refers to following rules, commandments, or religious rituals outwardly—acting in accordance with laws and expectations but without an inward change or genuine commitment. External compliance can be superficial; it might look good on the outside but lack true motivation or alignment of will. It often arises from obligation, fear, or societal pressure rather than from inner conviction.

- **Receiving a “New Heart”:**  
  This involves an inward transformation—changing the core of a person’s being. A "heart" in biblical language often symbolizes the center of emotions, will, and moral character. To receive a new heart means to have one’s desires, motivations, and attitudes renewed or recreated. It implies a fundamental change in nature that leads naturally to faithful living, not just out of obligation but out of genuine desire and spiritual vitality.

### Why is Hardness Described Almost Like a Material Condition?

- **Concrete Imagery to Convey Spiritual Reality:**  
  The description of a "stony heart" uses vivid, physical imagery to make an abstract spiritual reality easier to grasp. Hardness here conveys resistance, unresponsiveness, and inability to be molded or affected. Just as stone is hard, cold, and unyielding, so an unrepentant or rebellious heart is portrayed as closed off to God’s will, love, and transformation.

- **Indicates the Extent of the Problem:**  
  The metaphor of a stony heart represents how deeply ingrained the resistance to God has become—it's not just a surface issue but a fundamental condition of the person. By saying God will "take away the stony heart" and "give you a heart of flesh," the text emphasizes that this is not a minor adjustment but a radical replacement from a state of hardness and resistiveness to one of softness, responsiveness, and life.

- **Material Condition Symbolizes Inability and Need for Divine Intervention:**  
  Stone cannot change itself on its own—it must be broken or replaced. Similarly, a person’s natural tendencies toward hardness (such as stubbornness, sinfulness, pride) cannot be changed by human effort alone. The change must come from God who can remove the old heart and implant a new one.

### Summary

- External compliance is outward and often superficial adherence to rules.
- Receiving a new heart is a profound, inward transformation at the core of one’s being.
- Hardness as a material condition symbolizes spiritual resistance, an inability to respond to God which requires divine intervention to change.
- God’s promise to replace the stony heart with a heart of flesh underscores the radical, life-giving renewal that enables true obedience and relationship with Him.
