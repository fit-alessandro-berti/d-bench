 The short moral summary, which is taken from Micah 6:8 in the Bible, is demanding because it encompasses three core virtues: justice, mercy, and humility. These three virtues seem to contradict each other initially, but upon further scrutiny, they correct one another and are essential components of living a well-disposed and virtuous life.

### Answer (Biblical)
1. Justice demands that we treat others fairly and equitably, not give an advantage to our families, nor favor our friends.
2. Mercy, on the other hand, suggests that we have compassion and kindness for others, even for those who have wronged us.
3. Finally, humility urges us to admit our shortcomings, remain open to correction, and not let our pride overshadow our ability to empathize and show mercy to others.

### Response
Justice, mercy, and humility, though seemingly at odds with each other, are interdependent. In this summary, justice is called for in the form of treating others fairly and equitably. Mercy is needed to show compassion and kindness to others, even those who have wronged us. Lastly, humility is the humility to admit our shortcomings and not let our pride overrule our other virtues.

Thus, while it may seem that the virtues of justice, mercy, and humility contradict one another, they work synergistically, correcting and enhancing one another. All three are necessary in leading a virtuous and virtuous life, leading humans, in this case, to walk humbly and dojustly, as commanded by God in Micah 6:8.
