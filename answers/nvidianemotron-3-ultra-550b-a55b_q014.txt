The text **does both**, but the grammar and logic of the passage prioritize **formation (training)** over mere revelation. The trajectory of the Sermon on the Mount is ethical and teleological: Jesus is not merely diagnosing a static condition; he is prescribing a way of life that *shapes* the condition.

Here is a breakdown of how the mechanics of possession, attention, and love bind together in Matthew 6:19–21.

---

### 1. The Grammar: Imperative Logic Creates Indicative Reality
The structure of the passage is **Imperative $\rightarrow$ Imperative $\rightarrow$ Indicative (Ground/Reason)**.

*   **v. 19 (Negative Imperative):** *Do not lay up* ($\mu\eta\ \theta\eta\sigma\alpha\upsilon\tau\epsilon$) treasures on earth.
*   **v. 20 (Positive Imperative):** *Lay up* ($\theta\eta\sigma\alpha\upsilon\tau\epsilon$) treasures in heaven.
*   **v. 21 (Gar/$\gamma\alpha\rho$ — "For"):** *Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.*

The **"For" ($\gamma\alpha\rho$) in verse 21 explains *why* the commands in 19–20 are urgent.** If the text only meant "treasure reveals the heart," the logic would be: *"Don't worry about where you put your treasure; it just shows who you are anyway."* But Jesus says: **"Actively move your treasure (v. 20), *because* your heart follows your treasure (v. 21)."**

**Conclusion:** The heart is **malleable**. Treasure is the *rudder*; the heart is the *ship*. You steer the ship by moving the rudder.

---

### 2. The Mechanics: How Possession, Attention, and Love Bind Together

In the biblical anthropology of the Sermon on the Mount, these three categories are not sequential steps but a unified **orientation of the self**.

#### A. Possession ($\theta\eta\sigma\alpha\u\rho\iota\zeta\omega$ — *Thesaurizō*) = *Weighting* Reality
The verb means "to store up," "to heap up," or "to treat as capital." It implies **intentional allocation of finite resources** (time, money, worry, status).
*   **Possession creates gravity.** What you "lay up" becomes a center of mass in your life. Earthly treasure (v. 19) is defined by *corruptibility* (moth, rust) and *insecurity* (thieves). Heavenly treasure (v. 20) is defined by *permanence* and *security*.
*   **The Act of Possessing is an Act of Trust.** To "lay up" is to say: *"This asset is reliable enough to bear the weight of my future."* You cannot "lay up" something you do not trust.

#### B. Attention = The Eye of the Heart (The Bridge)
Immediately following this pericope (Matt 6:22–23), Jesus says: *"The eye is the lamp of the body... If your eye is healthy, your whole body will be full of light."*
*   **Attention is the mechanism of "laying up."** You cannot store treasure you do not look at, calculate, admire, or fear losing.
*   **Possession directs Attention:** If your capital is in a volatile stock market, your *attention* (anxiety, checking apps, reading news) is enslaved to the market. If your capital is "in heaven" (the Kingdom, righteousness, mercy), your attention is freed to scan for Kingdom opportunities.
*   **Attention is the currency of Love.** In the ancient world (and modern psychology), *what you attend to is what you worship.* Simone Weil famously said, *"Absolutely unmixed attention is prayer."*

#### C. Love ($Kardia$ — *Heart*) = The Center of Convergence
In Hebrew/Jesus’ anthropology, the *heart* ($kardia$ / $lev$) is not the seat of *emotion* (as in modern English) but the **command center of the will, intellect, and desire.** It is the "CEO" of the person.
*   **Love is not a feeling that precedes action; Love is the *alignment* of the will toward its ultimate Good.**
*   **"Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also"** means: *Your functional ultimate concern (Heart) will inevitably migrate to match your functional ultimate investment (Treasure).*

---

### 3. The "Training" Dynamic: A Feedback Loop
The text describes a **formative loop**, not a one-time revelation.

1.  **Initial Choice (Possession):** You decide to invest in *X* (a retirement fund, a reputation, the poor, the Kingdom).
2.  **Sustained Attention:** To maintain that investment, you must watch it, protect it, think about it, measure it.
3.  **Habituation (Training):** The daily discipline of attending to *X* rewires your desires. You begin to *want* what *X* produces. You begin to *fear* what threatens *X*.
4.  **Reorientation of the Heart (Love):** Your "Heart" (your defining loyalty, your "Master" — cf. Matt 6:24) becomes *X*.

**This is why Jesus commands the *move* (v. 20).** He knows that if you *act* as if the Kingdom is your treasure (giving alms, praying in secret, fasting, forgiving debts), your heart will eventually *become* Kingdom-shaped. This is **"faith seeking understanding"** enacted through **"treasure-seeking-heart."**

---

### 4. Why "Revelation Only" Fails the Context
If verse 21 were merely *diagnostic* ("Check your bank statement to see your spiritual state"), the preceding commands (vv. 19–20) become nonsensical:
*   **Diagnostic View:** "Don't store earthly treasure... *because* it reveals you are worldly." (But the command is to *stop* doing it, implying you *can* stop).
*   **Formative View:** "Don't store earthly treasure... *because* it will *make* you worldly. Store heavenly treasure... *because* it will *make* you heavenly."

The context of **Matthew 6:24** ("You cannot serve God and Money") confirms this. *Mammon* is personified as a **rival Master**. Masters don't just *reveal* who you are; they *command* who you become. You become like the Master you serve. Treasure is the mechanism of that service.

---

### Summary: The Triad United

| Category | Function in Text | The Dynamic |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Possession** (Treasure) | **The Anchor** | The concrete, tangible allocation of *self* (resources, time, self-worth) into a specific object/system. |
| **Attention** (The Eye) | **The Cable** | The daily, mental/emotional tether connecting the self to the possession. "Where treasure is, attention flows." |
| **Love** (Heart) | **The Destination** | The resulting *identity* and *ultimate loyalty*. The heart is the gravity well that forms around the orbit of your attention. |

**The Text says: Treasure *trains* the Heart via the discipline of Attention.**
Therefore, the command "Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven" is the primary spiritual technology Jesus offers for the renovation of the heart. You do not wait for your heart to change before you move your money; you move your money (and time, and status) *so that* your heart will change.
