The passage from Ezekiel 36:26–27 speaks metaphorically about spiritual transformation and renewal. The difference between external compliance and receiving a "new heart" lies in the motivation and the nature of the obedience to God's statutes.

**External Compliance:**
- External compliance refers to following rules or commands without internal conviction or genuine transformation. It's about outward actions that may be performed due to pressure, obligation, or fear of consequences, but do not necessarily reflect a person's inner beliefs or desires.
- This kind of compliance can be superficial, as it might change based on circumstances or personal interest, rather than a true commitment to the principles behind the actions.

**Receiving a "New Heart":**
- Receiving a "new heart" signifies a profound internal change where one's values, desires, and motivations are aligned with those of God. This transformation is described as a change from a "stony heart" to a "heart of flesh."
- A "stony heart" implies rigidity, insensitivity, and resistance to divine influence, similar to how stone is hard and unyielding. It suggests a state of spiritual stubbornness or rebellion.
- In contrast, a "heart of flesh" is soft, responsive, and receptive to God's guidance and spirit. It represents openness and willingness to be shaped according to God's will.

The metaphor of replacing a stony heart with a heart of flesh emphasizes the necessity for a foundational transformation, not just a superficial change. It's about moving from mere rule-following to embracing God's spirit within oneself, which leads to a genuine desire to walk in God's statutes and keep His judgments. This internal change is depicted as essential for true compliance with God's will, as it arises from within rather than being imposed from without.
