### Short answer  
The passage **does not say that “treasure trains the heart.”**  
It says **“where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.”**  
In other words, **what we value most (the place of our treasure) determines where our heart is focused.**  

---

## 1.  What the text actually states  

| Phrase | Literal meaning | Implication |
|--------|----------------|-------------|
| “Lay up for yourselves treasures **on earth** …” | Accumulate material wealth that is vulnerable to decay and theft. | Earthly treasure is insecure and temporary. |
| “Lay up for yourselves treasures **in heaven** …” | Accumulate spiritual, eternal blessings that are secure and incorruptible. | Heavenly treasure is lasting and safe. |
| “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.” | The location of what you value most will be the location of your heart’s desire and affection. | **Heart follows treasure**, not the other way around. |

The verse is a **comparative statement**: it contrasts two possible “treasure locations” (earth vs. heaven) and then draws a simple logical conclusion about the heart’s orientation.

---

## 2.  Possession, attention, and love are linked

1. **Possession → Focus of attention**  
   - When we *possess* something (money, a house, a status symbol), we naturally devote time, thought, and energy to it.  
   - The more we *own* or *control* a thing, the more we are **drawn to it** (the “eye” that looks at it).  

2. **Attention → Love/Desire**  
   - Attention is the gateway to affection. The more we look at, think about, or talk about something, the more **love or desire** for it grows.  
   - In the biblical language, “treasure” is a **metaphor for what captures our attention and affection**.  

3. **Heart’s orientation**  
   - The “heart” in Scripture denotes the **center of desire, intention, and identity**.  
   - If our treasure is on earth, the heart is **oriented toward earthly concerns** (security, comfort, status).  
   - If our treasure is in heaven, the heart is **oriented toward spiritual realities** (God’s kingdom, righteousness, eternal life).  

Thus the passage shows a **causal chain**:

> **Treasure (what we value/possess) → draws our attention → shapes our heart’s love/desire → determines where our ultimate loyalty lies.**

---

## 3.  Why “treasure trains the heart” is a mis‑reading  

- **“Trains” implies an active, formative process** (e.g., practice makes a skill).  
- The verse does **not** claim that *the act of accumulating* something changes the heart; it claims that **the *location* of what we already value determines the heart’s direction**.  
- The emphasis is on **where we place our treasure**, not on **how we acquire it**.  

If we read the verse as “treasure trains the heart,” we would be adding a causal step (the *process* of accumulation) that the text does not provide.

---

## 4.  Practical implications  

| Situation | Earthly treasure focus | Heavenly treasure focus |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| **Daily routine** | Prioritizing work, income, possessions; anxiety about “having enough.” | Prioritizing prayer, Scripture, service; seeking God’s kingdom first. |
| **Emotional security** | Feeling safe because of savings, property, status. | Feeling secure because of relationship with God, eternal promises. |
| **Decision‑making** | Choices guided by “what will make me richer?” | Choices guided by “what honors God and advances his kingdom?” |

The passage therefore **challenges the believer to examine where the heart’s true treasure lies**, not to prescribe a method for reshaping the heart.

---

### Bottom line  

- **“Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.”**  
- The verse links **possession (treasure) → attention → love/desire (the heart)** in a **one‑way causal flow**: the heart follows the treasure, not the other way around.  
- It does **not** say that *accumulating* treasure *trains* the heart; it says that **the heart’s orientation is revealed by where we place our treasure**.
