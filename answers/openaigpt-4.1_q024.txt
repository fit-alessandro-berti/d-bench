Great questions! Let's break down both parts:

---

### **1. External Compliance vs. Receiving a “New Heart”**

#### **External Compliance**
- **Definition**: External compliance means obeying rules or laws outwardly, often because of pressure, fear, or habit—not because it’s what one genuinely desires.
- **Motivation**: From outside—maybe social expectations, religious duty, or fear of punishment.
- **Result**: Behavior may change, but inner attitudes, desires, and values remain the same. There can be resentment, hypocrisy, or inconsistency.
- **Biblical Example**: Jesus often criticized the Pharisees for outwardly following the law while their hearts were far from God (see Matthew 23:27).

#### **Receiving a “New Heart”**
- **Definition**: This is an inner transformation. God gives a person new desires, motivations, and purpose. The “heart” in biblical language is the center of will, emotion, and decision.
- **Motivation**: Comes from within, as a result of God’s Spirit working in a person—obedience flows from love, gratitude, and a real relationship with God.
- **Result**: The person *wants* to walk in God’s ways. It’s not forced or grudging; it’s genuine and lasting.
- **Biblical Parallel**: Compare Jeremiah 31:33 – “I will put my law within them, and I will write it on their hearts.”

---

### **2. Why Is Hardness Described as a Material Condition to be Replaced?**

#### **Stony Heart**
- **Symbolism**: A “stony heart” represents spiritual hardness: being insensitive, unresponsive, or resistant to God.
    - **Stones**: They are cold, inflexible, and dead. Water (life) runs off; seeds can’t grow; they don’t feel or pulse with life.
    - **Implication**: With a “stony heart,” a person cannot truly love, respond to, or be changed by God.

#### **Heart of Flesh**
- **Symbolism**: A “heart of flesh” is alive, sensitive, warm, and responsive.
    - **Flesh**: Soft, living, able to feel, to be moved, to grow, and to heal.
    - **Implication**: With a “heart of flesh,” a person is able to love, trust, and obey God authentically.

#### **Material Imagery**
- The Bible uses physical, *material* language to communicate spiritual truths. Swapping out a “stone” for “living tissue” paints a vivid, visceral image of transformation—not just incremental improvement, but an entire renewal at the core of who a person is.

---

### **Summary**

- **External compliance** = outward behavior changes, but the inner person may not be transformed.
- **Receiving a new heart** = God giving real, internal change—so obedience and relationship with God flow from within.
- **Hardness** is pictured as a “stone” to show complete, lifeless resistance, which only God can replace with a living, responsive “heart of flesh.”

*Ezekiel 36:26–27 is about God’s promise to give us the capacity to truly know, love, and follow Him—from the inside out, not just in appearance.*
