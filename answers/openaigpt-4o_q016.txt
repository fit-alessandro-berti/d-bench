The elder brother's deepest problem seems to be a combination of wounded fairness and discomfort with mercy itself, manifesting through anger and envy. He feels a strong sense of injustice because he perceives that his loyalty and obedience have not been rewarded in the same way as his brother's return. His response indicates resentment toward the father's mercy and grace extended to his younger brother, showing discomfort with the father's willingness to forgive and celebrate. The elder brother's focus on strict fairness and his inability to rejoice in the return of his brother point to an underlying struggle with understanding the nature of mercy and grace.
