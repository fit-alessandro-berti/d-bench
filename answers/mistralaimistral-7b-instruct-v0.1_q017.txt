 The passage from John 3:19-21 suggests that people prefer darkness to light because their deeds are evil, and they hate the light because it exposes their wrongdoings. The reason for this aversion to light and truth can be explored through the human emotions of shame and fear.

Shame is a complex emotion that arises when we feel that we have failed to meet our own or others' expectations. It can be a powerful motivator for hiding our actions and avoiding being seen clearly. When we engage in behaviors that we know are wrong, we may feel ashamed and want to keep those actions hidden from others. The light, in this context, represents truth and exposure, which can lead to feelings of shame and embarrassment.

Fear is another emotion that can contribute to the desire not to be seen clearly. We may fear the consequences of our actions being discovered, such as reprimands, loss of status, or even physical harm. The fear of being exposed can be so strong that it leads us to prefer the darkness, where our deeds can remain hidden.

The passage also highlights the importance of truth and the desire to live a life that is pleasing to God. Those who do truth come to the light, as they want their deeds to be made manifest and recognized as good. They are not afraid of being seen clearly because they have nothing to hide.

In summary, the passage from John 3:19-21 explores the human tendency to prefer darkness to light due to feelings of shame and fear. It emphasizes the importance of truth and living a life that is pleasing to God, and encourages us to come to the light and be honest about our actions.
