In Ezekiel 36:26–27, the concept of a "new heart" and the transformation from a "stony heart" to a "heart of flesh" carry deep theological significance, illustrating a fundamental shift from mere external compliance to genuine, internal transformation.

### External Compliance vs. Receiving a "New Heart"

1. **External Compliance:**
   - **Definition:** External compliance refers to following religious laws, rules, or rituals without a genuine understanding or internalization of their meaning. It is about outward behavior without inner change.
   - **Characteristics:** It often involves going through the motions of religious practices without true commitment or understanding of the spiritual principles underlying them.
   - **Limitation:** This type of adherence can be superficial and lacks the depth of heartfelt obedience. People might follow rules out of habit, fear of punishment, or social pressure rather than a sincere desire to honor God.

2. **Receiving a "New Heart":**
   - **Divine Transformation:** The promise of a "new heart" and a "new spirit" signifies a transformative work of God within an individual. It is an internal change initiated by God, resulting in a genuine desire to follow and honor Him.
   - **Internal Motivation:** With a new heart, the motivation to obey God's statutes and judgments comes from within, driven by love and understanding rather than obligation or fear.
   - **Sincerity and Authenticity:** This transformation leads to sincere and authentic worship and obedience, as actions are now rooted in a renewed nature and spirit.

### Hardness Described as a Material Condition

- **Symbolism of Stony Heart:**
  - The "stony heart" symbolizes hardness, unresponsiveness, and resistance to God's ways. It represents an emotional and spiritual condition where empathy, understanding, and love are absent.
  - Stones, being hard and unyielding, metaphorically describe a lack of compassion or receptivity. This hardness can manifest as stubbornness, rebellion, and an inability to connect with God or others on an emotional level.

- **Material Metaphor for Spiritual Condition:**
  - By describing the heart as "stony," the text conveys the idea that this hardness is not just a metaphor but a tangible condition that must be addressed and replaced.
  - The transformation involves replacing this unyielding material (stony heart) with a "heart of flesh," symbolizing softness, sensitivity, and the capacity for emotional and spiritual connection.
  - This imagery underscores the necessity of divine intervention, as such a profound transformation cannot be achieved through human effort alone.

In summary, Ezekiel 36:26–27 emphasizes the difference between superficial conformity to religious practices and a deep, God-given transformation that changes the very core of a person. The use of "stony heart" and "heart of flesh" illustrates the necessity of replacing an unyielding, resistant nature with one that is compassionate, responsive, and eager to follow God's ways. This transformation is portrayed as a divine work, highlighting the need for God's intervention to truly change one's heart and spirit.
