Great question! Let’s look closely at what **1 John 4:20** is saying:

> “If a man say, I love God, and hateth his brother, he is a liar: for he that loveth not his brother whom he hath seen, how can he love God whom he hath not seen?”

**Why is love for the visible neighbor a test for love of the invisible God?**

1. **Visibility & Tangibility:**  
   Our brothers and sisters—other people—are physically present and interact with us tangibly. If we claim to have love for God, who is invisible and beyond our immediate senses, but cannot show love in concrete actions to those we see daily, our claim is put into question.  
   - Love for neighbor is *practical and visible*: We express it through real deeds.
   - Love for God is *spiritual and invisible*: We may claim it without obvious evidence, but if it's genuine, it should overflow into action.

2. **Expression of True Faith:**  
   The New Testament consistently teaches that true faith and love for God manifest themselves in actions, especially in love towards others. See also 1 John 4:7-8: “let us love one another, for love is from God...”

3. **Rooted in God’s Character:**  
   Since God is love (1 John 4:8), to truly know and love Him is to be shaped into a loving person. Refusing to love others signals a disconnect between what we claim and what we practice.

---

**What kind of self-deception is being exposed?**

1. **False Profession of Faith:**  
   The verse exposes the tendency to claim spiritual maturity (“I love God”) while failing at the fundamental commandment to love others. It unmasks the inconsistency: loving the unseen God should naturally lead to love of other people.

2. **Compartmentalized Spirituality:**  
   Sometimes people think of love for God as a purely private, inward feeling—unattached from their actions and relationships. This is self-deception: the Bible insists our love for God *must* become visible in how we treat others.

3. **Moral Blindness or Hypocrisy:**  
   The self-deception is thinking we are loving, spiritual people while acting with hostility or indifference toward others—a kind of hypocrisy. John calls this person “a liar,” because his life contradicts his words.

---

### Summary

**1 John 4:20** treats love for neighbor as the *test* of love for God because:
- Neighbor-love is tangible and observable.
- Love for God always expresses itself in practical love for others.

The self-deception it exposes is imagining that we can truly know and love God while refusing to love those around us. John insists this is impossible, exposing any disconnect between profession (“I love God”) and practice (“I hate my brother”) as a lie.

---

**In other words:**  
*“Don’t fool yourself: If you can’t love those right in front of you, you’re not truly loving God, no matter what you claim.”*
