James is exposing a contradiction between **what the tongue claims to worship** and **what the tongue actually does to people**.

### The inner contradiction
The same mouth that:
- **blesses God**, and
- acknowledges human beings as made in **God’s image**,

can also **curse those same human beings**.

That is the contradiction:  
if a person truly honors God, then how can that person despise, demean, or curse people who bear God’s likeness? James is saying that this reveals a divided heart. The problem is not merely “bad language,” but a deeper inconsistency in the person speaking.

### Why speech reveals moral disorder so quickly
Speech is one of the quickest ways the inner life leaks out. Jesus says something similar: **“out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh”** (Matt. 12:34). Words are fast, natural, and often unguarded, so they expose:

- **what we really value**
- **what we feel**
- **what we habitually love or hate**
- **whether our heart is integrated or divided**

A person can maintain appearances for a while in actions, but speech often reveals the actual state of the heart almost immediately. That is why James treats the tongue as such a serious moral indicator: it shows whether the soul is unified under God or inwardly split between reverence and contempt.

### In short
James is exposing **double-mindedness**: blessing God while insulting God’s image-bearers.  
Speech reveals this disorder quickly because it is the **most direct outward expression of the heart**.
