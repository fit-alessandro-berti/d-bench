### 1. Does the text say treasure *reveals* the heart, or that treasure *trains* the heart?

Grammatically and logically, **the text primarily states that treasure *reveals* the heart, but the broader biblical context—and the psychology of the passage—implies that treasure also *trains* the heart.**

*   **The Revelation Interpretation:** 
    Verse 21 is phrased descriptively: *“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”* This sounds like a diagnostic statement. Jesus is saying, "Look at where you actually invest your time, money, and energy, and that will tell you the current condition of your heart." In this sense, treasure is a compass or a mirror. It doesn't create the heart's allegiance; it exposes it. 
*   **The Training Interpretation:** 
    However, the passage is also an imperative command: *“Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven.”* Jesus is telling His listeners to *actively do something* with their resources. By shifting the location of investment from earth to heaven, the follower of God is actively reorienting their desires. In spiritual formation, what you invest in eventually captures your affection. Therefore, managing your treasure is a mechanism for training your heart.

**Conclusion:** It is a loop. Your current treasure reveals your current heart, but where you *choose* to put your future treasure will dictate and reshape where your heart eventually ends up.

### 2. How do possession, attention, and love become tied together here?

In these three short verses, Jesus constructs a tight psychological and spiritual feedback loop connecting what we own, where we look, and what we love. They are tied together in the following progression:

#### A. Possession (The Treasure)
Possession is not just physical currency; it is anything of ultimate value that a person hoards, secures, or trusts in. Jesus points out the fragility of earthly possessions: they are subject to "moth and rust" (decay from within) and "thieves" (loss from without). 

#### B. Attention (The Focus)
Attention is implied by the nature of treasure. You naturally watch, guard, and think about what you value. If your wealth is in a retirement account, foreign real estate, or social status, your mind is constantly tethered to those things to ensure they are safe. Attention follows the asset.

#### C. Love (The Heart)
In biblical terminology, the "heart" is the command center of human existence—the seat of the will, emotions, desires, and loyalties. 

#### The Tie-In:
1. **Action precedes affection:** We often think love must come first, and then we give our resources. Jesus reverses this: *action follows investment*. 
2. **Security breeds attachment:** Whatever you invest your life energy into (Possession), you will inevitably fix your mind upon (Attention), and eventually, your soul will be bound to it in devotion and fear of loss (Love). 

Ultimately, Jesus is arguing that you cannot split your life into compartments where your money is worldly, but your heart is spiritual. Whichever realm holds your tangible investments will inevitably capture your ultimate devotion.
