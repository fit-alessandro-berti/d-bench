The intensity of Amos’s rejection is not a critique of "imbalance," but an indictment of **systemic hypocrisy.** To understand why God uses words like "hate" and "despise" rather than calling for "more justice and less ritual," we have to look at the specific nature of the fraud being exposed.

In the prophetic tradition, this is not a rejection of worship itself, but a rejection of **worship used as a substitute for morality.**

### 1. The Fraud of "Ritualism as Expiation"
The people of Israel in Amos's time were not atheists; they were deeply religious. They were performing the correct sacrifices, singing the correct songs, and observing the correct festivals. The "fraud" lay in their belief that these rituals functioned as a spiritual transaction—a way to "pay off" God so they could return to their exploitative lives.

They believed that the blood of a burnt offering could cancel out the blood of the poor they had oppressed. By rejecting the offerings, God is exposing the lie that **ceremony can act as a shield against accountability.** To God, their worship was not an act of devotion, but an act of manipulation.

### 2. The Decoupling of Ritual and Ethics
The passage exposes a fundamental theological error: the belief that the "sacred" (the temple/altar) and the "secular" (the marketplace/legal system) are two separate realms.

The people thought they could be "holy" in the sanctuary while being "unholy" in the streets. They treated God as a deity who only cared about vertical relationships (Man $\leftrightarrow$ God) while remaining indifferent to horizontal relationships (Man $\leftrightarrow$ Neighbor). Amos argues that **vertical worship is fraudulent if it does not produce horizontal justice.** If your worship does not change how you treat the vulnerable, the worship is not just "unbalanced"—it is a performance designed to soothe the conscience of the oppressor.

### 3. Why "Hate" and "Despise"? (The Sensory Rejection)
The language is visceral because the sin is visceral. Notice the sensory language:
* *"I will not **smell** in your solemn assemblies."*
* *"Take thou away from me the **noise** of thy songs."*

God is describing a sensory revulsion. When ritual is divorced from righteousness, it ceases to be "sweet incense" and becomes a "stench." This suggests that **sinful worship actually offends the nature of God.** It is not that God is a moody deity who is "annoyed"; it is that God is *Truth*, and a lie (the ritual) cannot coexist with Truth. The songs are "noise" because they are dissonant with the reality of the suffering they ignore.

### 4. The Definition of True Worship: The "Mighty Stream"
The final verse (*"But let judgment run down as waters..."*) provides the corrective. Amos is redefining worship. 

In the ancient Near East, a "mighty stream" or a "torrent" (Hebrew: *nahal*) was a life-giving force that carved through the landscape, bringing fertility and order. By using this imagery, Amos is saying: 
**True worship is not a static event held in a building; it is a dynamic, flowing commitment to justice that permeates the entire social fabric.**

### Summary of the Moral Fraud
The fraud being exposed is **The Religious Mask.** It is the attempt to use the language of piety to mask a heart of exploitation. Amos is declaring that God is not interested in being "bought off" by music and meat; He is interested in being "met" through justice and righteousness.
