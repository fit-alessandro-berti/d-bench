• The core issue in Matthew 6:1 is not visibility itself, but motivation.

  - Visibility alone can be neutral or even useful (good witness, accountability).
  - “To be seen of men” in this verse points to seeking human approval, status, or praise as the real goal.

  So the moral danger is that public goodness can become self-serving theater:

  - the deed is done, but the heart is still oriented toward reputation.
  - that turns a moral/spiritual act into a performance.
  - Jesus warns that it loses its intended orientation toward God (“of your Father who is in heaven”).

  In short: the problem is craving to be seen and approved, not the mere fact of being visible. If visibility is
  secondary to love and obedience, it is not the issue; if it is primary, the act is morally compromised.
