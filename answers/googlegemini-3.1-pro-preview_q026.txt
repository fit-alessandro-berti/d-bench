In Matthew 12:34–35, Jesus established an inextricable, organic link between the inner life of the soul and the outward expression of the tongue. He uses vivid metaphors of a **storehouse** (treasure) and **overflow** (abundance) to illustrate that speech is never generated in a vacuum; it is always manufactured in the factory of the heart.

To answer your question directly: the passage suggests that words function as **symptoms, revelations, and choices—all three**. 

Here is a breakdown of how the passage connects speech to our hidden moral stockpiles, and how words operate in these three specific ways.

### The Connection: Speech and the "Moral Stockpile"
Jesus uses the word "treasure" (from the Greek *thēsauros*, meaning a deposit, storehouse, or stockpile). He pictures the human heart as a vault. Throughout a person’s life, they continuously deposit thoughts, desires, resentments, affections, and values into this vault. 

Speech is simply the currency withdrawn from that vault. The mechanism connecting the heart and the mouth is what Jesus calls "abundance" (or overflow). Like a cup bumped by life’s circumstances, whatever the heart is full of is what will spill over the brim and out of the mouth. You cannot withdraw gold from a vault filled with dirt; similarly, Jesus argues, you cannot extract words of grace and truth from a heart stockpiled with malice. 

Because of this dynamic, words act in three distinct ways:

### 1. Words as Symptoms (The "Abundance")
A symptom is an outward, often involuntary sign of an underlying medical or psychological condition. In this text, words are presented as the primary symptoms of spiritual health or spiritual disease.
*   **The Inevitability of Speech:** Jesus asks a rhetorical question: *"How can ye, being evil, speak good things?"* He is stating a psychic impossibility. Just as a chronic cough is a symptom of an underlying lung condition, venomous words are a symptom of an underlying soul condition.
*   **The Viper Metaphor:** Jesus calls them a "generation of vipers." A viper stores venom in hidden glands, but the venom is delivered through the mouth. The snake's bite is merely a symptom of its internal biological nature. Similarly, corrupt speech is merely a symptom of a corrupt nature. 

### 2. Words as Revelations (The "Treasure")
A revelation is the unveiling of something previously hidden. Because the heart is entirely invisible to other human beings, humans can easily masquerade as something they are not (which the hypocritical Pharisees frequently did). 
*   **Making the Invisible Visible:** Words are the bridge between the invisible interior and the visible exterior. When a person speaks, they pull an item out of their dark "stockpile" and display it in the light. 
*   **Unmasking the Speaker:** A person might try to hide behind a facade of piety, but eventually, the sheer mechanics of "overflow" will betray them. Their words will ultimately reveal the true, hidden contents of their heart-vault to everyone around them.

### 3. Words as Choices (The Act of "Bringing Forth")
At first glance, Jesus’ statement *"how can ye, being evil, speak good things?"* sounds deterministic—as if a spiritually sick person has no choice but to speak sick words. However, the concept of choice is deeply embedded in the text for two reasons:
*   **The Act of Stockpiling:** A "treasure" or stockpile does not appear instantly; it is accumulated over time through a thousand tiny, daily choices. What a person chooses to dwell on, nurture, and value becomes the treasure they store up. The evil man has *chosen* to hoard evil thoughts, resentments, and pride.
*   **The Act of Dispensing:** Jesus says the man *"bringeth forth"* these things. This implies moral agency. Uttering a word is an active choice to open the vault and throw its contents at another person. This is why, completely logically, Jesus states in the very next verse (Matthew 12:36) that humans will be held morally accountable for "every idle word." If words were solely involuntary symptoms and not choices, judging someone for their words would be unjust.

### Summary
In the theology of Jesus, the mouth is the most accurate diagnostic gauge of the human soul. Words are **symptoms** because they are the inevitable overflow of our inner spiritual condition; they are **revelations** because they make the hidden contents of our inner storehouse visible to the world; and they are **choices**, both reflecting what we have actively chosen to stockpile over time, and what we choose to withdraw and spend on others in any given moment.
