The passage from Jeremiah 17:9–10 presents a complex view of human nature and motivation, which can be interpreted both pessimistically and realistically. It ultimately reflects a realistic perspective on self-deception, acknowledging the propensity for people to be unaware of their true motives while also emphasizing accountability.

The passage highlights the inherent unreliability of the human heart, a core aspect of human nature that is deceitful and desperately wicked. From this viewpoint, one could argue that it expresses a pessimistic stance on human nature, suggesting an inherent flaw in human beings. However, this portrayal leans more toward a realistic depiction of self-deception, as it acknowledges the common human tendency to be unaware of their true intentions. 

If one's heart is inherently deceitful, then they cannot rely on it as an accurate witness of their motives. To get a genuine understanding of motives, it is essential to look beyond one's own heart. This could involve the reflection of one's deeds or actions or through spiritual insight as evident in the passage with God's ability to search the heart and "reins" and assess an individual's integrity. A person's actions and deeds are not always in alignment with their motives, and these can be a powerful witness to their true character and intentions that align with God's judgment. 

When discussing this passage, it's important to recognize the dual emphasis on human weakness and divine knowledge. This duality underscores the need for a realistic assessment of one's motivations, acknowledging that self-perception may be clouded while trusting in a higher understanding of true motives.
