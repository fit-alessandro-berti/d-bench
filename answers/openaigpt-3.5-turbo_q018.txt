The text in Ephesians 4:31-32 is highlighting the contrast between bitterness and forgiveness to emphasize the importance of choosing forgiveness over holding onto bitterness. Bitterness can stem from feelings of resentment, anger, and hurt, and if left unchecked, can lead to a hardened heart, negative attitudes, and a unwillingness to forgive others. By juxtaposing bitterness with forgiveness, the passage is encouraging believers to let go of negative emotions and instead choose kindness, compassion, and forgiveness towards one another. It is a reminder that holding onto bitterness not only affects the individual harboring those feelings, but can also spread negativity and toxicity to others. Ultimately, the text is calling for a change in attitude and behavior, urging believers to follow God's example of forgiveness through Christ.
