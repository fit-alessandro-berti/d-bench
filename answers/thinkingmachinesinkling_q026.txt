In Matthew 12:34–35 Jesus uses an economic/storage metaphor: the heart (καρδία) is a **hidden treasury** (θησαυρός) where “good” or “evil” goods are accumulated over time. Speech is then the withdrawal from that account—“out of the abundance [περίσσευμα, surplus/overflow] of the heart the mouth speaketh.”

The connection works like this:
- **Hidden stockpile:** The heart is the invisible vault of motives, desires, beliefs, and accumulated moral choices.
- **Public withdrawal:** The mouth does not generate its own content; it spends what has already been deposited inside. A “good man” draws from “good treasure”; an evil man draws from “evil treasure.”

As to whether words are **symptoms, choices, revelations, or all three**—the passage supports **all three**, but with different weights:

**1. Revelations (primary meaning)**
The central point is disclosure. Speech makes the hidden inventory audible. Jesus can condemn the Pharisees’ accusation (that He casts out demons by Beelzebul) not merely because it is wrong in the moment, but because it exposes the “evil treasure” they have stored up. The mouth reveals what the eye cannot see.

**2. Symptoms (strong contextual support)**
This comes immediately after verse 33: “the tree is known by his fruit.” Words are diagnostic fruit. Just as a physician reads symptoms to identify an internal disease, Jesus reads their blasphemous speech and diagnoses an evil heart. Their words are not random missteps; they are the predictable symptom of a corrupt treasury.

**3. Choices (present but qualified)**
The passage does not reduce speech to mere mechanical reflex. Jesus addresses these men as responsible agents—“how can *ye*, being evil…”—and the very next verses (12:36–37) warn that people will “give account” for “every idle word” and be “justified” or “condemned” by them. So words involve real agency and accountability.

Yet the emphasis here is that **choice flows from stockpile**. They *chose* to speak blasphemy, but Jesus says they did so *because* they are evil and have evil in storage. The choice reveals prior accumulation; it does not happen in a vacuum. They are not neutral people who randomly picked evil words from a blank shelf—they brought forth evil things because that is what filled them.

**Summary:** Words are **revelatory symptoms** that flow from hidden moral capital, and because they flow from the heart’s accumulated state, they also entail **moral choice and accountability**. You cannot have good speech from an evil treasury, not because the mouth is powerless to choose, but because the heart’s stored abundance determines what the mouth is able—and inclined—to dispense.
