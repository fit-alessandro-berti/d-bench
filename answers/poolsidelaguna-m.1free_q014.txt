
The text in Matthew 6:19–21 addresses how our relationship to material wealth and spiritual priorities interact, particularly in terms of **possession**, **attention**, and **love**. Here's a breakdown of the key question and its implications:

---

### Does the Text Say Treasure **Reveals** the Heart or **Trains** the Heart?
**Both**, but the emphasis leans slightly toward **training** the heart through intentional choice.

1. **Revealing the Heart**:  
   The verse states, *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* This could mean that your current investments (possessions, focus, or priorities) *already indicate* the state of your heart. If you store treasures on earth (material wealth, status, etc.), it signifies an earthly focus. Conversely, heavenly treasures (e.g., acts of charity, spiritual disciplines, generosity) show a heart oriented toward eternal values. In this sense, the location of your treasures acts as a metric for self-examination.

2. **Training the Heart**:  
   However, the context of the passage provides a directive: *“But lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven…”* This is an **imperative**, implying that the act of intentionally investing in heavenly priorities will *guide or redirect* your heart. The logical structure suggests that choosing to “store up” treasures in heaven causes your heart to follow. Thus, treasure is not just a byproduct of your heart but a means of shaping it.

---

### **Possession, Attention, and Love**: How They Interconnect
The verse entwines these three elements into a cohesive web of relationships:

1. **Possession** (material or spiritual assets):  
   - Your choice to possess or invest in something (earthly vs. heavenly) determines the *direction* of your life.  
   - Earthly treasures are fleeting (subject to decay/theft), while heavenly treasures (e.g., eternal relationships, spiritual growth) are enduring.  

2. **Attention** (where you direct energy/resources):  
   - Your attention follows your treasure. The act of storing treasures—whether in accumulating possessions or serving others—takes mental and emotional energy.  
   - Example: Prioritizing savings for a material goal versus giving to the needy shifts attention away from self-centeredness toward altruism or faith (where treasures are “stored” spiritually).

3. **Love** (the heart’s attachment):  
   - The heart’s ultimate loyalty follows where your treasure lies. *“Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.”* This is both a promise and a warning: love is not passive; it is drawn to what you actively value.  
   - If you love God, you’ll “lay up” treasures in heaven (e.g., forgiveness, generosity, prayer). If you “treasure up” material wealth, love becomes tied to self-reliance or anxiety (cf. Matthew 6:22–23 on eyes being the body’s lamp).

---

### Parallels and Implications
- **Mutual Reinforcement**: Possession, attention, and love form a feedback loop. Where you direct your treasures pulls your attention, which in turn deepens your attachment (love). Conversely, what you truly love (God’s kingdom) will naturally command your attention and resources.  
- **Transformation**: The emphasis on *choice* (“lay not up… but lay up”) underscores that this relationship isn’t fixed. By deliberately shifting where we “store” treasures, we train the heart to align with eternal values.  
- **Warning and Hope**: The passage is both cautionary (earthly treasures lead to anxiety; see v. 19–21) and hopeful (heavenly treasures secure the heart’s focus on God).

---

### Conclusion
The text suggests that treasure is both a **reflection** of the heart (revealing existing priorities) and a **mechanism** for shaping it (training the heart to follow eternal priorities). The interplay among possession, attention, and love is symbiotic: all three converge on what you choose to value and invest in. In practical terms, this passage invites readers to ask: *“Where does my treasure—my time, resources, and mental energy—lead my heart, and is that aligned with God’s kingdom?”*
