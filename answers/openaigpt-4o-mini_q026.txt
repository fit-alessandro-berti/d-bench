Matthew 12:34–35 presents a profound connection between speech and the inner moral state of a person, suggesting that words are reflections of what resides in the heart. The passage emphasizes that the quality of one's speech—whether good or evil—arises from the "treasure" stored in the heart. Here’s how this connection unfolds:

1. **Symptoms**: The words a person speaks can be viewed as symptoms of their inner moral and emotional state. Just as a physical symptom can indicate a health issue, speech can indicate the underlying character and intentions of an individual. When Jesus refers to the "abundance of the heart," He suggests that what comes out in our words reveals much about who we truly are.

2. **Choices**: While the abundance of the heart is a determining factor, it’s important to recognize that individuals also exercise choice in their speech. People can choose how to express themselves, influenced by their internal "treasures." However, the tendency to speak good or evil ultimately reflects the nature of the heart’s content.

3. **Revelations**: The passage indicates that speech reveals what is truly inside a person. It acts like a window into the heart, showcasing one's character, beliefs, and moral alignments. The good person brings forth good out of a treasure of goodness, while the evil person does the opposite, making clear distinctions between the two.

In conclusion, the words we speak can indeed be seen as symptoms of deeper truths, choices influenced by our heart's condition, and revelations of our true selves. Therefore, all three aspects—symptoms, choices, and revelations—play a role in this connection between speech and the moral state of the heart as articulated in this passage.
