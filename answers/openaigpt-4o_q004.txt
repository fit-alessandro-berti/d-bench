The trust being asked for in Proverbs 3:5–6 is a deep, wholehearted reliance on God, characterized by humility and a recognition of human limitations. This passage encourages believers to trust in God completely, implying a humble self-distrust rather than mere anti-intellectualism.

Humble self-distrust involves recognizing that human understanding is finite and can be flawed. It acknowledges that despite our best efforts, our reasoning and perspectives are limited, and we can benefit from divine guidance. This mindset does not dismiss intellectual abilities or the pursuit of knowledge but places them within the context of faith, where one aligns their understanding with God's wisdom.

In contrast, anti-intellectualism is a dismissal or devaluation of intellectual efforts and reasoning. It often involves a skepticism or disdain for scholarly or scientific approaches. Proverbs 3:5–6 does not call for such a stance. Instead, it suggests that we should seek wisdom and understanding, but not to the exclusion of God's direction and insight.

Ultimately, this passage calls for a balance where intellect and faith coexist, prioritizing divine guidance while valuing the human capacity for thought and understanding. By acknowledging God's role in directing our paths, believers open themselves up to guidance that transcends their own understanding.
