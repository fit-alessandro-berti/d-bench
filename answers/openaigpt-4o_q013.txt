The passage from Amos 5:21-24 expresses a sharp rejection of the people's worship practices, not because the acts of worship themselves are inherently wrong, but because they are being performed in a context of moral and spiritual inconsistency. The reason for this sharp rejection is the disconnect between the people's religious rituals and their actual behavior, particularly in terms of justice and righteousness.

The prophet Amos is addressing a society where religious observance and ritual have become hollow and disconnected from ethical living. The people are engaging in feasts, sacrifices, and music, which are outward expressions of worship, yet they are neglecting the deeper moral obligations of justice and righteousness. This is a form of spiritual fraud because the external compliance with religious duties is being used to mask a lack of genuine commitment to God's values.

The message is that God desires more than mere ritualistic worship; He expects it to be accompanied by a life that upholds justice and righteousness. The rituals are meaningless if they are not backed by these virtues. Hence, the call is for "judgment [to] run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream," emphasizing that true worship is reflected in how people treat one another and maintain social justice, not just in their religious performances. The strong language serves to emphasize the seriousness of the issue and calls for an urgent and sincere return to true spiritual and moral integrity.
