Great question. In **Matthew 6:19–21**, Jesus is doing **both**:

1. **Revealing** the heart  
2. **Training/Forming** the heart

### 1) Treasure reveals the heart
Verse 21 is diagnostic:  
> “Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also.”

In biblical language, the **heart** is not just feelings; it is your center of desire, trust, and loyalty. So what you treat as “treasure” shows what already rules you.

### 2) Treasure also trains the heart
But verses 19–20 are commands:  
> “Do not lay up… but lay up…”

That means Jesus is not only describing reality; he is directing practice. What you repeatedly invest in (time, money, thought, sacrifice) gradually pulls your heart toward it. So treasure is also formative.

---

## How possession, attention, and love tie together

Jesus assumes a chain like this:

- **Possession / valuation** (“this is my treasure”)  
  ⮕  
- **Attention** (you watch it, think about it, protect it, plan around it)  
  ⮕  
- **Love / allegiance** (your heart gets attached)

So “treasure” is not merely what you own, but what you **prize**. And whatever you prize captures your imagination and eventually your devotion.

---

So the short answer: **Treasure both reveals and shapes the heart.** Jesus calls us to place treasure in heaven precisely because that reorients the whole person toward God.
