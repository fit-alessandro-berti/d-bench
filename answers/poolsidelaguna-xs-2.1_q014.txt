The passage in Matthew 6:19–21 presents a profound connection between **treasure**, **heart**, **possession**, **attention**, and **love**, framing them as interdependent aspects of human devotion. Here's a breakdown of how these elements interact:

---

### **1. Treasure Reveals the Heart**
The verse states, *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"* (ESV). This emphasizes that **treasure acts as a mirror of the heart's priorities**. Your treasure—whether material possessions, wealth, or even intangible values—reflects what you deeply cherish and desire. The heart’s location (its loves and interests) is revealed through the *choice* of where you invest your resources. This is not a one-time revelation but a dynamic, ongoing reflection: **where you consistently place your treasure, your heart will ultimately align with that value**.

---

### **2. Treasure Trains the Heart**
While the verse does not explicitly use the word "train," the act of *choosing* where to lay up treasure actively shapes the heart. By **prioritizing heavenly treasures over earthly ones**, believers are redirected toward eternal values. Jesus’ command to "lay up treasures in heaven" is both a *command* and a *practice*—an intentional act of redirecting attention and affection toward what is eternal. Over time, this practice disciplines the heart to love God and His kingdom more deeply. Thus, **treasure serves as a tool for training the heart** toward what is truly valuable.

---

### **3. Possession, Attention, and Love Are Intertwined**
- **Possession**: Your treasure (what you "lay up") is an expression of what you *possess*—whether material goods, relationships, or ideals. Jesus contrasts earthly treasures (vulnerable to decay and theft) with heavenly ones (eternal and secure).
- **Attention**: To "lay up" treasure is to *invest* in it—giving it your focus, resources, and care. This act of attention reinforces the treasure’s value in your life.
- **Love**: The treasure you choose reveals where your **love** lies. Love is not just an emotion but a commitment of the will, demonstrated through where you invest your time, energy, and resources.

The verse collapses these three into one: **where you place your treasure (possession), you give it your attention, and thus reveal your love**. This creates a feedback loop: your loves determine your treasures, your attention reinforces those loves, and your treasures (possessions) ultimately shape your heart’s orientation.

---

### **4. Theological Implications**
- **Divine vs. Human Values**: Jesus contrasts earthly (temporary, fragile) and heavenly (eternal, secure) treasures. This is not merely about materialism but about where one’s ultimate allegiance lies.
- **Heart Transformation**: The verse implies that the heart can be *trained* through deliberate choices. By investing in heavenly things, believers cultivate a love for God and His kingdom, aligning their hearts with eternal priorities.
- **Sovereignty of the Heart**: The heart is not a passive vessel; it responds to where treasure is placed. Thus, the verse calls for intentional redirection of love and attention.

---

### **Key Takeaways**
- **Treasure reveals the heart** by showing where its deepest values lie.
- **Treasure trains the heart** by shaping it through repeated acts of devotion and redirection.
- **Possession, attention, and love** are inseparable: the treasure you choose reflects your loves, which direct your attention, and this in turn shapes your heart’s orientation.

In essence, Matthew 6:19–21 teaches that **our choices about treasure are both symptoms and shaping forces of the heart**. It calls believers to a radical reorientation of love, attention, and possession toward what is eternal and secure in God’s kingdom.
