Matthew 6:19–21 presents a profound connection between treasure, the heart, and the orientation of one's life. The text does not explicitly state whether treasure *reveals* the heart or *trains* it, but it strongly implies both dynamics are at work, intertwined with possession, attention, and love. Here’s how these elements tie together:

### 1. **Treasure Reveals the Heart**
   - The verse concludes with a diagnostic statement: *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* This suggests that the location of one's treasure (earthly or heavenly) **discloses** the true allegiance of the heart. Jesus is observing a natural correlation: what you value most (your treasure) exposes what you love most (your heart).
   - **Possession as Revelation**: What you own or pursue (possessions, status, security) reveals your priorities. Earthly treasures (wealth, material goods) often compete with devotion to God (Matt. 6:24), so their presence or absence can expose divided loyalties.
   - **Attention as Revelation**: Where you invest time, energy, and focus (attention) signals what you treasure. If your mind is consumed by earthly concerns (e.g., anxiety over wealth, as in Matt. 6:25–34), your heart is likely anchored there.

### 2. **Treasure Trains the Heart**
   - While the text emphasizes revelation, the command to *"lay up treasures in heaven"* implies an active shaping of the heart. Treasure doesn’t just reflect the heart passively; it **molds** it over time.
   - **Possession as Formation**: What you accumulate (or refuse to accumulate) shapes your desires. Hoarding earthly wealth can harden the heart toward greed (1 Tim. 6:10), while generosity or investment in heavenly things (e.g., acts of love, justice, or worship) reorients the heart toward God.
   - **Attention as Formation**: Jesus’ warning about moth, rust, and thieves highlights the futility of earthly treasure. Directing attention toward eternal things (e.g., God’s kingdom, Matt. 6:33) trains the heart to find security and joy in what cannot be lost.

### 3. **The Unity of Possession, Attention, and Love**
   - **Possession**: What you *have* (or seek to have) reflects and reinforces what you love. Earthly treasure is fleeting, so clinging to it reveals a misplaced love. Heavenly treasure (e.g., relationship with God, righteousness) is enduring, so pursuing it aligns love with eternity.
   - **Attention**: What you *focus on* (earthly or heavenly) both reveals and cultivates your affections. Anxiety over material loss (Matt. 6:19) betrays a heart tied to the temporal, while trust in God’s provision (Matt. 6:20–21) nurtures a heart tied to the eternal.
   - **Love**: The heart’s love is the engine behind both possession and attention. Jesus assumes that love and treasure are inseparable: *"where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* This is a two-way street:
     - Your love determines your treasure (you invest in what you love).
     - Your treasure shapes your love (what you invest in, you grow to love).

### **Conclusion**
The passage suggests a **dynamic interplay**:
- **Revelation**: Treasure *shows* where the heart is (diagnostic).
- **Formation**: Treasure *moves* the heart toward what it values (formative).

Possession, attention, and love are bound together because:
- What you **possess** (or pursue) reveals and reinforces what you **love**.
- What you **pay attention to** both exposes and deepens your **love**.
- Your **love** drives you to **possess** and **attend to** certain treasures.

Thus, Jesus’ teaching is both a **mirror** (revealing the heart’s current state) and a **plow** (directing the heart toward heavenly priorities). The call is to align treasure, attention, and love with God’s eternal kingdom, not the fleeting things of earth.
