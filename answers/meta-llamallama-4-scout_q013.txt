The passage from Amos 5:21-24 is a scathing critique of the Israelites' worship practices, rejecting their religious rituals and festivities not because they were imperfect or lacking in some way, but because they were being practiced in a manner that was disconnected from genuine moral and spiritual commitment. Here's a breakdown of why the passage rejects worship so sharply and the kind of moral or spiritual fraud being exposed:

1. **Disconnect between Worship and Ethical Living**: The Israelites were observing religious rituals such as feast days, offering burnt offerings, meat offerings, and peace offerings, and enjoying musical worship. However, these acts were not accompanied by a life of justice and righteousness. The passage suggests that their worship was hypocritical because it was not integrated with a lifestyle that reflected the values they were professing in their worship.

2. **Superficial Religiosity**: The Israelites seemed to be going through the motions of worship, but their hearts and actions were not aligned with the true spirit of their faith. They were more concerned with the external acts of worship rather than the internal transformation and the ethical implications of their faith. This superficial religiosity is a form of moral or spiritual fraud because it pretends to honor God while ignoring His true requirements.

3. **God's Rejection as a Call to Authentic Faith**: By rejecting their worship, God is not abolishing the practices outright but is highlighting the hollowness of their religious observance. The rejection is a call to return to a more authentic form of faith that integrates worship with righteous living.

4. **Prioritizing Ritual over Righteousness**: The Israelites were prioritizing ritual observance over living righteously. They were focusing on the external aspects of their faith (rituals, sacrifices, music) at the expense of the internal and ethical aspects (justice, righteousness). This prioritization is seen as a form of spiritual fraud because it misrepresents the true nature of their relationship with God.

5. **Prophetic Call for Justice and Righteousness**: The passage ends with a call for "judgment to run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream." This imagery indicates a desire for an abundant, continuous flow of justice and righteousness, suggesting that these are the true measures of a community's faithfulness to God. The call is not to abandon worship but to ensure that it is accompanied by a life of justice and righteousness.

The moral or spiritual fraud being exposed here is the substitution of ritual and religious observance for genuine faith and righteous living. The passage is not arguing against the practices of worship per se but against their abuse as a means to evade the true demands of faith, which include living justly and righteously. The sharp rejection is meant to shock the Israelites into recognizing the disparity between their religious practices and their ethical failures, calling them back to a more authentic and holistic expression of their faith.
