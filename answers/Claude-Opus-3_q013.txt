This passage from the Book of Amos delivers a stinging rebuke of worship practices that are disconnected from justice and righteousness. The sharp rejection of feasts, offerings, and songs reveals that these acts of worship have become spiritually bankrupt because the worshippers' lives do not reflect the values and commands of God.

The prophet Amos, speaking on behalf of God, is exposing the hypocrisy and emptiness of religious rituals when those performing them are not living justly. The people are going through the motions of worship - celebrating feasts, presenting offerings, singing songs - but their worship is fraudulent because their day-to-day conduct is devoid of justice, mercy and compassion for others, especially the poor and vulnerable. 

Their worship has become self-serving and superficial rather than a authentic expression of reverence, humility and obedience to God. They mistakenly believe that elaborate religious ceremonies alone will earn God's favor, even while they ignore His clear directives to "let justice roll on like a river, righteousness like a never-failing stream!"

The jarring language – "I hate, I despise your religious festivals...I cannot stand your assemblies" – reveals the depth of God's disdain for worship without integrity. He has zero tolerance for the disconnect between pious rituals and unrighteous living. 

Amos is not suggesting worship should be abandoned for social justice. Rather, true worship and justice must go hand-in-hand. Worship is fraudulent when the worshipper's life is marked by injustice, greed, idolatry or mistreatment of others. Conversely, pursuing justice and defending the oppressed is itself an act of worship that honors God.

The passage calls the people to account – their worship is detestable to God because their conduct is detestable. Amos urges them to let "justice roll on like a river", allowing God's standards of righteousness to flow through every aspect of their private and public lives. Only then will their worship be acceptable. Rituals devoid of righteousness are worthless to God.
