The idea presented in this passage is that light represents truth and righteousness, while darkness represents evil and deceit. When light shines, it exposes what is hidden in darkness, bringing things to light and revealing the truth. This can be uncomfortable and even shameful for those who are engaging in or hiding from wrongdoing.

People who are doing evil deeds often prefer to remain hidden in the darkness, away from the light that would expose their actions. They fear their deeds will be reproved or judged if brought to light. This could be due to a sense of shame, guilt, or a desire to avoid consequences for their actions.

On the other hand, those who live according to truth and righteousness are not afraid of the light. They willingly come into the light because they have nothing to hide. They want their deeds to be seen and recognized as coming from a place of goodness and Godliness.

In essence, the aversion to light and truth comes from a fear of exposure and the potential consequences that may follow. It is easier to hide in the darkness and continue in wrongdoing than to face the truth and make changes in one's life. Ultimately, this passage highlights the importance of seeking and embracing truth, even when it may be uncomfortable or challenging.
