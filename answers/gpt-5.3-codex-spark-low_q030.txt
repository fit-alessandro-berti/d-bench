• Because these verses define love as a chosen character, not a feeling.

  The negatives are doing two things:

  1. Setting the boundary of what love is not
      - Pride, envy, arrogance, vanity, quick temper, and suspicion are all ego-centered habits.
      - Paul is removing anything that makes love self-protective or self-promoting.
  2. Shaping love into active endurance
      - Love is not passive “weakness”; it is a disciplined will that refuses to control, retaliate, or resent.
      - “Rejoiceth not in iniquity” forbids delight in another’s failure; “believeth…hopeth…endureth all things” is
        about resilience under strain.

  So the proposed strength is paradoxical strength: power over oneself.
  Not dominance over others, but mastery over ego. In this portrait, strength is measured by how steady, generous, and
  truth-honoring someone remains when they have every reason to become defensive, proud, or cruel.
