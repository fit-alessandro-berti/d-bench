This passage from Gospel of Matthew suggests both interpretations: treasure reveals the heart and also trains it, although the primary emphasis is arguably on how treasure directs and shapes the heart.

The verse states explicitly: "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also," implying that the location and nature of what we value determine where our attention, love, and ultimately our character will be drawn. Treasure here is not just a passive indicator; it actively shapes and conditions the heart.

### Treasure as Revealer:

* On one level, our possessions and values provide a clear picture of our inner priorities. The things we invest our resources in (time, energy, money) expose what we truly care about, often even more accurately than our words.

### Treasure as Trainer:

* More deeply, however, the text implies that choosing where to place treasure intentionally guides the heart. Investing in earthly things—material goods vulnerable to loss—anchors the heart in anxiety and transience. Conversely, investing in heavenly treasures—such as acts of compassion, justice, humility, and spiritual integrity—cultivates the heart toward enduring values and eternal perspective.

### Connection of Possession, Attention, and Love:

* Possession anchors attention. What we own or invest in naturally draws our mental focus.
* Attention nurtures love. Constant attention to something gradually deepens emotional attachment and shapes affection.
* Love ultimately shapes identity. What we love defines who we become.

Thus, Matthew 6:19–21 highlights the dynamic interaction between our choices (treasures) and our inner life (the heart). It urges deliberate placement of value—so that possessions, attention, and love lead to spiritual depth rather than superficial insecurity.
