 The trust being asked for in Proverbs 3:5-6 is not mere anti-intellectualism or a rejection of one's own understanding. Instead, it is a call for humble trust in God. Humble self-distrust means recognizing the limitations of one's own knowledge and understanding, and acknowledging that God's wisdom and guidance are greater. It is not a rejection of the intellect or reason, but rather a recognition of the need for God's guidance in all areas of life.

Mere anti-intellectualism, on the other hand, is a rejection of reason and knowledge in favor of unquestioning faith or emotion. It can lead to irrational beliefs and actions, and is not in line with the biblical call to love God with all our mind (Matthew 22:37).

So, the trust being asked for in Proverbs 3:5-6 is a humble trust that acknowledges the limitations of our own understanding and seeks God's guidance in all things. It is a trust that recognizes the importance of both reason and faith, and seeks to integrate them in a way that honors God and brings wisdom and understanding.
