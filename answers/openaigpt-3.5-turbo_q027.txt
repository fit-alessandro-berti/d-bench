The Pharisee is condemned for his pride, comparison, and contempt towards others. His attitude of self-righteousness and looking down upon others is what ultimately condemns him in this parable. On the other hand, the tax collector's posture of humility, repentance, and acknowledgment of his own sinfulness is seen as spiritually truer because it reflects a genuine self-awareness and dependence on God's mercy. The tax collector recognizes his need for forgiveness and approaches God with a contrite heart, which is in contrast to the Pharisee's self-righteous attitude. This parable emphasizes the importance of humility, repentance, and not comparing ourselves to others in our spiritual life.
