In analyzing Matthew 6:19–21, the answer is not an "either/or" but a "both/and." The text describes a dynamic relationship where treasure acts as both a **diagnostic tool** (revealing the heart) and a **gravitational force** (training the heart).

Here is how possession, attention, and love become tied together in these verses:

### 1. Treasure Reveals the Heart (The Diagnostic)
The concluding statement, *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also,"* functions as a spiritual x-ray. 
*   **The Indicator:** Jesus suggests that if you want to know what a person truly loves, you don't look at their professions of faith; you look at their ledger. 
*   **The Logic:** The "heart" in Hebraic thought is the seat of the will, intellect, and emotions. By identifying where a person invests their best resources (time, money, talent), you identify the object of their highest devotion. In this sense, treasure is the **revealer** of current priorities.

### 2. Treasure Trains the Heart (The Gravitational)
While the text *reveals* the heart, the command to *"lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven"* suggests that the heart can be *led*.
*   **Motion Follows Investment:** The verse does not say, "Where your heart is, there you will put your treasure." It says the opposite: the heart *follows* the treasure. 
*   **The Training:** By consciously redirecting physical resources away from "moth and rust" (earthly security) and toward "heaven" (God’s kingdom and purposes), a person effectively "tethers" their heart to the divine. If you invest deeply in a cause, you inevitably begin to care about that cause. Thus, treasure is the **trainer** that pulls the heart toward a specific destination.

### 3. How Possession, Attention, and Love Tie Together
The passage illustrates a psychological and spiritual cycle:

*   **Possession (The Anchor):** What we "possessing" or "lay up" creates an anchor. Earthly possessions are inherently fragile (subject to decay and theft). If our anchor is in the earth, our sense of security is constantly threatened, leading to anxiety.
*   **Attention (The Focus):** The verses immediately following this passage (6:22–23) speak about the "eye" being the lamp of the body. What we treasure dictates our focus. If we treasure earthly things, our attention is consumed by the maintenance, protection, and accumulation of those things.
*   **Love (The Result):** Love is the "heart" mentioned in verse 21. Love is not a static feeling; it is an orientation of the soul. When our **possessions** are placed in God's hands and our **attention** is fixed on eternal values, our **love** (the heart) becomes securely fastened to heaven.

### Summary
The text presents a **Law of Spiritual Gravity**: Your heart is not a free agent; it is a follower. It is caught in the orbit of whatever you value most. Therefore, Jesus’ command is a practical strategy for spiritual formation: **If you want your heart to love God more, move your treasures into His kingdom, and your heart will inevitably follow.**
