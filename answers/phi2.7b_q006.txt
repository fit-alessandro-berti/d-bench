 The self being denied in this passage appears to be the ego or the desire for personal convenience and enjoyment. The Jesus is asking his disciples to deny themselves and follow him in order to fully commit to his teachings and beliefs. This can be seen as liberating as it challenges the notion of individualism and emphasizes the importance of serving a higher purpose and following a moral code. However, some may find it offensive as it requires a level of submission and self-denial that may be uncomfortable for those who prioritize personal liberty and freedom.
