In Matthew 6:19–21 Jesus says both, but in a particular order:

1. **Treasure reveals the heart.**  
   “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” is a statement of *diagnosis*: your treasures show where your heart already is. What you have invested in—money, time, security, status—exposes what you actually love and trust.

2. **Treasure also trains the heart.**  
   The commands that come *before* the statement—“Lay not up for yourselves treasures upon earth… but lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven”—are a prescription. Jesus assumes we can *choose* where to put our treasure, and that choice will in turn shape our desires, attention, and affection.

So the text presents treasure and heart as tied together in a kind of loop:

- **Possession → Attention → Love**  
  What you possess (or pursue as your own) captures your attention. What captures your attention gradually captures your love. And what you love becomes what you call “treasure.”

- **Love → Attention → Possession**  
  Conversely, what you love directs your attention, and your attention directs how you spend your resources.

In short, **treasure is both a mirror and a magnet**: it shows where your heart is, and it draws your heart to where it is placed.
