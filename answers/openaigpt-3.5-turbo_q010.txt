The power being praised here is the power of humility. It is the ability to willingly set aside one's own status, reputation, and rights for the sake of serving others. 

Humility joined to greatness can feel more unsettling than open domination because it goes against the common understanding of power and authority. In a world where strength and assertiveness are often valued, humility can be seen as weakness. However, true greatness lies in the ability to humble oneself and serve others, as demonstrated by Christ in this passage. It challenges our preconceived notions of power and invites us to consider a different way of leading and relating to others.
