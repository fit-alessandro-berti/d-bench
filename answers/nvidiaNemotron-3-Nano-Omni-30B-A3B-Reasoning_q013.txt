The passage’s abrupt, uncompromising rejection of Israel’s worship reveals a **fundamental moral‑spiritual fraud**: they are performing religious rituals while their social and ethical life is corrupt.  

1. **Hypocritical ritualism** – The “feast days,” “burnt offerings,” “meat offerings,” and “peace offerings” are outward acts that the prophet says God “will not accept.” The worship is reduced to a **formal, empty performance** that masks inner injustice.  

2. **Suppression of true justice** – By demanding that “the noise of your songs” be taken away, Amos shows that God cares **only about the justice that flows like water** (“let judgment run down as waters”). The worship is a **distraction** from the real requirement: a society where the poor are protected, oppression is curbed, and righteousness is lived out.  

3. **Moral fraud** – The people are **complicit in oppression** (e.g., exploitation, corruption, neglect of the needy) while pretending piety. This is a classic case of **“religion as a cover”**—using sacred rites to legitimize or conceal unjust behavior. The fraud is not merely a lack of balance; it is a **deliberate misalignment** where ritual replaces ethical integrity.  

Thus, the passage condemns a **spiritual fraud** in which outward religiosity is used to **obscure and justify systemic injustice**, rather than a simple call for a more harmonious blend of worship and ethics.
