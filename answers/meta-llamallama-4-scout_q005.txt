### Why Enemy-Love is Challenging to Take Seriously

The command to love one's enemies, as stated in Matthew 5:44-45, presents a profound moral and spiritual challenge. There are several reasons why enemy-love can be difficult to take seriously:

#### 1. **Counterintuitive Nature**

Loving one's enemies goes against the natural human inclination to respond to hostility with hostility. Our instincts often drive us to defend ourselves and retaliate against those who wrong us. For example, in the face of verbal aggression, it's natural to want to fire back with a witty retort or a sharp comeback. However, enemy-love requires us to transcend this instinctual response and choose a different path.

#### 2. **Perceived Weakness**

Some people may view enemy-love as a sign of weakness or passivity. If someone is being hurtful or aggressive, it can seem that loving them would make us vulnerable to further harm. For instance, in a situation where someone is being bullied, it may seem that standing up for oneself or fighting back is the only way to protect oneself. However, enemy-love encourages us to respond to aggression with non-violent resistance and compassion.

#### 3. **Difficulty in Defining "Love"**

The concept of love can be abstract and open to interpretation. What does it mean to "love" someone who is harming or opposing us? Is it merely tolerating them, or does it involve a deeper emotional or spiritual connection? For example, in the context of romantic relationships, love is often associated with feelings of affection and attachment. However, in the context of enemy-love, love may involve a more selfless and sacrificial approach.

### Is Enemy-Love Sentimental, Strategic, or a Deeper Form of Moral Strength?

Enemy-love is not primarily sentimental, strategic, or a superficial display of moral strength. Rather, it involves a profound transformation of the heart and a reevaluation of one's values and priorities.

#### 1. **Not Merely Sentimental**

While sentimental feelings may accompany enemy-love, it is not based on emotions or a superficial sense of niceness. Enemy-love requires a deep commitment to treat others with kindness and compassion, even when it's difficult or uncomfortable. For instance, Mother Teresa's selfless care for the poor and marginalized is an example of enemy-love in action. Her actions were not driven by sentimental feelings, but by a deep sense of compassion and empathy.

#### 2. **Not Purely Strategic**

Some may view enemy-love as a strategic approach to conflict resolution or social manipulation. However, this perspective reduces enemy-love to a means to an end, rather than an end in itself. Jesus' teaching on enemy-love emphasizes the intrinsic value of treating others with love and respect, regardless of the outcome. For example, in the context of international diplomacy, a strategic approach to enemy-love may involve using kindness and compassion as a means to achieve a specific goal. However, this approach neglects the deeper moral and spiritual implications of enemy-love.

#### 3. **A Deeper Form of Moral Strength**

Enemy-love requires a profound level of moral strength, self-awareness, and spiritual maturity. It involves:

* **Self-reflection and humility**: Recognizing one's own flaws and vulnerabilities, and being willing to let go of pride and ego.
* **Empathy and compassion**: Understanding the humanity and dignity of those who oppose or harm us, and responding with kindness and care.
* **Courage and resilience**: Standing firm in the face of adversity, and choosing to respond with love and non-violence, even when it's difficult or costly.

In the context of Matthew 5:44-45, enemy-love is presented as a way to embody the character of God, who shows kindness and generosity to both the good and the evil. By loving our enemies, we demonstrate our commitment to following Jesus' teachings and our desire to be children of God.

### Conclusion

In conclusion, enemy-love is a challenging and complex concept that requires a deeper understanding of its moral and spiritual implications. While it may seem counterintuitive or weak to some, enemy-love is actually a profound expression of moral strength, self-awareness, and spiritual maturity. By embracing enemy-love, we can cultivate a more compassionate and empathetic approach to those who oppose or harm us, and demonstrate our commitment to following Jesus' teachings.
