Great question.  
Psalm 139:23–24 models a **radically different posture** than “God, prove I’m right” or “God, make me feel better.”

To ask God to **search** you means:

1. **I may not fully know myself.**  
   The psalmist admits self-deception is possible—hidden motives, fears, pride, resentments.

2. **I want truth more than comfort.**  
   Comfort can soothe symptoms; searching exposes roots. This is a prayer for healing at the source.

3. **I trust God’s love enough to be examined.**  
   Divine scrutiny isn’t requested from terror, but from relationship: “You know me, and I’m safe with You.”

4. **I want transformation, not just protection.**  
   “See if there is any wicked way in me” is repentance language.  
   “Lead me in the way everlasting” is discipleship language.

So the honesty modeled here is **humble, courageous self-surrender**:  
“God, don’t just stand between me and my enemies—stand between me and my own blindness.”
