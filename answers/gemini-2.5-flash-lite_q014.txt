This passage from Matthew 6:19-21 presents a profound connection between treasure, the heart, and how our affections are directed. Let's break down your questions:

**Does this text say treasure reveals the heart, or that treasure trains the heart?**

The text primarily suggests that **treasure reveals the heart**, but it also strongly implies that **treasure trains the heart** as a consequence of this revelation.

*   **Reveals the Heart:** The concluding verse, "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also," is a direct statement of revelation. It asserts that the object of our ultimate pursuit, what we dedicate our resources and efforts to accumulating, is a clear indicator of what we truly value and where our deepest affections lie. Our "treasure" is a mirror reflecting the state of our heart.

*   **Trains the Heart:** While not stated as directly, the implication is powerful. If our hearts follow our treasures, then by *choosing* to lay up treasures in heaven, we are actively training our hearts to be directed there. Conversely, if our treasures are earthly, our hearts will inevitably be drawn to them, conditioning our desires and priorities. The act of "laying up" suggests a deliberate effort, and this effort, over time, shapes our inner landscape. The text encourages a redirection of our efforts, which in turn will redirect our hearts.

**How do possession, attention, and love become tied together here?**

The passage intricately links possession, attention, and love through the concept of "treasure" and its influence on the "heart."

*   **Possession (and the Pursuit of it):** "Treasures" are inherently about what we possess or what we are actively striving to possess. The passage contrasts two types of possessions: earthly ones (subject to decay and theft) and heavenly ones (eternal and secure). The act of "laying up" emphasizes the pursuit and accumulation of these treasures. This pursuit itself requires an investment of our resources – time, energy, and material wealth.

*   **Attention (and Direction):** Where our treasure lies, our attention will follow. If we are accumulating earthly treasures, our thoughts and efforts will be consumed by managing, protecting, and acquiring more of them. Our gaze will be fixed on the temporal. Conversely, if we "lay up treasures in heaven," our attention will be drawn to spiritual realities, eternal values, and things that endure beyond this life. The act of laying up treasure *directs* our focus.

*   **Love (and Affection):** This is where possession and attention coalesce into love. Jesus states, "where your treasure is, there will your heart be also." The "heart" in biblical terms represents the core of our being – our desires, affections, will, and motivations. We love what we treasure. Our deepest love is reserved for that which we hold most valuable. If our treasure is earthly wealth, our love will be tied to material possessions and the security they offer. If our treasure is heavenly, our love will be directed towards God, righteousness, and eternal things. The passage suggests that by *choosing* what we treasure, we are effectively choosing what we will love.

In essence, the text argues that our **possessions** (or the pursuit of them) demand our **attention**, and that this directed attention, sustained over time, cultivates and solidifies our **love**. Therefore, the object of our "treasure" becomes the focal point of our entire inner life, revealing and shaping our deepest affections.
