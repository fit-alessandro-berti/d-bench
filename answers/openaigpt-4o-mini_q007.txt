The verse from Luke 6:37 emphasizes the ideas of judgment, condemnation, and forgiveness, advocating for a compassionate approach to others. However, it does not imply that moral judgments are meaningless; rather, it delineates the manner and attitude with which those judgments should be approached.

### Key Differences Between Moral Judgments and Condemnation

1. **Nature of Discernment vs. Condemnation**:
   - **Discernment** is the ability to judge well, to make thoughtful distinctions regarding right and wrong based on factors such as context, intent, and consequences. It is a necessary part of ethical reasoning and decision-making.
   - **Condemnation**, on the other hand, is often characterized by a harsh, inflexible attitude that seeks to assign guilt and blame without consideration of nuance or understanding. It can be an expression of superiority, a desire to punish, or a means of distancing oneself from others.

2. **Motivation**:
   - The motivation behind discernment is often rooted in love, understanding, and the desire for growth—both for oneself and others. It aims for improvement and the betterment of situations.
   - In contrast, the appetite to condemn frequently stems from a sense of moral superiority or the need to exert control. It can foster division and bitterness rather than healing and reconciliation.

3. **Context of Judgment**:
   - Discernment can lead to constructive criticism, encouragement, and guidance. It engages with the complexities of human behavior and acknowledges the potential for change and forgiveness.
   - Condemnation tends to oversimplify moral situations, often ignoring the context and the humanity of individuals involved. It does not allow for redemption or the possibility of change.

### The Call for Forgiveness

The second part of the verse—“forgive, and ye shall be forgiven”—highlights the value of compassion and the imperative to extend grace to others. While moral judgments play a role in identifying right from wrong, the call to forgiveness emphasizes the need for humility and the recognition that everyone is fallible. 

### Conclusion

Ultimately, Luke 6:37 advocates for a balanced approach: we are called to discern right from wrong but to do so with an understanding of our own faults and with a spirit of forgiveness. This verse does not suggest that moral judgments are irrelevant; rather, it emphasizes how we should engage in moral reasoning—not from a place of superiority or a desire to condemn, but with love, humility, and a readiness to forgive. Discernment can coexist with compassion, leading to a more constructive and understanding society.
