The passage from John 3:19–21 elaborates on why light is "hated" by those who engage in deeds that are considered evil. To understand this, we need to explore the symbolic meanings of "light" and the motivations of those who associate with darkness.

1. **Symbolism of Light**: In the biblical context, "light" often symbolizes truth, goodness, holiness, and God's presence. Light exposes that which is hidden, bringing clarity and revealing reality. It is associated with enlightenment, understanding, and divine revelation.

2. **Exposure and Vulnerability**: Those who commit evil deeds may fear exposure because being exposed removes the shield of anonymity and deceit. Light, by making everything visible, leaves no room for hiding. It brings one's true nature and actions into focus.

3. **Truth and Accountability**: The passage highlights that light is related to truth, and truth is linked with accountability. When one is brought into the light, their actions are open to scrutiny and judgment. There is an implicit accountability associated with light, as one's deeds are made visible and can be judged by others as well as by a higher moral or divine standard.

4. **Shame and Fear of Condemnation**: There's an underlying fear of shame and condemnation among those who "do evil" when exposed to the light. Evil deeds, typically done in the dark (or hidden from the public eye), become sources of shame when revealed in the light. This shame can be both social (from the community) and spiritual (from God).

5. **Desire to Avoid Transparency**: Evil deeds are often conducted in secrecy because their perpetrators understand they will not stand up to the ethical and moral standards that "light" exposes. There's a desire not to be seen clearly because transparency reveals the discrepancy between one's actions and one's professed values or beliefs.

6. **Contrast with Doing Good**: Conversely, those who "do truth" come to the light willingly because their actions are aligned with truth and goodness. They have no fear of exposure because their deeds can withstand scrutiny and are inherently worthy of making manifest in the presence of God. This positive association with light signifies righteousness, integrity, and a desire for truth to prevail.

In summary, light is "hated" by those who do evil because it represents truth, exposure, and accountability, all of which threaten to bring shame and condemnation upon them. Conversely, light is embraced by those who do good because it aligns with their deeds and values, demonstrating their integrity and righteousness.
