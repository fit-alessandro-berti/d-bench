The passage from Luke 6:37 emphasizes the importance of emotional, ethical, and spiritual discernment over the zealous pursuit of moral condemnation. This differs significantly from the assertion that moral judgments never matter, focusing instead on the ethical path of forgiveness and empathy. Let’s break this down:

---

### **Moral Judgments vs. Discretion:**
1. **Moral Judgments:** These are intentions or principles (e.g., honesty, compassion, justice) that dictate actions in accordance with one’s moral code. They are internal standards that an individual uses to assess the rightness or wrongness of behavior. Micromanagement in moral judgments can lead to judgmental attitudes or unrealistically high expectations.
2. **Discernment:** Discernment involves careful consideration and analysis of different choices, goals, or circumstances. It requires understanding the underlying reasons and consequences of actions, allowing for a balanced judgment. This process fosters wisdom and allows for nuanced responses adjusted to unique contexts.

**Difference:** While moral judgments direct behavior toward ethical standards, discernment balances ethical intentions with practical realities, personal growth, and adaptability. Discernment allows for flexibility in moral positions as new information emerges, whereas moral judgments may rigidly uphold previous principles.

---

### **Apites to Complain vs. appetite to condemn:**
1. **Apites to Complain:** This term refers to the instinctual drive to criticize or point out flaws in others, often driven by self-righteousness or small-mindedness. It assumes readiness to perpetual verbal or emotional battle against perceived weaknesses.
2. **Appeite to condemn:** This suggests an inherent desire to blame, accuse, or criticize, often leading to hurtful or unproductive outcomes. It implies a closed mindset focused on negative aspects rather than growth or understanding.

**Difference:** The former encourages open dialogue with empathy, the latter with closed-mindedness and criticism. The former cultivates understanding through discernment, while the latter thrives on accusation.

---

### **Forgiveness vs. Condemnation:**
1. **Forgiveness:** Lived action based on understanding, grace, and empathy. It involves releasing resentment, allowing forgiveness, and seeking reconciliation without condoning wrongdoing.
2. **Condemnation:** Action based on judgment, botsch, and reproach. It may involve harsh statements, Permanent evaluation of others, and a refusal to accept their flaws or mistakes.

**Difference:** Forgiveness fosters healing and unity, while condemnation isolates and maintains divisions. Forgiveness promotes peace, whereas condemnation perpetuates conflict.

---

### **Conclusion:**
This passage from Luke 6:37 underscores a strong distinction between moral judgments as internal rules and discernment as practical, adaptive responses. Discretion allows for nuanced moral positions, while apites to condemn emphasizes rigid moralistic attitudes. Forgiveness, rooted in discernment, contrasts with condemnation, which prioritizes judgment. These concepts collectively shape ethical behavior by encouraging empathy, understanding, and growth rather than condemnation and judgment.
