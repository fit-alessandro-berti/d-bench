This passage from Matthew 12:34-35 powerfully connects speech with "hidden moral stockpiles in the heart" by presenting the heart as the **source and reservoir** from which our words flow. Let's break down how it achieves this and what it suggests about the nature of our speech:

**Connecting Speech with Hidden Moral Stockpiles:**

The core of the connection lies in the phrase: **"for out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh."**

*   **Abundance:** This word implies a large quantity, a plentiful store. It suggests that the heart isn't just passively holding things, but is full, overflowing with them. This "abundance" is the "hidden moral stockpile."
*   **Heart as a Treasure Chest:** The subsequent verses further clarify this by contrasting a "good man" with an "evil man," and their respective "treasures." The heart is presented as a treasure chest, or a storehouse.
    *   A **good man** has a **good treasure** in his heart, and from this, he brings forth **good things** (words).
    *   An **evil man** has an **evil treasure** in his heart, and from this, he brings forth **evil things** (words).

Therefore, the passage argues that our spoken words are not random occurrences. Instead, they are the direct **manifestation** of what is deeply ingrained within us, the accumulated "stuff" (thoughts, desires, beliefs, attitudes) that fills our inner being.

**Does it suggest that words are symptoms, choices, revelations, or all three?**

This passage suggests that words are **all three**, and more. Let's examine each:

*   **Symptoms:** Yes, words are undeniably **symptoms**. The "abundance of the heart" that spills out through the mouth is a diagnostic indicator of the state of the heart. Just as a fever is a symptom of illness, evil speech is a symptom of an evil heart, and good speech is a symptom of a good heart. The words betray what lies beneath the surface.

*   **Choices:** While the passage emphasizes the automatic outflow from the "abundance," it doesn't entirely negate the element of **choice**. The very fact that the heart can be filled with "good" or "evil" implies a process of accumulation. This accumulation, over time, is influenced by choices we make regarding what we dwell on, what we believe, and what we cultivate within ourselves. Furthermore, once the "abundance" is present, the *choice* to speak, or not to speak, still exists. However, the passage strongly implies that if our hearts are filled with evil, the *natural inclination* and *easiest outflow* will be evil words, making the "choice" to speak good things incredibly difficult. Conversely, for a good heart, the outflow of good words is the natural, easy choice.

*   **Revelations:** Absolutely, words are powerful **revelations**. They reveal the true character and inner disposition of the speaker. The passage explicitly states that the mouth "speaketh" what is in the heart. Our words are therefore a window into our soul, exposing what we truly value, what we believe, and what we are. This is why Jesus is so direct in calling them "vipers" – their words are a poisonous revelation of their ungodly nature.

**In summary, Matthew 12:34-35 proposes that:**

*   Our words are **symptoms** of the internal state of our hearts.
*   The accumulation that fills our hearts is a result of ongoing **choices** and influences.
*   Our words are direct **revelations** of these hidden moral stockpiles, exposing our true selves to ourselves and others.

The passage emphasizes the **inevitability** of this connection. It's not about holding back evil words while harboring an evil heart; it's about the fundamental idea that the internal "treasure" will inevitably find its external expression. This is a call for internal transformation, recognizing that true change in speech must begin with a change in the heart.
