In Isaiah 55:6–7 the prophet pairs **“his way”** and **“his thoughts”** because genuine repentance must reach the whole person: not only what we do, but how we think, plan, and imagine.

- **“Way”** = outward conduct, the path someone walks, their daily behavior, habits, and lifestyle.  
- **“Thoughts”** = inward reasoning, schemes, motives, assumptions, and the imagination of the heart.

By commanding both, the text teaches:

1. **Repentance is more than behavior modification.** It is possible to stop certain sinful actions while still cherishing the same rebellious thoughts, resentments, or self-justifying reasoning. God calls for the “unrighteous man” to abandon those too.

2. **Repentance is more than private feeling.** It is possible to feel sorry or have religious thoughts while continuing in the same sinful “way.” The “wicked” must actually forsake his path.

3. **Sin and renewal are comprehensive.** The pairing of “way” and “thoughts” covers outer and inner life, action and intention, body and mind. True returning to the Lord involves a whole-soul reorientation.

This also highlights the **possibility and generosity of mercy**. The call is urgent—“while he may be found … while he is near”—but it is also open: the wicked and unrighteous are invited to return. And God’s response is not grudging or partial: *“he will have mercy … he will abundantly pardon.”* The word translated “abundantly” suggests a rich, overflowing forgiveness for everyone who turns from both evil ways and unrighteous thoughts to the Lord.
