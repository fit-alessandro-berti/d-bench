 
The text is presenting bitterness and forgiveness as contrasting attitudes or choices. Bitterness, being the source of strife and division, is portrayed as a negative and detrimental emotion. On the other hand, forgiveness, particularly one that is tender-hearted and forgiving, is seen as a means to move from the bitterness and cultivate a more harmonious relationship. The text suggests that forgiveness is a practice or posture that must be actively chosen. It may be inferred that the text is suggesting that choosing to forgive and practicing tolerance and kindness towards others can be a way of breaking free from bitterness and its negative impacts. However, whether bitterness is seen as an emotion or a state of mind or a contagious attitude is open to interpretation.
