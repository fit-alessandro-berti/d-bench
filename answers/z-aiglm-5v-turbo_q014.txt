Based on the text of Matthew 6:19–21, the answer to whether treasure **reveals** the heart or **trains** the heart is nuanced. Most theologians and biblical scholars would argue the text supports **both**, but the specific grammar of verse 21 leans heavily toward **causality** (training/orientation).

Here is an analysis of how possession, attention, and love are tied together in this passage.

### 1. Does Treasure Reveal or Train the Heart?

To answer this, we must look at the function of the word "**For**" (*gar* in Greek) at the start of verse 21.

> *"Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven... **For** where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."*

Jesus is giving a reason (ground) for the command He just gave. He is saying, "Do X, **because** doing X results in Y." This structure suggests that the location of your treasure has a magnetic pull on your spirit.

#### The Argument for "Revealing" (Diagnostic)
In one sense, the verse is a mirror. It exposes the truth of human nature. If you want to know what a person truly loves, do not just ask them; look at their bank statement or their calendar. Earthly treasures are a barometer of the heart’s current condition. In this view, the verse is descriptive: *Your treasure is already located where your heart already resides.*

#### The Argument for "Training" (Formative/Causal)
However, the context implies a strategy for spiritual growth. Jesus commands us to move the treasure *in order to* move the heart.
*   **The Law of Spiritual Gravity:** The text suggests that the heart does not stay stationary while the treasure moves; the heart follows the treasure.
*   **The Mechanism:** By forcing yourself to give generously (laying up treasure in heaven through charity, kindness, and Kingdom investment), you are physically dragging your affections away from earth and re-attaching them to God.
*   **Conclusion:** While treasure certainly *reveals* the heart, Jesus uses this verse to teach that treasure **trains** the heart. We manage our assets not just to manage our wealth, but to manage our desires.

---

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Are Tied Together

The passage illustrates a chain reaction of human psychology and spirituality. Here is how these three elements bind together:

#### Step 1: Possession (The Anchor)
**"Treasure" (*Thēsauros*)**
We rarely care about things we do not own or have no stake in. When you "lay up" a treasure, you are taking ownership. You are storing value in a specific place (whether a bank account, a property, or "heaven" via good works).
*   **The Tie-In:** Possession creates **responsibility**. Once something is yours, you feel the weight of keeping it safe (hence Jesus’ warning about moths, rust, and thieves). You cannot be indifferent to what you possess.

#### Step 2: Attention (The Focus)
**"Where... There..."**
Because you possess the treasure, your mental focus shifts toward its location. If you have $1 million invested in the stock market, your eyes are glued to the market reports. If your treasure is your reputation, your eyes are constantly scanning social media for mentions of your name.
*   **The Tie-In:** Possession dictates **attention**. You will inevitably obsess over the security and status of what you value. Jesus implies that you cannot separate your *mind* from your *money*.

#### Step 3: Love (The Heart)
**"Your Heart" (*Kardia*)**
In biblical terminology, the "heart" is not just the seat of emotion; it is the seat of the **will** and the **affections** (what you worship).
*   **The Synthesis:** Over time, **attention turns into affection**.
    *   Because you *own* it (Possession), you *watch* it (Attention).
    *   Because you *watch* it (Attention), you eventually *love* it (Heart/Love).
*   **The Danger:** If your treasure is on earth, your love is trapped in a decaying system. If your treasure is in heaven, your love is tethered to eternity.

### Summary
Matthew 6:21 teaches that **we are not designed to hold our treasure loosely.** We are wired so that our love follows our labor and our heart follows our holdings.

Therefore, the text advises that if you want to love God more (Train the heart), you must invest in God’s purposes more (Move the treasure). You cannot simply "try harder" to love heaven; you must change your investment portfolio.
