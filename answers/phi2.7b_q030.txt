
The portrait of love includes these negations of pride, envy, irritability, and suspicion to express the beauty and goodness of love in its purest form. Love is portrayed as patient, kind, generous, self-disciplined, and not inflamed by negative emotions. The vision of strength being proposed here is that of a courageous, joyful, contented, and serene spirit who acts selflessly and is always ready to do good. This is the ideal of unselfish love that is being expressed in the poem. Through the image of the faithful lamb and the shepherd-figure, the poet is conveying the idea that love requires sacrifice, patience, and self-control in order to maintain its perfect harmony and beauty, and that it is not easy or natural, but rather requires discipline and effort. Ultimately, the picture of the selfless, loving spirit described in the poem is the true measure of human strength and goodness.
