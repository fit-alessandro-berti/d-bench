
This passage presents a profound theological anthropology — a view of human nature where the **heart** (the seat of will, desire, and moral character) is the **source**, and **speech** is the **stream** that flows from it. Let’s unpack how it connects speech with "hidden moral stockpiles" and consider whether words are symptoms, choices, or revelations.

---

### 🔑 Key Connection: Speech as Overflow

Jesus’ metaphor — *"out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh"* — is not merely about cause and effect. It's about **nature revealing itself**. The heart is not just a passive reservoir; it's the **active source** of life. What you treasure (your *logistical* stockpile of values, desires, and priorities) will inevitably shape what you say.

So, **speech is not arbitrary** — it’s the **inevitable fruit** of what has been cultivated in the heart.

---

### 🧭 How Does This Passage Categorize Speech?

#### 1. **Speech as Symptom**
Yes, absolutely. Just as a fever reveals infection, **speech reveals the state of the heart**. If someone consistently speaks evil, it's a **symptom** that evil has gained dominion in their life. Jesus isn’t saying, “You are evil because you spoke evil once,” but rather, “Your speech *exposes* the chronic condition of your heart.”

#### 2. **Speech as Choice**
Undoubtedly. While the heart shapes the mouth, people are still accountable for what they choose to say. Jesus assumes moral agency — you can choose to guard your tongue or let it flow freely. The passage implies that **treasuring good or evil is a deliberate act**, and speech is the visible choice that confirms (or betrays) that choice.

#### 3. **Speech as Revelation**
Definitely. The passage says that **the hidden things of darkness** become visible through speech. Your words don’t just reflect your heart — they **uncover** it. There's something detective-like about this: **your life is a testimony to what you've been feeding on**.

---

### 💡 The Heart as a Moral Treasury

The image of the heart as a **"treasury"** is striking. Treasuries aren’t just storage units — they’re places of value that are regularly accessed and distributed. Similarly, your heart isn’t static — it’s **constantly being filled** (by media, community, Scripture, desires) and **constantly disbursing** (through your words and actions).

So when Jesus says a good man brings forth good things from good treasure, He’s teaching that **character is cumulative** — we become what we store up, and what we store up shapes what we give away.

---

### 🔄 Synthesis: Words as Symptoms, Choices, and Revelations

To answer your question directly: **Yes, words function as all three**:

- As **symptoms**, they show the internal disease or health of the soul.
- As **choices**, they express the will's direction and alignment with what is treasured.
- As **revelations**, they make the invisible heart visible to others and to ourselves.

This triad forms a **feedback loop**:  
Your choices shape your heart → your heart influences your speech → your speech reveals and reinforces your heart.

---

### 🌱 Implications

This passage calls for **heart-level transformation**, not just behavioral correction. If speech flows from stored treasure, then true change begins with **what we feed our hearts** — through media, relationships, worship, and Scripture.

It also underscores **accountability**: we will give an account not only for what we did in body but for what we **spoke from the abundance of our hearts**.
