1 John 4:20 is saying that **love for God cannot be separated from love for people**. The “visible neighbor” becomes the practical test of whether one’s claim to love the “invisible God” is real.

### Why this test?
Because love for God is not merely an inward feeling or a verbal profession. It has to show itself in concrete, observable ways. A person’s treatment of a brother or sister is the easiest and clearest place to see whether divine love is genuine.

John’s logic is:

- **God is invisible**: so claims about loving Him can’t be directly verified by sight.
- **Other people are visible**: so how we act toward them reveals what is really in the heart.
- If someone cannot love a brother they can see, it is implausible that they truly love God they cannot see.

So love of neighbor is a **necessary expression** of love for God.

### What self-deception is being exposed?
The verse exposes the lie of **religious profession without moral reality**—in other words, **hypocrisy**.

A person may deceive themselves by thinking:

- “I love God,”
- while at the same time harboring hatred, contempt, resentment, or indifference toward others.

John says this is self-contradictory. The person is not merely mistaken; he is **a liar** because his claim does not match his life.

### The deeper problem
The self-deception is that someone imagines they can have:

- **spiritual devotion upward** to God,
- while lacking **practical love outward** to people.

1 John rejects that split. Genuine love of God necessarily produces love for fellow believers—and, by extension, others made in God’s image.

### In short
The verse teaches that **love for the unseen God is authenticated by love for the seen brother**. It exposes the illusion that one can sincerely love God while refusing love to the people God has placed right in front of us.
