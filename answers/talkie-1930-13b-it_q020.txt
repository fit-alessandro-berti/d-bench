Bitternes is described as a root rather than an act, because it suggests hidden moral growth, delayed damage, and the way one person’s resentment can spread to others. A bitter feeling in the heart is like a seed sown in fertile ground; it germinates and grows secretly, until at last it bears fruit in an open outbreak of quarrel and strife. The outbreak may be between the man himself and some other person, or between him and society at large. In either case, the quarrel can pollute the lives of many. The seeds of bitterness are sown broadcast, and the harvest is a crop of hatred, malice, and uncharitableness.
