John 4:20 is part of a larger discourse within the Gospel of John where the theme of love is central to understanding the Christian life. This verse, in particular, highlights the interconnectedness of loving one's neighbor and loving God. Here's why treating the love of the visible neighbor as a test of love for the invisible God is significant and what kind of self-deception it exposes:

1. **Relational Connection**: The interconnectedness of loving one'thy neighbor and God emphasizes the importance of genuine interpersonal relationships as a reflection of one's love for God:
   - Our love for others is intimately tied to our love for God since ultimately, all others are made in God's image (Colossians 3:11).
   - Loving tangible, relational experiences with people challenges us to extend that love to unseen, transcendent realities.

2. **True Faith vs. Hypocrisy**: Actual love must be demonstrated in interactions rather than merely stated or felt internally:
   - When we fail to love those we see, who have flesh and bone, it often reveals a superficial or self-centered faith.
   - True spiritual love transcends natural love and manifests in consistent care and kindness.

3. **Selfless Love**: Christ's love for humanity was selfless and sacrificial (John 3:16), setting a standard for Christians. Loving neighbors:
   - Demands that we put aside our pride, prejudices, and personal grievances.
   - Encourages selflessness toward others, modeling the ultimate love of God for humanity.

4. **Unity of God's Love**: There's an understanding that God's love does not discriminate; it encompasses all people:
   - God's love is inclusive and unconditional; thus, our love for neighbors must mirror this characteristic.
   - Through acting with love for the marginalized and misunderstood, we experience God's boundless grace.

5. **Sincerity of Faith**: Genuineness of faith is proven through caring actions and attitudes:
   - Our deeds and expressions of love for others are a litmus test for the authenticity of our faith.
   - Insincere relationships or unloving actions are signs of a faith that's not deeply embedded in God's heart.

6. **Self-awareness and Redemption**: Recognizing our shortcomings in love can lead to personal redemption and growth:
   - Self-examination can bring to light unloving patterns within ourselves.
   - Sincere repentance and corrective action can lead to spiritual maturity and deeper love for all aspects of God's creation, including Heaven.

The self-deception exposed by this verse may result from:

1. **Worldly Influence**:
   - The values and norms of society can often prioritize self-interest, which undermines authentic love:
   - Prioritizing personal gain or reputation can distract us from genuine charity.

2. **Superficial Religion**:
   - Religious practices, rituals, and ceremonies may be performed without accompanying a heartflaus heart of love:
   - The outer show of religious observance can mask spiritual emptiness and indifference.

3. **Philosophical Hypocrisy**:
   - Intellectual assent or philosophical belief doesn't necessarily translate to love:
   - Believing in religious doctrines but not feeling compelled to act in love can indicate a cold or theoretical form of belief.

4. **Love Fetishism**:
   - Viewing love as a means to receive something in return:
   - Treating love as a transaction can rob it of its purity and transformative power.

5. **Social Darwinism or Individualism**:
   - Viewing people as means to an end or competing for resources:
   - Not caring for others often disguises a selfish or survival-driven worldview.

6. **Phantom Friends**:
   - Using social connections instead of true spiritual relationships:
   - Preferring certain people while neglecting others shows unequal love that lacks God-like impartiality.

7. **Self-Love**:
   - Prioritizing one's comfort and happiness:
   - Treating oneself with more care than others demonstrates imbalanced love that does not extend to them with due consideration.

By examining how we love others, particularly those we are in close interaction with, we are held accountable to the love of God. Failure to do so can be a form of self-deception because it may betray a shallow faith, materialistic attitude, or even an exclusive and self-seeking mentality. The love for the invisible God must therefore transpire in visible ways—through kindness, charity, and genuine concern for the well-being of others.

In essence, love for God has to be more than a private feeling; it must be shown in action, affecting the way we engage with and treat everyone around us, especially our neighbors. When we see this love not as manifest, there's a disconnect between our professed beliefs and demonstrated attitudes, which may indicate hypocrisy, selfish motives, or a form of religious or spiritual complacency. The call is for sincere faith that is enlivened by actual love, not just professed by the tongue. The Gospel challenges us to transform our hearts and minds so that our love for neighbor is a pure manifestation of our affection for God himself.
