Amos’s sharp rejection isn’t about *improving* worship; it’s about a system designed to *replace* genuine relationship with God.  

The passage exposes a **ritual fraud** — worship that is performed with meticulous externals but divorced from justice and righteousness. The people offer sacrifices, sing songs, and celebrate feasts, yet their conduct violates the covenant’s moral core.  

When Amos says, *“I hate, I despise your feast days,”* the intensity signals that their worship has become **idolatrous in practice** — not in theology, but in lived reality. They treat religion as a transaction: rituals to appease God while ignoring oppression, injustice, and social corruption.  

The “fraud” is the illusion that God can be manipulated by ceremonial observance while perpetrating systemic injustice.  

So the call for *“judgment to run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream”* is not an add‑on to worship — it **is** the true worship. Without it, the rest is hollow performance, a moral masquerade.
