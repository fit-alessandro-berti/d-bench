Based on the linguistic structure and the theological context of Matthew 6:19–21, the answer is that the text suggests **both**, but it functions primarily as a principle of **spiritual gravity.**

Here is a detailed breakdown of how the text addresses the relationship between treasure, attention, and love.

### 1. Does treasure reveal or train the heart?

**It reveals the heart (Diagnostic):**
In a descriptive sense, the text acts as a diagnostic tool. If you look at where a person invests their time, money, and emotional energy, you will find their heart. In this sense, treasure *reveals* the heart's current orientation. If one's "treasure" is an investment portfolio or social status, their heart (their anxiety, their joy, and their identity) is anchored in the earth.

**It trains the heart (Directive):**
However, the context of the passage is an **imperative**. Jesus does not say, "Observe where your treasure is," but rather, "**Lay up** for yourselves treasures in heaven." 

By commanding a change in *where* we store our resources, He is proposing a method of spiritual discipline. He is suggesting that the heart follows the investment. If you intentionally move your "treasure" (your efforts, your generosity, your service) toward heavenly things, your heart—which is naturally inclined to follow its treasure—will be "pulled" toward God. In this sense, the act of investing in heaven *trains* and relocates the heart.

### 2. The Tie Between Possession, Attention, and Love

The logic of verse 21 ("For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also") suggests a psychological and spiritual loop:

**Possession $\rightarrow$ Attention**
Possessions are not neutral; they require maintenance. Earthly treasures require protection from "moth, rust, and thieves." Therefore, what you possess dictates where your **attention** goes. If you own a great treasure on earth, you must spend your mental energy worrying about its depreciation or theft. Your attention is captured by the fragility of the possession.

**Attention $\rightarrow$ Love (The Heart)**
In the biblical sense, the "heart" is the seat of the will, the intellect, and the emotions. Love is not merely a feeling but a focused orientation. Whatever captures our consistent attention eventually captures our affection and our identity. When our attention is locked onto earthly possessions, our "heart" becomes tethered to the temporary.

**The Cycle of Attachment:**
1. **Investment:** You put your resources (time/money) into something.
2. **Anxiety/Attention:** You begin to care for, protect, and think about that thing.
3. **Attachment:** Your sense of security and happiness becomes dependent on that thing.
4. **Identity:** Your "heart" now resides there.

### Summary
The text teaches that **the heart is a follower, not a leader.** We often think we must "love God first" before we can be generous or selfless. However, Jesus suggests a practical reversal: **Move your treasure, and your heart will follow.** By shifting your possessions and investments from the corruptible (earth) to the incorruptible (heaven), you effectively relocate your heart.
