
In this text, treasure does not reveal the heart, rather it implies that what we cherish in physical possessions can become a reflection of our own thoughts and emotions. The text suggests that by laying up treasure in heaven, we are training ourselves to be more focused on abstract concepts like purity, righteousness, and justice, rather than material possessions. By doing so, we are creating a connection between our thoughts and emotions, and our physical treasures. The possession, attention, and love that are tied to this concept are not necessarily selfish or materialistic, but rather are based on a spiritual pursuit of inner beauty and strength.
