This is an excellent and deeply perceptive question that gets to the heart of Jesus' teaching. The text suggests a profound, bidirectional relationship between treasure and heart—they mutually reinforce each other. It's not strictly an either/or, but a both/and.

Let's break down the connection:

### 1. Treasure Reveals the Heart
At its most basic level, the principle is diagnostic: **"Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."** The heart, meaning the core of your desires, affections, and will, follows your investments. This is similar to the modern saying, "Follow the money." If you want to know what someone truly values, observe where they put their time, energy, and resources. In this sense, the location of your treasure acts like a map to your inner landscape.

### 2. Treasure Trains the Heart
However, Jesus' imperative ("Lay up...") shows He sees this as a moral and spiritual discipline. By choosing *where* to invest, you are actively **cultivating** the orientation of your heart. This is the transformative aspect:
*   **Possession → Attention:** What you own demands your care. A house requires maintenance, a car needs repair, a stock portfolio needs monitoring. Physical treasures constantly pull your mental and emotional energy toward themselves, toward their decay ("moth," "rust") and security ("thieves").
*   **Attention → Love:** What you attend to, you grow to love. The daily focus on earthly treasures trains your heart to find security, identity, and joy in them. Conversely, investing in "heavenly treasures"—things of eternal, spiritual, and relational value (like generosity, compassion, justice, and faith)—trains your heart to find its fulfillment there.

### How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together:
They form a reinforcing cycle:
1.  **Possession** creates a point of **attention**.
2.  **Attention** deepens emotional **attachment (love)**.
3.  This **love** motivates further investment and protection of the **possession**, and the cycle continues.

Jesus warns that earthly treasures are fundamentally insecure—subject to decay and theft. Therefore, making them the center of this cycle sets your heart on a foundation of anxiety and loss. In contrast, heavenly treasures are eternal and secure. Investing in them trains your heart toward a stable, joyful, and other-focused love that is not threatened by the shifting circumstances of the world.

### In Summary:
The text presents a principle of spiritual gravity: **Your heart will inevitably orbit your deepest investments.** Jesus' command is a call to audit that investment portfolio. By choosing to lay up treasures in heaven, you are not just revealing a prior heart-orientation; you are actively and wisely **re-training your heart** to love what is imperishable, redirecting the powerful cycle of possession-attention-love toward eternal good. It’s both diagnostic and therapeutic, both revealing and reshaping.
