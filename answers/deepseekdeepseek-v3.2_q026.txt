This passage from Matthew 12:34–35 presents a profound connection between speech, inner character, and moral causality. To analyze it, let's break down its logic and then apply it to your question.

### The Connection: Heart → Abundance → Speech/Action

1.  **The Heart as the Reservoir:** The "heart" in the Hebrew and biblical context represents the core of a person—the seat of intellect, will, emotions, and moral character. It is not merely the source of feelings but the inner control center.
2.  **The "Abundance" or "Treasure":** This is the *stored-up content* of the heart. It is what fills and defines the person's inner being. A "good treasure" is a stockpile of goodness (love, wisdom, integrity, faith), while an "evil treasure" is a stockpile of evil (hatred, deceit, selfishness, unbelief).
3.  **Speech as the Overflow:** Speech is depicted not as a carefully curated product, but as an **inevitable overflow** of what fills the reservoir. You cannot draw water from a well that contains something else. The mouth "speaks what the heart is full of" (NIV).

### Applying the Terms: Symptom, Choice, Revelation

Your proposed terms—**symptoms, choices, revelations**—are not mutually exclusive here. The passage's genius is that it presents speech as all three, in a specific, layered sequence.

#### 1. Speech as a **Revelation**
This is the most immediate and direct teaching. Words *reveal* the hidden state of the heart. You cannot consistently hide your true moral and spiritual condition. In moments of pressure, spontaneity, or unguarded conversation, what is inside will come out. Jesus is telling the Pharisees that their malicious speech (accusing Him of being in league with Satan) is a direct revelation of their own "evil treasure." It exposes them. This is a diagnostic tool for discerning character.

#### 2. Speech as a **Symptom**
This takes the idea of revelation a step further into causality. A symptom is an outward sign of an inward condition. A fever reveals ("reveals") an infection, but it is also a direct *result* of that infection. Similarly, evil speech is not merely an isolated bad choice; it is the symptomatic outflow of a diseased heart. The heart is the cause; speech is the effect. This challenges the idea that we can simply manage our words through social etiquette. True change requires addressing the "treasure" itself, not just the symptoms.

#### 3. Speech as a **Choice**
While the imagery of overflow suggests an almost automatic process, the context implies choice is involved in two crucial ways:
*   **The Accumulation of Treasure:** The "abundance" in the heart is built up over time by countless choices—choices of what to dwell on, what to desire, what to love, and what practices to engage in. The Pharisee chooses self-righteousness and legalism; the disciple chooses to follow Christ. These choices stockpile the treasure.
*   **The Expression of Treasure:** Even an overflow follows the path of least resistance shaped by habit. Habitual choices solidify the "treasure." Thus, a spontaneous, harsh word is both an overflow *and* a choice that is consistent with the moral habits formed over time.

### Conclusion: The Integrated View

The passage suggests a powerful, integrated sequence:

**Past Choices → Form the Moral Treasure (Heart) → Which Overflows as Present Speech → Which Reveals the True Inner State.**

Therefore, **speech is all three**:
*   Primarily, it is a **revelation** of the heart's contents.
*   Causally, it is a **symptom** of a deeper moral condition.
*   Ultimately, it is rooted in the **choices** that built up the "treasure" from which it flows.

**The Practical Implication:** Jesus' teaching moves the ethical focus from mere behavior modification ("watch your language") to inner transformation ("guard your heart, for everything you do flows from it" - Prov. 4:23). You don't get good fruit from a bad tree by painting the fruit; you must make the tree good. So, to speak good things consistently, one must be made good from the inside out—a theme central to the Gospel's message of redemption and new creation.
