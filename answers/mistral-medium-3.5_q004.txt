The trust described in Proverbs 3:5–6 is a **wholehearted, radical reliance on God** that involves both **humble self-distrust** and **active acknowledgment of His lordship**—not a rejection of reason but a recognition of its limits.

### **1. The Nature of the Trust: Wholehearted Reliance**
- **"Trust in the Lord with all thine heart"** – This is not a partial or hesitant trust but a **total surrender** of one’s being to God. It implies that God, not our own wisdom, is the ultimate foundation of life.
- **"Lean not unto thine own understanding"** – This does not mean abandoning reason (God gave us minds to use) but **not making human reasoning the final authority**. It is a rejection of **autonomy**—the idea that we can fully comprehended or control life apart from God.

### **2. Humble Self-Distrust vs. Anti-Intellectualism**
- **Humble self-distrust** recognizes that:
  - Human understanding is **finite and fallen** (1 Corinthians 1:19–20; Isaiah 55:8–9).
  - We are prone to **self-deception** (Jeremiah 17:9; Proverbs 14:12).
  - True wisdom begins with **fear of the Lord** (Proverbs 1:7), not self-sufficiency.
  - This is **not anti-intellectual**—Scripture commends study (2 Timothy 2:15), logic (Isaiah 1:18), and wisdom (Proverbs 9:10). But it **subordinates reason to revelation**.

- **Anti-intellectualism**, by contrast:
  - Rejects the **proper use of the mind** (which God commands, e.g., Matthew 22:37).
  - Often stems from **laziness, fear, or false piety** rather than true humility.
  - Leads to **mysticism or fideism** (blind faith without engagement).

### **3. The Positive Command: Acknowledge Him in All Your Ways**
- **"In all thy ways acknowledge Him"** – This means:
  - **Conscious dependence** on God in decisions, thoughts, and actions.
  - **Seeking His will** through Scripture, prayer, and wise counsel (not just feelings or intuition).
  - **Submitting plans to His sovereignty** (James 4:13–15).
- **"He shall direct thy paths"** – God’s guidance is **not passive** but comes as we **actively trust and obey** (Psalm 37:23; Isaiah 30:21).

### **Key Distinction:**
- **Humble self-distrust** = **"I will think, but I will not trust my thinking more than God’s Word."**
- **Anti-intellectualism** = **"I will not think at all; I will just feel or follow tradition blindly."**

### **Conclusion:**
The trust here is **a reasoned, worshipful submission**—using our minds fully but never making them ultimate. It is the difference between **a child trusting a father’s wisdom** (while still learning) and **a child refusing to learn at all**. True biblical trust **engages the mind but transcends it**, resting in God’s perfect knowledge and love.
