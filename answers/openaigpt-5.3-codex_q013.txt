Great question. In Amos, God is **not rejecting worship in itself**; he is rejecting worship that has become a **lie**.

### Why such sharp language?
Because in Amos 5, the people are doing two opposite things at once:

- In the sanctuary: sacrifices, songs, festivals.
- In society: crushing the poor, corrupt courts, bribery, exploitation (see Amos 5:10–12).

So God doesn’t ask for “better balance” because this is not a scheduling problem (“more ethics + keep same worship”). It is a **covenant betrayal**: their worship is being used to **cover** injustice.

### What fraud is exposed?
A kind of **religious fraud** where ritual is treated like payment to God while life remains unjust.  
In other words:

- “We gave offerings, so we’re fine with God.”
- “Our music proves we are faithful.”
- “God can be honored liturgically while neighbors are denied justice.”

Amos says that is spiritually impossible. Their songs are “noise” because their social life contradicts their prayers.

### “Let justice roll down…”
That line is the key: God wants **mishpat** (justice in public life) and **tsedaqah** (right-relatedness/righteousness) to flow constantly, not occasionally—like a strong, never-failing stream, not a seasonal trickle.

So the passage is sharp because the issue is not imperfect worship but **counterfeit worship**—piety used as camouflage for oppression.
