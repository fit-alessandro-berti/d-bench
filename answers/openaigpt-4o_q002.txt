Asking for a clean heart rather than merely a pardon involves seeking a deeper transformation beyond just forgiveness for past actions. Here are some key differences between wanting to escape guilt and desiring inner change:

1. **Depth of Transformation:**
   - **Pardon:** Seeking pardon often focuses on being forgiven for specific actions or thoughts, addressing the immediate need for absolution or feeling relieved from guilt. It can be seen as addressing the symptoms of a problem rather than the root cause.
   - **Clean Heart:** Asking for a clean heart is a request for a fundamental change in one's nature or character. It is about transforming the inner self to prevent future wrongdoing, leading to sustained and meaningful growth.

2. **Long-term Impact:**
   - **Pardon:** While being pardoned is important, it does not necessarily lead to long-term change or improvement. One might fall back into the same patterns that led to the need for forgiveness in the first place.
   - **Clean Heart:** A clean heart implies a lasting change in perspective and behavior, reducing the likelihood of repeating past mistakes and aiming for a more virtuous life.

3. **Relationship with the Divine:**
   - **Pardon:** Focuses on restoring a damaged relationship by acknowledging wrongdoing and seeking forgiveness, which is essential for reconciliation.
   - **Clean Heart:** Seeks to deepen and enrich the relationship by aligning oneself more closely with the values and nature of the divine. It is about nurturing an ongoing relationship rather than merely patching up a breach.

4. **Motivation:**
   - **Pardon:** May be driven by a desire to alleviate feelings of guilt or fear of punishment.
   - **Clean Heart:** Reflects a genuine desire for self-improvement and spiritual growth. It is motivated by the wish to align one's life with higher principles and values.

5. **Sustainability of Change:**
   - **Pardon:** Provides momentary relief but may not contribute to sustained change if one's mindset remains unaltered.
   - **Clean Heart:** Emphasizes a renewal of spirit, fostering a transformation that leads to long-lasting behavior change and spiritual well-being.

In essence, by asking for a clean heart, one seeks a comprehensive and enduring transformation that goes beyond the boundaries of forgiveness. It's about becoming a better person in a meaningful way and fostering a deeper connection with the divine.
