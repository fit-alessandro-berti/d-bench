**Answer:** The problem with public goodwill in this verse lies in **the craving to be seen** rather than just the visibility itself. Here's the breakdown:

1. **Visibility as a Casual Factor:**  
   While the verse warns against showing alms for display (visibility), the primary concern is not that people see it, but that the intention behind the action is driven by a desire for recognition or praise. It suggests that if you are genuinely helping others out of genuine compassion (for the sake of God), the act should be done anonymously or in humility, not to attract attention.

2. **Craving to Be Seen:**  
   The key issue is the human tendency to seek validation or recognition. When you publicly display your charity, you may be more focused on how others view your generosity than on the genuine impact of your kindness. This can lead to pride in your actions, creating a disconnect between personal faith and outward behavior. The verse cautions against allowing public acts of goodness to overshadow the purpose of serving others.

3. **Moral Dangers**:  
   By prioritizing external approval, you risk undermining the intrinsic value of your actions. It can disconnect you from the spiritual roots of your generosity, making it empty or opportunist behavior. The call to " McLaren" (a biblical term meaning to hide yourself) reinforces the importance of acting with humility and love, not just for humanity’s gaze but for the divine gaze.

**Conclusion:** Public goodwill is morally dangerous when the motivation is driven by the need for human recognition rather than the compassion and reverence for God’s mercy. The verse encourages living a life of quiet trust in God’s reward, where the true reward comes from serving others selflessly without seeking recognition.
