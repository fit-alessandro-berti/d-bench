Matthew 6:19–21 teaches that **your “treasure” reveals where your heart already is and, in that sense, also forms/redirects your heart**—because what you consistently value and secure becomes what you mentally occupy and emotionally belong to.

### 1) “Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also”
The key phrase is **“there will your heart be also.”** That most directly connects **the location of your treasure** (i.e., what you store up, pursue, and rely on) with **the location of your heart** (i.e., what you love and truly desire).

- It **doesn’t just say** “treasure trains the heart” in a purely educational sense.
- It says that **the heart naturally follows the treasure**: where your treasure is, your inner life gravitates there.

So you can read it both ways, but with emphasis on this: **what you treat as treasure is a window into what you love (and therefore where your heart is).** At the same time, **choosing and accumulating that treasure will keep your heart focused in that direction**.

### 2) Possession, attention, and love are braided together
Here’s how the passage ties them together:

#### Possession (“treasures upon earth” / “treasures in heaven”)
“Treasure” in the context of 6:19–21 means more than money; it means **what you invest your security and resources into**—what you are effectively “storing up” as valuable.

- **Earthly treasure** is vulnerable (“moth and rust,” “thieves”).
- **Heavenly treasure** is secure (it won’t be corrupted or stolen).

So possession is not neutral—**what you possess as your security expresses what you value.**

#### Attention (“lay not up… but lay up…”)
The contrast is framed as a directive about **what you should be doing**: storing up one kind of treasure rather than another.

That “laying up” is a picture of **ongoing attention and intention**:
- If you continually pursue earthly security, your mind and energy get trained toward that horizon.
- If you continually “lay up” heavenly treasure (by faithfulness, righteousness, generosity, obedience—consistent with the surrounding Sermon on the Mount themes), your attention gets trained toward God’s kingdom.

So attention follows the pursued “treasure.”

#### Love (“your heart”)
In biblical usage, “heart” often means the **center of desire, loyalty, and affection**—not just emotions but the whole inner orientation.

The logic is:
- **Treasure shows what you’re oriented toward (love/loyalty).**
- **Love then concentrates attention and desire where the treasure is.**
- Therefore, storing up one kind of treasure tends to locate the heart there.

### 3) The direction of the “cause”
A helpful way to summarize the passage is:

- **Treasure discloses the heart** (“where your treasure is…” implies the heart is already drawn toward what you value).
- **But treasure also disciplines the heart** (because continued investment of time, trust, and resources will keep drawing the heart toward it).

So it’s not either/or. It’s a feedback loop:
**what you pursue as treasure → where your attention settles → where your love/loyalty (“heart”) ends up → which then shapes what you keep pursuing.**
