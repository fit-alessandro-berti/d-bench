
Excellent question. This passage from Matthew 12:34-35 uses powerful organic metaphors to connect speech and the inner moral life. Let's break down the connection and then address your specific options.

### The Core Metaphor: The Heart as a Treasury
Jesus frames the **heart** (Greek: *kardia*) not as the seat of emotions alone, but as the **core of one's being, the inner person, the source of thoughts, intentions, and moral character**. It is a "treasure" storehouse.

*   **"Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks":** Speech is presented as a natural, spontaneous outflow. The "abundance" is what fills the heart—its dominant contents, its character. What is stored up inevitably spills out.
*   **"A good man... bringeth forth good things... an evil man... bringeth forth evil things":** The "treasure" (Greek: *thēsaurou*) is the accumulated moral substance of a person's life—their values, habits, choices, and character. This treasure is not static; it is "brought forth" or "produced" through actions and words.

### Connecting Speech to the "Hidden Moral Stockpile"
The connection is **organic and necessary**. Speech is not an isolated act; it is a **product** of the heart's contents. You cannot consistently speak gracious, truthful, loving words if your heart is filled with malice, envy, and deceit, because the inner "treasure" determines the outer "fruit."

---

### Does it suggest speech is a symptom, a choice, a revelation, or all three?

**It strongly suggests all three, with a particular emphasis on the revelatory and symptomatic, grounded in a history of choices.**

1.  **Speech as a *Symptom* (of the inner state):**
    *   This is the most immediate reading. The words are the *evidence* or *sign* of what is already present in the heart. A "generation of vipers" speaks viper-like words (hateful, deceptive) because their hearts are evil. The symptom points to a deeper disease.

2.  **Speech as a *Choice* (that shapes the stockpile):**
    *   Crucially, the passage implies a **reflexive relationship**. The "treasure" is not magically implanted; it is built over time through **repeated choices**. A "good man" becomes good by consistently choosing good, which fills his heart with good "treasure." An "evil man" fills his heart with evil through his choices. Therefore, speech is both an *outcome* of past choices and a *present act* that can further shape the heart. Every word spoken is a choice that either reinforces the existing treasure or begins to alter it.

3.  **Speech as a *Revelation* (that exposes the stockpile):**
    *   This is the primary function in this context. Jesus is using their own words to **expose** the true nature of the Pharisees' hearts before him. Their accusation (v. 24) revealed not just a mistaken opinion, but a heart aligned with Satan. Speech is the moment the hidden "treasure" becomes publicly visible. It reveals one's true loyalties and character.

### Synthesis: A Unified Biblical Principle
Matthew 12:34-35 presents a **unified principle of moral causality and accountability**:

*   **The Heart is the Reservoir:** Your inner life is a treasury built by a lifetime of choices (volition).
*   **Speech is the Outflow:** Words are the natural, revealing product of what that reservoir contains (symptom/revelation).
*   **Words are also Acts:** Each word spoken is a moral act that both displays the heart *and* contributes to its future shape (choice).

**Conclusion:** The passage suggests that **words are symptoms of a deeper reality, they are choices in the moment, and they are revelations that make the hidden heart known.** They are the acoustic evidence of the heart's treasure, and in the divine economy, they will be the basis for judgment (v. 36-37). Therefore, to examine one's speech is to begin the necessary work of examining and disciplining the heart.
