The passage from Luke 6:37 speaks about refraining from judging or condemning others and practicing forgiveness. At first glance, the message of not judging others might seem to suggest that moral judgments never matter, but it is crucial to note that there is a notable difference between discernment and the craving to condemn. This distinction is vital in understanding the passage's message, as it does not reject moral judgment entirely but instead proposes a more thoughtful and loving way to approach the moral decisions we make concerning others.

To get a better understanding of discernment and the appetite to condemn, here are some examples to illustrate the difference:
- **Discernment:** It is worth making the effort to understand the reasons behind someone's actions and consider the situation from their perspective. For example, we can discern that it is not right for someone to use harmful drugs without condoning their actions, considering the potential consequences and the person's mental health.
- **Appetite to condemn:** Condemning someone is when we disregard their circumstances and pass judgment without putting in the effort to consider why they have been involved in the actions they have. For example, instead of seeking to understand why someone uses harmful drugs, we condemn them as individuals and cast the blame upon them without taking their mental health or other factors into consideration.

It is essential to know that while we need to make moral judgments about our actions and help others do the same, we also need to ensure that we do not become fixated on being judgmental or condemning others. It is a difference that needs to be highlighted as it is possible to see wrongdoing and take appropriate action while also showing compassion and avoiding being overly harsh in our judgments. This is a distinction that is greatly emphasized in the passage from Luke 6:37, which highlights the importance of discerning and guiding rather than condemning and excluding.

If moral judgments never matter, then it would not be possible to understand what is acceptable or unacceptable regarding actions that require forgiveness. This highlights the difference between discernment and the appetite to condemn as discernment will allow us to understand clearly what requires forgiveness, while the appetite to condemn could blind us to the things we need to let go of to forgive someone. 

In conclusion, discernment and the appetite to condemn require different approaches to making moral judgments about other people's actions. While discernment calls for understanding, the appetite to condemn is characterized by harsh and unforgiving judgment. We can uphold our moral standards and values by understanding this difference while also being compassionate and avoiding being overly harsh in our judgments.
