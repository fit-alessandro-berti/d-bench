The passage from James 3:9–10 exposes a profound inner contradiction: using the same mouth to bless God and curse others, particularly those made in God's image. This act is contradictory because blessing God signifies reverence and acknowledgment of His divinity, while cursing those created in His likeness undermines the inherent dignity and worth that comes from bearing His image. 

This moral conflict reveals a disordered heart, where one claims to honor God but devalues the very beings He has made. 

Regarding why speech reveals moral disorder quickly: the tongue is often referred to as a "small part" but holds great power (James 3:5–8). It serves as a direct expression of what is in the heart. When one’s words clash—blessing God while cursing others—it exposes a lack of integrity and moral clarity. Speech provides an immediate window into one’s inner state, as it cannot hide the conflicting motivations or beliefs that reside within. Thus, the inconsistency in speech quickly betrays underlying moral confusion or disorder.
