The reason repentance in Isaiah 55:6-7 involves both "way" (outward conduct) and "thoughts" (inward imagination) is that true repentance is a holistic transformation that encompasses both the external actions and the internal motivations and desires of an individual. This implies that outward conduct (way) is not the only aspect that needs to change, but also the inward thoughts, imaginations, and intentions that drive those actions.

The inclusion of "thoughts" suggests that repentance requires a transformation of the mind, a change in the way one thinks, perceives, and imagines. This is because our thoughts and imaginations can often precede and influence our actions, and can also reveal the underlying motivations and desires that drive our behavior. Therefore, repentance must involve a renewal of the mind, a change in the way we think about ourselves, others, and God.

The fact that God will "have mercy upon him" and "abundantly pardon" those who repent implies that mercy is available to those who are willing to turn away from their wicked ways and thoughts. This suggests that mercy is not just a cancellation of punishment, but a transformative power that can change the heart and mind of the individual. It implies that God is willing to forgive and restore those who are willing to let go of their sinful thoughts and behaviors, and to turn towards Him in humility and repentance.

Furthermore, the use of "way" and "thoughts" together implies that repentance is not just a one-time event, but a ongoing process of transformation. It requires a continuous effort to align our outward conduct with our inward thoughts and intentions, and to seek God's mercy and pardon when we fail. This process of repentance and transformation is ongoing, and it requires a deepening awareness of our own sinfulness and a growing dependence on God's mercy and grace.

In summary, the inclusion of both "way" and "thoughts" in Isaiah 55:6-7 implies that repentance is a comprehensive transformation that involves both outward conduct and inward imagination. It requires a change in our actions, but also a renewal of our minds and a transformation of our thoughts and desires. This process of repentance and transformation is ongoing, and it is made possible by God's abundant mercy and pardon, which are available to those who are willing to turn towards Him in humility and repentance.
