Pride is seen as a moral blindness because it obscures the true state of one’s character and circumstances, leading the proud to ignore warning signs and to overestimate their ability to avoid negative outcomes. This overconfidence removes humility, which is the capacity to recognize limits and to heed counsel. When pride precedes destruction, the “haughty spirit” is already in a state of self‑deception; the inevitable fall is therefore not merely a punishment but a logical consequence of being blind to one’s own vulnerability. The contrast with “the lowly” highlights that genuine moral strength lies in recognizing one’s dependence on others and on a higher moral order, rather than in self‑exaltation. Humility thus restores sight, allowing one to navigate life without the catastrophic collapse that pride inevitably brings.
