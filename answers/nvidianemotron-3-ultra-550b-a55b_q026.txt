This passage operates through a **metaphysics of overflow**. Jesus does not treat speech as an isolated behavior but as the necessary hydraulic consequence of what the heart *stores*. The Greek vocabulary is deliberate: **θησαυρός** (*thēsauros*, “treasure/storehouse”) and **περίσσευμα** (*perisseuma*, “superabundance/overflow”).

Here is how the text connects speech to hidden moral stockpiles, and why the answer to your triad is **“all three—simultaneously.”**

---

### 1. The Mechanism: Storage → Abundance → Overflow
The logic is agricultural/architectural, not merely behavioral:
*   **The Heart as Treasury (*thēsauros*):** The heart is not a passive container but an active *depository*. The "good man" and "evil man" are defined by what they have *deposited* over time—thoughts meditated upon, desires cultivated, intentions nurtured. This is the "hidden moral stockpile."
*   **The Mouth as Pressure Valve (*perisseuma*):** "Out of the abundance (*perisseuma*) of the heart the mouth speaks." The word implies a vessel filled past capacity. Speech is not a *selection* from the treasury; it is the **spillage** of a full vessel. You do not "decide" to spill; you spill because you are full.

---

### 2. Words as **Revelation** (Ontological Exposure)
**Primary sense:** Speech *unmasks* the possessor.
*   The Pharisees’ blasphemy (attributing the Spirit’s work to Beelzebul, v. 24) was not a slip of the tongue; it was a **diagnostic readout**. Jesus says, in effect: *"Your words didn't betray you; they *identified* you."*
*   The "generation of vipers" (v. 34) imagery reinforces this: venom is not something a viper *chooses* to express; it is what a viper *is*. Speech reveals the species.
*   **Theological weight:** On judgment day (vv. 36–37), words are the evidence entered into the record. They are the "fruit" by which the "tree" is known (v. 33). Words are **revelation** because they make the invisible heart visible and legally admissible.

---

### 3. Words as **Symptoms** (Pathological Indication)
**Secondary sense:** Speech *diagnoses* the condition.
*   The metaphor of "evil treasure" (*ponēros thēsauros*) implies a corrupted inventory. Just as fever is a symptom of infection, malicious speech is a symptom of a heart infected with *ponēria* (malignancy/rot).
*   The rhetorical question—*"How can you, being evil, speak good things?"*—denies the possibility of sustained counterfeit output. A corrupted source cannot produce pure stream (cf. James 3:11–12).
*   **Pastoral implication:** You do not cure the symptom (speech) by silencing the mouth; you cure it by purging the treasury. Behavioral modification without heart transplantation is impossible in this framework.

---

### 4. Words as **Choices** (Moral Responsibility)
**Tertiary sense:** Speech *ratifies* the deposit.
*   Jesus holds the speakers accountable: *"By your words you will be justified, and by your words you will be condemned"* (v. 37). If words were *only* involuntary symptoms, judgment would be unjust.
*   The "treasure" is built by **volitional deposits**—what a man "brings forth" (*ekballō*, v. 35, often "casts out" or "produces"). Every time the heart assents to a thought, cherishes a grudge, or nurtures a lust, it makes a *deposit*.
*   Speech is the moment the internal choice goes public. The "overflow" is not a reflex like a sneeze; it is the **inevitable expression of a cultivated disposition**. You chose the inventory; the overflow is simply the inventory showing up.

---

### Synthesis: The Triune Unity
The genius of the passage is that it refuses to separate these categories.

| Category | Function in Text | Why it fails alone |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Revelation** | Shows *who you are* (Identity) | Without **Choice**, identity is fatalistic determinism. |
| **Symptom** | Shows *what ails you* (Condition) | Without **Revelation**, condition is private, not judicial. |
| **Choice** | Shows *what you've built* (Responsibility) | Without **Symptom**, choice is disconnected from nature. |

**The "Treasure" is the hinge:**
1.  You **choose** the deposits (Choice).
2.  The deposits constitute your **nature** (Symptom source).
3.  The overflow **reveals** the nature (Revelation).

### Conclusion
Jesus presents a **hermeneutic of the heart**: *Listen to a man long enough, and you hear his theology, his anthropology, and his eschatology—all stored in the treasury, all spilling out in his vocabulary.*

Words are **revelations** of what the heart *is*, **symptoms** of what the heart *has*, and **choices** of what the heart *will answer for*. The "generation of vipers" cannot speak good things not because their mouths are broken, but because their treasuries are bankrupt—and they are the ones who filled them.
