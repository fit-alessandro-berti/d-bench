In **Matthew 6:19–21**, the text does not explicitly say that **treasure reveals the heart**, but rather that **treasure shapes and directs the heart**. The passage emphasizes that where one stores their treasure, there their heart will naturally be inclined. This implies that **possession, attention, and love are deeply connected**—what we prioritize in life will ultimately govern how we think, feel, and act.

### **How Possession, Attention, and Love Are Tied Together:**
1. **Treasure as a Focus of Attention**
   - The passage contrasts storing treasures on earth (which are subject to decay, theft, and neglect) with storing them in heaven (which is eternal and secure).
   - This suggests that **where we direct our focus and energy** determines our long-term priorities. If we fix our attention on material wealth, we will naturally love and seek those things more than spiritual or eternal goods.

2. **Treasure as a Training Ground for the Heart**
   - The phrase *"where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"* does not mean that treasure *reveals* the heart (as if it were a mirror), but rather that **treasure forms and influences the heart**.
   - Just as a farmer trains a horse by feeding it the right food, so too does **what we value most shape our affections and desires**. If we prioritize earthly treasures, our hearts will be drawn toward them, making us more concerned with possessions than with God’s kingdom.

3. **Love as a Response to What We Treasure**
   - The passage does not say that love is *objective* or *neutral*—it is shaped by what we hold most dear.
   - If we love money, we will act in ways that prioritize it (e.g., hoarding, greed, neglect of others). If we love God and His kingdom, we will act in ways that align with His values (e.g., generosity, trust in Him, service to others).

### **Key Takeaway:**
The text does not say that treasure *reveals* the heart (as if it were a passive reflection), but that **treasure trains and directs the heart**. What we treasure most will naturally become the object of our love, attention, and ultimate concern. This is why Jesus warns against storing treasures on earth—because such a focus will lead to a heart that is misaligned with God’s will.
