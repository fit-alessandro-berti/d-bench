This passage, Matthew 12:34-35, establishes a direct connection between speech and the moral condition of the heart, suggesting that words are a reflection of the inner self. Here's a breakdown of how it connects speech with hidden moral stockpiles in the heart and the nature of words:

1. **Connection between Speech and the Heart**: The passage posits that speech is an overflow of the heart. The phrase "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh" (Matthew 12:34) implies that what is stored or accumulated in the heart eventually finds expression through words. This indicates that the heart, here used metaphorically for a person's inner being or soul, contains a kind of moral stockpile or treasure.

2. **Moral Stockpiles**: The heart is described as containing either a "good treasure" or an "evil treasure," which suggests that individuals have an inner repository of values, intentions, and moral inclinations. This inner storehouse influences their actions and, specifically, their speech.

3. **Nature of Words**: According to the passage, words are primarily **revelations** of the heart's condition. What a person says reveals what is stored in their heart. This implies that speech can be an indicator of a person's moral and spiritual state.

4. **Symptoms, Choices, Revelations**: 
   - **Symptoms**: In suggesting that speech is an expression of the heart's condition, the passage implies that words can also be seen as symptoms of a deeper issue. Just as a fever can be a symptom of an underlying infection, evil or good speech can be a symptom of the heart's moral state.
   - **Choices**: While the passage focuses more on the revelatory aspect of speech regarding the heart's condition, it indirectly suggests that speech is also a result of what one chooses to bring forth from their heart. The heart's treasure is not merely passive but influences active expressions through speech and actions.
   - **Revelations**: As discussed, the passage strongly supports the idea that words are revelations of the heart, unveiling its true nature.

In conclusion, this passage suggests that words are at least revelations of the inner moral stockpiles of the heart. Additionally, they can be considered symptoms of the heart's condition and the result of choices made regarding what is brought forth from the heart. The passage underscores the idea that speech is not merely a superficial act but is deeply connected to a person's moral and spiritual core.
