Matthew 12:34–35 links speech to what is already “stored” within a person—specifically, what the heart has been filling up with.

- **It suggests words are symptoms** of inner moral condition:  
  “Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh” means speech comes *from what overflows inside*. So words reveal what is already there.

- **It also treats words as revealing (a kind of disclosure)**:  
  The point of “how can ye… speak good things?” is expositional—by their speech you can tell what kind of heart they have. The “treasure” imagery implies an internal reality that becomes visible outwardly.

- **But it also implies choice and responsibility** (not just passive disclosure):  
  The “good man… bringeth forth good things” and “evil man… bringeth forth evil things” uses “bringeth forth” language—speech isn’t merely an involuntary reflex; it’s something that issues from the person’s practiced inner orientation. The heart can be filled (or corrupted) over time, and then speech manifests that moral “stock.”

So, overall: **the passage presents speech as all three**—  
1) **Symptoms** (words flow from the heart),  
2) **Revelations** (speech discloses the heart’s “abundance/treasure”), and  
3) **Outgrowth of choices/character formation** (the “treasure” of the heart is what the person has become and has been gathering).
