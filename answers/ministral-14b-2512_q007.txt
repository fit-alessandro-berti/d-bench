Luke 6:37 is often misunderstood as an absolute prohibition against making any moral judgments. However, Jesus is not advocating for moral relativism or passivity in the face of evil. Instead, He is addressing a specific attitude of the heart—**the appetite to condemn others out of pride, self-righteousness, or a desire to control or harm them**. The passage contrasts this with **discernment, which seeks truth, justice, and restoration** rather than destruction.

### **Key Distinctions:**
1. **Condemnation vs. Discernment**
   - **Condemnation** is a judgment that rejects, excludes, or punishes without mercy, often rooted in self-righteousness (e.g., Pharisees in Jesus’ time who judged others while ignoring their own sins).
   - **Discernment** is a wise, compassionate assessment of truth, aimed at correction, healing, and restoration (e.g., Paul’s rebuke of Peter in Galatians 2:11-14, which was not condemnation but a call to repentance).

2. **Motivation Matters**
   - Jesus forbids judging with a **hypocritical, vengeful, or self-exalting** heart (Matthew 7:1-5).
   - He does not forbid **loving correction** (e.g., Proverbs 27:5-6, "Better is open rebuke than hidden love").
   - The difference lies in **why** we judge: **Is it to expose sin for the sake of truth and repentance, or to elevate ourselves while tearing others down?**

3. **Forgiveness as the Counterbalance**
   - Jesus pairs "judge not" with "forgive" because **mercy is the antidote to condemnation**.
   - If we withhold forgiveness, we risk becoming like those we judge (Matthew 7:3-5).
   - True discernment is **graced by forgiveness**—we see sin as a problem to address, not a weapon to wield.

4. **Biblical Examples of Discernment**
   - **Paul’s Correction of the Corinthians** (1 Corinthians 5:1-5) – He exposed sin but did so with the goal of restoration, not destruction.
   - **Jesus’ Confrontation of the Pharisees** (Matthew 23) – He exposed their hypocrisy not to humiliate them but to call them to repentance.
   - **The Shepherd’s Concern for the Lost Sheep** (Matthew 18:15-17) – Correction is given in love, with the hope of recovery.

### **Practical Application:**
- **Ask:** *"Am I judging to expose truth or to elevate myself?"*
- **Discernment** seeks to **uphold justice, protect the vulnerable, and call people to repentance**—but always with humility and a heart of forgiveness.
- **Condemnation** seeks to **destroy, exclude, or shame**—often masking a deeper pride or bitterness.

### **Conclusion:**
Jesus is not saying, *"Never call out sin."* He is saying, *"Do not judge with a heart that is hardened, self-righteous, or lacking in mercy."* True discernment is **both bold in truth and tender in love**—just as Jesus was.
