### Analysis of 1 John 4:20: Why Love of the Visible Neighbor Tests Love of the Invisible God and What Self-Deception It Exposes

The verse from 1 John 4:20 presents a striking assertion about the nature of love for God and neighbor. It challenges the notion that one can claim to love an unseen God while harboring hatred for a visible brother, deeming such a claim a lie. The underlying reasoning is rooted in the idea that if one cannot love a neighbor they have seen, it is impossible for them to love God whom they have not seen. This has profound implications for understanding the test of love and the self-deception it uncovers.

#### Why Love of the Visible Neighbor as a Test of Love of the Invisible God
- **Nature of Love and Action**: Love is not merely an emotion but an expression of care and concern. The verse implies that love is demonstrated through actions. If a person claims to love God but shows ill will or hatred towards their brother, their claim is hollow because love for others is a tangible manifestation of love for God. Just as loving one's brother is an act of love, love for God should also be evident in how one treats others.
- **Unity of Commandments**: In the Christian tradition, Jesus emphasized the two greatest commandments: to love God and to love one's neighbor. These commandments are interconnected. 1 John 4:20 aligns with this unity, suggesting that loving one's neighbor is an integral part of loving God. Failing to love the visible neighbor indicates a disconnect from the love that should be directed towards the unseen God.
- **Tangibility of Love for God**: Love for God is often abstract as it is directed towards an unseen being. However, love for the neighbor is concrete and visible. By testing one's love for the visible neighbor, the verse assesses the authenticity of their professed love for the invisible God. It shows that love for God must be lived out in daily interactions with others.

#### What Kind of Self-Deception It Exposes
- **False Disunity of Love**: The verse exposes the self-deception of those who try to separate their love for God from their love for their neighbor. They may claim to love God deeply but do not extend that love in practical ways to others. This is a form of self-deception because they fail to recognize that loving God is not just an internal feeling but a way of life that includes loving others.
- **Superficial Faith**: There are individuals who have a superficial faith that focuses on external practices or beliefs but lacks the transformative power of love. They may go through religious motions but do not embody the love that God calls for. The verse challenges this by showing that such a faith is not genuine if it does not translate into love for the neighbor.
- **Ignorance of the Nature of Love**: Another aspect of self-deception is the ignorance of what love really is. Love is not just about positive feelings but about action and sacrifice. The verse shows that if one cannot love the visible neighbor, they do not understand the nature of love as God intends it. They may mistakenly believe that love for God is enough, but God calls for a love that is expressed in relationships with others.

In conclusion, 1 John 4:20 uses love of the visible neighbor as a test of love of the invisible God because it reveals the authenticity of one's professed faith. It exposes the self-deception of those who separate their love for God from their love for their neighbor, suggesting that a genuine love for God must be evident in how one treats others. Ultimately, the verse calls for a holistic and lived-out faith that integrates love for God and love for neighbors.
