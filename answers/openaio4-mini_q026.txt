Jesus here pictures the mouth as the overflow of an inner “treasure”—the moral contents of the heart.  Two things are in view:

 1. Words as symptoms or signs.  What you say betrays what you carry inside.  Just as a tree’s fruit is a natural sign of its rootstock, your speech is a symptom of your heart’s true character.  

 2. Words as revelations.  Speech doesn’t merely hint at your inner life, it actually reveals it.  You cannot consistently speak “good things” from a heart overrun with evil, nor “evil things” from a genuinely good heart.  

On top of that stands moral choice:

 3. Words as deliberate choices.  You do choose what you say.  By exercising restraint or by speaking kindly, you are choosing to draw on the “good treasure” of your heart rather than the “evil treasure.”  Thus speech both exposes and exercises your moral will.

In short, Jesus is saying: our words are at once
• Symptoms (they indicate what is really in us),  
• Revelations (they make our hidden moral stockpiles visible),  
• Choices (we actively decide what “treasure” to bring forth).  

The teaching warns us that if our hearts remain stocked with bitterness, malice or hypocrisy, those attitudes will leak out in our speech.  Conversely, a heart filled with love, mercy and truth will naturally express itself in gracious, life-giving words.
