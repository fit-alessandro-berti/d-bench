In Matthew 6:19–21, Jesus teaches about the relationship between treasure and the heart.  
The text indicates that **treasure reveals the heart** more than it trains it—though the act of consciously “laying up treasure in heaven” can also shape one's desires over time.  

Here is a closer look:

---

### 1. **Treasure reveals the heart**
The concluding statement — *“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also”* — shows a diagnostic principle: what you value (where you invest resources, time, energy) exposes where your inner allegiance lies.  
The heart naturally follows what it treasures. If your treasures are earthly, your heart is set on temporary things; if heavenly, your heart is set on eternal realities. This reveals, rather than primarily trains, the heart's current orientation.

---

### 2. **Possession, attention, and love**
- **Possession** → what we store up (earthly goods vs. heavenly reward).  
- **Attention** → what we focus on, worry about, or strive for.  
- **Love** → what we ultimately cherish.  

These three are intrinsically linked:  
What we **possess** (or seek to possess) captures our **attention** (mind and efforts), and what holds our attention wins our **love** (affections and loyalty).  

Earthly treasures are subject to decay and theft, so investing in them leads to anxiety and misplaced love.  
Heavenly treasures are secure, so investing in them reorients our concerns toward God and His kingdom, cultivating a heart anchored in divine priorities.

---

### 3. **But there is a formative aspect too**
Though the primary thrust here is **revelatory**, Jesus also commands: *“lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven.”*  
This intentional action **trains** the heart by redirecting our pursuits. By choosing to value eternal things through generosity, faith, and obedience, the heart gradually becomes more aligned with heaven’s values.  
So treasure both *reveals* the heart’s current state and, through deliberate spiritual discipline, *trains* the heart toward God.

---

### Summary
- **Treasure reveals** the heart’s true allegiance.
- **Possession, attention, and love** are intertwined: what we store up focuses our mind and captures our affections.
- The call to store treasure in heaven implies that our investments can also **train and redirect** the heart over time.

Thus the passage presents a dynamic where our treasures are both a **thermometer** (showing where our heart already is) and a **thermostat** (setting where our heart will go).
