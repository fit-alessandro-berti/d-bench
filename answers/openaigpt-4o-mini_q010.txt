In this passage from Philippians 2:5–8, the kind of power being praised is the power of humility and selflessness. The verses highlight the extraordinary nature of Christ's character—his willingness to relinquish status and reputation, embracing the role of a servant and ultimately facing death on the cross. This portrayal of power is distinct from traditional notions of power that often emphasize dominance, control, and authority. Instead, it emphasizes a profound strength found in humility, sacrifice, and obedience.

Humility joined to greatness can feel more unsettling than open domination for several reasons:

1. **Subversion of Expectations**: We often associate power and greatness with assertiveness and superiority. When someone of high status chooses to humble themselves, it challenges our understanding of authority. It forces us to reconsider what true greatness looks like and confronts our values.

2. **Emotional Complexity**: The juxtaposition of greatness and humility evokes complex feelings. We may feel admiration but also discomfort, as it highlights our own shortcomings and the gap between how we perceive leadership and how it can be manifested.

3. **Authenticity and Vulnerability**: A humble leader showcases vulnerability, which can feel unsettling because it breaks the facade of invulnerability often associated with power. This authenticity can provoke introspection and challenge us to reflect on our own lives and attitudes towards power.

4. **Challenge to Social Norms**: In many societies, humility in the face of power can be perceived as weakness. When someone with great influence embodies humility, it disrupts social norms and expectations, presenting a radical model of leadership that can be both inspiring and disconcerting.

5. **Moral Authority**: Humility linked to greatness can lead to a more compelling moral authority. Such a leader is not just powerful; they have a deep understanding of the human experience and a commitment to serve others, which can provoke a sense of vulnerability in those who hold more traditional views of power.

In summary, the power praised in Philippians 2:5-8 is an unsettling yet transformative force that subverts traditional notions of authority. It invites deeper reflection on the nature of greatness, encouraging a radical redefinition of strength through humility and service.
