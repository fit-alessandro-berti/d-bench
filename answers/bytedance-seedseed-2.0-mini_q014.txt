Based on the text of Matthew 6:19–21, we can unpack two key aspects of the relationship described between treasure and the heart:

First, the passage articulates how treasure reveals the state of one's heart. The line "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" suggests a correlation between where we direct our treasure (our resources, time, and focus) and the orientation of our heart. This does not imply that possession itself shapes or trains the heart in a directional sense, but rather that what we prioritize materially offers a window into what truly matters to us inwardly.

When examining the interplay between possession, attention, and love in this context:
- **Possession and attention:** When we lay up treasures on earth, we dedicate time to acquire, maintain, and protect those material possessions. This active engagement with our treasures occupies our attention, framing how we allocate our mental and emotional energy.
- **Attention and love:** The attention we grant to our treasure naturally reflects our affections. The passage implies that the things we invest our attention in are those we value deeply, indicating a form of love or desire tied to those material goods.
- **Possession and love:** Ultimately, choosing to lay up treasures on earth reveals a heart oriented towards the things of this world. Conversely, directing those resources to heavenly treasures suggests a heart focused on spiritual priorities, with love for God and eternal values taking precedence over material concerns.

In this sense, the passage does not posit that treasure trains the heart, but rather that treasure—through the possession, attention, and love it entails—unveils the heart's true allegiance, showing where our affections and trust ultimately lie.
