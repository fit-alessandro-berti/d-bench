
Great question—this is a classic text for reflection on how our priorities shape our character. Let's break down Matthew 6:19–21 and answer both parts of your question.

### 1. **Does this text say treasure reveals the heart, or that treasure trains the heart?**

The text is best understood as **saying that where your treasures are, *that* will shape your heart** (your affections, priorities, and inner life). The wording is:

> “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”

The phrase “there will your heart be also” (Greek: ἐν τῷ ἑν ἐστὸν ἀνθρὸς) is a translation of a well-known Greek proverb meaning “the heart is where the treasure is.” The text is not explicitly stating that the treasure *reveals* the heart, but rather that the *location* of your treasure *determines* where your heart (your inner focus, desires, and attention) is drawn.

So, it’s closer to **treasure trains the heart** (i.e., shapes it) than that the treasure *reveals* it. Having treasures in one place forms the pattern of your heart’s affections and priorities.

### 2. **How are possession, attention, and love tied together here?**

Let’s look at how these three elements connect in the passage:

#### a) **Possession (of treasures)**
You choose where to “lay up” your treasures—on earth (material things subject to decay and theft) or in heaven (eternal, God-pleasing things).

#### b) **Attention**
The passage implies that your heart’s attention is drawn to what you possess. If you accumulate treasures in the world, your attention, energy, and affection will be focused there (on earthly things). If you store up treasures in heaven (i.e., in God and His kingdom), your heart’s attention is naturally directed there (toward God’s purposes, spiritual things, and relationships rooted in Him).

This is echoed in the Old Testament proverb (Proverbs 3:13–14): “The Lord loves the upright, and those who are found righteous. Wealth and honor are in their house, but in the hand of the wicked is ruin.”

#### c) **Love**
What you value and prioritize you love and serve. If your treasures are in heaven (God, God’s Word, spiritual growth), then your heart is oriented toward loving God above all and loving others in a way aligned with His will. If your treasures are on earth (material gains), your heart may be more focused on earthly success or security, potentially at the expense of deeper, more eternal relationships or spiritual growth.

**In summary:**  
- **Possession** determines what you value.
- **Possession shapes your attention**, guiding where you invest your energy and thought.
- **Attention shapes your love**, because the things you pay attention to become the objects of your affection and devotion.

Thus, in Matthew 6:19–21, laying up treasures in heaven trains (or forms) the heart to be centered on God, which then influences both how you attend to life and the nature of your love for God and others. The heart is the seat of our priorities, and those priorities are shaped by what we choose to treasure.
