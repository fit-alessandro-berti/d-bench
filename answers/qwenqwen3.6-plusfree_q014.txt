This is a sharp exegetical and theological question. The short answer is: **the text does both, but with a stronger emphasis on the formative ("trains") dimension.** Here’s how the text works and how possession, attention, and love are woven together.

### 1. Reveals or Trains?
The Greek reads: `ὅπου γάρ ἐστιν ὁ θησαυρός σου, ἐκεῖ ἔσται καὶ ἡ καρδία σου.`  
The key is the verb **ἔσται** ("will be"). It’s future and consequential, not merely descriptive. Jesus isn’t just saying your treasure is a mirror that shows a fixed heart; He’s declaring a spiritual law of gravity: **your heart will inevitably drift toward whatever you fund, guard, and prioritize.**

- **Reveals**: Yes, your current investments expose your current priorities. If you hoard wealth, it shows where your trust already lies.
- **Trains/Forms**: More importantly, the verse is part of a command (`θησαυρίζετε` / "lay up"). Jesus commands heavenly investment precisely because He knows it’s formative. What you repeatedly pour your resources into pulls your affections, reshapes your desires, and reorients your allegiance. The heart doesn’t just sit behind treasure directing it; the heart follows where treasure is placed.

Most major commentators (Carson, France, Davies & Allison) note this dual function: diagnostic *and* directional. But the imperative mood and future tense make the **formative** sense primary. Jesus is warning that you cannot remain spiritually neutral toward your possessions; they will either corrode your devotion or cultivate it.

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Are Tied Together
In biblical anthropology, the καρδία ("heart") is not merely the seat of emotion; it’s the control center of the person: mind, will, moral orientation, and ultimate loyalty. Jesus binds these three elements into a single spiritual ecosystem:

| Element | Role in the Text | How It Connects |
|--------|------------------|----------------|
| **Possession** (what you "lay up") | Not passive ownership, but active accumulation (`θησαυρίζετε`). Earthly treasures decay or get stolen; heavenly treasures are secure. | Possession requires choices. Every dollar, hour, or energy spent is a vote for a kingdom. |
| **Attention** (where the "heart" fixes) | The heart in Scripture encompasses focus, planning, worry, and mental bandwidth (see Matt 6:25–34 immediately after). | Where you store treasure, your mind naturally goes: you calculate, protect, optimize, and lose sleep over it. Attention follows investment. |
| **Love** (allegiance/devotion) | Jesus immediately follows with Matt 6:24: "No one can serve two masters... You cannot serve God and money." In biblical terms, love = covenant loyalty. | Attention crystallizes into love. What consistently captures your focus becomes what you ultimately serve. Treasure trains the heart to love what it trusts. |

**The feedback loop**:  
`Possession → captures Attention → forms Love → reinforces Possession.`  
Jesus interrupts the earthly loop by commanding heavenly investment, knowing it will retrain attention toward eternal realities, thereby anchoring love in God alone.

### In Summary
- The text says treasure **both reveals and trains** the heart, but the grammatical structure and imperative context emphasize the **formative** reality: your heart will go where your treasure goes.
- Possession, attention, and love are inseparable in this passage because biblical psychology sees the human person as an integrated whole. What you accumulate commands your focus; what commands your focus shapes your allegiance; and your allegiance determines your ultimate master.

Jesus isn’t giving financial advice; He’s diagnosing discipleship. Where you put your treasure is where your soul is being shaped.
