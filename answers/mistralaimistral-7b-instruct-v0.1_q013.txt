 The passage in Amos 5:21-24 rejects worship sharply because the people were engaging in hollow and insincere religious practices while ignoring social justice and moral righteousness. The prophet Amos was expressing God's displeasure with their external displays of worship, such as feast days, burnt offerings, meat offerings, and peace offerings, when their hearts were far from Him.

The moral or spiritual fraud being exposed here is hypocrisy. The people were going through the motions of religious rituals without truly seeking God or living according to His ways. They were neglecting the essential aspects of God's law, such as justice, mercy, and righteousness, while focusing on the external forms of worship. God desired their hearts to be fully committed to Him and to reflect His love and compassion towards others.
