Based on the text of Matthew 12:34–35, the connection between speech and the heart is not merely incidental; it is organic and inevitable. Jesus presents the human heart as a **reservoir** or **treasury** that inevitably overflows through the mouth.

To answer your question directly: **The passage suggests that words are all three—symptoms, revelations, and choices—but it prioritizes them in a specific order of operation.**

Here is a breakdown of how the text supports each of these categories:

### 1. Words as Symptoms (Indicators of Nature)
The primary function of speech in this passage is diagnostic. Jesus argues that speech is a reliable indicator of spiritual health because it is a **symptom** of the heart’s condition.

*   **The Logic:** Jesus asks a rhetorical question: *"How can ye, being evil, speak good things?"* He implies a law of moral botany or physics: a bad tree cannot produce good fruit, and a corrupt fountain cannot yield sweet water.
*   **The Evidence:** The Pharisees’ words (blasphemy against the Holy Spirit) were the **symptom** that proved their diagnosis ("generation of vipers"). The speech did not create the evil; the internal evil nature created the speech. Therefore, if you want to check the health of a person’s soul, you listen to their speech—it is the fever that reveals the infection.

### 2. Words as Revelations (Uncovering the Hidden)
Jesus explicitly uses the metaphor of a **treasure** (*thesaurus* in Greek). A treasury is a place where valuables are stored away, hidden from public view.

*   **The Mechanism:** The phrase *"out of the abundance [overflow/perisseuma] of the heart"* suggests a container filled to the brim. When you bump into someone (or when they speak), what is inside spills out.
*   **The Unveiling:** Before the man speaks, his moral stockpile is hidden. The moment he opens his mouth, he **reveals** exactly what kind of "treasure" he has been storing up. The Pharisees likely thought they could maintain a facade of religious respectability, but their speech revealed the "evil treasure" hidden within. In this sense, speech acts as an X-ray machine for the soul.

### 3. Words as Choices (Results of Accumulation)
This is the most nuanced aspect of the passage. While we often think of "choice" as deciding what to say in the *moment*, Jesus shifts the focus of choice to the *past*.

*   **Immediate Choice vs. Long-term Choice:** The text suggests that in the **immediate moment**, the evil man cannot choose to bring forth good things. His options are limited by his inventory. If his warehouse is stocked only with evil, he can only ship out evil.
*   **The Real Choice:** Therefore, the "choice" implied here is **cumulative**. A man becomes "good" or "evil" by what he chooses to store in his heart over time. Verse 35 describes the heart as something that must be filled ("good treasure"). The words spoken today are merely the **cashing out** of the checks written by past thoughts, desires, and choices.

### Summary
Matthew 12:34–35 presents a causal chain:

1.  **The Stockpile:** The heart accumulates a moral inventory (Good or Evil Treasure) based on long-term choices.
2.  **The Symptom:** The nature of that inventory dictates the capability of the speaker (an evil tree cannot bear good fruit).
3.  **The Revelation:** When pressure is applied or the mouth opens, the hidden abundance overflows, **revealing** the true contents of the heart to the world.

**Verdict:** Words are **revelations** of the **symptoms** caused by the **choices** of the heart.
