This passage from Matthew 6:19–21 presents a profound connection between physical stewardship and spiritual orientation. To answer your questions, we must look at how the text describes the human response to God's word regarding value.

### **Question 1: Does the text say treasure reveals the heart, or that treasure trains the heart?**

The text leans more strongly toward **"treasure trains the heart,"** though it functions like a revealer through its consequences.

*   **Why it "Trains" rather than merely "Reveals":** The verse describes a causal mechanism ("For where your treasure is..."). It is not just telling you what your heart *looks like* to an observer; it is describing how your values shape the direction of your affections and will. In Christian ethical terms, this process is **training** (formation/cultivation) rather than passive disclosure. You don't "see" your true heart; you actively place your treasures (assets, time, attention) in specific places, and the location of those assets *shapes* where your heart settles.
*   **The Implication:** By asking the reader to "lay up for yourselves," the text is instructing the mind's focus (value orientation). Your heart doesn't naturally go one way; it moves wherever you put your value in this life. Therefore, treasure acts as a tool or training ground for directing spiritual commitment.
*   **The Nuance of "Reveals":** While the verse does not use the word "reveal," there is a secondary truth: A heart that values one thing over another *discloses* its true priority to others (e.g., "for where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"). However, the primary mechanism described in the text is alignment and direction-setting (training), which precedes the actual act of revelation.

### **Question 2: How do possession, attention, and love tie together here?**

The passage connects the three elements through the metaphor of an investment with no return on a different life plan. They are mutually reinforcing actions that determine spiritual reality.

**1. Possession (The Physical Anchor)**
*   **"Lay not up... for yourselves"**: This calls for a strategic shift from hoarding earth-based goods to investing them in God's Kingdom. "Possession" here is not about ownership of worldly property but the **commitment of spiritual assets**. By choosing what you possess, you dictate what your heart follows.
*   **The Connection:** To *possess* heaven requires that your physical heart and resources be dedicated to it. If you don't take possession of something (like God or His values), it remains inaccessible.

**2. Attention (The Directional Focus)**
*   **"Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also":** This implies a direct linkage between value and attention. When you invest in one place, your gaze and focus naturally shift to that reality. If the object of possession (God/Temple) is high-value, your *attention* cannot stray.
*   **The Connection:** Possession dictates Attention. You must actively direct your energy toward the spiritual "treasure," making worldly distractions impossible. The heart does not wander unless attention is withdrawn from a higher place and focused on earthly things.

**3. Love (The Emotional Alignment)**
*   **"Love of this world" vs. "Love of God":** While not explicitly using the word "love" in this verse, it implies a form of **devotion** that requires love to be true. It is difficult to *value* or *possess* what you don't genuinely love.
*   **The Connection:** Treasuring treasure (even the spiritual one) is an expression of love: love for God, love for eternity. But it is also a form of **gratitude** (Love for His gifts). Without a heart that loves the divine, one cannot "lay up" treasures in heaven. The text ties love to the act of value placement. To treasure something is, by definition, to treat it as precious and worthy of priority—which inherently requires a loving heart.

### **Summary: A Coherent Unity**
In Matthew 6, the text creates a closed loop where the three elements converge:
1.  You place your **Possession** in Heaven (Investment of life).
2.  This draws your **Attention** to that reality (Focus of the mind).
3.  Consequently, your **Heart** aligns with it, reflecting true **Love** for God over false values (Outcome of prioritization).

This passage teaches us that "treasure" is not just an object; it is a lifestyle decision. The heart becomes who you choose to be.
