 passage from Matthew 12:34–35 offers a profound anatomy of the human heart, illustrating that speech is never merely superficial. Jesus connects the spoken word directly to the "treasure" (or stockpile) stored within the heart.

Here is an analysis of how this passage connects speech to the heart, and why words function as symptoms, choices, and revelations simultaneously.

### 1. The Connection: The "Treasure" and the "Mouth"
Jesus uses the metaphor of a **treasure** (Greek: *thesaurou*) to describe the inner reservoir of a person’s character, shaped by their moral and spiritual orientation.

*   **The Mechanism:** The text states, "out of the abundance (*perisseumatos*) of the heart the mouth speaketh." The word *perisseuma* implies something that remains over, a surplus, or a flood. The image is that the heart is a container filled to the brim; when pressure is applied (through conversation, stress, or casual interaction), whatever is inside spills out.
*   **The Inevitability:** Verse 35 uses the word **"bringeth forth"** (*ekballō*), which literally means "to cast out" or "to expel." This suggests that speech is an act of bringing the internal inventory to the external world.
*   **The Moral Alignment:** The passage creates a binary:
    *   **Good Treasure:** A heart aligned with God produces "good things" (righteousness, grace, truth).
    *   **Evil Treasure:** A heart hostile to God (like the "generation of vipers" addressed here) produces "evil things" (slander, hypocrisy, malice).

### 2. Are Words Symptoms, Choices, Revelations, or All Three?
The passage supports the view that **words are all three**, functioning simultaneously depending on the perspective:

#### A. Words as Symptoms (The Medical Diagnosis)
The text functions like a medical diagnosis. Jesus asks, "How can ye, being evil, speak good things?" The assumption is that the root determines the fruit. Just as a diseased tree cannot bear healthy fruit, a heart stocked with "evil treasure" cannot fundamentally produce "good things."
*   **The Connection:** In this sense, speech is a **symptom** of a deeper condition. You are not judged merely for the word itself, but the word reveals the spiritual pathology (or health) of the heart from which it originated.

#### B. Words as Choices (The Banking Metaphor)
The term "treasure" implies accumulation. A treasure is not built in an instant; it is built through **choices** over time.
*   **The Connection:** What we fill our hearts with is the result of countless decisions—what we watch, who we spend time with, what we meditate on, and how we respond to conviction. Therefore, when we speak, we are "withdrawing" from the bank account we have willingly built. We choose what goes into the heart, and consequently, we choose (to an extent) what comes out of the mouth.

#### C. Words as Revelations (The Exposure of the Heart)
This is the most explicit function in the text: "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh."
*   **The Connection:** Jesus is telling the Pharisees that their words are "giving them away." They were experts at external religion, but their words against the Holy Spirit (blasphemy, vs. 31-32) revealed the true nature of their "treasure." Speech pulls back the curtain. It is the **revelation** of the hidden man.

### 3. Synthesis: The Flow of Influence
The passage suggests a cycle of influence that encompasses all three concepts:

1.  **The Heart is Stocked (Choice):** Through habitual thoughts, actions, and moral alignment, a person chooses to store up either good or evil.
2.  **The Heart Overflows (Symptom):** The mouth acts as a pressure valve. You cannot *stop* the heart from influencing the tongue any more than you can stop a full cup from spilling if tipped.
3.  **The Heart is Revealed (Revelation):** The listener hears the words and gains access to the speaker's "treasure."

**Conclusion:**
Jesus is arguing that **you cannot separate the talk from the walker.** If the "generation of vipers" speaks evil, it is not a slip of the tongue; it is the **symptom** of a venomous heart, the **result** of choices to reject God, and the **revelation** of their true spiritual state. Therefore, changing speech requires more than just "watching your mouth"; it requires changing the "treasure" of the heart.
