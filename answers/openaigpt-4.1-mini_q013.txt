The passage from Amos 5:21–24 expresses a sharp rejection of worship because it exposes a profound disconnect between outward religious observance and genuine ethical living. Rather than simply asking for a better balance between worship and morality, the prophet Amos condemns the worship practices outright to highlight that these rituals have become empty, hypocritical acts—mere performances that mask ongoing injustice and unrighteousness.

### Why such a sharp rejection?

1. **Hypocrisy of Ritual Without Justice:**  
   The people are engaged in solemn assemblies, feast days, burnt offerings, and peace offerings, all traditional religious acts meant to honor God. However, their social conduct is unjust and corrupt. God, through Amos, is saying that He detests their worship because it is disconnected from the fundamental demands of justice and righteousness. The rituals serve as a cover or distraction, pretending all is right while ethical violations continue unabated.

2. **Worship as Insincere and Deceptive:**  
   The passage exposes a form of *moral and spiritual fraud*: the idea that religious ceremonies and sacrifices alone can secure God’s favor, regardless of the moral character of the worshipers. This is a critique against reducing faith to mere external compliance rather than authentic transformation and social responsibility.

3. **God's Priority on Justice and Righteousness:**  
   Verse 24 encapsulates the core demand: **“Let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream.”** The true worship God desires is not in the performance of rituals, but in living out justice and righteousness. It is this ethical commitment that pleases God, not empty ceremonies.

### What kind of moral or spiritual fraud is exposed?

- **Ritualistic Self-Deception:** Believing that religious rites can compensate for unethical behavior. This creates a false sense of security and tries to "buy" divine approval without changing one’s ways.
  
- **Social Injustice Hidden Behind Piety:** Using worship to legitimize or ignore exploitation, inequality, or oppression. The people maintain religious observance while failing to uphold justice for the vulnerable or marginalized.

- **Superficial Religion:** Worshiping God with the lips and forms but having hearts and lives removed from God’s commands about righteousness and justice.

### In summary:

The harsh rejection reveals that God is not interested in empty displays of religiosity but demands integrity between worship and ethical living. Amos unmasks a moral fraud where people deceive themselves by putting on religious acts while neglecting justice, exposing their worship as hypocritical and unacceptable in God’s eyes.
