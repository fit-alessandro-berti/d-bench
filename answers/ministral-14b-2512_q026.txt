This passage in **Matthew 12:34–35** (parallel to **Luke 6:43–45**) establishes a profound connection between **speech and the moral state of the heart**, framing words as both **symptoms** and **revelations** of one’s inner life. Jesus’ teaching here suggests that our words are not merely random expressions but **flow naturally from the "treasure" stored in our hearts**. Here’s how the passage breaks down:

### **1. Words as Symptoms of the Heart**
Jesus describes speech as a **diagnostic indicator** of one’s moral condition:
- *"Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks"* (v. 34).
- *"A good man brings forth good things, and an evil man brings forth evil things"* (v. 35).

This implies that **what we say is not independent of our character**—it is a **manifestation** of what we value, cherish, or nurture in our hearts. Just as a doctor examines symptoms to diagnose an illness, Jesus suggests that **our words reveal the spiritual health (or corruption) of our inner life**.

- **Example:** A person who habitually speaks with **bitterness, gossip, or sarcasm** likely has a heart that harbors resentment, pride, or malice.
- **Contrast:** Someone who speaks with **grace, truth, and kindness** reflects a heart that has been renewed by God’s love (cf. **Ephesians 4:29**).

Thus, **words are symptoms**—they expose what is already present in the heart, whether good or evil.

---

### **2. Words as Choices (Implied Agency)**
While the passage emphasizes that words **flow naturally** from the heart, it also carries an **implicit call to moral responsibility**. Jesus’ audience (and us) are not passive observers of their speech but **stewards of their hearts**.

- The **"treasure" of the heart** is something we **cultivate** (or neglect). Just as a gardener tends to soil to produce good fruit, we **choose what to feed our hearts** (Scripture, sin, bitterness, etc.).
- **James 3:2** later expands on this: *"For in many things we offend all. If any man offend not in word, the same is a perfect man, and able also to bridle the whole body."*
  - This suggests that while words are **symptomatic**, they are also **choices** we can learn to control through the Holy Spirit’s power.

Thus, **words are not just symptoms but also opportunities for moral growth or decline**—we can either **nurture the good treasure** (Scripture, prayer, repentance) or **allow the evil treasure to fester**.

---

### **3. Words as Revelations of the Heart**
Jesus’ words in **Matthew 12:34** are **accusatory**—*"O generation of vipers!"*—because the Pharisees’ speech **exposed their hypocrisy**. Their **righteous-sounding words** were actually **masking a heart full of pride, legalism, and hostility toward Jesus**.

This means:
- **Words reveal what we truly worship** (e.g., self-righteousness vs. humility).
- **Words expose our deepest desires** (e.g., flattery reveals a love for approval; cursing reveals a love for control).
- **Words can either glorify God or betray our rebellion** (cf. **Psalm 19:14**, *"Let the words of my mouth and the meditation of my heart be acceptable in Your sight"*).

Thus, **words are not just symptoms but also divine revelations**—God uses them to **expose our hearts** (for conviction) and **judge our sincerity** (cf. **Proverbs 18:21**, *"Death and life are in the power of the tongue"*).

---

### **4. All Three: Symptoms, Choices, and Revelations**
The passage suggests that **words are all three simultaneously**:
1. **Symptoms** – They **diagnose** the heart’s condition (like a fever reveals sickness).
2. **Choices** – They **reflect** (and sometimes **reinforce**) what we’ve allowed to take root in our hearts.
3. **Revelations** – They **expose** the truth about us, whether to ourselves or to God (and others).

**Practical Implications:**
- **Examine your speech** – Are your words **life-giving** (Eph. 4:29) or **corrosive** (James 3:8)?
- **Guard your heart** – What you **feed your mind** (media, conversations, entertainment) shapes your speech (Prov. 4:23).
- **Repent and renew** – If your words betray a corrupt heart, **turn to Christ** (Rom. 12:2), who gives a **new heart** (Ezek. 36:26).

### **Conclusion**
Jesus’ teaching here is **both a warning and an invitation**:
- **Warning:** Your words **cannot hide** the true state of your heart.
- **Invitation:** **Change your heart**, and your words will follow (Matt. 15:18–20).
