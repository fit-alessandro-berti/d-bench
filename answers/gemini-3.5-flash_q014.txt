Based on Matthew 6:19–21, the short answer is that **treasure does both, but its primary function in this passage is to *train* (or direct) the heart.** 

Jesus is not merely offering a passive psychological observation about how to diagnose our current spiritual state; he is giving a command on how to actively shape our future spiritual state.

Here is a deeper look at how this passage addresses the relationship between the heart and treasure, and how **possession, attention, and love** are inextricably tied together.

---

### Part 1: Does Treasure "Reveal" or "Train" the Heart?

To understand this, we must look at the direction of the movement in verse 21: 
> *“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”*

#### 1. The Diagnostic View (Treasure *Reveals* the Heart)
In a secondary sense, treasure certainly reveals the heart. If you want to know what someone truly values, you look at their bank statement, their calendar, and their thoughts. In this sense, "treasure" is the fruit, and the "heart" is the root. It reveals what is already there.

#### 2. The Formative View (Treasure *Trains* the Heart)
However, Jesus’ grammatical structure suggests something more dynamic. He does not say, *"Where your heart is, there you will put your treasure."* He says the exact opposite: **where the treasure goes, the heart follows.** 

The verb tenses are crucial: where your treasure **is** (present), there your heart **will be** (future). 

This means treasure acts as a **magnet** for human affection. 
* If you buy shares in a company, you suddenly start checking the stock market daily. Your heart (attention/anxiety) followed your treasure (money).
* If you invest your time and resources into helping a person in need, you begin to care deeply about their well-being. Your heart (love) followed your treasure (effort/assets).

By commanding his followers to *“lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven,”* Jesus is giving a pedagogical instruction. He is saying: **You cannot easily command your feelings, but you can command your investments. If you want your heart to love God and eternal things, invest your resources there, and your affections will naturally follow.**

---

### Part 2: How Possession, Attention, and Love are Tied Together

Jesus weaves possession, attention, and love into a psychological and spiritual feedback loop.

```
[ POSSESSION ] (Where you invest your resources)
      │
      ▼
[ ATTENTION ]  (Where your focus, worry, and vigilance go)
      │
      ▼
   [ LOVE ]    (Where your heart/devotion resides)
```

#### 1. Possession (The Treasure)
"Possession" in this text is what Jesus calls "treasure." This is not just money; it is anything we value, store up, and rely on for security, identity, or joy. Crucially, Jesus notes that possessions are intrinsically tied to vulnerability. On earth, possessions are subject to decay (moth and rust) and external threat (thieves). 

#### 2. Attention (The Mind/Worry)
Because earthly possessions are vulnerable, they demand our constant **attention**. 
* If you possess earthly treasure, you must spend mental energy protecting it. You worry about inflation, market crashes, home security, and obsolescence. 
* If you possess heavenly treasure, your attention is directed upward. Because heavenly treasure is invulnerable ("neither moth nor rust doth corrupt"), it frees your mind from anxiety. 

Where you put your possession dictates where you must pay attention.

#### 3. Love (The Heart)
In biblical thought, the "heart" (*kardia*) is not just the seat of emotions, but the center of the human will, intellect, and devotion—it is the seat of **love**. 

Ultimately, what we pay attention to is what we grow to love. If our attention is consumed by preserving earthly possessions, our love becomes earthbound, anxious, and fragile. If our attention is fixed on the kingdom of God (where our heavenly treasure is stored), our love becomes secure, eternal, and peaceful.

### Summary
Matthew 6:19–21 teaches that **we do not just invest in what we love; we grow to love what we invest in.** 

Possession drives attention, and attention drives love. By commanding us to place our possessions in heaven, Jesus is showing us how to train our attention and ultimately rescue our hearts from the decay of the earth.
