
##

The concept of receiving a "new heart" is symbolic in the King James translation, often interpreted allegorically. It doesn't mean physically replacing the heart with a new organ, but rather, making a spiritual or emotional transformation within oneself. 

The statement "I will take away the stony heart out of your flesh" is not a literal removal of the heart but a metaphor portraying a hardened heart or negative emotions that hinder compassion and good judgment. It implies an internal process of replacing this hardness with love and compassion, which is the intended spiritual or emotional transformation. The metaphor of replacing hardness with a "new heart of flesh" is not a physical replacement but rather a metaphor for replacing negative emotions with positive ones.

In other words, external compliance is merely obeying the laws and rules externally, while internal compliance involves internal transformation, making you behave and act as the spirit within you desires. 

## Ezekiel 


A game developer is designing a Christian-themed game based on the teachings and stories from the New Testament. You are tasked to create two scenarios: one depicting a character named Ezekiel 36:26–27, the other featuring a character named John. 

John's world is a physical reality. His actions are based on external compliance, following the rules and laws set. Ezekiel's world is metaphorical, where internal transformation replaces external compliance.

Your challenge is to find the commonalities between the two worlds in which the game is played. 

**Question:** 
How can the game developer make these seemingly disparate worlds co-exist harmoniously in this Christian-themed game? What elements should be mirrored between John and Ezekiel in this gaming narrative?


Identify and map the fundamental principles and teachings from the story of Ezekiel 36:26–27 and John.

Consider the game mechanics. Decide on the common actions, rules, and conflicts. These would form the core of common ground between John and Ezekiel in the game.

Incorporate the principle of internal transformation in John's actions. Create situations where breaking rules leads to internal conflict, pushing the player to question their own actions. 

Incorporate the concept of an internal "new heart" and compassion into John's life. Make the actions of John and the consequences of his actions directly impacted by his internal transformation.

Design a "world transformation" in the game, where the environment changes depending on the decisions made by the user. These changes can mirror the concept of an "inner world" created by the game's story.

The game must also highlight the concept of external compliance - it shows John interacting with the rule-based reality that he lives in.

Lastly, the game developers should ensure that the two worlds are balanced. They need to make sure that just as John's actions have consequences, so too does the player's in Ezekiel's metaphorical world.

Answer: The game developer can create a Christian-themed game that incorporates elements from the story of Ezekiel and John's perspectives. John and Ezekiel can represent two different realms - a conventional (external compliance) and a more personal (internal transformation) world. The player is provided with opportunities to make choices that influence both worlds, creating an interactive story that combines aspects of both John and Ezekiel's worlds.
