The verse in 1 John 4:20 uses the love for one’s visible neighbor (i.e., fellow human beings) as a litmus test to reveal the sincerity of claimed love for God, whom believers cannot physically see. This approach exposes several aspects of self-deception:

1. **Contradiction Between Professed and Practiced Love:** If someone claims to “love God” but simultaneously shows hatred toward his brother—another who is visible to him—it indicates a contradiction between what the person says (professes) and what they do (act). Genuine love, as defined in this context, involves actions of kindness, compassion, and care—not merely words.

2. **Self-Deception About True Nature:** The verse exposes a form of self-deception often referred to as “the spiritual counterfeit.” Believers may intellectually affirm their love for God but neglect or harm those they can see around them, suggesting that their faith is superficial rather than rooted in genuine transformation and obedience.

3. **Misplacing Priorities:** By treating the love for visible neighbors as a test, it highlights a tendency to prioritize invisible, spiritual matters over concrete relational duties among people. This misplacement of priorities signifies an inner struggle where external expressions (like professing love) diverge from internal motivations and behaviors toward others.

4. **False Sense of Spiritual Superiority:** Those who claim devotion to God yet ignore or disdain their visible brothers may be projecting a false image of themselves as spiritually superior or “chosen” by God, while in reality they fail to embody the love and righteousness that Jesus models through serving even those who are least important to them (e.g., healing lepers, feeding the hungry).

In summary, this verse functions like a mirror reflecting back on believers their true state of heart. If one cannot genuinely love someone visibly present—another flesh-and-blood human being—that person cannot truly love God in an authentic sense. It serves as a sober warning against allowing self-importance or misguided notions about spiritual status to eclipse compassionate action toward others.
