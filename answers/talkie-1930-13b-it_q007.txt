This is different from saying that moral judgments never matter, because it forbids them. Discernment is one thing, appetite to condemn another. Discernment is impartial, appetite to condemn is partial. Discernment contemplates an act or a state as simply presented to the view, appetite to condemn contemplates it in reference to a personal feeling of blame. Discernment may leave us cold, appetite to condemn leaves us hot. Discernment neither praises nor dispraises, appetite to condemn dispraises. The former is an intellectual process, the latter an emotional one. The former has to do with truth, the latter with feeling. The former may be exercised towards ourselves, the latter can be exercised towards others only. The former is a permanent possession, the latter a transitory passion. In a word, discernment is an affair of the judgment, and appetite to condemn an affair of the passions.
