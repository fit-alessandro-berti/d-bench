Great question. In Matthew 12:34–35, Jesus treats speech as **the visible output of an invisible inner storehouse**.

### The logic of the passage
- **“Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh”** = words are not random; they overflow from what fills the inner life.
- **“Good treasure … evil treasure”** = the heart is pictured as a **moral stockpile** (values, desires, loyalties, resentments, faith, pride, etc.).
- What is stored is what gets spent: eventually, in speech.

### So are words symptoms, choices, revelations, or all three?
**All three**—with nuance:

1. **Symptoms**  
   Speech is a diagnostic sign of heart condition. Bitter, deceitful, cruel speech signals inner disorder; truthful, gracious speech signals inner renewal.

2. **Choices**  
   Jesus still holds people responsible for words (see the next verses about judgment for every careless word). So speech is not mere involuntary leakage; it is morally accountable action.

3. **Revelations**  
   Words disclose the real person, often more than external religious performance. In context, Jesus is confronting leaders whose speech reveals hostility toward God despite outward piety.

### Bottom line
Jesus is saying: **your mouth is the window of your heart’s treasury**.  
You can monitor words at the surface, but lasting change requires a changed heart—because speech both expresses and exposes what is stored within.
