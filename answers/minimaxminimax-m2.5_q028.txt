The question is no longer “who deserves punishment?” but “who are *we* to administer it?”

The verse is destabilizing because it removes our ability to stand on moral high ground—not just regarding others, but regarding ourselves. That is both terrifying and strangely liberating—terrifying because it demolishes our pride, liberating because it invites compassion over condemnation.

In essence, it doesn’t just challenge *how* we judge—it questions *whether* we are even capable of judging justly. That is why it remains one of the most unsettling and transformative verses in scripture.
