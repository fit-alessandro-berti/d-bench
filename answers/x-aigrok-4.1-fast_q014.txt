### Does the Text Say Treasure *Reveals* the Heart or *Trains* the Heart?

The text primarily teaches that **treasure *trains* or *directs* the heart**, though it carries a diagnostic implication of revelation as a byproduct. Here's why, step by step:

1. **Grammatical and Causal Structure**:
   - The key phrase is: "For **where your treasure is**, **there will your heart be also**."
   - This is a causal statement (introduced by "for," explaining the prior commands). The location of the *treasure* determines ("will be") the location of the *heart*. It's not "where your heart is, there your treasure will be," which would purely reveal the heart's prior state.
   - The logic flows prescriptively: Jesus *commands* "lay up... treasures in heaven" (v. 20), implying that *choosing and investing in* the right treasure will cause the heart to follow. Treasure leads; heart follows.

2. **Context in the Sermon on the Mount**:
   - Verses 19–21 are part of a broader section on kingdom priorities (Matt. 6:1–34), contrasting earthly anxiety with heavenly trust. The imperative "lay not up... but lay up" is active: *your choices* about treasure shape your inner life.
   - This aligns with Jesus' pattern elsewhere (e.g., Matt. 6:33: "Seek first the kingdom... and all these things will be added"). Investment in heavenly treasure *reorients* the heart toward God, training it away from corruptible pursuits.

3. **Reveals vs. Trains: A Both/And Dynamic**:
   | Aspect | Reveals the Heart | Trains the Heart |
   |--------|-------------------|------------------|
   | **Primary Thrust** | Secondary (diagnostic: your current treasures expose misplaced affections). | Primary (prescriptive: store up heavenly treasure to relocate your heart). |
   | **Textual Support** | "Where your treasure *is*" (present tense) shows existing priorities. | "*Lay up*... *there will your heart be*" (future imperative + "will be") promises heart transformation. |
   | **Implication** | Self-examination tool (e.g., audit your bank statements, time, or worries). | Discipleship method (e.g., generosity and eternal focus reshape desires). |

   In short, it *trains* proactively but *reveals* retrospectively—like a compass: what you pursue shows (reveals) your direction but also sets (trains) your trajectory.

### How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together

The passage weaves these three—**possession** (what you acquire/store), **attention** (where you focus), and **love** (heart's affection)—into a unified chain reaction, with "treasure" as the binding mechanism:

1. **Possession as the Starting Point**:
   - "Lay up for yourselves treasures" (Greek: *thēsaurizō*, "to store up as treasure" or "hoard"). This is deliberate acquisition and retention—your possessions, investments, or pursuits.
   - Earthly treasures (wealth, status) are vulnerable to "moth and rust" (decay) and "thieves" (loss), making possession illusory and risky.

2. **Possession Captures Attention**:
   - What you *possess* (or labor to possess) demands maintenance, protection, and worry. Thieves "break through and steal" only if you've concentrated value there—your vigilance follows your valuables.
   - Heavenly treasures are secure ("neither moth nor rust doth corrupt"), freeing attention for higher pursuits.

3. **Attention Ignites Love (Heart)**:
   - "Heart" (Greek: *kardia*) means the core of will, emotion, and devotion—the seat of love (e.g., Deut. 6:5: "love the Lord with all your heart").
   - Possession → Attention → Love: Your treasures pull your focus, which bonds your affections. As Jesus says, the heart "will be also" where treasure is—love flows to what absorbs you.

**Tied Together Visually**:
```
Possession ("Lay up treasures") 
     ↓ (demands protection/maintenance)
Attention (vigilance against moth/rust/thieves)
     ↓ (captivates focus/desire)
Love/Heart ("There will your heart be also")
```

- **Earthly Cycle**: Possess stuff → anxious attention → divided love (idolatry).
- **Heavenly Cycle**: Possess eternal good (e.g., faith, generosity, obedience) → secure attention → undivided love for God.

This triad echoes the First Commandment (Exod. 20:3–5): Don't let created "treasures" steal God's place in your heart. Modern application? Your calendar, checkbook, and clicks reveal *and* train where your love lies—redirect them heavenward to retrain the heart.
