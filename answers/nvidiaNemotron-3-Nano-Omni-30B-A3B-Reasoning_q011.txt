The progression from “cease to do evil” to “learn to do well” signals that stopping wrongdoing is only the first step; true goodness requires actively cultivating what is right. It moves from a negative, defensive posture (avoidance) to a positive, constructive one (practice). This suggests that goodness is not merely the absence of evil, but a learned, intentional way of living that embodies love, justice, and compassion.
