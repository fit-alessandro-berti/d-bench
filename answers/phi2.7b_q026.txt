 
This passage suggests that words are both symptoms and choices as they reflect the moral character of a person. The phrase, "a good man out of the good treasure of the heart bringeth forth good things," implies that our actions, which are often translated into vocal expressions, reflect the goodness and morality we possess. Likewise, the phrase, "an evil man out of the evil treasure bringeth forth evil things," implies that our words can also reflect our evil or immoral character, even when these traits are concealed.

So, it could also be viewed as a revelation that our words are not just simple expressions, but also indicators of our innermost thoughts, feelings, and values. In a way, speaking good things comes naturally to those who possess positive moral compasses.
