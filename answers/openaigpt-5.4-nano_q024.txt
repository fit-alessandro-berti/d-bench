In Ezekiel 36, the key contrast is between **trying to behave rightly from the outside** versus **having God change the inner person so obedience becomes possible from the inside out**.

### 1) External compliance
**External compliance** is when someone performs the “right” actions—keeping rules, doing religious duties, refraining from wrongdoing—**because of pressure, habit, fear, social expectation, or self-discipline**. The behavior may look the same, but the inner motive and capacity remain largely unchanged. People can “do the commandments” for a time and still be inwardly resistant, indifferent, resentful, or unable to sustain real faithfulness.

### 2) Receiving a “new heart”
“A new heart” and “new spirit” point to an **inner transformation**—God giving:
- a new *desire* (inclination toward God’s ways),
- a new *capacity* (ability to respond),
- and a new *source of motivation* (“I will put my Spirit within you…”).

So the difference is not merely *what the person does*, but **what makes the person able and willing to do it**. Ezekiel even states the purpose: God will “cause you to walk in my statutes… and do them.” In other words, obedience is not only commanded; it is *enabled* by inner change.

### 3) Why “hardness” is described like a material condition
Ezekiel describes a “stony heart” and then says God will “take away” that stone and give “an heart of flesh.” The metaphor works like this:

- A **stone** is stiff, unresponsive, resistant to being shaped.
- “Stony heart” conveys a picture of a person whose inner life is **unyielding to God**—not just mistaken externally, but **incapable of responsiveness** as things stand.
- A **heart of flesh** implies something **sensitive, receptive, alive**, capable of change, affection, and responsiveness.

Calling it “stony” suggests that the problem is not only *behavioral* but *structural*—almost like the “material” nature of the person’s inner life must be replaced. That’s why God speaks in divine action terms (“I will take away… and I will give…”): the solution is not mainly new instruction or better effort, but **a radical renewal by God**.

### Summary
- **External compliance:** behavior changes, but the inner person may remain resistant or unchanged.
- **New heart:** God renews the inner person—desire and ability—so walking in God’s ways becomes something the person can actually do.
- The “stony heart” metaphor emphasizes that the blockage is so deep that it requires a change as fundamental as replacing a hard substance with living tissue.
