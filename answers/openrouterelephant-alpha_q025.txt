The danger is not visibility itself, but the **craving to be seen**—the *motive* behind the act.

In this verse, the issue is **hypocrisy** (from the Greek *hypokrisis*, meaning "play-acting"). If the "goodness" is performed *for* an audience, the reward sought is human praise rather than divine approval. The transaction is shifted from God to man.

Visibility merely enables the sin; it is the **lust for honor** that corrupts the act. The public deed becomes a stage for self-display, void of the hidden sincerity required for authentic moral action.
