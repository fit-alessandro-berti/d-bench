Based on the text of James 3:9–10 and the broader context of the Epistle of James, here is an analysis of the inner contradiction and the diagnostic power of speech.

### 1. The Inner Contradiction: Theological Inconsistency
The primary contradiction James exposes is not merely behavioral hypocrisy, but a **theological impossibility**.

*   **The Conflict of Honor:** The passage highlights a direct conflict between honoring the Creator and dishonoring His creation. To bless God is to acknowledge His sovereignty and holiness. To curse a human being is to treat them as worthless or evil.
*   **The Imago Dei (Image of God):** The crucial link is the phrase *"which are made after the similitude of God."* James argues that humans are not merely biological entities; they are bearers of the divine image. Therefore, when a person curses a human, they are effectively casting a shadow of disrespect onto the God whose image that human bears.
*   **The "Fountain" Paradox:** While verses 11–12 (immediately following this text) clarify the metaphor, the logic is present here: a single source cannot yield two contradictory outputs. One cannot simultaneously worship the Author of Life and seek to diminish the life He created. This exposes a **divided heart**. It reveals a person who claims to belong to God in the sanctuary (blessing Him) but treats God’s children as enemies in the marketplace (cursing them).

**Summary of the Contradiction:** It is the contradiction of **worship without love**. It exposes a faith that is performative toward God but hostile toward the neighbors God loves.

### 2. Why Speech Reveals Moral Disorder So Quickly
James argues that the tongue is the "ruler" of the body (James 3:4–5). Speech reveals moral disorder quickly for three specific reasons:

*   **Speech is the Overflow of the Heart:** As Jesus taught in Matthew 12:34, "Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks." Speech is the most immediate outlet for the internal state of a person. While actions can be planned and rehearsed, speech often happens in the heat of the moment. It is the "canary in the coal mine" that detects the air quality of the soul. If the heart is disordered (filled with bitterness, pride, or unrepentant sin), the tongue will inevitably leak that toxicity out, regardless of how holy one’s prayers sound.
*   **The Speed of the Tongue:** James describes the tongue as a "fire" and a "world of iniquity." It is the most difficult part of the body to tame. Because speech is instantaneous, it bypasses the slower processes of reason and reflection. It reveals what we value most in our immediate impulses. If we value our anger more than our neighbor's dignity, the curse will leave our lips before we can check it.
*   **The Exposure of "Double-Mindedness":** In the context of the letter, James is addressing a community struggling with integrity. Speech reveals whether a person is "double-minded" (James 1:8). A morally ordered life requires consistency. When the same mouth that praises the Father curses His image, it proves that the worship is disconnected from the will. It shows that the "blessing" is likely just habit or ritual, while the "cursing" is the true expression of the person’s internal disposition.

### Conclusion
James 3:9–10 exposes the **incompatibility of holy worship and unholy treatment of people.** Speech reveals moral disorder quickly because it is the unfiltered output of the heart; it proves that true faith cannot compartmentalize the love of God from the love of God’s image-bearers. As James concludes, *"these things ought not so to be"* because a life of faith demands a unified integrity where the mouth aligns with the heart, and the heart aligns with God.
