Short answer: because Amos is condemning not a minor imbalance but a deep, systemic hypocrisy—worship offered as a cover, a bribe, or a social badge while covenant obligations (justice, fair courts, care for the vulnerable) are flagrantly violated. The prophet’s harsh language exposes the moral fraud of ritual without righteousness.

Why the sharp rejection?
- In the prophetic mind cultic practice and social ethics belong together. Sacrifice and song are valuable only when they flow from covenant faithfulness. When ritual becomes divorced from justice it is not merely incomplete; it is counter‑feit religion.
- The blunt denunciation is rhetorical and theological: Amos, speaking for God, must wake people from complacency. Vivid, uncompromising judgment (I hate… I will not smell…) is designed to shock a society that uses religion to soothe its conscience while continuing oppression.
- The people’s abuses were systemic, not incidental. A request for “better balance” would have papered over the corruption; Amos calls for radical reorientation: “let judgment run down… and righteousness as a mighty stream” — sustained, public, transformative justice, not token worship.

What moral/spiritual fraud is exposed?
- Performative piety: public festivals, music, and offerings used to polish reputations while private and public behavior violates God’s law.
- Transactional religion: thinking that sacrifices and songs can placate God and erase the consequences of injustice—religion as insurance or bribe.
- Separation of the sacred and the social: treating temple rites as sufficient while courts are corrupt, the poor are exploited, and covenant obligations (care for widow, orphan, sojourner) are ignored.
- Religious legitimation of power: liturgy and ritual confirm elite status and distract from economic or legal abuses.

Parallel prophetic critiques (Isaiah 1:11–17; Micah 6:6–8; Jeremiah 7) show the same point: God desires ethical fidelity, not empty ritual. The point isn’t that worship is bad; it’s that worship that masks injustice is a fraud and unacceptable.
