Great question. John is doing something very practical and very diagnostic.

In **1 John**, he repeatedly challenges claims like “I know God,” “I’m in the light,” etc., by asking: *What does your life show?*  
So in 4:20, his logic is:

- **God is unseen** → claims about loving Him can be easy to make.
- **Brother/sister is seen** → love here is concrete, costly, verifiable.
- Therefore, **visible love is the evidence of invisible love**.

### Why this test?
Because biblical love is not mainly a feeling or slogan; it is active self-giving (4:9–11), modeled by Christ.  
If someone cannot love the person in front of them, their claim to love God is exposed as unreal.

Also, in biblical thought, the neighbor bears God’s image. To reject the image-bearer while professing devotion to God is a contradiction.

### What self-deception is John exposing?
At least three layers:

1. **Abstract spirituality without obedience**  
   “I love God” becomes a religious idea with no embodied practice.

2. **Selective love**  
   Loving God “in theory” while withholding love when it is inconvenient, costly, or personal.

3. **Moral blindness about oneself**  
   Thinking one is spiritually mature while persistent hatred reveals an unchanged heart.

So John isn’t saying, “Love people perfectly, then God will accept you.”  
He is saying: **love for others is the necessary evidence that one truly knows and loves God.** Without that, “I love God” is self-deceived speech.
