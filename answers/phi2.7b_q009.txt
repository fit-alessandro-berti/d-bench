
1. How does this passage define victory over evil?

The passage defines victory over evil as defeating your enemy's evil intentions with good intentions. It suggests that if anyone is seeking to harm or intimidate you, you should use their own tools against them, such as feeding them when they are hungry or giving them water when they are thirsty. By doing so, you are showing that you are not going to be overcome by their evil intentions and are instead going to use their tools against them.
