This famous passage from 1 Corinthians 13, often referred to as the "love chapter", presents a vision of love that is characterized by humility, patience, kindness and endurance rather than pride, envy, anger and suspicion. The repeated use of negations serves to contrast true love against common human weaknesses and failings.

The passage suggests that authentic love is not envious or boastful, not arrogant or rude, not self-seeking or easily angered. In other words, love is the opposite of our inherent human tendency toward pride, jealousy, irritability and self-centeredness. Instead, love is patient and kind, rejoices with the truth, always protects, trusts, hopes and perseveres. 

By defining love in opposition to these negative human traits, the passage is proposing that real strength and power is found in the ability to rise above pettiness, ego and emotionality. Love's strength lies in its humility, its capacity for patience and forgiveness, its unshakeable hope and fidelity.

The vision presented is one where the highest human virtue is not being better than others, but rather being for others - rejoicing in their good, bearing their burdens, believing the best of them, and enduring all things on their behalf. It's a sacrificial, others-centered love that finds its strength in laying down personal pride.

In contrast to worldly notions of strength being about dominance, status and self-exaltation, this passage upholds the profound strength of a love that is humble, constant and indefatigable in the face of all things. It suggests that true power is moral and spiritual in nature - the capacity to love unconditionally and unwaveringly.

So in repeatedly emphasizing what love is not - proud, envious, self-seeking, irritable, resentful - the portrait of love in 1 Corinthians 13 challenges readers to find strength and victory through the power of radical humility, generosity of spirit, and relentless commitment to the good of others. It is a high calling to rise above our lesser human instincts and demonstrate real love.
