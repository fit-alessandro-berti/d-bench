## Step 1: Understand the Image
The image described in Matthew 7:3–5 is that of a person trying to remove a small speck (mote) from someone else's eye while having a large plank (beam) in their own eye. This vivid metaphor critiques hypocrisy.

## Step 2: Analyze the Sharpness of the Image
The image is sharp because it uses an absurd and exaggerated scenario to highlight the hypocrisy of judging others while ignoring one's own significant flaws. The contrast between the "mote" and the "beam" emphasizes the disparity between the minor issue in the brother's eye and the major issue in one's own eye.

## Step 3: Examine the Criticism of Hypocrisy
The passage criticizes hypocrisy by pointing out that it is unreasonable and unhelpful to try to correct others when one has a more significant problem themselves. The act of trying to remove the mote from someone else's eye while having a beam in one's own is seen as hypocritical because it indicates a lack of self-awareness and a failure to address one's own major flaws.

## Step 4: Understand How the Passage Allows for Moral Correction
The passage does not forbid moral correction altogether. Instead, it advises that before attempting to correct others (remove the mote from their eye), one should first address their own major flaws (remove the beam from their own eye). This ensures that the person giving correction is doing so from a place of integrity and self-awareness, making their judgment and assistance more valid and helpful.

## Step 5: Synthesize the Information
The sharpness of the image lies in its powerful critique of hypocrisy. The passage teaches that moral correction is acceptable and necessary, but it must be done with self-awareness and after one has addressed their own flaws. This approach makes the act of correction more sincere and effective.

The final answer is: The passage criticizes hypocrisy by highlighting the need for self-reflection before attempting to correct others, thus making moral correction more sincere and effective.
