Here is my analysis of this passage from Proverbs and the questions you raise:

The biblical proverb "pride goeth before destruction" captures a profound insight into human nature and moral psychology. Pride and arrogance are presented not just as character flaws, but as precursors to ruin and "a fall." This suggests that prideful thinking leads people into perilous blind spots.

When someone is haughty and arrogant, they tend to greatly overestimate their own abilities, knowledge and righteousness while looking down on others. This leads to rash decisions, reckless actions and a disregard for wise counsel. The prideful person, overly confident in their own judgment, ignores warning signs and blunders forward - often straight off a cliff. 

By contrast, a "humble spirit" is linked with "the lowly" - those who society may look down upon but who have a clearer moral vision. Freed from egotism and grandiosity, the humble can see situations more objectively. They are open to learning, admitting errors, and heeding advice. As a result, they avoid many of pride's pitfalls.

The proverb also hints that pride is its own punishment. Division of "spoils" evokes images of wartime pillaging. The arrogant may appear to prosper for a time as they seize wealth, power and acclaim. But eventually, their pride spawns destruction from within, while the humble integrity of "the lowly" endures and is vindicated.

At its root, pride is a failure to acknowledge one's own flaws, limitations and smallness in the grand scheme. It's a dangerous moral blindness, disconnecting us from reality, wisdom and right conduct. By elevating the virtue of humility, Proverbs calls us to a more truthful and harmonious way of being - one based not on illusions of personal grandeur, but on recognizing our shared human vulnerability and dependence on things greater than ourselves. Only by keeping our pride in check can we walk a balanced path.
