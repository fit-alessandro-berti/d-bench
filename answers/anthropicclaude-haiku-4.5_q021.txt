# On Pride's Blindness and Collapse

## Why Destruction Follows Pride

The proverb suggests pride isn't merely a moral failing—it's a *cognitive distortion*:

- **Pride narrows vision.** The arrogant person becomes fixed on self-justification and superiority, unable to see approaching dangers or their own limitations
- **It inverts perception.** What appears as confidence to the proud looks like blindness to those around them—they miss warnings others see clearly
- **Collapse follows naturally**, not as punishment imposed externally, but as consequence of miscalculation born from distorted judgment

## The Moral Psychology of Arrogance

The contrast with "the lowly" reveals something sharp:

**The humble person:**
- Remains calibrated to reality (seeing themselves accurately)
- Stays attentive to others' perspectives
- Retains flexibility when circumstances demand it

**The arrogant person:**
- Becomes brittle—committed to a false image
- Isolates themselves (choosing "the proud" whose company reinforces delusion)
- Locks into rigid positions until they shatter

The second verse suggests a quiet insight: **better a lesser material share with people grounded in reality than riches surrounded by those who mirror your self-deception back to you.** Humility keeps you in contact with truth. Pride is ultimately lonely precisely because it severs you from accurate feedback.
