This passage establishes that speech is not an independent act but is intrinsically tied to the moral state of the heart. The connection between words (speech) and hidden stockpiles (the internal treasure/abundance) works on three levels that encompass the nature of moral agency, human reaction, and spiritual reflection.

### 1. How Speech Connects with Hidden Moral Stockpiles
*   **Abundance vs. Abuse:** The text argues that what a person speaks out of is the content of their heart ("abundance"). It posits a direct causal line: The inner treasure dictates the external words. If one has "goodness" stored up, it flows physically into speech. Conversely, a corrupted or "evil" heart (viper-like) produces corrupt language.
*   **External Manifestation:** Speech acts as the visible fruit of the internal "treasure." Just as a garden produces fruit only where plants are planted and nurtured, a person speaks words only from what is inside them. Therefore, speech is not just noise; it is evidence ("fruit").

### 2. Addressing Your Question: Symptoms, Choices, Revelations
The text suggests that these concepts function in the following way:
*   **Symbolic Function (Symptoms):** The "words" are a symptom of the heart's condition. If someone speaks good things while acting evil internally, those words are a sign or indicator ("symptom") of the underlying moral corruption. They reveal how one is behaving regarding their values without needing external witnesses.
*   **Human Agency (Choices):** This implies speech is a result of choice and action. We do not speak "out of habit" alone; we choose what we put into our internal "treasure." The text suggests that the power to store words in the heart is a gift for free will. It affirms human responsibility: we make choices about how we handle truth and righteousness before speaking.
*   **Disclosure (Revelations):** Ultimately, speech is a revelation. What one utters reveals their spiritual nature. If a person speaks good things but has an evil treasure in their heart, these words are actually a "lie" that reveals a deeper sin. Therefore, the mouth and the heart are not isolated; they disclose truth to others.

### 3. Conclusion: All Three
The passage is best understood as suggesting that **all three** functions are present together:
1.  **Symptom:** Words reveal the current state of the person's internal morality.
2.  **Choice:** Speaking involves exercising a moral choice where good or evil "treasures" are curated and stored within one.
3.  **Revelation:** Speech discloses the true character to others because it comes from what is actually stored inside.

The text ultimately points to **speech** as not just a byproduct of heart conditions, but as an exercise of free will where we actively cultivate good or evil goods in our private storage until they are spoken.
