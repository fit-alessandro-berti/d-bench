Matthew 7:3–5 uses a vivid and exaggerated image—a *mote* (a tiny speck of dust) in someone else’s eye contrasted with a *beam* (a large wooden plank) in one’s own eye—to sharply highlight the absurdity of hypocrisy. The humor and hyperbole make the critique memorable and cutting. Jesus doesn’t condemn moral correction itself but exposes the self-righteousness of those who focus on others’ minor faults while ignoring their own glaring sins. The passage implies that genuine moral guidance requires self-awareness and repentance first; only then can one "see clearly" to help others. It’s a call to humility, not silence.
