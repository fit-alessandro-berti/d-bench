 The text in Ephesians 4:31-32 is placing bitterness and forgiveness in direct opposition to each other to emphasize the importance of choosing forgiveness over bitterness. Bitterness is treated as a feeling or an attitude that can lead to harmful behaviors such as wrath, anger, clamor, evil speaking, and malice. Forgiveness, on the other hand, is a chosen posture or response to those who have wronged us.

The text is encouraging the readers to put away bitterness and all the harmful behaviors associated with it, and instead, to be kind, tenderhearted, and forgiving towards one another. The reason given for this is that God, through Christ, has forgiven us. By forgiving others, we imitate God and demonstrate the love and grace that we have received.

Furthermore, the text suggests that bitterness is contagious and can spread from person to person, leading to further harm and division. By choosing forgiveness, we not only heal our own hearts but also contribute to the healing and unity of our relationships and communities.
