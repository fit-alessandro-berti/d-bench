### Does it Reveal or Train the Heart?

The text provides a framework that transcends the binary of "reveal" versus "train." Rather than choosing one, we must understand that this passage describes a relationship of **dynamic alignment**.

While "treasure" certainly **reveals** (diagnoses) the heart's affections—showing us where our true allegiances rest—it primarily functions to **direct** (orient) the heart.

Here is how Jesus structures the argument in verses 19–21:

1.  **The Imperative (v. 19–20):** Jesus first commands an action: *"Lay not up... But lay up..."* This suggests that investment is a deliberate choice. If you deliberately alter what you possess and store, you are choosing to create a new gravitational pull for your affections.
2.  **The Principle (v. 21):** Jesus then gives the reason: *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* The phrasing "will be" implies a shift in location.

**The Synthesis:**
The text implies that the heart often follows the investment. It is not merely that "if you have treasure X, you already feel love for X." Rather, when we consistently invest (give time, money, energy to) the "heavenly," our hearts migrate to that location. The treasure acts as a **lighthouse**; by directing where we put our hoard of resources, we are inevitably directing the north of our soul. Therefore, one could argue the treasure **trains** the heart by shaping its habits of value and security, which in turn **reveals** its final orientation.

### How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied

The connection between these three elements in Matthew 6 connects to the ancient biblical understanding of the **Heart**.

**1. The Heart as the Center of Will and Emotion**
In the Hebrew and biblical Greek imagination, the "heart" is not just a physical organ, nor is it merely a feeling center. It is the internal chamber of the mind where thought and emotion meet to make a decision. It is the engine of volition. When Jesus says, "your heart will be also," he means your **ultimate focus** and **deepest loyalty** (volition).

**2. Possession as the Physical Anchor**
We cannot *serve* two masters (Matthew 6:24), and we cannot *store* two locations (Verse 19).
*   **Possession** refers to "what remains with you." It is what you secure against the morning (Mom & Rust).
*   To choose "earthly treasure" is to seek stability in temporary things.
*   To choose "heavenly treasure" is to seek stability in the permanent.
*   When you secure a place as your "possession" (investment), that place becomes your "safety net." You cannot be safe while afraid to lose it.

**3. Attention as the Path to the Heart**
This is the crucial link. Where you look, your attention goes. Where your attention goes, your love follows.
*   **Attention** here is the mental focus required to nurture that treasure. To "lay up" a treasure requires planning, protecting, and watching over it.
*   If I have a treasure on earth, I must *pay attention* to it—protecting it from thieves, rusting it from rot.
*   If I have a treasure in heaven, I must direct my attention toward acts of generosity and faith, for they are stored in the eternal context of God.
*   The passage ties the Act of **Looking** (Attention) directly to the **State of Obedience** (Love).

**4. The Tie: Gravity of Value**
The three concepts are fused by the metaphor of a **Center of Gravity**.
*   If you possess a sword (earthly power), your attention focuses on the battlefield, and your love becomes dependent on victory/security.
*   If you possess a vessel (heavenly provision), your attention focuses on the stewardship of God, and your love becomes dependent on God's faithfulness.

**Summary:**
*   **Possession** dictates where our security rests.
*   **Attention** keeps that security in view.
*   **Love** is the resulting dependency on that security.

Jesus is debunking the idea that we can "have" a treasure without having a "heart." You cannot physically separate your bank account from your bank account *personality*. Where you put your gold determines where your soul stands. Thus, **treasure does not merely reflect the heart; it dictates the soul's trajectory.**

The command of Jesus, therefore, is a call to **swap habits**: If you want your allegiance to the "Heavenly Kingdom" (Love), you must physically invest in that realm first (Possession/Attention). By tithing generosity rather than accumulating "lay-ups," you actually *train* your heart to believe in God's sufficiency, shifting your allegiance from the earth to heaven.
