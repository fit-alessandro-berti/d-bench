# The Fraud of Separated Worship

The sharpness serves a specific diagnostic purpose—Amos isn't rejecting worship itself but **exposing a fundamental disconnect**:

## The Core Fraud

The people are performing religious ritual while **committing systematic injustice**. Looking at the broader context (5:10-12), they're:
- Oppressing the poor in legal proceedings
- Extracting excessive taxes from the vulnerable
- Selling the needy for trivial debts

This isn't mild hypocrisy. It's **using worship as cover for exploitation**.

## Why Not "Better Balance"?

Amos uses categorical rejection because:

1. **Half-measures won't work** — suggesting "add some justice to your offerings" lets them believe the current system is salvageable. It isn't.

2. **The worship itself becomes complicit** — performing solemn assemblies while oppressing the poor doesn't balance out; it *sanctifies* the injustice. The ritual becomes a performance that reassures them they're righteous.

3. **God refuses to be co-opted** — accepting their offerings would implicitly endorse their arrangement ("you worship, we're good").

## What's Actually Being Exposed

The passage reveals that their religiosity is **precisely what allows them to ignore the contradiction**. Ritual creates psychological distance from consequences. You can sing beautifully to God on the Sabbath and crush a debtor on Monday if worship and justice operate in separate compartments.

Amos forces them back into a single moral frame: **You cannot do both.**
