### Understanding the Difference: Discernment vs. Condemnation

The verse Luke 6:37 is often misunderstood as suggesting that making any moral judgments is wrong. However, a closer examination reveals it's actually teaching about the intent and attitude behind our judgments, highlighting the difference between discernment and condemnation.

#### Discernment

- **Definition**: Discernment is the process of making wise, informed decisions or judgments. It involves understanding and interpreting situations, people, or teachings. In a moral and spiritual context, discernment is about distinguishing right from wrong, truth from falsehood, or wisdom from folly.
- **Biblical Context**: The Bible encourages discernment. For example, Hebrews 5:14 says, "But solid food is for the mature, who by constant use have trained themselves to distinguish good from evil." This shows that the ability to make moral and ethical judgments is a sign of spiritual maturity.
- **Purpose**: The purpose of discernment is constructive. It aims to guide individuals toward righteousness, understanding, and wise decision-making. Discernment involves evaluating situations or actions based on standards of truth and righteousness not merely on personal opinions or biases.

#### Condemnation

- **Definition**: Condemnation involves judging someone to be unworthy or guilty, often with a negative and unforgiving attitude. It focuses on punishing or criticizing rather than understanding, guiding, or restoring.
- **Biblical Context**: The verse in question, Luke 6:37, warns against condemnation. This isn't about refraining from all judgment but about not condemning others with a malicious or unforgiving heart. Jesus teaches about a different approach to dealing with others' wrongs, such as the approach of restoration and forgiveness.

#### Key Differences

- **Intent**: The intent behind discernment is to guide, understand, and sometimes correct with love and wisdom. The intent behind condemnation is to judge and punish, often without regard for restoration or understanding.
- **Attitude**: Discernment is carried out with humility, compassion, and a willingness to understand and help. Condemnation is characterized by harshness, a lack of empathy, and often a refusal to see the humanity in others.
- **Outcomes**: Discernment leads to growth, understanding, and potentially positive change. Condemnation leads to hurt, separation, and often more harm.

#### Moral Judgments and Their Importance

The statement in Luke 6:37 does not imply that moral judgments never matter. Instead, it emphasizes the manner in which we make judgments. Moral judgments are essential; they help us distinguish between right and wrong. However, Jesus teaches that we should approach these judgments with a spirit of forgiveness, mercy, and understanding.

In essence, the verse encourages a posture of grace and mercy. It suggests that if we are quick to condemn, we can expect condemnation in return. But if we approach others with discernment and a heart ready to forgive, we foster an environment of understanding, growth, and redemption. The goal is not to refrain from making moral judgments altogether but to ensure these judgments are made with love, compassion, and a desire for restoration rather than mere condemnation.
