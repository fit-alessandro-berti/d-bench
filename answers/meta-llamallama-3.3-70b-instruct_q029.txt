According to Galatians 6:1, the moral posture required for correction is one of **meekness**. Meekness implies a humble, gentle, and non-judgmental attitude. This is essential for correction because it allows the person correcting to approach the situation with empathy and understanding, rather than with a self-righteous or condescending tone.

The reason **restoration**, **meekness**, and **self-suspicion** are kept together is to emphasize the importance of a humble and reflective approach to correction. Here's why:

1. **Restoration** implies a desire to help the person overcome their fault and return to a right path, rather than simply punishing or condemning them.
2. **Meekness** ensures that the correction is done in a gentle and non-judgmental manner, which is more likely to be received positively by the person being corrected.
3. **Self-suspicion** (or considering oneself, lest thou also be tempted) serves as a reminder that the person correcting is not immune to similar faults or temptations. This self-awareness helps to prevent a self-righteous or hypocritical attitude, which can be damaging to the correction process.

By keeping these three elements together, the passage emphasizes that correction should be done with a humble, gentle, and reflective attitude, recognizing that we are all susceptible to faults and temptations. This approach is more likely to lead to genuine restoration and growth, rather than simply condemnation or punishment.
