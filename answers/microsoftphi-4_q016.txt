The elder brother's reaction in Luke 15:28-32 reveals a complex mix of emotions, each contributing to his deepest problem. Here's a breakdown of the potential issues:

1. **Anger:** The elder brother is clearly angry at his father's celebration for the returning son. His anger is evident when he refuses to join the festivities.

2. **Envy:** He expresses envy regarding the attention and rewards given to his brother, who he perceives as undeserving due to his past actions.

3. **Wounded Fairness:** The elder brother feels a sense of injustice. He believes that his loyalty and obedience over the years have been overlooked, while his wayward brother is rewarded after a brief absence.

4. **Pride in Obedience:** He highlights his years of faithful service, suggesting pride in his obedience. This indicates that he values his consistent adherence to his father's commands and sees it as something worthy of recognition and reward.

5. **Discomfort with Mercy:** The elder brother struggles with the concept of mercy and grace extended to his brother. He has difficulty accepting that the father's love and forgiveness are unconditional and not based on merit.

The deepest problem likely stems from a combination of wounded fairness and discomfort with mercy itself. His sense of injustice and difficulty accepting his brother's forgiveness highlight a struggle with understanding the father's unconditional love and forgiveness. This suggests that, while he values obedience, he has a limited understanding of grace and mercy, which should be at the heart of the father's actions and love. His reaction underscores a challenge in embracing a worldview where love and forgiveness are not contingent upon one's merits or past failures.
