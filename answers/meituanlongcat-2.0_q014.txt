This passage from the Sermon on the Mount is one of the most psychologically and spiritually profound statements Jesus ever made. To answer your questions, we have to look at the mechanics of how Jesus links external actions (laying up treasure) with internal realities (the heart).

### Does treasure reveal the heart, or train it?

The answer is **both**, but in a specific sequence: **Treasure trains the heart, and in doing so, it ultimately reveals it.**

**1. Treasure Trains the Heart (The Formative Aspect)**
In verses 19 and 20, Jesus gives an imperative command: *“Lay up for yourselves treasures...”* This is an active, ongoing process. The Greek word for "lay up" (*thēsaurizete*) is in the present continuous tense—it implies a habitual, ongoing action. Jesus is not merely describing a static reality; He is prescribing a discipline. 

Where you choose to invest your time, energy, and resources is a training ground for your soul. Just as a musician trains their fingers through repetitive practice, a person trains their heart by repeatedly directing their investments toward either earth or heaven. If you continually invest in earthly security, you are training your heart to be earthly. If you invest in the Kingdom, you are training your heart to be heavenly. 

**2. Treasure Reveals the Heart (The Diagnostic Aspect)**
Verse 21 acts as the diagnostic result of that training: *“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”* The word "will be" (*estai*) is future indicative. It implies an inevitable trajectory. If you want to know where someone’s heart truly is, don't just listen to their words; look at their bank account, their calendar, and their investments. The treasure acts as an X-ray, exposing the true condition of the heart that has been formed by its investments.

So, the sequence is: **You invest your treasure $\rightarrow$ your heart is trained by that investment $\rightarrow$ your heart is ultimately revealed by where your treasure ends up.**

### How do possession, attention, and love become tied together?

Jesus weaves these three concepts together through the contrast between earthly and heavenly realities. They form a causal chain: **Possession dictates Attention, and Attention shapes Love.**

**1. Possession (The Treasure)**
"Treasure" represents whatever you possess that you consider ultimately valuable. It is your ultimate security, your functional savior. Jesus notes that earthly treasure is inherently insecure—it is subject to *moth* (decay), *rust* (erosion), and *thieves* (loss). Heavenly treasure is the only secure possession. What you choose to possess reveals what you trust to keep you safe.

**2. Attention (The Moth, Rust, and Thieves)**
This is the crucial link between possession and love. Notice the difference in the two types of treasure:
*   **Earthly treasure demands anxious attention.** Because it can be eaten by moths, rusted through, or stolen, possessing it requires constant vigilance, worry, and maintenance. Your attention is consumed by protecting what you have.
*   **Heavenly treasure demands no anxious attention.** Because it cannot be corrupted or stolen, it frees your attention from the exhausting task of self-preservation.

Therefore, **possession dictates attention.** If your treasure is on earth, your attention will be consumed by the horizontal—guarding, maintaining, and worrying. If your treasure is in heaven, your attention is freed to focus on the vertical—on God and His Kingdom.

**3. Love (The Heart)**
In the biblical worldview, the "heart" is not primarily the seat of emotions (as in modern Western thought), but the seat of the will, the mind, and the affections—the governing center of a person. To say your "heart" is somewhere is to say your ultimate love and allegiance reside there.

This is where the chain completes itself. **Attention shapes love.** We inevitably love what we pay attention to. 
*   If your attention is consumed by the maintenance of earthly possessions, your love will be tethered to the earth. You will become anxious, possessive, and fearful.
*   If your attention is freed by the security of heavenly treasure, your love will ascend. You will become generous, secure, and God-focused.

### Summary
Jesus is exposing a spiritual law of gravity: **What you possess captures your attention, and what captures your attention captures your love.** 

If you want to know what you truly love, look at what you possess. If you want to change what you love, change what you possess. This is why Jesus commands us to lay up treasure in heaven—not as a way to earn salvation, but as a means of grace to detach our affections from a decaying world and reorient our hearts toward the only Treasure that will never fade, rust, or be stolen.
