Humble trust in God is asked for, as opposed to self-confidence. The trust required is not anti-intellectual, but intellectuates itself in childlike dependence upon divine guidance. The man who trusts in himself, and not in the Lord, walks in darkness; but he who trusts in the Lord walks in light. The former knows not whither he goes, but the latter knows the path in which he treads to be the path of life. The one is like a ship at sea, without rudder or compass; the other like a ship sailing steadily towards a desired haven, under the direction of a skilful pilot. The self-confident man is ever liable to stumble, and to fall into mischief; but he that trusteth in the Lord shall stand firm.
