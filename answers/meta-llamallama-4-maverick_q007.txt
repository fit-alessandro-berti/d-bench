The passage from Luke 6:37 is often misunderstood as advocating for a complete abandonment of moral judgment. However, a closer reading reveals that Jesus is not discouraging discernment or moral evaluation, but rather warning against a judgmental attitude that leads to condemnation.

To understand the nuance, let's explore the difference between discernment and the appetite to condemn.

### Discernment vs. Condemnation

**Discernment** involves making moral judgments or evaluations to understand a situation, person, or action. It is a neutral or objective assessment that helps us navigate the world, make informed decisions, and maintain healthy relationships. Discernment can be exercised with empathy, compassion, and a willingness to understand different perspectives.

**The appetite to condemn**, on the other hand, is driven by a desire to criticize, judge harshly, or punish others. This attitude is often rooted in a self-righteous or superior mindset, where one feels entitled to judge others without considering their own flaws or circumstances.

### Key Differences

The key differences between discernment and the appetite to condemn lie in their motivations, tones, and outcomes. While discernment is driven by a desire to understand, the appetite to condemn is driven by a desire to judge or punish. Discernment is characterized by empathy, compassion, and a willingness to listen, whereas the appetite to condemn is marked by a harsh, critical, or punitive tone. Ultimately, discernment aims to understand, help, or correct, whereas the appetite to condemn seeks to criticize, shame, or condemn.

### Illustrating the Difference

To illustrate this distinction, consider the following examples:

* A friend confides in you about their struggles with addiction. A discerning response might be, "I'm here to support you. I can see that you're struggling, and I want to help you find resources to overcome this challenge." In contrast, a condemning response might be, "You're so weak for giving in to addiction. You need to get your act together."
* You're evaluating a complex social issue, such as poverty or inequality. A discerning approach might involve analyzing the root causes, considering multiple perspectives, and seeking solutions that address the underlying problems. A condemning attitude, on the other hand, might lead you to simplistically blame certain individuals or groups for the issue, without considering the nuances and complexities involved.

### Jesus' Teaching in Context

In the context of Luke 6:37, Jesus is warning against the appetite to condemn, not discernment. He's encouraging his followers to adopt a non-judgmental, non-condemning attitude, which is characterized by forgiveness, mercy, and compassion.

By saying "Judge not, and ye shall not be judged," Jesus is cautioning against the hypocrisy of judging others while being blind to one's own flaws. He's promoting a culture of self-reflection, humility, and empathy, where individuals recognize their own limitations and vulnerabilities.

### Conclusion

In summary, Luke 6:37 is not advocating for a complete rejection of moral judgments, but rather encouraging a nuanced and compassionate approach to evaluating others. By distinguishing between discernment and the appetite to condemn, we can cultivate a more empathetic, understanding, and constructive way of engaging with others, while still maintaining a moral framework for evaluating the world around us. This approach allows us to navigate complex situations with wisdom, compassion, and a deep understanding of the human experience.
