Great question.  
Luke 6:37 is **not** teaching “nothing is right or wrong.” It is warning against a **certain posture of heart**.

## 1) What Jesus is *not* saying
He is not banning all moral evaluation.

Elsewhere, Scripture clearly calls for discernment:

- “Judge with right judgment” (John 7:24)
- “Test the spirits” (1 John 4:1)
- Correct a brother/sister gently (Gal. 6:1)

So Christians are expected to recognize good vs evil, truth vs error.

## 2) What Jesus *is* forbidding
He forbids the spirit that loves to condemn:

- playing God as final judge of another person’s worth
- harsh, smug, merciless fault-finding
- ignoring our own sin while spotlighting others (cf. Matt. 7:1–5, speck/log)
- treating people as cases to prosecute, not neighbors to restore

In short: **not “never evaluate,” but “never delight in condemning.”**

## 3) Discernment vs. appetite to condemn

### Discernment
- Asks: “What is true? What is good? How do we help?”
- Is humble: “I, too, need mercy.”
- Targets actions/teaching, not the person’s ultimate destiny.
- Aims at restoration, protection, repentance, healing.
- Uses truth + mercy together.

### Appetite to condemn
- Asks: “How can I prove I’m better?”
- Is proud and eager to expose.
- Rushes to motives and final verdicts.
- Enjoys punishment more than repentance.
- Withholds the same mercy it wants for itself.

## 4) A practical test
Before speaking moral judgment, ask:

1. **Am I applying the same standard to myself?**  
2. **Do I want this person restored, or just defeated?**  
3. **Would I say this with tears, patience, and willingness to forgive?**

If not, it’s probably condemnation, not discernment.

So Luke 6:37 calls us to a kingdom ethic: **clear moral vision without a merciless heart**.
