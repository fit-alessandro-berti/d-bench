Certainly! Let’s break down **Proverbs 16:18–19** step by step:

### 1. **“Pride goeth before destruction, and an haughty spirit before a fall.”**

- **Pride as a precursor:** The verse suggests that pride is not merely a character flaw—it is the *root cause* or the *first step* toward ruin. When someone is overly proud, they fail to see their own limitations or the perspectives of others.
- **Destruction and fall:** Pride leads to destructive outcomes—both personal (destruction) and social (fall from status, relationships, etc.). The “haughty spirit” refers to an attitude of superiority that blinds one to reality and invites downfall.

### 2. **“Better it is to be of a humble spirit with the lowly, than to divide the spoil with the proud.”**

- **Humility vs. Division:** Dividing spoils among the proud implies taking advantage of others for personal gain, often at their expense. Humility, on the other hand, means sharing, valuing others, and avoiding exploitation.
- **Moral Psychology of Arrogance:** Arrogance blinds people to the needs and dignity of others; it fosters selfishness and isolation. The “lowly” are those who do not seek power over others—they are approachable, teachable, and less likely to retaliate against humility.

### 3. **Why is pride linked to blindness and collapse?**

- **Blindness:** Pride makes people unable to recognize their own flaws or mistakes. They cannot learn from criticism or admit weakness because they believe themselves above reproach.
- **Collapse:** Because pride distorts judgment and alienates others, it sets up conditions for failure—whether in relationships, leadership, or self-understanding.

### 4. **Contrast with “the lowly”**

- **Lowliness as Strength:** The “lowly” are humble, open, and receptive. Their humility allows them to avoid the pitfalls of pride: they don’t seek to dominate, so they don’t provoke resentment or division.
- **Moral Psychology:** Arrogance isolates; humility connects. The contrast highlights that true security and wisdom come not from dominance, but from modesty and empathy.

---

**Summary:**  
Pride leads to destruction because it blinds us to our own faults and the needs of others, making us vulnerable to collapse. In contrast, humility with the “lowly” fosters connection and avoids the divisiveness and eventual downfall that pride brings. The proverb thus teaches that moral strength lies in humility, not in self-aggrandizement.
