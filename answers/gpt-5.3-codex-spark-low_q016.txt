• The elder brother’s deepest problem is wounded fairness wrapped in self-righteousness (a prideful “I earned more”
  mindset).

  - Anger is visible, but it’s a symptom.
  - Envy is present (resentment of gift to the prodigal), but not the root.
  - Pride in obedience is the core frame: he measures relationship by compliance (“I never transgressed”) and expects it
    to earn proportionate reward.
  - Discomfort with mercy follows from that framework: the father’s generosity breaks his moral accounting, so he
    interprets mercy as unfair.

  So the key issue is not that mercy is evil, but that his idea of justice (“I worked hard, he wasted everything”)
  cannot bear grace.
