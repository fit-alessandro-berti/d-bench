1. The inner contradiction that James points to  

   • With the tongue we “bless God.” That act claims that we revere, love and submit to Him.  
   • With the same tongue we “curse men, who are made in the likeness (similitude) of God.”  

   If a craftsman’s work is precious to us, we do not spit on it in the next breath. Likewise, to honour the Creator while despising the bearers of His image is logically and morally inconsistent.  
   James therefore unmasks a double-minded piety: a posture of worship that is cancelled out by contempt for people. It is the practical form of the “double soul” he condemned earlier (James 1:8).

2. Why the tongue exposes the disorder so fast  

   a. Direct pipeline from the heart  
      “Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks” (Matt 12:34). Because words are formed before much reflection, what is hidden in the heart surfaces through speech almost unfiltered.

   b. Ease and frequency  
      It costs nothing to talk; we do it constantly. Every conversation gives the inner life a chance to show itself—far more often than deeds do.

   c. Minimal social brakes  
      Actions usually require planning, resources or risk. Words can be uttered in a second, before conscience or courtesy can restrain them.

   d. Disproportionate power  
      As James has just said (3:5-6), the tongue is a small member that can set a whole forest ablaze. Because its effects are felt immediately in the people we address, any moral flaw it carries is instantly obvious.

   e. Theological inconsistency is audible  
      Blessing God is formal and intentional—prayer, worship, confession. Cursing tends to be spontaneous—irritation, gossip, insults. The contrast therefore becomes public and unmistakable.

In short, the passage exposes the hypocrisy of professing love for God while verbally assaulting those who bear His image. Speech reveals the disorder quickly because it is the heart’s shortest, easiest, and most frequent exit to the outside world; whatever is wrong inside will therefore be heard long before it is seen in other ways.
