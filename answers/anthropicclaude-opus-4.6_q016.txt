# The Elder Brother's Deepest Problem

This is one of the most carefully constructed passages in the Gospels, and it rewards equally careful reading. The elder brother's complaint *layers* several things, but they are not all at the same depth.

## Peeling Back the Layers

**On the surface:** anger and wounded fairness. He makes what sounds like a legitimate grievance — years of faithful service with no reward, while the wastrel gets a feast. This is the layer most immediately visible.

**Beneath that:** envy, certainly. But envy of *what* exactly? Not merely of the calf. He has had access to everything all along — the father says so explicitly: *"all that I have is thine."* He envies something he does not understand and therefore cannot name.
