Here is my analysis of why Micah 6:8 presents such a demanding moral summary, and how the virtues of justice, mercy and humility interact:

On the surface, this famous verse seems to present a simple, concise summary of what God desires from humans - to act justly, love mercy, and walk humbly with Him. However, upon reflection, living out these three imperatives in balance is extremely challenging. 

Justice and mercy often exist in tension with one another. Pure justice demands that wrongdoing be punished and that everyone receive exactly what they deserve based on their actions. Mercy, on the other hand, compels us to forgive, to show compassion, and to give offenders better than what they deserve. Pursuing justice can lead us to be harsh, while focusing solely on mercy can lead to permissiveness and a failure to protect victims or society at large.

Humility acts as an essential moderating force between these two. Recognizing our own moral failings and need for grace prevents us from meting out justice without mercy. It leads us to temper justice with forbearance and compassion. At the same time, humility prevents mercy from turning into mere sentimentality or a failure to stand for what is right. True humility stems from an awareness of God's perfect character; it compels us to promote the justice and righteousness He desires.

Walking "humbly with God" provides the unifying principle. If we live in constant awareness of God's presence and our accountability to Him, it will enable us to combine justice and mercy in the proper proportion. We will uphold justice because we know God is just, but we will also show abundant mercy because we know how much mercy God has shown us. 

The lofty standard of Micah 6:8 also confronts our natural selfishness. Justice, mercy and humility are all outwardly focused - concerned with the well-being of others and one's stance before God. They leave no room for self-centeredness, pride or ego. To truly fulfill this "good" that God requires, one must be willing to sacrifice and die to self on a daily basis.

In the end, only Jesus Christ perfectly embodied justice, mercy and humility in His earthly life. So while Micah 6:8 gives us a standard to continually strive for, it also points to our need for a Savior. Fully living out this moral summary is impossible apart from the grace of Christ and the empowerment of the Holy Spirit. It requires daily dependence on God.

So in summary, this short verse is profoundly demanding because it requires us to perfectly balance competing virtues, to die to self-interest, and to live in complete submission to and reliance upon God. Justice, mercy and humility each play an essential role in the moral life, but walking them out is impossible on our own. Micah 6:8 ultimately drives us to the cross of Christ, which is where justice and mercy meet, and where we find the grace to walk humbly with our God.
