
This is a profound question that gets to the heart of Jesus' teaching on the motivation of the heart. Let me walk through the layers of meaning here.

## The Core Issue: Motive, Not Location

The problem isn't visibility *itself* but rather **the craving for human recognition and approval**. Here's why:

**The Greek word behind "seen" (φανερωθῶσιν)** suggests making oneself appear or gain visibility. The issue is the *intention* behind the action - when the primary goal shifts from helping others or honoring God to receiving human praise.

## Jesus' Broader Teaching Context

Notice the pattern in this passage:
- **Public hypocrisy** → **secret faithfulness** → **divine reward**
- Verse 4: "your Father who sees in secret will reward you"

Jesus assumes God sees *everything* anyway, so human visibility becomes unnecessary and actually dangerous as a motive.

## The Danger of Visibility-Craving

When we crave public recognition for good deeds, several things happen:

1. **Incentives get misaligned** - We may avoid genuinely helpful but less visible acts
2. **Heart posture shifts** - Love for God/others becomes secondary to love for human approval  
3. **Spiritual reward gets withheld** - We miss the eternal perspective of heavenly reward

## Modern Application

Today's equivalent might be:
- Social media activism performed for "likes" rather than genuine concern
- Public charitable acts that become brand-building exercises
- Religious practices done for Instagram rather than devotion

## The Deeper Answer

The problem is ultimately **idolatry of the heart** - when we make human opinion our ultimate audience rather than God. Visibility itself is morally neutral; it's the craving for visibility that reveals a deeper spiritual malady.
