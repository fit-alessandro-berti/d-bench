Great question. In **Matthew 6:1**, the danger is **not mere visibility**—it is doing righteousness **“to be seen”** (for applause).

Jesus is correcting motive, not banning every public good deed.

- In **Matthew 5:16**, he says: *“Let your light shine before others…”*  
- In **Matthew 6:1**, he warns against practicing righteousness *“in order to be seen by them.”*

So the issue is the **audience of the heart**:

- If the audience is **God**, public visibility can still be faithful.
- If the audience is **human approval**, the act becomes spiritually hollow.

Why morally dangerous?

1. **It turns worship into performance** (goodness as image-management).
2. **It feeds pride and hypocrisy** (looking righteous over being righteous).
3. **It changes the reward system**: you get human praise now, but forfeit deeper reward from God (“they have received their reward”).

So, in this verse, the problem is chiefly the **craving to be seen**, not being seen itself.
