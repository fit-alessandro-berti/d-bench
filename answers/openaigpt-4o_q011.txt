The transition in the text from "cease to do evil" to "learn to do well" suggests that goodness is not simply about refraining from bad actions but involves actively learning and practicing positive actions. This implies that goodness is a practiced form of love and justice, requiring intention and effort. 

Ceasing to do evil is about removing harmful behaviors, which is a necessary first step. However, the passage doesn't stop there; it emphasizes the importance of engaging in positive and proactive actions: seeking justice, relieving the oppressed, and caring for the vulnerable like the fatherless and widows. This demonstrates that true goodness involves cultivating virtues and actively contributing to the well-being of others and the community.

Thus, the biblical text underscores that while avoiding evil is important, it is insufficient on its own. The pursuit of righteousness involves a commitment to learning and doing good, characterized by acts of love, justice, and mercy.
