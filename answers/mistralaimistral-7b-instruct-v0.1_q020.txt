 The image of bitterness as a root suggests that it is a deep-seated emotion that can grow and spread over time, hidden beneath the surface. Bitterness is not just an act or a momentary feeling, but rather a moral growth that can take hold in one's heart if left unchecked.

The metaphor of a root also implies that the damage caused by bitterness can be delayed and far-reaching. Just as a small root can eventually grow into a large and destructive tree, a small seed of bitterness can grow into a large and damaging resentment.

Moreover, the way one person's resentment can spread to others is suggested by the idea of many being defiled by it. Bitterness is contagious, and it can easily spread from one person to another, causing harm and defiling the hearts of those around them.

Therefore, the image of bitterness as a root emphasizes the importance of dealing with this emotion before it takes hold and causes extensive damage. It also highlights the need for ongoing vigilance and the importance of maintaining a heart of peace and holiness in order to avoid the pitfalls of bitterness and its destructive consequences.
