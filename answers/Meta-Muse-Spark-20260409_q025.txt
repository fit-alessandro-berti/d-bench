**The danger isn't visibility. It's the *craving* to be seen.**

Matthew 6:1 is the thesis statement for the whole section on giving, praying, and fasting. Jesus isn't banning public goodness — He just did a bunch of public miracles. He's diagnosing the heart behind it.

### **What the verse actually targets**

**“Take heed that ye do not your alms before men, *to be seen of them*”**  
The Greek phrase is *pros to theathēnai autois* — literally “with the purpose of being gazed at by them.” It’s teleological. The problem is the *telos*, the end goal.

So break it into two scenarios:

| Scenario | Action | Motive | Jesus' verdict |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Craving visibility** | You give alms *so that* people see | Applause is the reward you’re chasing | You already got it. Heaven’s register is empty |
| **Visible goodness** | People happen to see you give | Obedience/love is the reward you’re chasing | Father who sees in secret will reward you, Matthew 6:4 |

### **Why the craving makes goodness dangerous**

1. **It replaces the audience**  
   You trade “Father which is in heaven” for the crowd. The reward shifts from eternal to Instagram-level: here now, gone tomorrow. Jesus calls that “they have their reward” in v2. Full payment, no tip coming later.

2. **It corrupts the act itself**  
   When being seen is the goal, the goodness becomes a performance. The poor person isn’t the neighbor you’re loving — they’re a prop in your morality play. The act is no longer love of neighbor, it’s love of reputation.

3. **It creates hypocrisy**  
   V2 literally calls these people *hypokritai* — stage actors. The danger is splitting yourself: the outer “righteous person” vs the inner “validation hunter.” That split is spiritually corrosive.

### **So is visibility itself the problem?**

No. Jesus resolves this tension two chapters earlier:  
**“Let your light so shine before men, that they may see your good works, and glorify your Father which is in heaven.”** Matthew 5:16

The difference:

- **Matthew 5:16** → They see your works → God gets glory
- **Matthew 6:1** → They see your works → *You* get glory

Same visibility, opposite gravitational pull.

**Bottom line:** Public goodness becomes morally dangerous when the public is the point. Visibility is neutral — it’s the craving that hijacks the act and cashes out the eternal reward for human applause.
