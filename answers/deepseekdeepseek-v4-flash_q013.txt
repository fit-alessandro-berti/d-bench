This is an excellent question that cuts to the heart of the prophetic message. The passage from Amos is so sharp precisely because it is *not* asking for a better balance. It is diagnosing a fundamental disease, not a minor imbalance.

Here is a breakdown of why the rejection is so absolute and what moral/spiritual fraud is being exposed.

### Why Not a Call for Balance?

A call for "balance" would imply that their worship was essentially good, just a bit lacking in moral emphasis. Amos, speaking for God, says the opposite: the worship itself, in its current state, is **an abomination**.

1.  **The Worship was Ritual Without Reality:** The people were meticulously performing all the correct rituals—feast days, solemn assemblies, burnt offerings, grain offerings, peace offerings, and elaborate music. They checked every religious box. But their daily lives were a systematic violation of God's covenant. They were cheating the poor, perverting justice in the courts, and exploiting the vulnerable (as seen throughout Amos 2-5). The worship was a lie. It was a performance that pretended to honor a God of justice while actively defying him in practice.

2.  **The Worship was a Cover for Injustice:** The most dangerous spiritual fraud is when people use religion to feel good about themselves *while doing evil*. The people of Israel likely thought, "We are God's chosen people! We make the right sacrifices! We sing the right songs! Surely God is pleased with us." This piety acted as a moral anesthetic. It allowed them to oppress the poor and then come to the temple to feel forgiven and righteous. God's "I hate, I despise" is the tearing down of this self-deception. He is not rejecting worship in general, but *their* specific brand of hypocritical worship that served as a smoke screen for systemic sin.

3.  **The Verb "Smell" is Key:** The phrase "I will not smell in your solemn assemblies" is a powerful Hebrew idiom. In the ancient Near East, the smoke of a pleasing sacrifice was described as a "pleasing odor" to God (e.g., Noah's sacrifice in Genesis 8:21). God is saying, "Your rituals don't just fail to please me—they *stink* to me." They are offensive and repulsive *because* they are disconnected from the life he commands.

### The Moral or Spiritual Fraud Exposed

The fraud is **hypocrisy**, but a specific, systemic kind: **the fraudulent claim that religious devotion can substitute for ethical living.**

This is the core of the prophetic critique found throughout the Old Testament. The fraud works in two ways:

- **Fraud Against God:** The people pretend to honor God with their lips and rituals, while their hearts are far from him (Isaiah 29:13). They treat God as if he were a pagan deity who could be bribed by sacrifices and placated by ceremonies, instead of the holy God who demands justice, mercy, and humility (Micah 6:8). They are committing a fraud by trying to buy off God with a counterfeit currency of ritualism.

- **Fraud Against Neighbors:** The people fraudulently claim the title of "people of God" and enjoy the benefits of the covenant community while actively breaking the covenant's most fundamental commands: to love one's neighbor, to care for the poor, and to ensure justice. They are stealing the identity of the faithful while living as the faithless.

### The Deeper Logic: "Let Justice Roll Down"

The famous finale in verse 24 is not just a nicer alternative. It is the **diagnosis and the cure**.

> "But let judgment [justice] run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream."

- **The Diagnosis:** Their worship was stagnant, like a polluted pond. It was self-serving, ritualistic, and disconnected from life.
- **The Cure:** True worship—the kind God desires and accepts—is a dynamic, powerful, ever-flowing force for justice and righteousness in the world. The "stream" is not a gentle trickle; it is a "mighty," unceasing torrent that sweeps away corruption, oppression, and self-deception.

**In summary:** Amos is not rejecting worship. He is exposing the **fraud of worship that is detached from justice.** The sharp rejection is a divine act of tearing down the false security of empty ritual. It is God saying, "Your songs are noise to me because the cries of the poor you are creating are louder. Do not offer me a beautiful sacrifice with dirty hands. First, let justice flow. *That* is the only offering I will receive."
