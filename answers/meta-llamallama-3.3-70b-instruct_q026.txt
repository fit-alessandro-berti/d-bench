This passage connects speech with hidden moral stockpiles in the heart by suggesting that the words we speak are a direct reflection of the condition of our heart. The phrase "out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh" (v. 34) implies that our words are a overflow of what is stored in our heart. The heart is portrayed as a treasure trove that contains either good or evil things, and the words we speak are a manifestation of the treasure that is within.

This passage suggests that words are all three: symptoms, choices, and revelations. 

* **Symptoms**: Our words can be symptoms of the state of our heart, revealing the underlying moral condition of our inner being. Just as a doctor might diagnose a disease based on its symptoms, our words can be seen as symptoms of the spiritual condition of our heart.
* **Choices**: The passage implies that we have a choice in what we speak, and that our words are a reflection of the treasure we have chosen to store in our heart. A good person chooses to store good things in their heart, and therefore brings forth good words, while an evil person chooses to store evil things and brings forth evil words.
* **Revelations**: Our words can also be revelations of our true character, revealing the hidden contents of our heart to others. Jesus is essentially saying that we can't hide the true state of our heart forever; eventually, our words will betray us and reveal what is really inside.

Overall, this passage highlights the importance of guarding our heart and being mindful of the words we speak, as they have the power to reveal our true character and reflect the moral stockpiles we have stored within.
