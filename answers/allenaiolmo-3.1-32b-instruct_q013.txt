This passage from Amos 5:21–24 is one of the most scathing indictments of religious hypocrisy in the Hebrew Bible. Let’s break down what’s happening and why the tone is so harsh.

### Historical and Literary Context

Amos is a prophet who spoke in the eighth century BCE, during a time when Israel was prosperous but also deeply corrupt. The people were observing religious festivals, offering sacrifices, and going through the motions of worship, but their hearts were far from God. The nation was marked by social injustice—exploitation of the poor, oppression of the weak, and widespread dishonesty.

### The Passage Explained

**“I hate, I despise your feast days, and I will not smell in your solemn assemblies.”**  
The Lord is not rejecting worship in general, but the way it’s being performed. The religious festivals and assemblies, which were meant to be times of repentance, humility, and communal renewal, have become empty rituals. They’re performed out of habit or social expectation, not genuine devotion.

**“Though ye offer me burnt offerings and your meat offerings, I will not accept them...”**  
Sacrifices and offerings were central to Israelite worship, but here God says He will not accept them. Why? Because the acts are performed without heartfelt repentance or righteousness. The external act is hollow when the internal state is corrupt.

**“Take thou away from me the noise of thy songs... I will not hear the melody of thy viols.”**  
Even the music, which should accompany worship and express joy and devotion to God, is dismissed because it’s part of a show, not a sincere expression of faith.

**“But let judgment run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream.”**  
God calls for tangible justice and righteousness—not just ritual correctness. True worship must be accompanied by ethical living.

### Why Such a Harsh Rejection?

The passage is sharp because it exposes **moral and spiritual fraud**: the people are performing religion as a social performance, while ignoring the core requirements of justice and integrity that God demands. They are going through the motions—offering sacrifices, singing songs, observing festivals—while continuing to oppress the poor, cheat each other, and act unjustly.

This is **not a critique of worship itself**, but of worship that is divorced from moral life. God is saying: “Your rituals are worthless if your hearts and actions are corrupt. I don’t want empty sacrifices; I want a people who live righteously.”

### What Kind of Fraud Is Exposed?

The fraud is **hypocrisy**:  
- **Religious hypocrisy:** Using outward religious actions to maintain social standing or cultural identity, while ignoring the ethical demands of the religion.
- **Moral fraud:** Pretending to be righteous before God and others while actually oppressing the vulnerable.
- **Spiritual emptiness:** Worship that is mechanical, habitual, and devoid of genuine faith or transformed living.

### In Summary

Amos 5:21–24 rejects worship that is only external and not internal. The passage exposes the fraud of people who perform religious duties without allowing those beliefs to shape their moral and social actions. True worship, according to Amos, is inseparable from justice and righteousness. God will not be placated by empty rituals; He demands a life of integrity and compassion.

This is a central theme in Amos and much of the Hebrew prophets: true religion is lived, not just performed.
