• Because “light” in John 3:19–21 symbolizes moral disclosure as much as illumination. In that sense, light isn’t merely
  comfort—it is evidentiary: it reveals what is real.

  1. Light exposes actions to truth
      - To “do evil” in this passage is not just committing bad acts, but living apart from the reality of God.
      - “Light” means God’s presence and judgment, where hidden motives, self-deceptions, and patterns become visible.
      - So people who love darkness are not just avoiding physical light; they avoid being seen by the one reality they
        can’t control.
  2. Truth makes internal life visible
      - John’s contrast is between two selves:
          - one tied to deed-driven identity (“doeth evil”),
          - one aligned with truth (“doeth truth”).
      - If your deeds are inconsistent with your identity or values, truth feels threatening because it forces
        coherence.
      - Light reveals inconsistency.
  3. Exposure brings shame—not because shame is bad, but because denial is ending
      - “Shame” here is often social or moral disorientation: “if the truth is exposed, I lose the story I told about
        myself.”
      - That shame is a signal of conscience and accountability, but in this verse it becomes a reason for avoidance.
      - “Lest his deeds should be reproved”: the fear is not of being loved less, but of being corrected and unmasked.
  4. The deeper motive: control the self-image
      - Humans seek a self-image they can manage: respected, respectable, competent, spiritually competent, etc.
      - Light forces reality over image: motives, justifications, and excuses are no longer sufficient.
      - So people prefer darkness because it preserves autonomy and concealment.
  5. Why the one who does truth comes to the light
      - The honest person comes because he or she no longer needs concealment for identity.
      - “That their deeds may be made manifest, that they are wrought in God” means:
          - truthfully living is not about perfection;
          - it is about being transparent to a reality larger than self.
      - Manifestation is not annihilation; it is alignment and healing.

  In short: people resist light when it implies being truly known. Truth is cleansing, but it first removes pretense,
  and what is exposed can feel like humiliation before it becomes freedom.
