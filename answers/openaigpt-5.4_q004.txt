Proverbs 3:5–6 asks for **total-hearted reliance on God**, not the abandonment of thought.

## What kind of trust?

The trust here is:

- **Personal** — “trust in the Lord,” not merely in principles or outcomes.
- **Wholehearted** — “with all thine heart,” meaning one’s inner confidence, allegiance, and dependence.
- **Practical** — “in all thy ways acknowledge him,” so this trust shows up in decisions, habits, plans, and moral choices.
- **Submissive** — it accepts that God’s wisdom is higher than ours and lets his will correct our instincts.

This is not a call to stop thinking, but to stop treating one’s own judgment as **ultimate**.

## “Lean not unto thine own understanding”

That phrase does **not** mean:
- reason is evil,
- learning is unnecessary,
- careful judgment is unspiritual,
- evidence and wisdom should be ignored.

Instead, it means:
- do not make your own perspective your final authority;
- do not assume your insight is sufficient by itself;
- do not trust your interpretation of life apart from God.

Biblical wisdom literature regularly **praises understanding, prudence, instruction, and counsel**. So the warning cannot be anti-intellectual. It is a warning against **intellectual self-sufficiency**.

## Humble self-distrust vs. anti-intellectualism

### Humble self-distrust
This says:
- “My understanding is real, but limited.”
- “I can reason, but I can also deceive myself.”
- “I need God’s wisdom, correction, and guidance.”
- “I should seek counsel, revelation, and moral clarity beyond myself.”

This kind of self-distrust is actually compatible with serious thought. It makes a person:
- more teachable,
- slower to arrogance,
- more open to correction,
- more careful in judgment.

### Anti-intellectualism
This says:
- “Thinking deeply is a problem.”
- “Questions are bad.”
- “Study and discernment show lack of faith.”
- “Ignorance is spiritually superior to understanding.”

That is not what Proverbs teaches. In fact, Proverbs repeatedly urges people to **seek wisdom**, **receive instruction**, and **grow in discernment**.

## So what is the verse calling for?

It calls for a posture like this:

- Use your mind fully.
- But do not idolize it.
- Seek wisdom.
- But receive it as dependent creatures, not autonomous judges.
- Make plans.
- But submit them to God.
- Think carefully.
- But trust God more than your own unaided perspective.

## “He shall direct thy paths”

This does not necessarily mean effortless, moment-by-moment private signals. It means God will **make straight**, order, and govern the life of the one who acknowledges him. The promise is for guidance through faithful dependence, not for the elimination of thought.

## In one sentence

Proverbs 3:5–6 teaches **humble reliance on God over against self-sufficient confidence**, not a rejection of reason, study, or understanding.
