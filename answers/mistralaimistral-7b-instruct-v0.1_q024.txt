 In the passage from Ezekiel 36:26-27, God is promising to give the people of Israel a "new heart" and put a "new spirit" within them. This is a metaphorical description of a spiritual transformation that goes beyond mere external compliance with God's laws.

External compliance refers to following God's commands out of fear or obligation, without a genuine change of heart or attitude. It is often seen as superficial and can be easily broken or forgotten.

On the other hand, receiving a "new heart" is a metaphor for a deep spiritual transformation that changes one's inner disposition and motivations. It is a heart that is soft, pliable, and receptive to God's love and guidance. This transformation enables the person to walk in God's statutes and keep His judgments out of a desire to please Him and live according to His will.

The reason why hardness is described as a material condition that must be replaced is because it is seen as an obstacle to true spiritual growth. A hard heart is resistant to God's love and guidance, and it is difficult to receive and respond to Him when one's heart is hardened. By giving a "new heart," God is providing the necessary condition for the person to be receptive to His Spirit and to live a life pleasing to Him.
