The text moving from "cease to do evil" to "learn to do well" suggests that goodness is more than just mere restraint from doing wrong. It implies that true goodness involves actively practicing love and justice towards others. It is about actively seeking to do what is right, seeking justice for the oppressed, caring for the marginalized (fatherless and widows), and overall striving to live a life that reflects goodness and righteousness. Goodness, in this sense, is not just about avoiding evil actions, but actively engaging in actions that promote love and justice.
