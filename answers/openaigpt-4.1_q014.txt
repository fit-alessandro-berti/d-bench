Great question! Let’s explore **Matthew 6:19–21** closely:

### 1. Does this text say treasure *reveals* the heart, or that treasure *trains* the heart?

#### **Reveals the Heart**
- “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”
  - At first glance, this **seems** to say: *what you choose to invest in reveals what you truly love*. In other words, look at what you treasure (your investments, affections, attention), and you’ll see where your heart really is.
  - This is often interpreted as: *treasure reveals the heart.*

#### **Trains the Heart**
- But notice the order of the words: *“where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”*
  - It doesn’t say, “Where your heart is, there your treasure will be.”
  - The text places treasure **before** the heart: indicating that what you invest in, your heart will follow.
  - This suggests: *your heart is trained or shaped by where you place your treasure* (possessions, energy, attention).
  - In this reading, Jesus is telling listeners that our hearts are formed by our choices and investments; where we place treasure, our affection and attention grow.

**Conclusion:**  
The text most directly teaches that *treasure trains the heart*: your heart will follow your investments, not just the other way around. What you actively invest in, spend time and resources on, will shape what you love.

---

### 2. How do possession, attention, and love become tied together here?

- **Possession (Treasure):** “Lay up for yourselves treasures [in heaven or earth]…”
  - This refers to what you accumulate, protect, and value (not just money, but anything you prioritize).

- **Attention:** To “lay up … treasures” means *to focus your efforts*—your intention, your planning, your activity.

- **Love (Heart):** “Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”
  - “Heart” in biblical thought represents your *deepest affections, commitments, and desires*.

**Tie-In:**  
Jesus is explaining a spiritual principle:  
- **What you possess and actively “store up” draws your attention;**
- **Where you focus your attention, your love (heart) will follow;**
- **Where you put your treasure (possessions, efforts), your heart moves in that direction.**

**Therefore:**  
- If you invest in temporary, earthly things, your heart becomes bound up with them—and, ultimately, vulnerable to loss.
- But if you invest in eternal, heavenly things (generosity, faith, God’s kingdom), your heart becomes oriented toward those things.

#### **Summary Table:**

| Treasure (Investment) | → | Attention (Focus) | → | Heart (Love/Commitment) |
|-----------------------|---|-------------------|---|------------------------|
| What you store up     |   | Where you focus   |   | What you come to love  |


---

**In summary:**  
Matthew 6:19–21 teaches that your heart is *trained* by your treasure: what you habitually invest in draws your focus and ultimately shapes what you truly love. Possession, attention, and love become bound together by the direction you give your “treasure.” This is a call to intentionally align what you treasure with heavenly, not earthly, values.
