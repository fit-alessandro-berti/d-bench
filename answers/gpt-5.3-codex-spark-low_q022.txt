• Because “to be searched” means God’s full truth, not just emotional relief.

  In Psalm 139:23–24, the psalmist asks not for protection from truth, but for it.
  A request to be “defended or comforted” can leave the heart untouched; a request to be searched asks for:

  1. Exposure of hidden motives – “know my heart… know my thoughts.”
  2. Moral testing – “see if there be any wicked way in me.”
  3. Correction over comfort – not self-justification, but alignment with truth.
  4. Lifelong direction – “lead me in the way everlasting.”

  So the modeled honesty is vulnerable, concrete, and nonperformative:

  - It admits I am not a fully transparent judge of myself.
  - It prefers truth to self-image.
  - It invites divine scrutiny because love wants transformation, not a better story about me.

  In short: the prayer is not self-condemnation, but a desire to be made whole. It is the opposite of “defend me”; it is
  “reveal me, purify me, and redirect me.”
