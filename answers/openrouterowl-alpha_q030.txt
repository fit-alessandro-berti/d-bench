This passage from 1 Corinthians 13 presents strength not as dominance, but as **self-emptying resilience**—the capacity to absorb hardship, relinquish control, and maintain trust without guarantee.  

The negations dismantle the **self-protective ego** that confuses strength with accumulation (of status, rights, or security).  

Instead, it proposes a strength that chooses vulnerability:  
- **Enduring without retaliation** (“suffereth long”)  
- **Releasing comparisons** (“envieth not”)  
- **Refusing self-inflation** (“vaunteth not itself”)  
- **Absorbing wrongs without storing grievances** (“thinketh no evil”)  
- **Hoping beyond evidence** (“hopeth all things”)  

This vision redefines power as **the freedom to love without conditions**—a strength that holds nothing back, yet is never depleted, because it flows from a source beyond the self.
