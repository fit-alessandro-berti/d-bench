To understand the kind of trust requested in Proverbs 3:5–6, one must distinguish between **moral humility** (the recognition of human limitation) and **anti-intellectualism** (the rejection of the mind).

The text does not ask for the *absence* of thought, but for the *alignment* of the heart. Here is the distinction:

### 1. The Nature of "Total Trust"
The command to trust with "all thine heart" implies an exclusive ultimate reliance. In Hebrew thought, the "heart" (*lebab*) was the seat of the intellect, will, and emotion combined. Therefore, to trust God with the whole heart is to place one’s ultimate security in God’s character rather than in one's own predictive powers or situational control.

### 2. Humble Self-Distrust vs. Anti-intellectualism
The phrase *"lean not unto thine own understanding"* is often misinterpreted as a divine endorsement of ignorance. However, the distinction is vital:

*   **Humble Self-Distrust (The Biblical Intent):**
    This is a "epistemological humility." It recognizes that human perspective is finite, fallen, and subjective. To "lean" on something is to use it as your primary support or "crutch." The proverb warns against making human logic the *final arbiter* of truth. It suggests that when your limited logic contradicts a divine principle (e.g., your logic says "revenge is productive," but God says "forgive"), you defer to the higher wisdom. You use your mind to navigate the world, but you do not worship your mind as an infallible compass.
*   **Anti-intellectualism (The Misinterpretation):**
    Anti-intellectualism suggests that thinking is inherently dangerous or unspiritual. If the passage were anti-intellectual, the rest of the Book of Proverbs—which exhorts the reader to seek wisdom, knowledge, and discernment—would be contradictory. Anti-intellectualism discards the mind; humble trust *recruits* the mind to serve God’s purposes.

### 3. "Acknowledge Him" as an Intellectual Act
The command to *"acknowledge him in all thy ways"* uses the Hebrew root *yada*, which refers to an experiential, intimate knowledge. 
*   **Anti-intellectualism** would say: "Don't think, just feel."
*   **Proverbs** says: "In every decision-making process, consciously factor God’s reality into your calculations."

To "acknowledge" God is to intellectually recognize His presence and sovereignty in mundane affairs (business, relationships, health). It is a disciplined mental habit, not a lobotomy.

### 4. The Result: Directed Paths
The promise that He shall "direct thy paths" suggests a partnership. God does not move a stationary object; He guides a person who is already "walking." 

**Summary:**
The trust required is **integrative, not substitutive.** It does not substitute God for your brain; it integrates God as the foundation upon which your brain operates. You continue to "understand," but you no longer "lean" on that understanding as your ultimate source of truth. You use your intellect to serve God, while remaining humble enough to realize your intellect is not God.
