Toanswer your first question directly: the text primarily teaches that **treasure trains the heart**, though it assumes a reciprocal relationship where treasure also reveals the heart. 

However, the rhetorical thrust of Jesus’s argument is formative, not merely diagnostic. Understanding how possession, attention, and love tie together unlocks the profound psychological and spiritual mechanics of this passage.

### 1. Reveal vs. Train: Which is it?
Most people read the final verse—*“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also”*—as a statement of revelation. They interpret it backwards: *If you want to know what someone loves, look at their bank statement.* Your heart loves earthly things, and therefore your treasure goes there. The treasure merely reveals the pre-existing condition of the heart.

While this is practically true as a diagnostic tool, it misses the force of Jesus’s command. Jesus is not just making an observation; He is giving a command: *"Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven."* 

The word "For" (Greek: *gar*) connecting verses 20 and 21 gives the reason for the command. Jesus is saying: *“Lay up treasures in heaven... because your heart follows your treasure.”* 

If the heart merely dictated where the treasure went, Jesus’s command would make no sense. You cannot directly command the heart to love heavenly things. But you *can* command the hands to give, the feet to go, and the wallet to open. Jesus is teaching that **the way to train your heart to love heaven is to invest your treasure there.** 

This creates a reciprocal loop:
*   **The Reveal:** Your current spending reveals your current loves.
*   **The Train:** Your future spending dictates your future loves. What you do with your possessions pulls your heart in their direction.

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Tie Together
The genius of this passage is how Jesus connects the tangible (possessions) to the intangible (love) through the bridge of attention. Here is how the three become inextricably tied:

**Possession (Treasure)**
A "treasure" is anything we value highly and seek to secure. Jesus points out the fundamental flaw with earthly treasures: they are inherently vulnerable. Moths eat them (decay over time), rust corrupts them (decay from within), and thieves steal them (decay by force). The moment you possess an earthly treasure, it demands something from you.

**Attention (Heart/Mind)**
In biblical anthropology, the "heart" is not just the seat of emotions; it is the seat of the will, the intellect, and the attention. It is what you think about, worry about, and scheme about. 
This is where the tie between possession and attention occurs: **You cannot possess an earthly treasure without giving it your attention.** If you lay up treasure on earth, a portion of your mental and emotional bandwidth is permanently occupied with protecting it. You must watch for the moths, guard against the rust, and lock out the thieves. Your possession now owns your attention. (This is why Jesus immediately follows this passage with the teaching on anxiety in verses 25-34).

**Love (Allegiance/Affection)**
Attention is the soil in which love grows. Whatever captures your attention eventually captures your affection. As you pour your mental energy into securing, maintaining, and enjoying your earthly possessions, your heart becomes tethered to them. You begin to love them. You begin to define your security, identity, and joy by them. 

### The Feedback Loop of the Soul
Jesus is describing a spiritual feedback loop:
1.  You acquire a **possession**.
2.  The vulnerability of that possession demands your **attention** to protect it.
3.  The sustained attention cultivates **love** and affection for the possession.
4.  That love drives you to acquire *more* of the possession, demanding even *more* attention.

### Jesus's Solution: Breaking the Loop
Jesus doesn't just point out the problem; He provides the solution. The command to "lay up treasures in heaven" is an invitation to break the anxiety-producing loop of earthly ownership. 

Heavenly treasures are immune to moth, rust, and thieves. Therefore, when you invest your time, money, and resources into the Kingdom of God (possessions), they require zero defensive anxiety (attention). Because your attention is no longer fixated on protecting a decaying asset, your heart is freed to rest securely in God (love). 

By redirecting your treasure, you redirect your attention, which ultimately reorders your loves.
