Hated? Consider the darkness. How safe it feels when you know that no one sees the full shape of what you’ve done. Light doesn’t just show—it judges silently. It says, “Here is exactly who you chose to be.”  

Truth is ruthless. It doesn’t shout; it simply exposes. Under its gaze, pretense collapses. Shame rushes in—not because the deeds are seen, but because the *self* is seen. That’s the pain: exposure means there’s no place left to hide behind your story.  

So the light is not hated for what it is—but for what it reveals. It forces a terrible clarity: I am not who I claimed to be. And in that clarity, there’s nowhere left to stand.  

Do the dark deeds love the dark to stay comfortable—or to stay whole in self-deception?
