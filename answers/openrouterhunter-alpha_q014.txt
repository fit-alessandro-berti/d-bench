This is a profound and layered question. Let’s break it down carefully.

### Does the text say treasure reveals the heart or trains the heart?

The primary thrust of Matthew 6:21 — “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” — is about **revelation**, not training.  
It presents treasure as a diagnostic: your heart’s true orientation is exposed by what you value and invest in.  
The logic is:

1. You *lay up* treasure (an act of investment/possession).  
2. Therefore, your heart *follows* (emotional and spiritual allegiance).

But there’s a secondary, implicit layer: **training**.  
The passage is part of Jesus’ teaching on spiritual focus. By instructing listeners to “lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven,” he’s prescribing a practice — redirecting investment from temporal to eternal things.  
So, while verse 21 **reveals** the heart’s current state, the surrounding verses (19–20) **train** the heart toward a new allegiance.

In short:  
- **Treasure reveals the heart** (diagnostic truth).  
- **Choosing where to lay up treasure trains the heart** (prescriptive practice).

### How are possession, attention, and love tied together?

The passage suggests a causal chain:

1. **Possession (Treasure)**  
   What you consider “treasure” — what you protect, store, and invest in — becomes a focal point.

2. **Attention (Heart)**  
   The “heart” in biblical terms often includes mind, will, and affection — the seat of your attention and devotion.  
   Your treasure naturally draws your thoughts, plans, and emotional energy.

3. **Love (Ultimate Allegiance)**  
   The ultimate output of sustained attention and investment is love or worship.  
   What you treasure you will ultimately serve (cf. Matthew 6:24: “You cannot serve God and money”).

Jesus is pointing out a spiritual law:  
**Your inner life aligns with your external investments.**  
If you invest in fleeting things, your affections become unstable and corroded.  
If you invest in eternal realities, your heart becomes anchored in what is unshakable.

### In modern terms:

Think of “treasure” as your **allocated resources** — time, money, energy, focus.  
Think of “heart” as your **emotional and spiritual center of gravity**.  
The principle is: **You become what you repeatedly give yourself to.**

So the text is both a warning and an invitation:  
Be aware of what your investments reveal about you, and choose to invest in what forms your heart toward what lasts.
