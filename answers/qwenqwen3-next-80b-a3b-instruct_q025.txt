The problem in **Matthew 6:1** is **not visibility itself**, but **the craving to be seen** — the *motivation* behind the act.

### Key Distinction:

- **Visibility**: Simply being seen while doing good is not condemned. In fact, Jesus later says in Matthew 5:16, *“Let your light shine before others, that they may see your good deeds and glorify your Father in heaven.”* Here, good deeds are *intended* to point others to God — a different motive entirely.

- **Craving to be seen**: This verse condemns doing “alms” (acts of mercy or charity) *“to be seen of them”* — that is, with the primary intention of gaining human approval, praise, or admiration. The Greek phrase *“tou theatheēnai autois”* (“to be seen by them”) emphasizes *deliberate performance for an audience*.

### Why is this morally dangerous?

1. **Distorts the purpose of goodness**: Genuine almsgiving is an act of love and obedience to God — rooted in compassion and justice. When it becomes a performance, it transforms worship into self-worship.

2. **Replaces divine reward with human praise**: The verse warns, *“otherwise ye have no reward of your Father which is in heaven.”* If your goal is human applause, then you’ve already received your full reward — in the fleeting, shallow approval of people. God does not reward self-serving piety.

3. **Corrupts the heart**: Jesus consistently teaches that God looks at the heart (1 Samuel 16:7). Acting for show reveals inward idolatry — placing human opinion above divine approval.

### Jesus’ broader context deepens this:

- Verses 2–4: Giving to the needy — *“your left hand must not know what your right hand is doing”* — implies radical, unobserved generosity.
- Verses 5–6: Prayer — not condemned for being in public, but for using prayer as a spectacle.
- Verses 16–18: Fasting — again, not forbidden in public, but forbidden if done to gain pity or admiration.

### Conclusion:

**The danger is not being seen, but being seen *for the sake of being seen*.**  
The sin is not external visibility, but internal idolatry — seeking glory from people instead of from God. True righteousness, according to Jesus, is done in secret *so that the Father, who sees in secret, may reward you* (Matthew 6:4, 6, 18).

> **Motive matters more than method.**

The call is not to hide good deeds out of fear of visibility, but to do them out of pure love for God — whether anyone sees them or not.
