This passage from Amos is one of the most powerful indictments of ritual religion **divorced from justice and righteousness** in the Hebrew Bible.

### 1. **Why Does Amos Reject Worship So Sharply?**

Amos doesn’t call for a better *balance* between worship and ethics. Instead, he **rejects ritual worship entirely** when it is not accompanied by justice and righteousness. This is because:

- **Worship Without Justice Is Hypocrisy:** To perform sacrifices, celebrate festivals, and sing songs to God while neglecting the core demands of justice is a **sham**. God is not interested in mere outward displays of religion if the hearts and actions of the people are corrupt.
- **Complicity in Injustice:** In the context of Israel at the time, the nation was rife with **social injustice**—the wealthy oppressed the poor, the legal system was rigged, and exploitation was rampant. Their worship became an attempt to “buy God off” or mask societal sins, treating ritual as a substitute for true moral living.
- **Radical Prophetic Vision:** Amos insists that **proper worship is impossible without ethical living**. Rituals cannot be pleasing to God unless they express, and flow from, a community that pursues justice.

### 2. **What Kind of Moral or Spiritual Fraud Is Exposed?**

- **Religious Formalism:** The people maintain the forms and ceremonies of religion but violate its heart and intention. They try to satisfy God through rituals while ignoring what God truly wants—**justice, compassion, and righteousness.**
- **Double Life:** There’s a pretense that one can oppress others six days a week and make up for it by dutifully attending religious feasts and ceremonies. Amos says this is spiritual fraud.
- **Disconnect From God’s Character:** True worship reflects God’s own nature. For Amos, **God is a God of justice**; thus, any worship that tolerates exploitation and oppression is a lie about who God is.
- **Misplaced Confidence:** Israel’s confidence in their “chosenness” and their access to religious cultic practices leads them to believe they’re immune to judgment, but Amos shatters this illusion.

### 3. **Key Message**

> “But let judgment roll on like waters, and righteousness like a mighty stream.”

This call means that **God desires a community shaped by justice**, not just a congregation reciting prayers and offering sacrifices. **Real worship transforms society**; rituals without justice dishonor God rather than please him.

### **In Summary:**

Amos exposes the **moral and spiritual fraud** of trying to substitute ritual worship for ethical living. He rejects worship that masks injustice rather than enacts righteousness, insisting that **God’s true desire is not feasts or songs, but a society where justice and righteousness flow like never-ending streams**. The sharp rejection is a wake-up call: without justice, worship itself becomes an abomination.
