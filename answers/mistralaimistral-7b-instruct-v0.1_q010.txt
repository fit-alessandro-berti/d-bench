 The power being praised in this passage from Philippians is the power of humility and servitude, as demonstrated by Jesus. This kind of power is different from the typical notion of power as domination or control. Instead, it is a power that comes from serving others and putting their needs before one's own.

The reason why humility joined to greatness can feel more unsettling than open domination is that it challenges our expectations of what power looks like. It shows that true power comes not from dominating others, but from serving them and putting their needs above our own.

This kind of power can be unsettling because it requires us to let go of our desire for control and to put others first. It challenges us to be selfless and to put the needs of others before our own, even if it means sacrificing our own interests. It also requires us to recognize the inherent worth and dignity of all people, regardless of their position or status.

In a world that values power as domination and control, the power of humility and servitude can be a radical and transformative force. It invites us to reconsider our understanding of power and to strive for a more just and equitable society, where everyone is valued and treated with dignity and respect.
