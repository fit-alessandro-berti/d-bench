### Understanding the Connection Between Pride and Destruction

The proverb in Proverbs 16:18–19 suggests that pride is not just a moral failing, but also a cognitive and relational one, leading to a downfall. To understand why pride is linked to blindness and collapse, let's break down the key aspects of pride and its consequences.

### The Link Between Pride and Blindness/Collapse

Pride is associated with a lack of self-awareness, overconfidence, and a diminished capacity for empathy. When individuals become prideful, they tend to:

1. **Overestimate their abilities**: Pride can lead to an inflated sense of self-importance, causing individuals to take unnecessary risks and overlook potential pitfalls.
2. **Become isolated**: Pride can make individuals less receptive to feedback, advice, or warnings from others, leading to poor decision-making and a lack of accountability.
3. **Misjudge their circumstances**: Pride can distort one's perception of reality, leading to a failure to recognize potential dangers or weaknesses.

These factors contribute to the connection between pride and blindness/collapse, as pride can lead to a downfall by obscuring one's judgment and impairing relationships with others.

### The Contrast with "The Lowly"

The contrast between being "of an humble spirit with the lowly" and "divid[ing] the spoil with the proud" highlights the moral psychology of arrogance. The "lowly" are characterized by:

1. **Humility**: A willingness to learn, listen, and acknowledge one's limitations.
2. **Interdependence**: A recognition of one's place within a community and a willingness to collaborate and rely on others.
3. **Realism**: A more accurate assessment of one's abilities and circumstances.

In contrast, the "proud" are associated with:

1. **Self-sufficiency**: A tendency to rely solely on oneself, rather than acknowledging the value of others.
2. **Competition**: A focus on personal gain, often at the expense of others.

The proverb suggests that humility and a willingness to associate with the "lowly" are more valuable than the fleeting benefits of pride and arrogance. By being humble and recognizing one's limitations, individuals can avoid the pitfalls of pride and cultivate more positive relationships with others.

### Implications for Moral Psychology

The proverb implies that pride is not just a moral failing, but also a cognitive and relational one. The contrast with "the lowly" suggests that arrogance is associated with a distorted view of oneself and one's place in the world. By understanding the connection between pride and blindness/collapse, and the characteristics associated with humility and pride, we can better appreciate the importance of cultivating humility in our own lives.
