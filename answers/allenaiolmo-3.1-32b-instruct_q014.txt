Great question. Let’s look closely at the passage from Matthew 6:19–21 and what it says about treasure, the heart, and how they relate.

### 1. What does the passage say about treasure and the heart?

The key verse is the last one:  
> “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”

This is a direct statement: **the location of your treasure reveals the location of your heart.** It does not say that having treasure *trains* or *changes* your heart, but rather that your heart naturally follows your treasure. Possession (where your treasure is) determines the direction of your heart.

So, the passage says **treasure reveals the heart**, not that treasure trains or shapes the heart.

---

### 2. How are possession, attention, and love tied together here?

Let’s break down the three concepts in light of the passage:

#### a. Possession (Treasure)
- The text contrasts earthly treasures (money, possessions) with heavenly treasures (spiritual values, God, righteousness).
- What you choose to accumulate—what you "possess"—is where your focus is.

#### b. Attention
- In Jesus’ teachings, where your treasure is, is also where your attention (your heart) goes.
- The heart in biblical language often refers to the core of a person—their deepest affections, intentions, and focus.
- So, if your treasure is on earth (wealth, status), your heart is on earth—your attention is on those things.
- If your treasure is in heaven (spiritual things), your heart is there—your attention is on God and eternal things.

#### c. Love
- In Christian thought, the heart is also the seat of love.
- If your treasure is in God or in things of eternal value, your love is directed toward those.
- If your treasure is in things of the world, your love (affection, devotion) is toward those.

So, **possession (treasure) indicates where your heart (attention and love) is directed.** The three are intertwined: what you possess (treasure) shapes where your heart dwells, and thus where your love is placed.

---

### 3. Summary

- The passage says that **treasure reveals the heart**, not that treasure trains the heart.
- **Possession** (treasure) shows where your deepest focus is.
- Your **attention** follows your treasure.
- Your **love** is directed toward what you treasure.
- Therefore, in this teaching, what you value most reveals the true state of your heart and the object of your love.

---

**In short:**  
Treasure reveals the heart by showing where your attention and love are ultimately placed. Possession, attention, and love are tied together because the things you choose to treasure become the focus of your heart and the object of your love.
