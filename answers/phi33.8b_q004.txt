The proverb encourages reliance on divine wisdom instead of personal understanding, which could be deemed as humble self-distrust—it asks for a place of trust and submission to the divine. This kind of trust is not merely about acknowledging one's inherent limitations but also about a faith-based trust in God'self-reliance on God for direction and guidance. It encourages the believer to put faith in God’s providence and His plans for one’s life. This doesn't devalue intellect but instead advocates for interweaving spiritual reliance with intellect, not shunning intellect but recognizing that ultimate wisdom and direction come from God.
