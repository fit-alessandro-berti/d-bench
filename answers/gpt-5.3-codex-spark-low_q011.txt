• In this verse Isaiah shifts from stopping harm to practicing repair.

  - “Cease to do evil” is a negative command: stop the behaviors that damage God’s relationship and other people.
  - “Learn to do well” is a positive command: stop treating goodness as accidental; it must be learned and practiced.

  So the text treats goodness as more than restraint. “Learn” (as a verb of training/disciple-making) implies skill,
  repetition, and formation of character. Isaiah is saying:

  1. First, remove the evil.
  2. Then, actively do what is good—especially in justice-sensitive ways:
      - seek justice,
      - defend the oppressed,
      - speak up for the fatherless and the widow.

  That’s not merely private morality. It is a social ethic: repentance is personal, but righteousness is communal and
  justice-oriented.

  In short: the passage presents goodness as active love and justice, not just restraint.
