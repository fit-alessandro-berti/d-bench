This is a profound and important question—the distinction between *not judging* (as in Luke 6:37) and the idea that *all moral judgments are meaningless* or should be suspended.

### 1. What Jesus Says: *"Judge not..."*
In context, Jesus is warning against a spirit of harshness, self-righteousness, and condemnation.  
He’s speaking to those tempted to pronounce others irredeemable or to hold themselves up as morally superior.

**Key points in the verse:**
- **“Judge not, and ye shall not be judged”** — warns against taking up the role of final arbiter over others.
- **“Condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned”** — a caution against writing people off, excluding them, or wishing for their downfall.
- **“Forgive, and ye shall be forgiven”** — underscores mercy over vengeance or retribution.

### 2. This is *Not* a Denial of Moral Reality  
Jesus’ words here do NOT mean that:
- All actions are morally equivalent  
- There is no right or wrong  
- "Discernment" (the ability to distinguish good from evil) is pointless

Many times in the Gospels, Jesus makes clear moral judgments and tells His followers to “know them by their fruits” (Matthew 7:16). He warns about specific sins and instructs His followers in righteousness.

#### Jesus distinguishes between:
- **Just judgment (righteous discernment)**  
  “Judge not according to the appearance, but judge righteous judgment.” (John 7:24)
- **Self-righteous, condemning judgment**  
  Where we elevate ourselves and delight in shaming or excluding others.

### 3. Discernment vs. the Appetite to Condemn

#### **Discernment**  
- *Motivation*: Love, humility, desire for truth  
- *Action*: Identifies right and wrong, but does so out of care; seeks restoration, not ruin.  
- *Result*: Helps oneself and others move towards what is good and true, while admitting one’s own faults.

#### **Appetite to Condemn**  
- *Motivation*: Pride, insecurity, superiority, control  
- *Action*: Judges harshly, publicly, quickly, lacking compassion or understanding; writes off the person, not just the act.  
- *Result*: Alienation, hypocrisy, broken relationships, hardening of heart.

### 4. Why Judgment Matters
Jesus’ teaching is meant to:
- Check our tendency to hypocrisy  
- Prevent us from making ourselves the standard  
- Open us to compassion and mercy, recognizing our own need for grace

But **He also calls for honest evaluation of actions, both in ourselves and others**, so that we may guide, protect, and help.

### **Summary Table**

|          | Discernment                    | Appetite To Condemn              |
|----------|-------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| Purpose  | Love, correction, protection  | Superiority, exclusion           |
| Result   | Restoration, help             | Shame, alienation                |
| Attitude | Humility, self-reflection     | Pride, self-righteousness        |

---

### **Bottom Line**
**"Judge not..."** does *not* mean “Never make moral distinctions.”  
It means, “Do not usurp God’s place as Judge. Be slow to pronounce sentences over others, quick to forgive, and eager to restore. Let discernment be guided by humility and love, not by pride and condemnation.”
