The text in Matthew 6:19–21 functions on a principle that is both declarative and prescriptive. It suggests an inseparable link between **investment**, **attention**, and **love**.

Here is an analysis of how the passage addresses your specific questions regarding revelation versus training, and how those three elements intersect.

### 1. Reveals or Trains?
The text contains both aspects, but it is primarily designed to **train the heart through action.**

While Jesus describes our natural tendency (our hearts reveal our true priorities in what we value), He is giving us an imperative command: *"Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven."*

*   **The Revealing Aspect:** Verse 21 (*"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"*) serves as a diagnostic truth. It acts as a mirror to our current state. If you reveal that you are terrified of losing worldly possessions, your "treasure" was on earth, and therefore your "heart" was truly focused on survival rather than eternity.
*   **The Training Aspect:** However, Jesus is not merely telling you what you already look like in the mirror; He is telling you how to change it. The command is about *where* you lay up treasure (priority). In the ancient world, "laying up" (from Greek *apoteleuomai*) was more than money management; it was a decision of **resource allocation**. By choosing where your resources go, you force your heart to focus on that place.

Therefore, Jesus is not asking for an introspective test of what you already love; He is issuing a mandate that creates love by creating investment. The treasure *becomes* the mechanism by which the heart is trained.

### 2. The Relationship Between Possession, Attention, and Love
These three concepts are tied together in this passage because they all stem from our **definition of "valuable."** In human psychology and spiritual theology, what we protect (Possession) shows where we direct our eyes (Attention), which is the definition of what we love.

#### **Possession as Priority**
To say you have a treasure is to say, "This is what I need for my survival." To command someone to put their treasure in Heaven is to command them to trust that Heaven will provide for them. Possession (in the Greek sense of resources or value stored up) is the tangible object of our heart’s attachment.
*   *Earthly Treasure:* If you guard your wealth, you are "possessing" its fragility, decay, and theft. This makes your possession a source of anxiety.
*   *Heavenly Treasure:* To "lay up" something eternal is to possess God's promises without the fear of them being stolen or decaying.

#### **Attention Follows Possession**
Wherever we focus our attention, where our eyes and minds are directed, becomes our heart.
*   We cannot care for what is not known or valued.
*   If your resources (possession) go into a stock market, you cannot avoid watching its news; your **attention** is captured by the volatility of money.
*   Therefore, Jesus argues that you cannot have a peaceful heart if your attention is trained to watch for thieves breaking down doors.

#### **Love Is the Consequence of Focus**
"Where your treasure is..." means "What your heart is attached to."
If you love God (by giving away resources), your attention will naturally flow toward His work, and your possession will become a reflection of that love rather than an end unto itself. If you love yourself or the world enough to store up wealth for security, then necessarily you have placed your **love** in things you fear losing.

### 3. Putting It Together
The text essentially states: **"Your values determine where your heart lives."**

Jesus is teaching that a person cannot hold two distinct priorities at once without internal conflict ("Where your treasure is, there will your heart be *also*"). To train the heart to love God (treasure in heaven), you must first re-orient what you are willing to value, protect, and spend time on.

**The Conclusion:**
This passage does not say that we read our hearts by looking at what we have. It says that **by placing a treasure in a new place, we train our heart to live in the same place.** The instruction is active: Go store up in Heaven (give value to eternity), and your heart will follow you there because it will no longer find security, anxiety, or comfort in anything else.
