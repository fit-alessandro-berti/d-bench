Short answer: Jesus condemns the Pharisee’s inward posture — prideful self‑righteousness and self‑justification — not the forms of piety themselves. The Pharisee’s prayer shows him trusting in his own virtues, measuring himself against others, and despising the tax‑collector; his fasting and tithing become material for boastful display. Those attitudes (pride, contempt, comparison, using virtue to exalt oneself) are what condemn him.

Why the tax‑collector is “truer”: his prayer is humble, honest, and dependent — “God, be merciful to me a sinner.” He recognizes his sin, does not defend or compare himself, and appeals to God’s mercy. Jesus’ point is about the heart: God justifies the penitent who relies on God, not the one who trusts in his own righteousness. (See also James 4:6; Luke 18:9–14.)
