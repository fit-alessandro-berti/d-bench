The passage from Genesis 4:6–7 presents a picture of moral agency where individuals are given the choice to do well or not. Evil is presented as a temptation that lies at the door, waiting to be acted upon. The text places the responsibility on the person to make the right choice and not give in to sin. It emphasizes the importance of personal agency and the consequences of one's actions. Ultimately, the individual is held accountable for their choices and actions.
