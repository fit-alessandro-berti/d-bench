In Matthew 6:1, the danger is **not visibility itself**, but **the motive behind public goodness**.

### What Jesus is warning against
The verse says:

> “Take heed that ye do not your alms before men, to be seen of them...”

The key phrase is **“to be seen of them.”**  
That points to **performative righteousness**: doing good in order to gain human approval, admiration, status, or reputation.

### Why that is morally dangerous
Public goodness becomes dangerous when it turns charity into a kind of spiritual theater. In that case:

- the act is no longer mainly for God or neighbor,
- the giver seeks reward from people instead of from God,
- and the act can become self-serving rather than loving.

Jesus is not condemning the act of giving alms in public, since some charitable acts are necessarily visible. He is condemning the **intentional display** of goodness as a way of being honored.

### So is visibility itself bad?
No. In fact, elsewhere Jesus says believers should let their light shine so others may see their good works and glorify God (Matthew 5:16). That shows visibility can be good **when it leads others to praise God**, not the giver.

### The real issue
So the problem is:

- **not** that others can see the deed,
- but that **the giver wants to be seen**.

In short: **public goodness is morally dangerous when the desire for human applause replaces the desire to honor God.**
