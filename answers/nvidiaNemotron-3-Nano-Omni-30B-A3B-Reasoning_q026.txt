The passage makes clear that speech is **both a symptom and a choice that reveals what is already stored in the heart**:

1. **Symptom of the heart’s stockpile** – “out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh.” The words that come out are the outward sign of what the heart contains. If the heart is filled with good, the speech will be good; if it is filled with evil, the speech will be evil. In this sense, words are a *revelation* of the inner moral condition.

2. **Choice that reflects the heart’s stockpile** – The phrase “how can ye, being evil, speak good things?” implies that the speaker has the capacity to choose what to say. The ability to utter good or evil words depends on the moral resources (or lack thereof) that the heart supplies. Thus, speech is also an *active decision* that either aligns with or contradicts the heart’s contents.

3. **Revelation of the heart’s stockpile** – By hearing what someone says, an observer can discern whether the heart is “good” or “evil.” The passage treats the spoken words as evidence of the hidden treasure that resides within.

Therefore, the passage suggests that words are **all three**: they are symptoms of the heart’s condition, choices that the individual makes, and revelations of the moral stockpile that already exists in the heart.
