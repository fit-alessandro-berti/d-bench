In Mark 8:34–35, the "self" being denied is the individual ego, desires, and selfish ambitions. This passage emphasizes the importance of prioritizing Jesus' teachings and the values of the Kingdom of God over personal interests and societal norms. The call to "deny oneself" involves a radical reorientation of one's life, embracing humility, selflessness, and a willingness to embrace hardship and even suffering for the sake of following Jesus.

### Why it sounds liberating to some:
1. **Freedom from Ego**: Denying the self can be seen as liberation from the shackles of ego and pride. It allows individuals to pursue deeper, more meaningful relationships and a sense of purpose beyond material or superficial success.
   
2. **Alignment with Higher Values**: Followers may find freedom in aligning their lives with what they perceive as divine or ultimate truth, offering a sense of peace and fulfillment that transcends worldly concerns.

3. **Community and Connection**: Embracing this teaching can lead to stronger community bonds and a sense of belonging, as individuals prioritize others and work towards common spiritual goals.

4. **Spiritual Growth**: Many find personal growth and transformation in adopting such a mindset, as it encourages virtues like love, compassion, and selflessness.

### Why it sounds offensive to others:
1. **Loss of Autonomy**: For some, the idea of denying oneself can sound like a loss of personal freedom and autonomy. It may be perceived as demanding unconditional submission to a set of beliefs, which can be uncomfortable or unacceptable.

2. **Negative Connotations of Suffering**: The reference to taking up one's cross and potentially losing one's life can be seen as a call to endure suffering, which might not resonate with those who are seeking a faith that promises peace and joy.

3. **Conflict with Modern Values**: In contemporary societies that often emphasize individualism and personal success, the call to deny oneself can clash with mainstream cultural values, making it seem outdated or irrelevant.

4. **Misinterpretations**: Misunderstandings of this passage can lead to interpretations that justify self-harm or extreme asceticism, which would be deeply concerning and objectionable.

Overall, the perception of this teaching depends largely on individual beliefs, cultural contexts, and personal experiences. For those who embrace its spiritual implications, it offers a path to profound transformation and spiritual freedom. For others, it may challenge deeply held values and perceptions of personal freedom and fulfillment.
