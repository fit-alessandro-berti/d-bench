James 3:9–10 exposes a profound **inner spiritual hypocrisy**. It reveals how a single utterance can reflect a fractured state of mind or divided will where moral clarity is absent.

Here is a breakdown of the inner contradiction and why speech reveals disorder so quickly:

### 1. The Inner Contradiction: Split Will and Lack of Moral Clarity
The passage highlights a tension between two commands of love and obedience: **blessing God** and **cursing men**. Specifically, the contradiction lies in using "one voice" or "the same words" to contradict spiritual authority and moral reality simultaneously.

*   **The Object of Blessing vs. Cursing:** James describes a person who uses language designed for blessing ("even the Father") but applies it in cursing men ("men which are made after the similarity of God").
*   **The Conflict:** If men are "made after the likeness of God," they share the same divine image as the Father. Therefore, cursing them while still speaking of the Father's holiness creates a disconnect. It suggests that the person is **unable to distinguish** between their own moral standing and those who mirror it: blessing our Father without recognizing them; and cursing men whom we are commanded to love while using the same language powerfully as our words.
*   **The Core Issue:** The contradiction stems from a lack of "clear conscience." One cannot hold one's hands with both blessings and curses at the same time. It reveals that the speaker's heart is not unified or holy, even if they possess the language (power/authority) to speak it.

### 2. Why Speech Reveals Moral Disorder Quickly
James argues that moral disorder manifests immediately through speech because **words are the external reflection of an internal state.**

*   **The Mirror Effect:** In biblical wisdom literature (e.g., James 5:23; Luke 18:14), it is stated that "in one hour your life will be changed by a few things." When you say something, you expose who you are. If you bless God with one hand and curse a man who looks like Him with the same energy, it exposes that your words do not match your moral nature.
*   **The Immediate Consequences:** When someone speaks evil or uses language to bless or curse (without thinking or repentance), they reveal their lack of discipline or clarity in heart. You don't have the power to say "I am good" and "I am bad." The moment words are spoken, if they contradict each other (as James implies that "blessing" and "curse" can proceed from "the same mouth"), it means you don't know where your morality stands.
*   **Need for Repentance:** James concludes with a call to repent: *"These things ought not so to be [my children]."* Because the contradiction is visible through a single speech act, it requires immediate correction of the heart to restore unity in will and love between God and humanity (even those who bear God's image).

In summary, **James 3:9–10 exposes the hypocrisy of one speaking from divided will.** It shows that when words can curse men "made after the likeness of God" while blessing the Father, the speaker has no clear conscience or moral consistency. The passage teaches that true spiritual maturity requires the silence of division and the clarity to speak only what aligns with holiness.
