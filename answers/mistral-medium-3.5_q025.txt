In **Matthew 6:1**, Jesus warns against performing righteous acts (like almsgiving) *before men, to be seen by them*. The moral danger here is not **visibility itself** but rather the **motivation behind the action**—the craving for human approval rather than seeking God’s reward.

### Key Insights:
1. **The Problem is the Heart’s Intent** – The issue is not that others see the good deed, but that the deed is done *primarily* to gain their praise. Jesus later contrasts this with giving in secret (Matthew 6:3-4), where the reward comes from God, not men.
2. **Hypocrisy vs. Sincerity** – Public goodness becomes morally dangerous when it is **performative**—when the act is more about the *appearance* of virtue than the *reality* of it. This is hypocrisy (cf. Matthew 23:5).
3. **Reward from God vs. Reward from Men** – Jesus implies that seeking human approval forfeits a greater reward from God. The danger is **misplaced treasure**—valuing temporal praise over eternal blessing.

### Conclusion:
The problem is **not visibility** (since good works are meant to be seen in other contexts, e.g., Matthew 5:16) but **the craving to be seen**. The moral danger lies in the **selfish ambition** behind the act, not the act itself being public.
