This passage from Matthew 6:19–21 emphasizes the intimate connection between what we value (our "treasure") and the disposition of our heart — that is, our inner life, affections, and priorities.

### Does the text say treasure reveals the heart, or that treasure trains the heart?

The key phrase is:  
**"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."**

This suggests a close association, but it can be understood both ways:

- **Treasure reveals the heart:** What you consider treasure—what you invest in, protect, and prioritize—discloses what your heart truly cares about. The "treasure" serves as a window into your inner affections.

- **Treasure trains (shapes) the heart:** Conversely, the passage warns about *where* you put your treasure because that focus pulls your heart toward it. That is, what you consistently focus on or invest in will cultivate your desires and affections correspondingly.

Thus, treasure both **reveals** *and* **shapes** the heart. Jesus seems to imply an inseparable link: your treasure is a kind of magnet or indicator of your heart’s orientation.

---

### How do possession, attention, and love become tied together here?

- **Possession (having treasure):**  
  Treasure represents what you *actually own or hold.* Earthly treasure—wealth, possessions—are inherently vulnerable ("moth and rust… thieves"), highlighting their impermanence. Heavenly treasure—spiritual values and eternal investments—is incorruptible.

- **Attention (where your treasure is):**  
  Your treasure commands your attention. You guard it ("thieves break through and steal") and invest energy to maintain it. Often, your daily focus reveals what you treasure most.

- **Love (where your heart is):**  
  The "heart" in biblical terms concerns the seat of love, will, and desire. Your love is drawn toward your treasure, and vice versa. What you love shapes your desires, decisions, and priorities.

**In sum:**  
The passage teaches that what you possess or seek to possess (treasure) naturally captures your attention and channels your love and desire (your heart). Earthly treasure leads to a heart oriented toward transient, corruptible things; heavenly treasure cultivates a heart anchored in eternal values.

---

### Summary:

- **Treasure reflects what your heart values** and **directs where your heart focuses and loves.**  
- Possession/money/possessions invite your attention.  
- Attention cultivates love and desire.  
- Thus treasure and heart are deeply intertwined: your priorities either train or reveal your affections.

Jesus urges his followers to invest in eternal treasures to shape hearts that are heavenly-minded and not overly attached to corruptible, earthly things.
