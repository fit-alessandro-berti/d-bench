This passage from Matthew 12:34-35, by drawing a metaphorical linkage between the heart and a treasure of moral characteristics, intricately connects speech with one's internal moral reservoir, or as termed here, "hidden moral stockpiles." Here's a breakdown of how the passage suggests that words can be symptoms, choices, and revelations:

1. **Words as Symptoms**: The saying, "for out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh," indicates that what one says is a symptom or a natural overflow of the contents of one’s heart (inner being or character). Similar to how symptoms express underlying health conditions, words manifest what is abound within a person's heart. If the heart is filled with goodness, good words and actions flow effortlessly, just as malice begets harmful expressions when the heart habors evil.

2. **Words as Choices**: While the passage underlines a natural overflow, it inherently suggests the presence of choice—a person harbors 'good treasure' or 'evil treasure'. The type of "treasure" one chooses to fill their heart with will determine the nature of their words and actions. Thus, speech is not only a symptom but also a matter of personal choice regarding what one decides to store within oneself morally and spiritually.

3. **Words as Revelations**: Words, in this context, are more than just expressions; they are revelatory. They reveal the nature of the heart of the speaker. This aspect makes speech an important tool for discernment in understanding the character and moral state of individuals. What people choose to say can provide deep insights into who they fundamentally are and what they value.

In essence, this passage from Matthew portrays words as having a multi-dimensional relationship with the moral state of the human heart. Speech acts as a symptom revealing the nature of one's heart; it is a choice indicating what individuals prioritize morally within; and finally, it serves as a revelation, providing a glimpse into one's true character. Imagine speech as pathways emanating from a central hub, the heart, each utterance tracing back to the moral and ethical contents stored within.
