Matthew 6:19–21 encompasses a profound teaching by Jesus about the orientation of one's life values and priorities. The text specifically emphasizes the impermanence and vulnerability of material wealth ("treasures upon earth") in contrast to the eternal security of spiritual wealth ("treasures in heaven").

1. **Treasure and the Heart:** The expression "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" indicates that one's heart (in biblical terms often representing the center of one's emotions, desires, and will) follows what one values most. This suggests that treasure, in this context, reveals where the heart is already aligned. In other words, what one chooses to treasure indicates where one's affections and commitments lie. This aligns with the concept that humans naturally invest emotional and mental energy into their most valued possessions or pursuits, whether they are material or spiritual.

2. **The Manner of Investment:** The text also suggests a directive to proactively direct one’s heart by choosing where to "lay up" treasures. By investing in "treasures in heaven," one's heart is steered towards divine, eternal values rather than mere earthly, transient ones. This implies that treasure not only reveals but can also train or condition the heart, as deliberate choices about where to store one’s treasures can help cultivate spiritual affinities and commitment.

3. **Link Between Possession, Attention, and Love:** The passage intricately links possession (what one owns or values), attention (where one’s focus and efforts are directed), and love (a deep, enduring affection and priority). Essentially, it suggests that what one chooses to possess and give attention to inevitably shapes what one truly loves. The allocation of resources—time, energy, finances—illuminates and influences the object of one's affection. Therefore, if someone invests in heavenly treasures (qualities and actions valued in the spiritual realm like love, kindness, justice, etc.), their attention and love align more fully with these virtues.

In summation, Matthew 6:19–21 instructs individuals not only on the transitory nature of earthly treasures but more profoundly on the spiritual discipline of alignment and investment. By choosing where to store treasures, one actively molds the heart's desires and projects, shaping a life oriented towards transcendence and eternal significance.
