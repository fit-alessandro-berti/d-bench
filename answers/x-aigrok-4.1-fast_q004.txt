### The Kind of Trust Enjoined

The trust commanded in Proverbs 3:5–6 is **wholehearted, Godward reliance**—a total commitment of one's innermost being ("with all thine heart") to God's character, wisdom, and promises, rather than self-sufficient autonomy. It involves:
- **Prioritizing divine direction**: "In all thy ways acknowledge him" means actively submitting every decision and path to God's will, expecting Him to "direct thy paths" (i.e., make them straight and fruitful).
- **Reorienting confidence**: Human understanding is not discarded but demoted; we don't "lean" on it as our primary support, recognizing its limits.

This is ultimately **fear-of-the-Lord trust** (cf. Prov 1:7; 9:10), rooted in God's proven faithfulness (e.g., Exod 34:6; Deut 7:9), not blind faith but informed submission.

### Humble Self-Distrust vs. Mere Anti-Intellectualism

| Aspect                  | Humble Self-Distrust (Biblical)                          | Anti-Intellectualism (Unbiblical Extreme)               |
|-------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|
| **Core Attitude**      | Recognizes human finitude, sin's noetic effects (Rom 1:21; 3:23), and God's infinite superiority (Isa 55:8–9: "My thoughts are not your thoughts"). Reason is a gift but fallible and secondary. | Rejects reason, learning, or evidence outright as "worldly" or unnecessary; equates piety with ignorance. |
| **View of Intellect**  | Values understanding highly (Prov 4:5–7 urges "get wisdom"; 2:6 says God gives it) but subordinates it to God (Jas 1:5: pray for wisdom; 3:17: wisdom from above is "pure"). Anti-autonomy, not anti-mind. | Dismisses study, science, or logic as inherently suspect; "God hates brains." |
| **Practical Outworking**| Seeks wisdom diligently (Prov 2:1–6) while yielding to Scripture, prayer, and providence. E.g., Paul reasons rigorously (Acts 17:2–3; 1 Cor 1:20–25 critiques foolish self-reliance, not wisdom itself). | Avoids education, debate, or complex theology; "just feel it in your heart." |
| **Biblical Examples**  | Solomon seeks God's wisdom (1 Kgs 3:9); Jesus uses logic (Matt 22:15–46); Proverbs lauds the wise mind (Prov 16:22). | None; Scripture condemns folly (Prov 1:22; 12:15) and commands loving God with the *mind* (Matt 22:37; Rom 12:2). |

**Key Distinction**: Humble self-distrust is **theocentric humility**—elevating God above self without denigrating His gift of reason. It's Proverbs' own ethos: wisdom starts with God (Prov 1:7), not stupidity. Anti-intellectualism inverts this, idolizing emotion or simplicity while scorning the mind God calls us to renew. The proverb targets prideful self-trust (Prov 3:7; 28:26), promising blessing to the humbly dependent.
