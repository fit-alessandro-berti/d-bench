Excellent question. This passage is a pivotal text on the ethics of speech and its relationship to inner moral character. Let's break down its logic and address your categories.

### The Core Metaphor: The Heart as a Treasury
The passage uses the metaphor of a **"treasure" or "stockpile"** (Greek: *thesauros*) in the heart. This is not a passive storage unit but an active source that "brings forth" (*ekporeuetai*) or "overflows." The heart (*kardia*) in biblical anthropology is the **seat of the entire inner life**—including will, mind, emotions, and moral character. What you consistently *treasure* (value, store up, practice) becomes the defining content of your inner self.

### Connection to Speech
Jesus makes a direct causal link: **the mouth speaks from the overflow of the heart.** Speech is not an isolated act; it is the **external expression of the inner person's deepest commitments and accumulated habits.** Just as a tree's fruit reveals its genetic nature and health, our words reveal our "moral stockpile."

---

### Analysis of Your Categories: Symptoms, Choices, Revelations?

The passage powerfully suggests **all three, but with a specific priority:**

1.  **Symptoms (Unavoidable Manifestation):**
    *   This is the primary emphasis. The "how can ye... speak good things?" is rhetorical, implying it's **impossible** for an evil heart to consistently produce good speech. A corrupt "treasure" must, by its nature, yield corrupt "currency." In this sense, speech is a **symptom** of the underlying spiritual condition, much like a fever is a symptom of infection. You cannot fake a healthy fruit tree forever; eventually, its true nature is shown.

2.  **Revelations (Disclosure of the Hidden Self):**
    *   The act of speaking is a **revelation** that makes the invisible visible. Jesus is exposing the Pharisees' true condition. Their harsh, hypocritical, theologically-antisupernatural accusations (calling Christ's work demonic) reveal that their "treasury" is full of bitterness, prejudice, and spiritual pride, not goodness and truth. Their words are a **self-revealing act** that contravenes their public image of piety.

3.  **Choices (Volitional Outworking):**
    *   While the metaphor stresses cause-and-effect ("abundance of the heart... mouth speaketh"), it also uses active, volitional verbs: **"A *good man*... *bringeth forth* good things... an *evil man*... *bringeth forth* evil things."**
    *   This implies that the state of the heart is itself the result of **prior choices** about what to treasure. The "good man" has chosen to store up goodness (through discipleship, repentance, and practice), and now that goodness flows out naturally. The "evil man" has stored up evil, and it flows out. The speech is therefore a **symptom** of a **chosen** condition, and its choice reveals the state.

### Synthesis: A Seamlessweb
The genius of the passage is that these categories are not mutually exclusive but describe a **process**:

1.  **Choice & Formation:** You choose what to love, value, meditate on, and practice (**treasuring**). This **forms** your heart.
2.  **Condition & Symptom:** This formed heart **is** your moral state. It then **inevitably** produces speech that is its **symptom**—a natural overflow.
3.  **Act & Revelation:** In the moment of speaking, you are both **choosing** to articulate the overflow and simultaneously **revealing** the state of your heart to any listener with discernment.

### Important Nuance from Context
This is part of Jesus' **"Discourse on Blasphemy against the Holy Spirit"** (Matt. 12:22-37). He has just cast out a demon, and the Pharisees attribute it to Satan. Their words are not casual slips but **deliberate, settled judgments** emanating from a heart that has **treasured** opposition to God's work. Thus, their speech is:
*   A **symptom** of their enmity with God.
*   A **revelation** of their true spiritual alignment.
*   A **choice** that confirms their hardening heart.

### Conclusion
The passage **suggests all three aspects are true simultaneously.** Speech is:
*   A **symptom** of the heart's condition (it's the overflow).
*   A **revelation** of that hidden condition to others (it exposes the treasury).
*   A **choice** that both flows from and reinforces the treasured content of the heart.

The primary force is **deterministic but not fatalistic:** a corrupt *stockpile* will inevitably produce corrupt speech (symptom/revelation), but the state of the stockpile itself was built by choices. Therefore, the ultimate call is to **change what you are treasuring** (repentance, faith in Christ) so that the "overflow" of speech will be transformed from the inside out. The solution to evil speech is not just to clamp the mouth, but to **purify the heart's treasury.**
