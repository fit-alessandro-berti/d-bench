This is a profound question that touches on the psychological and spiritual mechanics of Jesus’ teaching in the Sermon on the Mount. The short answer is: **It says both, but it emphasizes that treasure *reveals* the heart’s existing allegiance, while simultaneously *training* (or fixing) the heart through habit.**

Let’s break this down carefully, looking at the logic of possession, attention, and love.

### 1. Does Treasure Reveal or Train the Heart?

The key verse is v. 21: *“For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”*

#### A. Treasure as Revelation (Diagnostic)
Jesus is giving us a diagnostic tool. He is saying: **If you want to know what you truly love, look at what you invest in.**
- Your “treasure” is not just money; it is whatever you value most highly—your time, energy, resources, and affection.
- The location of your treasure acts as a mirror. If you are anxious about earthly wealth, your heart is already anchored there. If you are generous toward eternal things, your heart is already oriented heavenward.
- In this sense, treasure **reveals** the true state of the heart. You cannot fake where your treasure is; your spending and prioritizing habits betray your inner loves.

#### B. Treasure as Training (Formative)
However, the imperative in v. 19–20 (“Lay not up… But lay up…”) implies that we have agency. We can choose where to place our treasure. And once we do, our heart follows.
- This suggests a **feedback loop**: Where you put your treasure, your heart becomes attached. By deliberately investing in heavenly things (acts of mercy, justice, worship, generosity), you *train* your heart to love those things more.
- Psychologically and spiritually, we become what we attend to. If you consistently give to the poor, your heart grows in compassion. If you hoard for security, your heart grows in fear and self-reliance.
- So, treasure also **trains** the heart by shaping its desires over time.

> **Conclusion:** Jesus presents a dynamic relationship. Treasure reveals where the heart *currently* is, but the act of laying up treasure is a discipline that *shapes* where the heart *will be*. It is both diagnostic and formative.

---

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together

Jesus connects these three concepts in a tight causal chain:

#### Step 1: Possession → Attention
- **“Where your treasure is…”**
- Treasure represents what you possess or seek to possess. But possession demands **attention**. You worry about what you own. You monitor it. You protect it.
- Earthly treasures require constant vigilance against “moth, rust, and thieves.” This forces your attention outward, onto fragile, temporary things.
- Heavenly treasures require no such vigilance—they are secure. This frees your attention to focus on God and others.

#### Step 2: Attention → Love
- **“…there will your heart be also.”**
- In biblical anthropology, the “heart” (*kardia*) is not just emotions; it is the center of will, intellect, and affection. It is what you *love* most.
- What you pay attention to, you come to love. Or conversely, what you love, you pay attention to. They are mutually reinforcing.
- If your attention is consumed by accumulating and protecting earthly wealth, your love becomes tied to material security. If your attention is fixed on eternal realities, your love becomes tied to God’s kingdom.

#### Step 3: Love → Identity (The Heart’s Home)
- The phrase “your heart will be also” suggests that the heart *dwells* where the treasure is.
- Your ultimate love defines your identity. If you love earthly things, you are an earth-bound person, vulnerable to loss and decay. If you love heavenly things, you are a citizen of heaven, secure and free.

### Summary Diagram

```
Possession (Treasure)
       ↓
   Demands Attention
       ↓
   Shapes Affection (Love)
       ↓
   Determines the Heart's Allegiance
```

### Practical Implication

Jesus is not merely giving financial advice. He is exposing the **idolatry of security**. 
- When we store up treasures on earth, we are trying to secure our lives apart from God. Our hearts become enslaved to fear and greed.
- When we store up treasures in heaven, we are trusting God with our future. Our hearts become free to love God and neighbor.

Thus, **how you handle your possessions is a spiritual discipline that trains your heart to love rightly.** You don’t wait until you “feel” like loving God to give generously; you give generously so that your heart learns to love God more than money.
