Pride is often linked to blindness and collapse because it involves an inflated sense of self-worth and an overestimation of one's abilities, which can obscure a person's perception of reality. This skewed perception can lead to poor decision-making, as those who are prideful may ignore advice, fail to see their limitations, and underestimate challenges or opposition. Furthermore, pride can create a sense of invulnerability or invincibility, leading individuals to take unnecessary risks, which can contribute to their downfall.

The contrast with “the lowly” in the proverb suggests a moral psychology where humility is associated with clarity, awareness, and openness to learning. Those who are humble are more likely to recognize their limitations and seek guidance, making them more adaptable and less prone to the errors that often accompany arrogance. Humility fosters a mindset that values collaboration, introspection, and respect for others, which can lead to more sustainable success and personal growth. This highlights the idea that true strength and wisdom come from self-awareness and community rather than from an inflated sense of solitary greatness. 

The moral lesson in contrasting pride with humility is that an arrogant person, blinded by their own perceived greatness, is more likely to stumble, whereas someone who approaches life with a humble, grounded spirit is more likely to succeed and maintain their integrity.
