The elder brother's deepest problem in Luke 15:28-32 is a combination of several factors, but the most prominent one is wounded fairness. His anger and refusal to enter the house reflect his sense of injustice and disappointment. He feels that he has been faithful and obedient, yet he has not received the same recognition or celebration as his younger brother. This sense of unfairness is deeply rooted in his perception of his father's favoritism towards the prodigal son. 

While envy and pride in obedience are also present, they are secondary to the core issue of fairness. His discomfort with mercy is also a significant aspect, as he is unable to reconcile the father's forgiveness and celebration with his own sense of righteousness.
