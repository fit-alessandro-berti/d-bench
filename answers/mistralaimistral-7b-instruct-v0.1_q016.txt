 The elder brother's deepest problem is a combination of envy, wounded sense of fairness, and pride in obedience. He is envious of the celebration for his prodigal brother's return, feeling that he has not received the same recognition for his years of faithful service. He also feels a sense of unfairness, believing that he deserves a reward for his obedience, while his brother receives a lavish welcome after squandering their father's wealth. Additionally, his pride in his own obedience may have blinded him to the true meaning of love and mercy, making it difficult for him to rejoice in his brother's restoration.
