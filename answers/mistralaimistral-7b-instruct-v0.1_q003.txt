 This short moral summary from Micah 6:8 is demanding because it calls for a complete transformation of the human heart and actions. Let's explore how justice, mercy, and humility correct one another:

1. Justice: Doing justly means treating others fairly and upholding moral principles. It requires us to recognize and respect the rights and dignity of all people. Justice can be demanding because it challenges us to confront and address inequalities and injustices in our society. It requires us to speak out against oppression and to work towards creating a more just world.
2. Mercy: Loving mercy means showing compassion and kindness to others, especially to those who are suffering or in need. Mercy can be demanding because it requires us to go beyond the call of duty and to extend ourselves to others in a selfless way. It challenges us to see the humanity in others, even those who may have wronged us or who are different from us.
3. Humility: Walking humbly with God means recognizing our dependence on God and acknowledging our limitations. Humility can be demanding because it requires us to let go of our pride and ego, and to put the needs of others before our own. It challenges us to be open to learning from others and to seek wisdom and guidance from God.

Justice, mercy, and humility correct one another in the following ways:

- Justice and Mercy: Justice and mercy are complementary. Justice ensures that everyone is treated fairly and that moral principles are upheld. Mercy ensures that we extend compassion and kindness to others, even when they have wronged us. Together, they create a balanced and just society where everyone is treated with dignity and respect.
- Mercy and Humility: Mercy and humility are also complementary. Mercy requires us to extend ourselves to others in a selfless way, while humility requires us to recognize our limitations and to put the needs of others before our own. Together, they help us to develop a compassionate and humble heart that is open to the needs of others.
- Humility and Justice: Humility and justice are also related. Humility requires us to recognize our dependence on God and to seek wisdom and guidance from Him. Justice requires us to uphold moral principles and to treat others fairly. Together, they help us to develop a heart that is both grounded in God and committed to doing what is right.

In summary, the moral summary from Micah 6:8 is demanding because it calls us to live out these three virtues in our daily lives. Justice, mercy, and humility correct one another and help us to create a more just, compassionate, and humble world.
