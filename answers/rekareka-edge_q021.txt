This **guilt** underscores a tendency toward overconfidence, causing individuals to underestimate the power of humility or feedback.

**4. **Cultural and Historical Context**
The proverbs likely emerged in ancient societies where social hierarchies were shaped by power dynamics, and pride was often a mark of status. The emphasis on the fall of the proud reflects a critique of individualism, urging a reevaluation of pride in favor of communal responsibility. Thus, these verse may resonate deeply in cultures that emphasize collective values and caution against excessive individualism.
