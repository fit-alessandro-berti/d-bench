The text from Matthew 6:19-21 suggests that treasure reveals the heart rather than trains it. The phrase “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” indicates that the things or values a person treasures—what they prioritize and invest in—are reflective of their inner desires and priorities. It points to the idea that one's heart and affections gravitate toward what they deem valuable.

In this context, possession, attention, and love are intricately connected. Here’s how they tie together:

1. **Possession**: The treasures one chooses to accumulate—whether material wealth or spiritual values—reflect personal priorities. When someone invests more in earthly treasures, their focus shifts toward those possessions.

2. **Attention**: What we treasure demands our attention, time, and energy. The act of investing in certain things (like wealth or status) often leads to a preoccupation with them, drawing our focus away from spiritual and eternal matters.

3. **Love**: Ultimately, where one's treasure is indicates what they love or aspire toward. Love often dictates what we pursue—if our treasures are earthly, then our hearts are inclined towards those temporary things; if our treasures are heavenly, our hearts are aligned with lasting spiritual realities.

In summary, this passage encapsulates a profound truth: our possessions and investments reveal our true priorities and affections, shaping the orientation of our hearts. Thus, rather than training the heart, treasures expose where our love and attention genuinely lie.
