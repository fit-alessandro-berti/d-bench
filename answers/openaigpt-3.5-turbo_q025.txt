In this verse, the issue with public goodness is not necessarily the visibility itself, but rather the temptation to do good deeds solely for the purpose of receiving recognition and praise from others. When one's intentions behind their actions are driven by a desire for public approval and admiration, they are not acting out of genuine sincerity and compassion. This can lead to hypocrisy and insincerity in one's actions, as they are more concerned with outward appearances rather than truly helping others and living out their faith. Thus, the moral danger lies in the selfish motives and lack of true altruism that can come with seeking to be seen and praised by others for one's good deeds.
