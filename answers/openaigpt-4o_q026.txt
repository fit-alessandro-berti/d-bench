This passage from Matthew 12:34–35 suggests a deep connection between a person's inner moral state and their speech. It implies that words are indeed symptoms, choices, and revelations of one's inner nature.

1. **Symptoms:** The passage indicates that speech reflects the condition of the heart, akin to symptoms reflecting an underlying ailment. This is evident in the phrase, "for out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh." It suggests that what people say is a natural overflow of their inner moral and emotional state.

2. **Choices:** It also hints that speech involves choices, as it distinguishes between "a good man" and "an evil man." The passage suggests that people choose to speak based on the "treasures" they have stored within their hearts, implying an element of volition in exercising their moral or immoral inclinations.

3. **Revelations:** The passage serves as an assertion that speech reveals what is in the heart, acting as a window into one’s inner moral landscape. As a "good man" brings forth good things from the "good treasure" of his heart, his words and actions reveal his true character, and the same is true for the "evil man."

In summary, Jesus' words in this passage portray speech as indicative (symptoms), volitional (choices), and demonstrative (revelations) of one's internal moral and ethical state. The heart is portrayed as a storeroom, where accumulated values and beliefs inevitably shape and surface through one's words.
