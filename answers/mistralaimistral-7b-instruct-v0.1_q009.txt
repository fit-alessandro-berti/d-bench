 This passage from Romans 12:20-21 does not define victory over evil as the elimination or defeat of the enemy, but rather as overcoming evil with good actions towards them. The metaphor of "heap coals of fire on his head" is believed to be an Old Testament reference to the shame and guilt that the enemy will feel when they receive kindness and generosity from their enemy.

The command is not weak or manipulative, but genuinely strong. It requires a great deal of self-control, compassion, and forgiveness to respond to an enemy with kindness and goodwill, especially when one might feel justified in retaliating with evil. By choosing to overcome evil with good, the believer is able to break the cycle of hatred and violence, and potentially turn the enemy into a friend or ally. This is a powerful demonstration of the transformative power of love and forgiveness, and a testament to the strength and resilience of the human spirit.
