Great question! Let’s break down **Ezekiel 36:26–27** with attention to the contrast between *external compliance* and *receiving a new heart*, as well as why "hardness" is described in material terms.

---

### 1. **External Compliance vs. Receiving a New Heart**

#### **External Compliance**
- This refers to outwardly following God’s laws, rituals, and commandments—doing what God says, keeping the statutes, observing religious practices.
- It’s about behavior, actions, and adherence to rules from the outside.
- In Ezekiel’s context (post-exile), the people might have been going through the motions of worship but lacked true transformation or devotion.

#### **Receiving a New Heart**
- This is an internal, spiritual change. God promises not just to make them *do* the right things, but to *become* the kind of people who naturally want to obey and love God.
- The “heart of flesh” symbolizes stubbornness, rebellion, and self-centeredness; the “heart of flesh” is hardened by sin and self-will.
- A “new heart” means a transformation at the core—an inner renewal that leads to genuine love for God and obedience to His ways.

**In summary:**  
- External compliance = doing the right thing outwardly  
- New heart = being changed on the inside, so obedience flows from love and desire, not just obligation

---

### 2. **Why Is Hardness Described Like a Material Condition?**

- The “stony heart” is a metaphor for spiritual hardness—being unable to respond to God’s call, resistant to His truth, and unable to repent.
- By describing it as something that can be *taken away* and *replaced*, God emphasizes that this isn’t just a metaphor—it’s a real, tangible change in one’s nature.
- Just as a stone cannot feel or change, a stony heart cannot truly love or obey. But God promises to replace it with a *heart of flesh*—capable of feeling, responding, and loving.
- This imagery makes the promise vivid: God doesn’t just give you rules to follow; He changes your very being so you can live them from the inside out.

---

### **Summary Table**

| External Compliance         | New Heart                         |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| Obeys rules externally      | Transformed internally           |
| Can be hypocritical         | Genuine, sincere devotion         |
| Law without life            | Life-giving change               |
| Like wearing a mask          | Like having a new soul/spirit    |

---

**In essence:**  
God’s promise in Ezekiel 36:26–27 is not just about better behavior, but about a profound inner renewal—a transformation from being “stony” to being “fleshly” (in the sense of being alive, responsive, and loving). This is why the “hardness” is described in physical terms: because only God can change what is fundamentally hard and unresponsive into something tender and alive.
