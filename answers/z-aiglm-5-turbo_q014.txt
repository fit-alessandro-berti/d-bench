To answer your first question directly: **This text says both, and the genius of Jesus’ teaching is in how those two realities work together.** 

Grammatically, verses 19 and 20 are *imperatives* (commands: "Lay not up... but lay up"), while verse 21 is an *indicative* (a statement of fact: "For where your treasure is..."). By placing the command before the principle, Jesus is teaching that **treasure trains the heart**, which ultimately **reveals the heart**. 

Here is a breakdown of how this works, followed by how possession, attention, and love become inextricably tied together.

---

### 1. Treasure Reveals the Heart (The Diagnostic)
Verse 21 is perhaps the most accurate spiritual diagnostic ever spoken. Jesus is saying that your bank statement, your calendar, and your digital search history are infallible mirrors of your soul. 

We often like to think of our "heart" (our deepest loves, allegiances, and desires) as a hidden, mysterious thing that only God can see. Jesus denies this. He says the heart is exposed by what we do with our resources. You do not ultimately *follow* your heart; your heart *follows* your money and your time. If you want to know what you truly love, look at what you are afraid of losing (which is why Jesus mentions "thieves" and "rust").

### 2. Treasure Trains the Heart (The Prescriptive)
If verse 21 were the only verse, we might despair, thinking Jesus is just giving us a spiritual X-ray to show us how sick we are. But verses 19 and 20 tell us what to *do* about it. 

Jesus commands us to *actively* lay up treasure in heaven. Why? Because He knows human psychology and spiritual formation: **we are shaped by what we pursue.** 

In the biblical worldview, you cannot simply conjure up love for God out of thin air. You train your heart to love God by redirecting your resources (money, time, energy) toward God's kingdom. When you give to the poor, when you invest in ministry, when you spend your Saturday serving instead of hoarding, you are doing "heart therapy." By practicing heavenly investment, you are training your affections to align with heaven. Action precedes and shapes affection.

---

### 3. How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together
Jesus is revealing a profound psychological and spiritual feedback loop. In the human makeup, these three things cannot be separated. 

**Possession (The Anchor)**
When Jesus says "treasure," He isn't just talking about a pile of gold. *Thesauros* (treasure) means a storehouse, a place where you stockpile what you need for survival and security. To "possess" a treasure is to stake your safety and identity in it. You believe your life depends on that possession.

**Attention (The Focus)**
Because you possess it, you must protect it. Earthly treasures demand constant, exhausting attention. Jesus points out that you have to worry about moths, rust, and thieves. When you lay up treasures on earth, your mental bandwidth is consumed by maintaining them, protecting them, and growing them. (It is no accident that Jesus immediately follows this passage in verse 22 by talking about the "eye"—the organ of attention—being either healthy or evil). 

**Love (The Heart)**
Here is the tie that binds them: **What captures your attention eventually captures your heart.** 
We usually think we love something first, and therefore give it our attention. Jesus says it works the other way around: we give our attention to what we possess, and the sustained focus of our attention *becomes* our love. 

Think of it like a marriage or a hobby. You may not feel deeply in love with your spouse on a given Tuesday, or passionate about playing the piano. But if you intentionally invest your time and focus (attention) into the relationship or the instrument (possession/investment), your affections (love) are kindled and trained. 

### The Earthly vs. Heavenly Trap
The tragedy of earthly treasure is the futility of the loop. You possess it, it demands your attention to protect it from decay and thieves, it consumes your life, and then you lose it. Your love is anchored to a sinking ship.

Heavenly treasure breaks the anxiety loop. When you invest your resources in eternal things—people, the Gospel, justice, kindness—those things cannot be destroyed by moth, rust, or thief. Your possession is secure. Therefore, your attention is freed from anxious hoarding, and your love is anchored in something eternal. 

**Summary:** 
Jesus teaches that we are not primarily thinking beings, or even feeling beings, but **desiring beings** who are shaped by what we chase. Possession captures our attention; sustained attention forges our love; and our love is the true location of our heart. Therefore, change where you put your treasure, and you will change the very center of who you are.
