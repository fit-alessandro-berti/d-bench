# A Subtle but Important Distinction

The text actually **reverses what we might assume**: it doesn't say "your heart reveals where your treasure is," but rather **"where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."**

This suggests treasure *follows* the heart's commitment, but the heart *follows* the treasure. It's bidirectional, but the direction of causation matters:

## The Three-Way Entanglement

**Possession → Attention → Affection**

- What you *own* (or pursue owning) claims your mental space
- What occupies your attention shapes what you love
- What you love determines what you protect and serve

A person who stores wealth in property must think constantly about maintenance, markets, theft, decay. Their mental energy gets captured. Their emotional investment follows naturally. They've trained themselves—through the logic of possession—into a particular posture toward life.

## The Reversal

Jesus isn't merely observing human nature ("people care about what they own"). He's suggesting you can *redirect the whole system* by changing what you treasure:

- **Reorient your treasure** (the object)
- **Your attention will follow** (the mechanism)  
- **Your heart will be transformed** (the result)

It's simultaneously a diagnosis (*show me your checkbook and I'll show you your god*) and a prescription (*choose different treasures and become a different person*).

**The radical claim:** You don't need to fix your heart first. Change what you're building toward, and your heart will reorganize itself.
