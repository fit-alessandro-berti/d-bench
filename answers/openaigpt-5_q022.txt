Because the psalmist wants truth more than victory. In a song that has just denounced the wicked, he refuses to stop at “defend me” or “comfort me.” He knows zeal can hide mixed motives, blind spots, and self-deception. So he invites God’s scrutiny—the only searching that is both perfectly wise and merciful—so that any hidden complicity is exposed and healed. Defense without diagnosis risks self-righteousness; comfort without correction leaves us unchanged.

The honesty modeled is:
- Humble self-suspicion: “I might be part of the problem; show me.”
- Full consent to examination: not a curated confession but “any” offensive way.
- Willingness to be contradicted and corrected: “try me” (like testing metal) implies pressure that reveals what’s real.
- Desire for formation, not just relief: the request ends with “lead me,” moving from exposure to guidance along the enduring/everlasting way.

In short, this is invited vulnerability before God: a brave, teachable posture that prefers being made true to being proved right.
