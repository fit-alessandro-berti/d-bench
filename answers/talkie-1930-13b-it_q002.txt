The former desire is negative, the latter positive; the former craves deliverance from penalty, the latter craves holiness of character. He who desires merely to escape from the punishment of sin, desires simply to be let alone; but he who desires a clean heart desires to become a new creature. The one wishes to cease from doing evil, the other wishes to learn to do well. The former is actuated by fear, the latter by faith. The former wishes to be spared punishment, because he dreads it; the latter, because he hopes for reward. In short, the one desires to be spared; the other, to be saved.
