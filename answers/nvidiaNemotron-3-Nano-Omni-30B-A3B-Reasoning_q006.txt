**Answer:**
The "self" being denied is the ego-centered, self-preserving identity that seeks comfort, security, and personal gain. It is the self that resists surrender, risk, or sacrifice.

This teaching sounds liberating to those who feel burdened by the pressures of self-assertion, consumerism, or existential anxiety — because it offers a path where true freedom is found not in clinging to oneself, but in letting go. In losing the self, one discovers a deeper, more authentic life.

To others, it sounds offensive because it challenges the modern emphasis on individualism, self-fulfillment, and personal rights. The idea that self-denial leads to life contradicts cultural narratives that equate identity with autonomy and success. It demands humility, discipline, and a willingness to suffer — things many are unwilling to embrace.

In essence, Jesus invites a radical reorientation: the path to true life is not upward in self-exaltation, but downward in humble service and self-giving love.
