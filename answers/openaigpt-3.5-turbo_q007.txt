While this verse may seem to suggest a blanket statement against making moral judgments, it is important to interpret it in the context of the overall teachings of Jesus. In other parts of the Bible, Jesus and other biblical figures do make moral judgments and call out sinful behavior. 

The key distinction here is between making discerning judgments with a humble and loving attitude, versus passing condemnatory judgments with a self-righteous and unforgiving attitude. Discernment involves evaluating actions or behaviors in light of God's standards and seeking to bring about positive change or correction in a spirit of love and understanding. On the other hand, the appetite to condemn involves looking down on others, casting judgment without offering forgiveness or redemption, and seeking to punish rather than help.

Jesus encourages us to be discerning in our judgments, but to do so with humility, love, and a desire for reconciliation. We are called to forgive others as we ourselves have been forgiven by God. This verse reminds us to avoid the temptation to condemn others and instead extend forgiveness and understanding, just as we hope to receive it ourselves. Ultimately, it is a call to approach others with grace and compassion rather than harsh judgment.
