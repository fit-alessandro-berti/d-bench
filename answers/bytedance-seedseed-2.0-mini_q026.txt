Matthew 12:34–35 strongly connects speech with the heart’s hidden moral stockpiles. It uses the metaphor of "treasure" in the heart, where the moral quality of what is spoken directly corresponds to what is stored within. 

Here, words are not arbitrary but are symptoms of the heart’s condition. They reveal the underlying moral state—whether it is filled with good or evil. This link underscores the idea that speech is a natural outflow from the heart’s moral reserves. Additionally, it implies that words are choices, as one consciously gives expression to what is held in the heart. Finally, speech serves as a revelation of the heart’s true nature, exposing its hidden moral stockpiles to others. Thus, this passage suggests that words are all three: symptoms, choices, and revelations of the heart’s moral state.
