The way Jesus says it is actually the reverse of how we usually quote it.

We tend to say it as: *Where your heart is, there your treasure will be also* - as if treasure just **reveals** the heart.

That's true elsewhere in Scripture, but that's not what this text says.

### What the text actually says

> **For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.** - Matt 6:21

Grammatically, treasure is the antecedent, heart is the consequent. Your heart *follows* your treasure. It doesn't lead it.

In Greek: *hopou gar estin ho thesauros sou, ekei estai kai he kardia sou.*

Where your thesaurus - your storehouse, your hoard, your investment - is, there your *kardia* will be.

Kardia in Jewish thought is not just emotion. It is the command center - your will, your loves, your attention, your allegiance.

Jesus is not saying "your money shows what you already love." He is saying **"your money trains what you will come to love."**

Treasure disciples the heart.

### How possession, attention, and love become tied

This is in the middle of the Sermon on the Mount's teaching on money. Immediately before and after this verse:

* v19-20: two ways to store - earth vs. heaven
* v22-23: the eye is the lamp of the body - what you look at fills you
* v24: you cannot serve God and Mammon

Jesus links the three together in a chain:

**1. Possession becomes Attention.**

What you call "treasure" is not just what you own, it's what you *lay up* - Greek *thesaurizete*, you stockpile, curate, protect, check on. You don't moth-proof something you don't care about. You don't put a lock on something you ignore.

In the ancient world, treasure was cloth vulnerable to moths, metal vulnerable to rust, and everything vulnerable to thieves who would literally "dig through" - *diorusso* - the mud-brick walls of your house. To keep treasure safe required constant vigilance.

Whatever you treasure, you watch.

**2. Attention becomes Love.**

That is why verse 22 about the eye comes next. What you fix your eyes on is what fills your inner life. We become what we behold. The psychologists call it attentional bias. The Bible calls it worship.

You cannot give sustained, anxious, protective attention to something for a long time without your heart wrapping itself around it.

**3. Love becomes Allegiance.**

Eventually that attachment becomes service. That's v24. You end up serving what you started out simply storing.

So the movement is:

> **Possession > Attention > Affection > Allegiance**

This is why Jesus' counsel is so practical, and so offensive to our modern ideal of authenticity. We think: *I need to get my heart right, and then I'll be generous. I need to love God more, and then I'll invest in heaven.*

Jesus says the opposite: *If you want your heart to love heaven, start investing there. Put your treasure there, and your heart will follow.*

You don't give because you love. You learn to love because you give.

If you put your money in the market, you'll check the market every morning. If you put your time and wealth into your house, you'll obsess about your house. If you put it into the Kingdom - into generosity, mercy, people, the word, the work of God - your heart will wake up there in the morning, too.

Your treasure doesn't just show where your heart has been. It decides where your heart is going.
