External compliance refers to following rules or regulations out of obligation or fear of consequences, without truly having a change of heart or genuine desire to do so. On the other hand, receiving a "new heart" refers to experiencing a deep internal transformation where one's attitudes, desires, and motivations are fundamentally changed. It is a change that comes from within, leading to a lasting change in behavior.

The metaphor of a "stony heart" being replaced with a "heart of flesh" emphasizes the hardness and coldness of a person's heart that resists God's ways and his teachings. Just as stone is unyielding and unresponsive, a stony heart is stubborn and resistant to change. By contrast, a heart of flesh is soft, pliable, and receptive. It signifies a heart that is open to God's leading and willing to be molded and transformed by Him.

By describing the need for a new heart in this way, Ezekiel highlights the necessity of an internal change that goes beyond mere external actions. The transformation that God offers is profound and all-encompassing, affecting not just behavior but also attitudes, motivations, and desires. It is a complete change of the inner person, made possible through the work of the Holy Spirit.
