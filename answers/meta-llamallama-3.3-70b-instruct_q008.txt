The verse treats love of the visible neighbor as a test of love of the invisible God because it's easier to claim love for an abstract or distant deity than to demonstrate love towards someone tangible and present in our lives. By saying that one cannot love God without loving their brother, the verse is highlighting the inconsistency of claiming a profound, abstract love while neglecting the immediate, practical opportunities to show love and kindness to those around us.

This verse exposes the self-deception of those who might use their profession of loving God as a way to avoid the challenges and responsibilities that come with loving their neighbors. It's a form of hypocrisy where individuals might prioritize their spiritual or theological claims over the actual practice of love and compassion in their daily interactions.

In essence, the verse suggests that true love for God should manifest in tangible actions towards others. If someone claims to love God but does not demonstrate this love through their treatment of their fellow human beings, their claim is fundamentally flawed. This principle emphasizes the importance of putting faith into action, particularly in how we treat those we can see and interact with, as a genuine reflection of our love for God, whom we cannot see.
