The self being denied here is the selfish and self-centered desires and ambitions of an individual. This passage is calling for a surrender of one's own will and desires in order to follow Jesus and his teachings. 

Some may find this idea liberating because it offers a path to selflessness, sacrifice, and service to others, as well as a deep connection to a higher purpose. It can be seen as a way to transcend the limitations of the ego and find true meaning and fulfillment in life.

On the other hand, this concept can be offensive to others who prioritize their own personal desires, ambitions, and autonomy above all else. The idea of self-denial and taking up a cross (symbolizing sacrifice and suffering) can be seen as restrictive, burdensome, and challenging to one's sense of individuality and freedom. It goes against the cultural emphasis on self-fulfillment and self-realization that is prevalent in modern society.
