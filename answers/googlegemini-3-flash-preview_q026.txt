Matthew 12:34–35 presents a psychological and spiritual landscape where the mouth acts as a pressure-relief valve for the inner self. Christ uses the metaphor of "treasure" (or "stockpile" in the Greek *thēsauros*) to describe the cumulative nature of a person’s moral character.

The passage connects speech with these hidden "moral stockpiles" in the following ways:

### 1. The Mechanic of "Abundance" (*Perisseuma*)
The Greek word translated as "abundance" refers to a surplus or that which overflows. This suggests that the heart is a container with a finite capacity; once it is filled with a certain type of content—whether bitterness, lust, and malice, or love, peace, and truth—it must eventually spill over. Speech is not an isolated event; it is the "overflow" of a saturated interior.

### 2. The Metaphor of the "Treasure" (*Thēsauros*)
By using the word "treasure," Jesus implies that our inner life is built through a process of **accumulation**. 
*   **The Good Man:** Has spent time "depositing" good things (scripture, prayer, virtuous thoughts) into his heart.
*   **The Evil Man:** Has "stockpiled" evil things (resentment, falsehood, pride). 

The speech that follows is not a random occurrence but a "withdrawal" from the established account. You cannot withdraw gold from a vault filled only with lead.

---

### Are words symptoms, choices, or revelations?

The passage suggests they are **all three**, functioning in a chronological sequence:

#### They are Symptoms (The Involuntary Aspect)
Jesus asks, *"How can ye... speak good things?"* implying a moral impossibility. In this sense, words are symptoms of a systemic condition. Just as a cough is a symptom of a lung infection, "viperous" speech is a symptom of a poisoned heart. This suggests that while we can mask our nature for a time, our "default" speech patterns under pressure will always betray our true health.

#### They are Revelations (The Epistemological Aspect)
The passage asserts that the mouth is the most reliable diagnostic tool for the soul. Speech "brings forth" what was previously hidden. For the Pharisees (the "vipers"), their words revealed a heart that was diametrically opposed to God, even if they performed outward religious rituals. Speech strips away the mask of hypocrisy, revealing the "stockpile" to the world and to the speaker themselves.

#### They are Choices (The Formative Aspect)
While the verse emphasizes the *result* of the stockpile, the word "treasure" implies an active agent who did the storing. We are not born with a full "treasure chest"; we fill it through a lifetime of choices—what we dwell on, what we value, and what we secretly rehearse in our minds. Therefore, our words are the *delayed result* of a thousand tiny choices made in the secrecy of the heart.

### Summary
In Matthew 12, Jesus rejects the idea that a "slip of the tongue" is an accident. Instead, he treats speech as **irrefutable evidence**. Connection to the heart is absolute: speech is the visible fruit of an invisible root system. If the words are rotten, the stockpile is documented; the "choice" was made long before the words were ever spoken, in the moments when the heart was being filled.
