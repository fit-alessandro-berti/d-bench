Short answer: Jesus is saying that treasure trains the heart. By telling you to place treasure in heaven and then adding “for where your treasure is, there your heart will be also,” he implies that your heart follows your investments. He does not say, “where your heart is, there your treasure will be,” which would make treasure merely a diagnostic. The future “will be” and the “for” (because) show cause and effect: choose where to invest, and your heart will be drawn there.

How possession, attention, and love tie together in the passage (Matt 6:19–24):
- Possession (treasure): What you store up attaches your heart to its location (earthly decay or God’s presence).
- Attention (the eye, vv. 22–23): What you fix your gaze on shapes your inner life—sound focus fills you with light; a distorted focus darkens you. Your investments pull your attention.
- Love/loyalty (master, v. 24): Attention hardens into allegiance. You end up serving what you’ve come to love. You cannot serve both God and Mammon.

Put together: Investment draws attention; attention cultivates affection; affection settles into allegiance. That works in both directions—earthly hoarding breeds anxiety and divided loyalty, while investing in God’s kingdom (generosity, secrecy, seeking the Father’s reward) retrains desire and consolidates love. If you want your heart to be with God, start sending your treasure—and therefore your attention—there.
