Amos 5:21-24 expresses sharp criticism of religious practices that are devoid of genuine morality and justice. This rejection of ritual worship is poignant and reflects a common theme in prophetic literature where external religious observances are meaningless without substantive ethical behavior.

1. **Critique of Hollow Ritualism**: This passage clearly indicates God's disdain for rituals carried out without true devotion or ethical consistency. The repeated declarations of hatred and rejection ("I hate, I despise", "I will not accept", "I will not hear") stress that mere ritualistic observance is insufficient. God's rejection is not of the worship per se but of worship that is disconnected from righteousness and justice.

2. **Exposing Spiritual Fraud**: The moral or spiritual fraud exposed here is the discrepancy between outward religious performance and inward moral integrity. The people of Israel were engaging in elaborate religious festivities and offerings, which from the outside looked devout and religious, but they neglected the broader, deeper moral requirements of justice and righteousness. This kind of religious practice is fraudulent because it projects a facade of holiness and devotion while failing to embody the fundamental ethical imperatives that should underpin such worship.

3. **Call for Justice and Righteousness**: The demand for justice ("judgment run down as waters") and righteousness ("righteousness as a mighty stream") as opposed to ritual observances indicates a prioritization of ethical living over religious formalism. Water imagery suggests purity, power, and pervasiveness — justice and righteousness should flow and fill every aspect of societal life, just as water covers the sea floor. The metaphor underscores the idea that social justice and moral righteousness are continuous and ever-renewing forces, essential to true religious life.

4. **Broader Context and Biblical Consistency**: This theme is not unique to Amos but consistent across much of the prophetic literature in the Bible. Prophets like Isaiah, Micah, and Jeremiah also emphasized that without a corresponding ethical life, worship is utterly displeasing to God. For instance, Micah 6:6-8 challenges the efficacy of burnt offerings and highlights the importance of doing justice, loving kindness, and walking humbly with God.

In summary, Amos 5:21-24 rejects worship that is not accompanied by true ethical and moral behavior, underscoring that the acts of worship are meaningless if they are conducted in the absence of a true commitment to justice and righteousness. This passage calls out the moral and spiritual fraud of performing religious rituals while neglecting the weightier demands of the law: justice, mercy, and faithfulness.
