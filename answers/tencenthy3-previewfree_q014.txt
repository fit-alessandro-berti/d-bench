This is a profound question that gets to the heart of Jesus’s economic and spiritual ethics. The text of Matthew 6:19–21 operates on the tension between these two ideas: **treasure as a revelation** and **treasure as a trainer**.

Here is a breakdown of how the text functions, specifically regarding your questions about revelation vs. training and the tie between possession, attention, and love.

### 1. Revelation vs. Training: Does treasure *show* the heart or *shape* the heart?

The answer is **both**, but with a specific order and flow.

**A. The Revelation: "Where your treasure is, there your heart will be also."**
The sentence structure here is crucial. Jesus places the "treasure" (the object) in the "if/when" position, and the "heart" in the "then/result" position.
*   **The Logic:** If you want to know what a person loves, look at where they put their money. The treasure is the *evidence*; the heart is the *reality*.
*   **The Diagnostic:** In this sense, the text is a revelation. You cannot hide your heart from God. If your bank account is full of earthly investments, your heart is, by definition, tethered to the earth.

**B. The Training: "Lay not up for yourselves..."**
However, the command to "Lay not up" implies that we have a choice, and that our choices regarding money have a formative effect on us.
*   **The Flow:** If you *lay up* earthly treasure, you are *training* your heart to rely on earth. If you *lay up* heavenly treasure, you are *training* your heart to rely on God.
*   **The Feedback Loop:** While the final verse says the heart follows the treasure, the preceding verses warn that the treasure corrupts the heart (via anxiety, security in wealth, etc.). Practice precedes belief. By acting generously (laying up treasure in heaven), you are effectively disciplining your heart to love God more than money.

**Synthesis:**
The text suggests a **circular relationship**. You generally store up treasure where your heart already is (Revelation), but the act of storing it there cements the heart's attachment further (Training). This is why Jesus warns against earthly treasure; it doesn't just *show* you are worldly; it actively *makes* you more worldly and anxious (as the surrounding context in Matthew 6 mentions anxiety about food and clothing).

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love are Tied Together

Jesus connects these three concepts through the mechanism of **corruption and security**.

**A. Possession (The "What")**
*   **Earthly:** Moth, rust, and thieves. These represent the futility of material possession. You can own a house, but you don't own the market value, the structural integrity, or the safety of the neighborhood.
*   **Heavenly:** Acts of charity, justice, and devotion. These are "non-rivalrous" goods. If I give you love or forgiveness, I don't lose it; I gain more of it.

**B. Attention (The "Where")**
*   The phrase "break through and steal" implies a fortress mentality. If your treasure is on earth, your attention is consumed by defense—checking the stock market, fearing theft, worrying about depreciation.
*   If your treasure is in heaven, your attention is freed up for the Kingdom. You stop looking at the lock on your door and start looking at the face of your neighbor.

**C. Love (The "Heart")**
*   **The Link:** Love is the gravity that pulls the possession and the attention together.
*   **The Mechanism:** We possess things we think will make us secure. Our attention fixates on protecting those things. Consequently, our heart (our loyalty, identity, and trust) becomes anchored to those things.
*   **The Danger:** Earthly treasure demands *worry* (attention) because it is insecure. Heavenly treasure invites *worship* (attention) because it is secure in God.

### Summary
The text teaches that **treasure trains the heart by capturing the attention.**

1.  **Possession** is the currency you trade in (Earth vs. Heaven).
2.  **Attention** is the energy you spend protecting or investing that currency.
3.  **Love** is the resulting orientation of your soul.

Therefore, Jesus isn't just saying "Money shows what you love." He is saying, **"Be careful where you put your money, because whatever you buy into is what you will end up worshiping."** The treasure reveals the heart, but the act of laying it up fundamentally shapes where the heart rests.
