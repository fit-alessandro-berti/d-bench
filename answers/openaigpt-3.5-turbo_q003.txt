This short moral summary is demanding because it encapsulates the core values and principles that the Lord requires of His people. Each of the elements - justice, mercy, and humility - plays a crucial role in guiding individuals towards a righteous and harmonious life.

Justice is the foundation of a fair and equitable society. It requires individuals to treat others with fairness, impartiality, and respect for their rights and dignity. By doing justly, individuals uphold the rule of law and ensure that everyone is treated fairly and without discrimination.

Mercy complements justice by emphasizing compassion, forgiveness, and kindness towards others. While justice focuses on enforcing laws and rules, mercy allows for understanding, empathy, and forgiveness in situations where strict justice might not be the most compassionate approach. Mercy recognizes the humanity and inherent worth of each individual, leading to a more compassionate and understanding society.

Humility is the attitude of acknowledging our limitations, weaknesses, and dependence on a higher power. It requires individuals to be humble in acknowledging their own faults and recognizing the value and worth of others. Walking humbly with God involves surrendering one's own pride and ego, and instead, seeking guidance, strength, and wisdom from a higher power.

When combined, justice, mercy, and humility work in harmony to correct each other and create a balanced and virtuous life. Justice ensures fairness and equality, mercy brings compassion and forgiveness, and humility fosters a sense of reverence and dependence on a higher power. By practicing all three virtues, individuals are able to live in alignment with God's will and contribute to a more just, merciful, and humble society.
