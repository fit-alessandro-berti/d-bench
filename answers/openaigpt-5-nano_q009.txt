Short answer
- Victory over evil in this passage means breaking the cycle of retaliation by responding to wrongdoing with good. It aims at reconciliation and the transformation of the other person, trusting God to heal what is broken and to judge what is just. The real power shown is moral and relational, not merely reactive or muscular.

What the passage says about how to win
- The command: If your enemy is hungry, feed him; if he is thirsty, give him something to drink. This is bold, countercultural behavior—non-retaliation in the strongest sense.
- The goal: “Be not overcome of evil, but overcome evil with good.” The victory is not winning a quarrel but resisting evil by a superior, godly response.
- The “coal on his head” line (Proverbs 25:21–22 as Paul cites it) is meant to convey a potential effect: your kindness may provoke guilt, shame, or repentance in the offender, leading to reconciliation. It is not a license to manipulate, but a reflection that genuine love can awaken conscience.

Is the command weak, manipulative, or genuinely strong?
- It is genuinely strong. It requires self-control, courage, and trust in God’s justice rather than taking vengeance. It demonstrates moral strength by choosing costly, generous actions toward someone who harms you.
- It is not designed to manipulate. Its aim is the good of the other person and the peace of the community, rooted in the belief that God will redeem and judge rightly.
- The reference to “coals of fire” is a poetic image for awakening conscience or causing the offender to rethink their hostility. It is not a formula for coercion but a possible moral byproduct of loving response.

Practical notes to keep in mind
- The principle applies best within the broad moral frame of “as much as it depends on you, live peaceably with all” (Romans 12:18). It does not require you to endure abuse or to abandon prudent boundaries—wisdom and safety matter.
- The Sermon on the Mount (Jesus’ call to love enemies, do good to them, bless them) aligns with this ethic of overcoming evil with good.
