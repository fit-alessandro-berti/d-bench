In **Matthew 6:1**, Jesus warns against performing acts of righteousness (like giving alms) *"before men, to be seen of them."* The moral danger here is not **visibility itself**—since some good deeds may naturally be seen by others (e.g., Matthew 5:16)—but rather the **motivation behind the visibility**: the craving for human approval rather than seeking God’s reward.

### Key Distinctions:
1. **The Problem: Hypocritical Motivation**
   - Jesus condemns acting righteously *primarily* to gain praise from people (*"to be seen of them"*).
   - The Greek word for *"to be seen"* (*θεαθῆναι, theathēnai*) implies a theatrical performance—doing good as a show for applause.
   - This turns a holy act into a self-serving performance, robbing it of spiritual value (cf. Matthew 6:2, 5, 16).

2. **Visibility vs. Intention**
   - **Visibility alone is neutral**: Jesus doesn’t forbid all public good deeds (e.g., He commends letting light shine before men in Matthew 5:16). The issue is the *heart’s motive*.
   - **The danger is the craving for human admiration**: When the reward we seek is earthly praise, we forfeit the eternal reward from God (Matthew 6:1b).

3. **The Deeper Principle: God-Centered Righteousness**
   - Jesus contrasts hypocritical display with **secret devotion** (Matthew 6:3–4, 6, 17–18), where the Father—who sees in secret—rewards openly.
   - The warning reflects a **theological priority**: God, not man, is the ultimate audience of our righteousness (cf. Colossians 3:23).

### Practical Implications:
- **Good deeds can be public**, but the *motive* must be love for God and neighbor, not self-glorification.
- **Self-examination is key**: Am I serving to honor God, or to feed my ego? (1 Corinthians 10:31).
- **Rewards are mutually exclusive**: Seeking human applause nullifies divine reward (Matthew 6:2, 5, 16).

### Conclusion:
The danger is not that others *see* our goodness, but that we *perform* goodness *for* their approval. The verse exposes the idolatry of human praise—a subtle but deadly distortion of true piety. As Jesus later says, *"How can you believe, when you receive glory from one another and do not seek the glory that comes from the only God?"* (John 5:44).
