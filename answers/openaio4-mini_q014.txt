In this deceptively simple saying Jesus is both diagnosing and prescribing.  He diagnoses what we already know from daily life—that whatever we value most (our “treasure”) will be the center of our attention and affection—and he prescribes a decisive re-orientation toward “treasures in heaven.”  

1. “Treasure” and “heart” in the Greek  
   • “Treasure” (thēsauros) is anything you accumulate and guard—wealth, reputation, security, even hobbies or relationships.  
   • “Heart” (kardia) is the biblical shorthand for the seat of one’s desires, loyalties, ultimate concerns.  

2. Does treasure reveal the heart—or train it?  
   – Reveal:  When Jesus says “where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” (v. 21), he’s first pointing out that what you already value shows who you really are.  If you’re spending sleepless nights worrying about your 401(k), it’s because your deepest loyalty is there, not in God.  Our patterns of spending, saving, collecting and protecting give away our true commitments.  
   – Train:  At the same time, Jesus knows that what we give attention to shapes our desires.  If I resolutely “lay up” treasure in heaven—by acts of generosity, obedience, worship—my heart is being retrained.  Habitual giving and trust cultivate new affections.  

3. The triangle of possession, attention and love  
   • Possession draws attention.  Anything we own or invest in demands our mental energy—insurances to buy, passwords to remember, homes to maintain.  
   • Attention expresses and reinforces love.  We watch over what we love; the more we watch, the more we love.  
   • Love determines the heart’s allegiance.  Ultimately the heart is heartened by what it loves most.  

Put together, these truths explain both sides of Jesus’ warning:  
   – If you “store up” for the earth, the constant care you lavish on your earthly assets reveals (and deepens) an earthly allegiance.  
   – If you instead invest in “treasures in heaven” (mercy, justice, faithfulness, people’s wellbeing), your habitual attention to kingdom values reveals—and reshapes—your heart’s true home.  

In short, treasure does double duty: it discloses where your heart already lies, and by what you choose to build up, it also molds the direction of your future affection.
