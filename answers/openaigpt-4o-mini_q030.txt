The passage from Corinthians 13:4–7 presents a profound portrait of love, characterized not just by its positive attributes but also by a series of negations—traits love does not embody. This intentional focus on what love is not serves to elevate a specific vision of strength and virtue that counters many common human failings.

1. **Negations of Negative Traits**: The negations—such as "envieth not," "vaunteth not itself," "is not puffed up," and "is not easily provoked"—highlight the notion that true love is humble and selfless. By emphasizing what love rejects, the text suggests that love is fundamentally about the well-being of others rather than self-aggrandizement or competition. Such traits often lead to discord and division.

2. **Vision of Strength through Restraint**: This portrayal of love embodies a strength that is often misunderstood. The description suggests that real strength lies not in dominance or assertiveness but in the ability to endure, show kindness, and remain unshaken by provocation. This is a strength rooted in patience, understanding, and compassion, which are often more challenging to maintain than expressions of pride or anger.

3. **Moral and Spiritual Fortitude**: Additionally, the negations indicate a moral fortitude where love requires a conscious choice to reject negative impulses such as jealousy or resentment. This fortitude implies that love is not merely an emotion but a deliberate practice that calls for discipline and commitment.

4. **Promoting Harmony**: By depicting love in this way, the passage promotes a vision of community and harmonious relationships. Love that does not delight in wrongdoing but rejoices in truth encourages accountability and integrity among individuals, fostering a bond that transcends selfishness and conflict.

5. **An Endurance Amidst Trials**: The concluding phrases—“beareth all things, believeth all things, hopeth all things, endureth all things”—highlight a resilient aspect of love, portraying it as something that not only persists through challenges but also actively supports and uplifts.

In summary, this portrait of love suggests that true strength lies in humility, patience, and an unwavering commitment to the good of others, offering a counter-narrative to common societal values focused on self-interest, pride, and competition. The vision presented is one that encourages individuals to strive for a love that actively fosters unity and resilience amidst life's trials.
