The passage from John 3:19-21 touches on profound themes of truth, moral behavior, and human psychology. The metaphor of "light" represents truth, righteousness, and divine revelation, while "darkness" symbolizes ignorance, sin, and evil. 

### Reasons Why Light Might Be Hated:

1. **Exposure of Deeds:**
   - Light reveals what is hidden in darkness. Those who engage in wrongful or unethical acts might avoid light (or truth) because it exposes their deeds to themselves and others. This exposure can lead to shame or guilt as light brings clarity and accountability to one's actions.

2. **Fear of Judgment:**
   - People might fear the moral or social repercussions of having their deeds exposed. Being in the light can lead to condemnation or criticism from others, which they would prefer to avoid. This fear of judgment can manifest as a dislike or hatred of light.

3. **Comfort in Darkness:**
   - Darkness can provide a false sense of safety and comfort, allowing individuals to continue in their ways without disruption. The familiar, even if negative, can be more comforting than confronting the unknown demands and changes that stepping into the light might necessitate.

4. **Rejection of Change:**
   - Stepping into the light often requires acknowledgment of wrongdoing and a willingness to change. Some might resist this out of pride, stubbornness, or a reluctance to change ingrained habits and behaviors.

5. **Conflict with Self-Perception:**
   - If one's identity is closely tied to their current ways, the light can be threatening as it challenges and potentially deconstructs their self-perception. Accepting the truth could lead to an identity crisis or discomfort.

6. **Truth and Vulnerability:**
   - Embracing truth involves vulnerability—a willingness to be seen as one truly is. This can be intimidating as it removes masks and pretenses people use to protect themselves. 

### The Relation Between Truth, Exposure, and Shame:

- **Truth and Exposure:**
  Truth serves as a light that uncovers what is hidden. This exposure can be uncomfortable for those accustomed to the darkness. The relationship between truth and exposure is intrinsic; truth's nature is to reveal reality as it is, often challenging the status quo.

- **Exposure and Shame:**
  When deeds are exposed, it can lead to feelings of shame, particularly if those deeds are at odds with societal or personal moral standards. Shame arises from the awareness of having fallen short of one’s own or society’s expectations.

### The Desire Not to Be Seen Clearly:

- People might fear being fully seen because it involves revealing both strengths and weaknesses. This transparency can lead to vulnerability and the possibility of rejection or misunderstanding.
- Avoiding the light allows individuals to maintain control over their self-image and how they are perceived by others, even if it means living inauthentic or morally ambiguous lives.

In summary, the passage suggests that while light (or truth) offers a path to righteousness and authenticity, the human tendency to hide or resist discomfort can lead to a preference for darkness. However, those who seek truth and righteousness willingly come into the light, embracing the opportunity for inner and outer transformation.
