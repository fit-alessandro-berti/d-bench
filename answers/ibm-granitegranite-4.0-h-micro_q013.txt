The passage from Amos 5:21-24 is a strong rebuke from the prophet Amos to the people of Israel. The rejection of worship in such a sharp manner can be understood in the context of the moral and spiritual state of the society at the time.

The Israelites were engaging in religious practices, such as feasts, burnt offerings, and meat offerings, which were part of their worship. However, Amos criticizes these practices, stating that God does not accept them. This is not because these acts are inherently wrong, but because they were performed without true devotion or sincerity. The prophet suggests that the people were going through the motions of worship, but their hearts were not in it. They were not seeking God for His benefit, but for their own, perhaps to gain favor or to fulfill societal expectations.

The moral or spiritual fraud being exposed here is the superficiality of the people's worship. They were not genuinely seeking God, but were using religious practices as a means to an end, perhaps to maintain their social status or to appease God. This is a common issue in many societies, where religious practices can become more about tradition or social conformity rather than genuine devotion and relationship with God.

Amos' call for judgment to "run down as waters, and righteousness as a mighty stream" is a call for a radical change in their moral and spiritual lives. He is not merely asking for a balance between worship and other activities, but for a complete transformation of their hearts and actions. He wants them to worship God sincerely, with all their hearts, and to live their lives in a way that reflects righteousness and justice. This is a call for a genuine, heartfelt relationship with God, rather than a superficial, ritualistic one.
