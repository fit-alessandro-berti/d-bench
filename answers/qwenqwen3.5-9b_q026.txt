Based on a close exegesis of Matthew 12:34–35, the passage establishes an **ontological connection**: the source of human speech is not the mouth itself, but the moral orientation of the heart.

Here is an analysis of how the text links speech with the "hidden moral stockpiles" within the heart, followed by a determination of whether words function as symptoms, choices, or revelations.

### 1. The Connection: The "Treasure" and the "Abundance"

Jesus uses a metaphorical economy in this text to describe the mechanism of human character.
*   **The Heart as the Vault:** Jesus describes the "heart" (*kardia* in the Greek) not as a physical organ, but as the seat of personal identity, will, and moral accumulation. When He speaks of "good treasure" or "evil treasure," He refers to a stored orientation. This "storage" is composed of one's fundamental beliefs, history, habits, and spiritual condition over time. It is the "hidden stockpile."
*   **Speech as the Overflow:** The Greek word used for "abundance" suggests an overflow or quantity. Jesus implies that speech is not a superficial performance; it is the **release mechanism** of the heart. Just as a tree cannot produce fruit contrary to its nature (Matthew 7:16–17), a person cannot be "divorced" from the inner moral condition they possess when they speak.
*   **The Diagnostic Valid:** Therefore, the connection is causal. The mouth is not an independent variable; it is the reporter for the heart. If the "treasure" (stated intention/ontology) is ethically bankrupt, the "abundance" (current words) must reflect that same moral deficit.

### 2. Analysis of the Three Categories

To answer whether words are symptoms, choices, or revelations, we must look at the implied function of the mouth in this passage.

#### A. Words as Revealed Truth (Revelation)
**Yes.** This is the primary function assigned to speech in the text.
The word "speaketh" implies an act of disclosure. In the ancient context, the mouth often functioned as a seal-breaking vessel. If the treasure is hidden, the mouth is the only way the hidden content becomes visible to others.
*   *Theological implication:* You cannot deceive God regarding your nature by your words. Words act as a mirror of the soul. Therefore, speech is **revealing** because it exposes the "abundance" that was previously "hidden."

#### B. Words as Moral Symptoms (Symptom)
**Yes.** The text offers a diagnostic picture.
By asking, "How can ye, being evil, speak good things?" Jesus suggests that words are the *signs* of a specific internal disease or health.
*   *Theological implication:* Just as a fever is a symptom of infection, a right speech pattern (or lack thereof) is a symptom of the condition of the spirit. If the words are consistent with the condition of the "evil generation," the words are the diagnostic evidence of the hidden state. They function as a living autopsy report of the soul.

#### C. Words as Moral Choices (Choice)
**This is the nuanced element.**
While the verse implies that the evil man *inevitably* spits out evil (abundance leads to speech), it does not absolve the speaker of the choice to maintain or alter that treasure beforehand.
*   *Theological implication:* While the *outflow* may be the natural result of the heart's state, the heart gets fullness of evil or good through the choices made yesterday (and today). The verse suggests agency in a cause-and-effect workflow. The heart is not a passive vessel; it is the "generation" of vipers. This implies that through previous choices, the heart has been "filled" with a viper-treasure. Thus, while the *current word* is automatic, the *holder of the treasure* is responsible for the content.

### Conclusion

**Does it suggest they are all three?**
**Yes, but with a hierarchy.**

1.  **Primarily: Revelation.** The mouth exists to make the hidden known. Its holy or unholy nature is inevitable; it has no independent "voice" separate from the heart.
2.  **Secondarily: Symptom.** The words function as evidence of the internal diagnosis. We judge the tree by the fruit (wherever the fruit is the word) to reveal the internal health.
3.  **Underlyingly: Choice.** The "abundance" mentioned in the text is not static; it is a result of a lifetime of moral choices (the "treasure"). Therefore, the choice was not merely to speak, but to fill the treasure well or poorly.

**Summary Answer:**
This passage argues that speaking is an **automatic revelation** of one's internal condition. Words are not a coat of paint (disguise) but a report of inventory. They are **symptoms** of the soul's health and **revelations** of its character. While they may not be entirely "voluntary" in the moment (they flow from the "abundance"), the speaker bears full responsibility for the *treasure* that governs them, making the words a direct result of prior **moral choices**.
