This passage from **Matthew 6:19–21** is rich with theological and practical implications about the relationship between **treasure, the heart, possessions, attention, and love**. Let’s break it down carefully.

### **1. Does the text say treasure *reveals* the heart or *trains* the heart?**
The text leans more toward **treasure *reveals* the heart** rather than *trains* it, though both ideas are connected. The key verse is:

> *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* (v. 21)

This suggests that **what we value (our treasure) is a reflection of where our deepest affections lie (our heart)**. In other words, our **possessions and priorities expose the true direction of our hearts**.

However, the passage also implies that **treasure *shapes* the heart** over time. If we consistently invest in earthly treasures (wealth, status, material goods), our hearts will become attached to those things. Conversely, if we invest in heavenly treasures (love, generosity, faith), our hearts will align with those eternal values.

So, while the primary meaning is **revelatory**, the secondary effect is **formative**—our treasure *trains* the heart to love what it accumulates.

---

### **2. How do possession, attention, and love become tied together?**
This passage weaves together **three key elements**:

#### **A. Possession (Treasure)**
- Jesus contrasts **earthly treasure** (moth, rust, theft) with **heavenly treasure** (imperishable, secure).
- **Possessions are not inherently evil**, but they become dangerous when they become our **ultimate treasure** (idolatry).
- The way we **use, save, and prioritize** our resources reveals what we truly value.

#### **B. Attention (Where the Heart Is)**
- The heart follows the treasure—**what we focus on, we love**.
- If our treasure is on **earthly security** (wealth, comfort, status), our attention will be fixed on **preserving those things**.
- If our treasure is on **heavenly rewards** (love, justice, God’s kingdom), our attention will be directed toward **eternal values**.
- This is why Jesus warns against **anxiety about material needs** (Matt. 6:25–34)—our attention follows our treasure.

#### **C. Love (The Heart’s Affection)**
- The **heart** in biblical terms is the **center of desire, will, and devotion**.
- Jesus is saying: **You love what you treasure.**
  - If you treasure **money**, you will love **security and self-preservation**.
  - If you treasure **God’s kingdom**, you will love **generosity, justice, and eternal things**.
- This is why **idolatry** (worshiping created things over the Creator) is so dangerous—it **distorts love**, making us serve what cannot truly satisfy.

---

### **3. Theological & Practical Implications**
- **Materialism vs. Generosity:**
  - Earthly treasure is **fleeting** (moth, rust, theft), so hoarding it is foolish.
  - Heavenly treasure is **eternal** (faith, love, righteousness), so investing in it is wise.
- **The Heart’s True Treasure:**
  - Jesus is diagnosing a **spiritual condition**—our hearts are **naturally inclined toward self-preservation** (Matt. 6:19–21) and **anxiety** (Matt. 6:25–34).
  - The solution is **reorienting our treasure** toward God’s kingdom (Matt. 6:33).
- **Attention as Worship:**
  - Where we **direct our money, time, and energy** is where we **worship**.
  - If our treasure is in **consumerism, status, or comfort**, our worship is **idolatrous**.
  - If our treasure is in **God’s mission, the poor, and eternal things**, our worship is **true**.

---
### **Conclusion: Treasure as a Mirror and a Magnet**
- **Mirror:** Our treasure **reveals** the true state of our hearts (what we *actually* love).
- **Magnet:** Our treasure **shapes** our hearts (what we *consistently* pursue, we grow to love).
- **Warning:** Earthly treasure **corrupts** (moth, rust, theft) and **distracts** (from God’s kingdom).
- **Solution:** Heavenly treasure **endures** and **aligns** our hearts with God’s purposes.

**Final Thought:**
Jesus isn’t just warning against **wealth**—He’s warning against **misplaced love**. The issue isn’t *having* treasure, but **what our treasure says about our hearts**. If our treasure is in heaven, our **love, attention, and devotion** will follow. If it’s on earth, our hearts will be **chained to what cannot last**.
