Based on the text of Matthew 12:34–35, the passage connects speech and the heart through a **hydrological and storage metaphor**. Jesus describes the heart as a **treasure vault** and the mouth as an **overflow valve**.

The passage suggests that words are **all three (symptoms, choices, and revelations)**, though with a specific hierarchy where the heart acts as the cause and speech as the inevitable effect.

Here is a breakdown of that connection and categorization:

### 1. The Connection: Speech as Overflow
The text posits a direct causal link between the internal state of a person and their external expression.
*   **The Metaphor of Treasure:** The heart is depicted as a storehouse ("treasure") containing accumulated moral stockpiles (either good or evil).
*   **The Metaphor of Abundance:** Speech is not presented as an isolated event, but as the result of an "abundance." Just as a full cup overflows, a person filled with a specific moral character will eventually discharge it through speech.
*   **The Constraint:** Jesus asks, *"How can ye... speak good things?"* This implies that if the stockpile (heart) is evil, the output (speech) is mechanically constrained to be evil. A good product cannot be manufactured by a defective source.

### 2. The Nature of Words: Symptom, Choice, or Revelation?

The passage supports all three descriptions, but each highlights a different aspect of the relationship.

#### A. Words as Revelation
This is the most immediate function of speech in this text. The heart is hidden, but the mouth exposes it.
*   **Textual Evidence:** *"Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh."*
*   **Analysis:** Speech is the window through which the hidden interior is made visible. You cannot truly know a person's moral state without listening to what they say, because speech reveals the "treasure" they are actually hoarding. It turns the invisible heart into a known quantity.

#### B. Words as Symptoms
This follows from the revelation aspect. The speech indicates the health or sickness of the soul, much like a fever indicates an infection.
*   **Textual Evidence:** *"O generation of vipers, how can ye, being evil, speak good things?"*
*   **Analysis:** Jesus diagnoses the condition of the listeners based on their speech. The words are not the root problem; they are the *symptom* of the root problem (the "evil" heart). If the "viper-like" nature were healed, the symptoms (evil speech) would cease. Therefore, words function as evidence or clinical data pointing to a deeper condition.

#### C. Words as Choices
This is the most nuanced category. While the heart dictates the *nature* of the speech, the mouth is the instrument of action.
*   **Textual Evidence:** *"A good man... bringeth forth good things."*
*   **Analysis:** To "bring forth" implies an action of volition. However, the text argues that the *freedom* of the choice is dependent on the *state* of the heart. A person makes a choice to speak, but they are choosing from a menu defined by their heart's inventory. They can only "choose" to bring forth what is actually stored within them.
    *   If the heart is evil, the "choices" regarding speech are limited to evil outputs.
    *   If the heart is good, the "choices" are naturally inclined toward good outputs.
    *   Thus, speech is a choice, but it is a choice constrained by moral nature.

### Conclusion
In Matthew 12:34–35, speech is the **symptom** of the heart's condition, the **revelation** of the heart's contents, and the **choice** of the human agent operating within the limits of that condition.

The passage ultimately argues that you cannot separate the fruit (words) from the tree (heart). While a person chooses to speak, they cannot choose a moral quality for those words that contradicts their internal moral stockpile. Therefore, the **Heart is the primary reality, and Speech is its secondary manifestation.**
