Enemy-love is difficult to take seriously because it is sentimental, strategic, and a deeper form of moral strength. The impulse to love an enemy springs from no mere calculation of profit, but from an original strand of kindness in human nature, which has to be reckoned with, whatever the cost. Sentimentally, to love an enemy is to feel kindly towards him, in spite of his unkinness to you; strategically, it is to make a friend of him, and so turn him from an enemy into an ally; morally, it is to conquer him by goodness. To love enemies is to be Christ-like, and Christ-like in this, as in other respects, is to be divine.
