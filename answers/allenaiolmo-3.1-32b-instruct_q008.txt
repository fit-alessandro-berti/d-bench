This is a profound and classic question about 1 John 4:20 and the nature of true love.

**The Verse in Context:**
1 John 4:20 says:  
> “If a man says, ‘I love God,’ and hates his brother, he is a liar; for he who does not love his brother, whom he has seen, cannot love God, whom he has not seen.”

**Why is love for the neighbor a test of love for God?**

1. **Divine Nature is Relational and Expressed Through Love for Others**
   - In the Gospel of John and the letters of John, God’s love is always expressed through love for others. Jesus says, “By this everyone will know that you are my disciples, if you love one another” (John 13:35).
   - God, as the ultimate being, is love (1 John 4:8, 16). But love is not just an abstract feeling—it is an action, especially toward others.

2. **You Can't Truly Love What You Don't Know Through Experience**
   - God is invisible and beyond our direct sensory experience. We don’t “see” God in the way we see a person.
   - But we *do* see our neighbors. Our love for God must be demonstrated in how we treat those we can see and know.
   - If someone claims to love God but hates their neighbor, their claim is unproven, because love must show itself in concrete relationships.

3. **The Logic of the Verse**
   - If you truly loved God, your love would overflow into love for others, especially those closest to you (your neighbors).
   - The verse inverts this: if you hate your neighbor, you have not shown love at all, so your claim to love God is false.

**What kind of self-deception is this exposing?**

- **Hypocrisy and False Piety:**  
  The verse exposes people who claim spiritual devotion but do not live it out in their relationships. It calls out those who have a “private” or “exclusive” love for God that doesn’t change how they treat others.

- **Lack of Genuine Transformation:**  
  True love for God transforms a person’s character and relationships. Loving God in name only, without changed behavior toward others, is empty and self-serving.

- **Religious Performance vs. Real Love:**  
  It exposes the self-deception of someone who performs religious acts or feels spiritual emotions without allowing those to affect how they treat others. Such a person may convince themselves they love God, but their actions betray the truth.

- **The Heart’s True Allegiance:**  
  If your heart is not moved to love your neighbor, it reveals that your affection is not truly directed toward God, but perhaps toward an idea, a feeling, or even yourself.

**In summary:**
The verse treats love for the neighbor as a test of love for God because love is shown through action, and God’s love is made visible through loving others. The self-deception it exposes is the illusion that one can love God sincerely without allowing that love to manifest in tangible, relational ways toward others. It’s a call to authentic, lived faith—not just belief or feeling.
