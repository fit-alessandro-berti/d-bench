

The passage from Jeremiah 17:9‑10 expresses a pessimistic view of human nature—it portrays the heart as deceitful and desperately wicked.  

If one regards the heart as an unreliable witness, then motives attributed to it should be treated with caution: they ought not be taken at face value, but examined critically, corroborated by other evidence, and spoken about only after verifying their truthfulness through external, trustworthy sources.
