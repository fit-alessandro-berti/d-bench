Pride is often linked to blindness and collapse because when someone is blinded by their own arrogance and pride, they fail to see their own faults and weaknesses. This lack of self-awareness can lead to destructive behavior and ultimately downfall. The contrast with "the lowly" suggests that humility and self-awareness are more conducive to moral well-being and success. When someone is humble and recognizes their own limitations, they are more likely to make wise and ethical choices, rather than being consumed by their own self-importance. Ultimately, the proverb is emphasizing the virtues of humility and warning against the dangers of pride and arrogance.
