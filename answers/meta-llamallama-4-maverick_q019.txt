## Step 1: Understand the passage
The passage from James 3:9-10 highlights a contradiction where the same mouth is used to both bless God and curse men, who are made in God's image.

## Step 2: Identify the inner contradiction
The inner contradiction exposed here is the inconsistency between the act of blessing God, who is revered and honored, and cursing humans, who are created in God's image and thus deserve respect and dignity. This contradiction reveals a moral disorder.

## Step 3: Analyze why speech reveals moral disorder
Speech is a direct reflection of one's heart and intentions. What we say reveals our inner thoughts, feelings, and values. When we use the same mouth to bless God and curse humans, it indicates a lack of coherence in our values and a disorder in our moral compass.

## Step 4: Explain the immediacy of the revelation
Speech reveals moral disorder quickly because it is a spontaneous expression of our inner state. Unlike actions, which can be planned and controlled, speech is often more immediate and less filtered, thus providing a clearer insight into one's true character and moral standing.

## Step 5: Conclude the significance of the passage
The passage emphasizes the importance of being mindful of our words, as they reflect our inner being and our relationship with God and others. It calls for consistency in our speech, urging believers to honor God and respect humanity, made in God's image, through their words.

The final answer is: The inner contradiction is between blessing God and cursing humans made in God's image, and speech reveals moral disorder quickly because it is a spontaneous and direct reflection of one's heart and values.
