 In the passage from Mark 8:34-35, Jesus is calling for people to deny themselves, take up their cross, and follow him. The self being denied here is the ego or the self-centered part of an individual. This means letting go of personal desires, ambitions, and attachments, and focusing on living a life aligned with God's will and the teachings of the gospel.

Some people find this message liberating because it offers the promise of a deeper and more fulfilling life. By denying the ego and focusing on serving others and following Jesus, individuals can experience a sense of purpose, meaning, and connection to something greater than themselves.

Others find this message offensive because it challenges the notion of individual autonomy and personal fulfillment. In a culture that values personal achievement and self-gratification, the idea of denying oneself and taking up a cross can seem like a restrictive and burdensome requirement. Additionally, some may interpret this passage as promoting self-sacrifice to the point of martyrdom, which can be a daunting prospect.

Ultimately, the meaning and impact of this passage depend on one's perspective and interpretation. Some may see it as a call to live a selfless and compassionate life, while others may view it as a restrictive and burdensome demand. Regardless of one's perspective, the passage offers a powerful reminder of the importance of living a life that is aligned with one's values and beliefs, and of the potential rewards that come from putting the needs of others before one's own.
