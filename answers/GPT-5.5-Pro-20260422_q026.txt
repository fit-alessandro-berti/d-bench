Matthew 12:34–35 connects **speech** to the **heart** by using a treasury/storehouse image.

The “heart” here is not merely emotion; it is the hidden inner person: desires, loves, judgments, loyalties, resentments, convictions. Jesus pictures that inner life as holding “treasure,” either good or evil. Speech is what comes out of that storehouse. The mouth does not create the heart’s condition from nothing; it **draws from what has been accumulated within**.

So yes, the passage treats words as **symptoms, choices, and revelations**.

Words are **symptoms** because “out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh.” Speech indicates an inner moral condition. Just as fruit reveals the nature of a tree, words reveal what the heart has been feeding on, storing, and becoming. Cruel, deceitful, cynical, self-exalting, or blasphemous speech is not treated as morally weightless noise; it points to something deeper.

Words are also **choices** because Jesus says a person “bringeth forth” good or evil things. That is active language. The speaker is not merely a passive victim of inner impulses. A person selects, frames, releases, and is accountable for speech. The passage does not excuse harmful words by saying, “That is just what was in my heart.” It says the heart matters, and therefore the words matter too.

Words are **revelations** because they make the hidden visible. The “treasure” is stored inwardly, but speech brings it into the open. This is especially sharp in the context: Jesus is confronting people whose words expose their hostility to God despite religious appearances. Their speech unmasks the moral reality beneath the surface.

The passage is not saying every single word is a perfect, complete map of a person’s soul. People can misspeak, perform, flatter, conceal, or lie. But Jesus’ point is that speech patterns—especially under pressure—are deeply diagnostic. The mouth is an overflow point. What is stored in the heart eventually finds a way out.

So the connection is this: **the heart is the treasury; speech is the withdrawal.** Words reveal what has been valued, stored, and cultivated within. They are symptoms of the heart’s condition, moral choices for which one is responsible, and revelations of the hidden person.
