In **Matthew 6:1**, Jesus warns against performing good deeds publicly so that others may see and praise them. The moral danger lies not simply in being seen, but in the *motivation* behind the action — specifically, the craving for human recognition rather than sincere devotion to God.

**Visibility itself** isn’t inherently wrong; many acts of kindness are meant to benefit others and can inspire further good. However, when the primary goal becomes gaining admiration from people, it corrupts the virtue. The act loses its spiritual sincerity and becomes self-serving. In other words, the danger is in allowing the desire for approval or status to drive one’s actions instead of love for God and neighbor.

So, while visibility alone isn’t evil, the *craving to be seen* is morally dangerous because it shifts focus away from authentic righteousness toward performance for applause. This can lead to hypocrisy, pride, and a loss of true humility before God.
