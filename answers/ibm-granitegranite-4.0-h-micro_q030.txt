The passage from Corinthians 13:4-7 presents a profound and comprehensive definition of love, often referred to as "charity" in the context of Christian theology. The reasons for the inclusion of numerous negations of pride, envy, irritability, and suspicion are multifaceted and serve to underscore the essence of true love as described by the Apostle Paul.

1. **Negation of Pride and Envy**: The negation of pride and envy highlights the humility and selflessness that true love embodies. Pride can lead to a sense of superiority or entitlement, which contradicts the selfless nature of love. Envy, on the other hand, is the desire for what others have, which can breed resentment and conflict. By negating these traits, the passage emphasizes that true love is not about personal gain or superiority but about giving and sharing.

2. **Negation of Irritability and Suspicion**: The passage also negates irritability and suspicion, which are often signs of insecurity or fear. These traits can lead to a defensive and guarded attitude, which is contrary to the open-heartedness and trust that love requires. By rejecting these negative traits, the passage suggests that love is characterized by patience, understanding, and a willingness to accept others as they are, without judgment or suspicion.

3. **Vision of Strength**: The vision of strength being proposed here is not one of physical power or dominance, but rather a strength of character rooted in love. This strength is demonstrated through qualities such as patience, kindness, humility, and selflessness. It is a strength that endures through trials and tribulations, as indicated by the phrase "endureth all things." This type of strength is not easily shaken or diminished, as it is grounded in a deep, abiding love that transcends circumstances.

In essence, the portrait of love presented in this passage is a vision of strength that is rooted in humility, selflessness, and unconditional acceptance. It is a strength that is not easily provoked or diminished, as it is anchored in a deep and abiding love that seeks the well-being and truth of others. This vision of strength is a powerful counterpoint to the more aggressive and competitive forms of strength often emphasized in the world, offering a more compassionate and unifying alternative.
