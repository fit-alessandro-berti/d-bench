**Short answer: all three.** 

Jesus frames speech as the overflow of what’s already stored up inside. The “treasure” image is key — think of a vault, pantry, or stockpile. What you’ve been depositing in your heart over time is what gets spent through your mouth.

### How speech connects to “hidden moral stockpiles”

**1. The heart as a storehouse**  
"Out of the abundance of the heart" and "treasure" both use economic metaphors. In first-century terms, a treasure was accumulated wealth kept in a storeroom. Jesus is saying your heart isn’t empty. You’re constantly stocking it with something: grudges, lust, pride, envy... or humility, mercy, truth, love. 

The word for "treasure" here is *thēsauros* — same root as "thesaurus." It’s a deliberate collection, not random clutter. What you rehearse, entertain, and justify becomes inventory.

**2. Speech as overflow, not accident**  
"For out of the abundance the mouth speaketh." The Greek for "abundance" is *perisseuma* — excess, what spills over because the container is full. 

So when you’re squeezed by stress, anger, or surprise, what comes out was already in there. The pressure didn’t create it. It revealed it. A bucket of coffee spills coffee when kicked. A bucket of gasoline spills gasoline.

### Symptoms, choices, revelations?

**Symptoms**: Yes. In v34 Jesus calls the Pharisees "evil" and then says "how can ye, being evil, speak good things?" The words diagnose the condition. Just like a fever signals infection, malicious speech signals an internal moral sickness. Words are downstream of the heart. You can’t consistently speak peace from a war zone inside.

**Choices**: Also yes. "Treasure" implies depositing. You choose what you store. Proverbs 4:23 — "Guard your heart, for it is the wellspring of life." You don’t choose what spills when bumped, but you did choose what you filled the cup with. Every time you dwell on bitterness, consume cynicism, or practice gratitude, you’re making a deposit. The speech is the receipt.

**Revelations**: Definitely. V35 — "A good man out of the good treasure... bringeth forth good things." "Bringeth forth" is *ekballei* — to throw out, cast out, produce. Same word used for exorcism and eviction. The treasure doesn’t stay hidden. The heart compels disclosure. Words drag the invisible into the visible. That’s why Jesus uses this to confront the Pharisees: their words exposed hearts they claimed were righteous.

### The implication Jesus is driving at

In context, Matthew 12:33-37, Jesus is responding to Pharisees who called His exorcisms satanic. He’s saying: You can’t write off your words as "just talk." Speech isn’t morally neutral. It’s evidence. 

V36-37 hammer it home: "Every idle word... they shall give account." Because words aren’t just sounds — they’re x-rays of the stockpile.

**So the chain goes like this:**

| Hidden stockpile | → | Moment of pressure | → | Speech overflow |
| What you treasure |  | Choice to store it |  | Symptom + Revelation of choice |

Your words don’t create your heart, but they do publish it. And because you choose what you treasure, you’re responsible both for the vault and the overflow.
