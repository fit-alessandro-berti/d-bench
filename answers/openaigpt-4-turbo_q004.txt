Proverbs 3:5-6 emphasizes a profound level of trust in the Lord that extends beyond mere anti-intellectual dismissal of human reasoning or understanding. This trust is characterized by a deep faith and reliance on God's wisdom and guidance, advocating for a posture of humility rather than a rejection of intellectual engagement.

**Humble Self-Distrust**: The phrase "lean not unto thine own understanding" encapsulates the concept of humble self-distrust. Here, self-distrust does not imply a devaluation of one’s intellect or reasoning capabilities per se, but rather it encourages an acknowledgment of the limitations of human understanding in the face of divine omniscience. It is an invitation to rely not solely on one's own insights or judgments, but to trust that God’s wisdom surpasses human wisdom. This form of self-distrust is not about self-deprecation but recognizes that human perspectives are inherently limited and can be flawed, hence the need to depend on divine guidance.

**Anti-intellectualism**: Anti-intellectualism, on the other hand, would involve a dismissive attitude towards intellectual pursuits and reasoning altogether, which is not the message being conveyed here. The instruction is not to abandon reason or intellect, but to supplement human understanding with divine guidance. The emphasis is on a balance where intellect and faith coexist, where one’s reasoning abilities are used but ultimately surrendered to the greater wisdom of God.

The key distinction lies in how intellect and understanding are utilized. Humble self-distrust as portrayed in this proverb advocates for using one's intellect to seek out and understand God’s teachings and intentions, but ultimately recognizing that all understanding should be subordinate to a greater trust in God. It encourages believers to engage intellectually with their faith, to study, and to seek understanding, but also to acknowledge God’s omnipotence and ultimate authority in guiding their lives.

In essence, Proverbs 3:5-6 encourages a profound trust in God that integrates intellectual engagement with spiritual reliance, navigating life’s complexities not purely through personal insight but through divine direction. This harmonizes the intellectual and the spiritual, encouraging a holistic approach to faith and life.
