This passage exposes the **hypocrisy of using the same instrument—the tongue—to offer worship to God while inflicting harm on fellow human beings, who are themselves made in God’s image.**

The inner contradiction lies in the simultaneous affirmation of God’s worth and the denial of human dignity. If one truly honors God as Creator, that honor should extend to His image-bearers. To bless God and curse people is to split one’s spiritual allegiance, revealing a heart that has not fully grasped the implications of the God in whom it professes to believe.

As for why speech reveals moral disorder so quickly:  
1. **Speech is an immediate window into the heart.** Jesus Himself said, “Out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaks” (Matthew 12:34). Words flow almost effortlessly from our inner convictions, emotions, and values.  
2. **It exposes inconsistency between belief and practice.** The tongue often acts before the mind can censor it, revealing what truly dwells within.  
3. **It reflects our view of God and others.** If our words demean others, it suggests we have not truly internalized God’s love and respect for His creation.  
4. **The tongue holds great power—for good or evil** (James 3:5–8). Its misuse can quickly unravel relationships, communities, and even one’s own spiritual integrity.

In short, speech is a moral litmus test because it is the direct outlet of our inner world—and when what comes out contradicts what we profess to believe, it lays bare a disorder that needs healing at the root.
