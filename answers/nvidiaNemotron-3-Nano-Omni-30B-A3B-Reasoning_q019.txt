### The inner contradiction

James 3:9‑10 points to a **contradiction between two opposing spiritual realities that coexist in the same person**:

| **What is being said** | **What is implied** |
|------------------------|---------------------|
| **“Bless we God, even the Father.”** | The speaker acknowledges God’s holiness, sovereignty, and the proper reverence due to Him. |
| **“Curse we men, which are made after the similitude of God.”** | The same speaker demeans, insults, or dehumanizes other human beings, who are created in God’s image. |

The contradiction is that **the same mouth that can praise the divine also spews contempt toward the created order that reflects the divine**. The person simultaneously honors the source of all life and denigrates the very beings that display that life.

### Why speech reveals moral disorder so quickly

1. **Speech is the outward expression of the heart**  
   - In the biblical tradition, the mouth is a *window* into the inner disposition (cf. Matt 12:34‑37; Prov 18:21). What a person says is a direct overflow of what they treasure, fear, or despise inside.

2. **Words are relational, not isolated**  
   - Unlike a private thought, spoken words inevitably affect others. A curse directed at a person violates the relational covenant that God established with humanity (the *imago Dei*—the image of God). The harm is immediate and visible.

3. **The mouth is a conduit for both blessing and cursing**  
   - The same organ that can utter a benediction can also utter a slander. Because the two are *mutually exclusive* in their moral direction (blessing builds up, cursing tears down), the coexistence of both in one mouth signals a **lack of internal consistency**—a divided loyalty.

4. **Speech is the most accessible evidence of inner character**  
   - Thoughts are internal and can be hidden; actions may be selective; but the *daily* use of language is continuous. If a person’s speech alternates between reverence for God and contempt for fellow humans, the inconsistency is exposed instantly, revealing a **heart that is not unified under a single allegiance**.

5. **Theological implication**  
   - True worship of God requires **integrity**—a heart that aligns its thoughts, desires, and actions with God’s character. When the mouth betrays a split allegiance, it shows that the person’s inner life is disordered, lacking the single‑minded devotion that genuine reverence demands.

### Summarizing the answer

- **Inner contradiction:** The passage exposes a person who **simultaneously blesses God and curses those made in God’s image**, a moral inconsistency that reveals a divided heart.
- **Why speech shows moral disorder quickly:** Speech is the *external, relational, and continuous* expression of the inner self. Because words directly affect others and cannot be easily concealed, a mouth that alternates between blessing and cursing instantly reveals a lack of internal harmony and a heart that is not fully aligned with God’s holiness.
