 This passage from Jeremiah 17:9-10 is often seen as a realistic acknowledgment of human self-deception and the complexity of the human heart. The statement that "the heart is deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked" is a recognition that people can be unaware of their own motivations and can deceive themselves. The passage goes on to say that God is the one who can truly understand and judge the heart and actions of individuals.

Therefore, someone talking about motives in light of this passage might acknowledge the potential for self-deception and the importance of seeking understanding and wisdom from a higher power or trusted source. They might also encourage humility and self-reflection, recognizing that our own perceptions of our motivations may not be entirely trustworthy. Ultimately, this passage underscores the importance of recognizing the limitations of human understanding and seeking divine guidance in understanding the complexities of human nature.
