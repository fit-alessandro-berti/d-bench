The proverb in Proverbs 16:18-19 establishes a strong connection between pride and downfall, suggesting that pride not only precedes destruction but also that humility is preferable to the gains obtained through pride. Let's break down why pride is linked to blindness and collapse and what the contrast with "the lowly" reveals about the moral psychology of arrogance.

### Pride Linked to Blindness and Collapse

1. **Pride and Self-Deception**: Pride is often associated with an inflated sense of one's own importance, abilities, or achievements. When individuals become prideful, they may start to overestimate their capabilities and judgment, leading to poor decision-making. This self-deception can blind them to potential pitfalls and the advice or perspectives of others, ultimately leading to their downfall.

2. **Isolation and Lack of Accountability**: Pride can isolate individuals from others. When someone is consumed by pride, they may become less receptive to feedback, criticism, or guidance, believing they have nothing to learn from others. This isolation can prevent them from recognizing and correcting their mistakes until it's too late, contributing to their collapse.

3. **Risk-Taking Behavior**: Pride can encourage riskier behavior, as individuals may feel invincible or superior to the constraints that apply to others. This can lead to taking unwarranted risks that eventually result in failure or downfall.

### Contrast with "the Lowly"

The contrast drawn between being "of an humble spirit with the lowly" and "divid[ing] the spoil with the proud" offers insight into the moral psychology of arrogance.

1. **Humility and Association**: Associating with the "lowly" implies a willingness to identify with, learn from, and be among those considered less significant or powerful. Humility allows for openness to others' experiences and wisdom, fostering a more realistic understanding of oneself and the world. This contrast highlights that humility is not about external status but an internal disposition towards others and oneself.

2. **Moral Value of Humility**: The preference for being humble with the lowly rather than sharing in the successes or spoils gained through pride underscores the moral value placed on humility. It suggests that true honor or worth is not measured by external achievements or status achieved through pride but by the quality of one's character and relationships.

3. **Arrogance and Relational Dynamics**: The proverb critiques the relational dynamics fostered by arrogance. Being "with the proud" and "divid[ing] the spoil" might bring temporary gains or a sense of superiority, but it lacks the depth and genuine connection that comes from humility. Arrogance can lead to superficial or transactional relationships rather than meaningful ones.

### Moral Psychology of Arrogance

The contrast highlights that arrogance distorts one's perception of self and others, leading to an imbalance in moral psychology. Arrogance:
- Fosters a false sense of self-sufficiency and superiority.
- Impedes genuine relationships and learning from others.
- Can lead to overconfidence and risk-taking.

In contrast, humility:
- Encourages openness and learning.
- Fosters genuine and meaningful relationships.
- Promotes a realistic self-assessment and adaptability.

In summary, pride is linked to blindness and collapse because it can lead to self-deception, isolation, and unwarranted risk-taking. The contrast with "the lowly" in Proverbs 16:18-19 suggests that arrogance distorts one's moral psychology by fostering a false sense of superiority, impeding genuine relationships, and leading to overconfidence. Conversely, humility is associated with openness, meaningful connections, and a more realistic understanding of oneself and the world.
