This text does not say treasure **reveals** the heart; it says treasure **determines** or **shapes** the heart. The direction of influence is formative: your treasure trains your heart.

### 1. Treasure trains the heart (formation, not mere revelation)
The key verb is **“where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”**  
This is less a diagnostic statement (“your heart shows what you treasure”) and more a generative one (“where you invest your treasure, your heart will follow”).  

- **Treasure as orientation**: The heart is not drawn first and then treasure placed accordingly; rather, the placement of treasure pulls the heart toward it.  
- **Training metaphor**: Like training an animal or cultivating a habit, repeated investment (treasure) conditions the heart’s desires and attention. Possession here is not neutral—it cultivates attachment, focus, and ultimately, love.

---

### 2. Possession, attention, and love tied together
The passage links possession, attention, and love in a single causal chain:

1. **Possession (treasure)**: What you store up, accumulate, or prioritize.  
2. **Attention (heart’s orientation)**: Where your heart is “also” — meaning it follows the treasure. Attention is not a separate faculty but the direction of your inner life toward the treasured object.  
3. **Love (ultimate relationship)**: In biblical language, “heart” is the center of willing, desiring, and loving. So if your treasure trains your attention, it is precisely your love that is being shaped. You love what you treasure.

Thus:  
- **Earthly treasure** → trains attention to material security, comfort, status → heart becomes anxious, grasping, distracted.  
- **Heavenly treasure** → trains attention to God, righteousness, mercy → heart becomes aligned with divine purposes.

---

### 3. The mechanism: corruption and theft as metaphors for futility
The contrast between “moth and rust” and “thieves” isn’t just about loss; it’s about **misplaced confidence**.  
- Possession of earthly treasure binds attention to what is fragile and insecure.  
- This shapes a heart that loves what cannot ultimately satisfy, leading to a misalignment of desire.  
- Conversely, treasuring heavenly things trains a heart that loves what is enduring, secure, and life-giving.

---

### Summary
- **Not “treasure reveals heart”** (which would imply heart is prior), but **“treasure trains heart”** (heart is formed by treasure).  
- **Possession** (what you store) directs **attention** (where your heart dwells), which defines **love** (what you most desire and value).  
- The text thus integrates material reality (treasure) with inner transformation (heart), showing that our investments shape our deepest loves and loyalties.
