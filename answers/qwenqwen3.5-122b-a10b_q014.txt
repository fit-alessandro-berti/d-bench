This passage presents a profound psychological and spiritual principle. To answer your question directly: **The text asserts that treasure both reveals the heart and trains the heart.** It describes a law of the human soul where the two are inextricably linked.

Here is an analysis of how the text functions regarding revelation, training, and the connection of possession, attention, and love.

### 1. Reveals or Trains?
The verse functions as both a diagnostic and a prescription.

**As Revelation (Diagnostic):**
The statement, *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also,"* acts as an observation of human nature. If you look at where a person invests their resources (time, money, energy), you can accurately diagnose where their affections and security lie. In this sense, treasure is the evidence of the heart's current allegiance. It reveals what you truly trust.

**As Training (Prescriptive):**
However, the context is imperative. Jesus is giving commands: *"Lay not up..."* and *"Lay up..."* He is instructing his listeners to *move* their treasure. If the heart naturally follows the treasure, then by intentionally choosing where to store treasure, you are actively directing the heart.
*   If you accumulate earthly goods, your heart will be trained toward anxiety, security in material things, and self-reliance.
*   If you accumulate heavenly goods (generosity, obedience, spiritual investment), your heart will be trained toward God, eternal perspective, and trust.

Therefore, while the treasure reveals where the heart *has been*, the command to move the treasure is a method for training where the heart *will go*.

### 2. The Connection: Possession, Attention, and Love
In biblical anthropology, the "heart" (*kardia*) is not merely the seat of emotions, but the center of the will, intellect, and moral character. It is the control room of the person. The text ties possession, attention, and love together through a gravitational pull.

**Possession (The Anchor)**
"Treasure" represents what we possess and what we feel possesses us. Possession is not neutral; it requires maintenance. You cannot have a treasure without guarding it.
*   **Earthly Treasure:** Requires guarding against "moth, rust, and thieves." This creates a mindset of defensiveness and scarcity.
*   **Heavenly Treasure:** Requires no guarding. This creates a mindset of security and abundance.

**Attention (The Focus)**
Because treasure requires guarding, it demands **attention**. You cannot ignore what you value.
*   If your treasure is on earth, your attention is constantly drawn to the volatility of the world (Will I lose my job? Will my house burn down? Is my reputation safe?).
*   If your treasure is in heaven, your attention is drawn to the "unseen" realities of God's kingdom.
*   The text implies that **attention follows value.** We do not watch what we do not care about; we watch what we own.

**Love (The Affection)**
In the ancient worldview, love was not just a feeling but a loyalty. To love something is to place your trust in it.
*   When you lay up treasure, you are declaring what you love.
*   The "heart" is the locus of this love.
*   The text suggests that **love is the fuel that moves the heart.** You cannot simply "decide" to love God while your treasure is entirely in the world, because your heart (your loyalty) is already anchored to the world.

### Summary: The Law of Alignment
Matthew 6:21 describes a spiritual law of gravity. **Treasure creates a gravitational pull on the heart.**

*   **Possession** captures the **Attention** (you watch over what you own).
*   **Attention** cultivates **Love** (you care for what you watch).
*   **Love** dictates the **Heart** (your center of gravity moves toward what you love).

Therefore, Jesus is not merely telling people to be charitable; He is telling them that **you cannot manage your heart without managing your treasure.** If you want your heart to be in heaven, you must move your treasure there first. The treasure trains the heart by providing the object upon which the heart rests.
