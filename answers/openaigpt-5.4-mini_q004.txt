The trust in Proverbs 3:5–6 is not a call to stop thinking. It is a call to stop **treating your own judgment as ultimate**.

### What kind of trust?
It is:

- **Personal trust**: not merely agreeing that God exists, but relying on him.
- **Wholehearted trust**: “with all thine heart” means undivided confidence, not partial dependence.
- **Practical trust**: “in all thy ways acknowledge him” means bringing God into the actual decisions of life.
- **Covenantal trust**: the wisdom literature assumes a relationship in which God is dependable, good, and worthy of obedience.

So the core idea is: **depend on God rather than on yourself as the final authority**.

### Humble self-distrust
This proverb does call for a kind of self-distrust—but a humble one:

- I know my judgment is limited.
- I know my motives can be mixed.
- I know I can be blind to my own biases.
- I know I need correction, counsel, and revelation.

That is not despair or passivity. It is epistemic humility: a recognition that finite, fallen human understanding is not sufficient to navigate life on its own.

### Not anti-intellectualism
This verse is **not** saying:

- “Don’t reason.”
- “Don’t study.”
- “Don’t ask questions.”
- “Don’t use evidence.”
- “Faith means believing without thought.”

In fact, Proverbs as a whole praises wisdom, discernment, instruction, and understanding. The book assumes that people should **think carefully**. The issue is not intelligence; it is **self-reliance**.

Anti-intellectualism rejects thinking. Proverbs rejects **thinking autonomously**, as if human reason were self-grounding and sufficient apart from God.

### The distinction in one sentence
- **Humble self-distrust** says: “My understanding is useful but limited, so I submit it to God.”
- **Anti-intellectualism** says: “Understanding is bad, so I won’t use it.”

Proverbs clearly teaches the first, not the second.

### A helpful summary
This passage asks for a trust that is:
- **deep**, not shallow;
- **dependent**, not self-sufficient;
- **reason-informed**, not reasonless;
- **God-centered**, not mindless.
