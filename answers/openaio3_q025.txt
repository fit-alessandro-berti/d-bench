What Jesus warns against is not the mere fact that other people happen to notice your good deed, but the impulse to do the deed for the sake of being noticed.

1. What the Greek actually says  
   προσέχετε… μὴ ποιεῖν… ἔμπροσθεν τῶν ἀνθρώπων **πρὸς τὸ θεαθῆναι αὐτοῖς**  
   “Beware … of doing … before people **in order to be seen by them**.”  
   The preposition πρὸς with the infinitive expresses purpose.  Visibility is only a problem when it is the purpose.

2. The immediate context (6:2-18)  
   • Giving, praying, and fasting are all treated alike: “do not … as the hypocrites … so that they may be seen by men.”  
   • Each time Jesus adds, “they have their reward in full” – the applause they wanted – “but your Father who sees in secret will reward you.”

3. The wider context in Matthew  
   Two chapters earlier Jesus had said, “Let your light shine before men, that they may see your good works and glorify your Father” (5:16).  Clearly He is not forbidding any public knowledge of good works; He is forbidding the quest for personal glory that can accompany them.

4. Why the craving to be seen is morally dangerous  
   a. It shifts the centre of the act from God and the neighbour to the self (pride).  
   b. It turns righteousness into performance (hypocrisy—“play-acting”).  
   c. It trivialises the deed, making human praise the only “reward” available.  
   d. It trains the heart to depend on human approval instead of God’s approval (cf. John 12:43).

5. Why secrecy is recommended  
   Performing the deed where no audience is possible is a practical safeguard: it removes the temptation to act for show and keeps motivation pure.  But secrecy is a means, not the moral end; the end is a God-centred motive.

Answer  
Public goodness becomes morally dangerous when the doer longs for the public’s praise. The verse condemns the craving to be seen, not simple visibility. A deed that is observed but done to honour God remains good; a deed done to attract applause, even if outwardly identical, has already received its only reward and gains none from “your Father who is in heaven.”
