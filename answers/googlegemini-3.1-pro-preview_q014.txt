In Matthew 6:19–21, Jesus is articulating a profound truth about human psychology and spiritual formation. To answer your first question directly: **while the text certainly implies that treasure *reveals* the heart, its primary and most radical point is that treasure *trains* the heart.** 

Here is a breakdown of how this works and how possession, attention, and love become inextricably tied together in this teaching.

### 1. Treasure Reveals vs. Treasure Trains

**The Diagnostic View (Revealing the Heart)**
We often read verse 21 backwards. We tend to think Jesus is saying, "Where your heart is, your treasure will follow." In this common view, our spending habits are simply a diagnostic tool: look at your bank statement or your calendar, and it will *reveal* what you already love. This is true—our investments do reveal our current priorities. 

**The Formative View (Training the Heart)**
However, the actual grammar and logic of Jesus’ statement flow in the opposite direction: *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* 

Jesus places the treasure *first* and the heart *second*. The heart is depicted not as the leader, but as the follower. Therefore, Jesus is giving a strategic command for spiritual formation: **Treasure acts as a magnet for the heart.** 

Because verses 19 and 20 are active commands ("Lay not up..." and "But lay up..."), Jesus is telling His listeners how to intentionally relocate their affections. He is essentially saying: *If you want to love the Kingdom of God, put your money, time, and resources there. Your heart will inevitably migrate to the place where your investments are housed.* In this sense, what you do with your earthly treasure is a tool used to **train** your heart to love heavenly things.

### 2. The Fusion of Possession, Attention, and Love

Jesus binds possession, attention, and love together through a psychological and spiritual chain reaction. 

**Possession demands Attention**
Jesus subtly introduces the concept of attention by mentioning "moth," "rust," and "thieves." When you possess earthly treasures, you must pay attention to them. You must protect them, insure them, clean them, and guard them. 
*   If you buy a new car, you suddenly pay attention to where you park. 
*   If you invest heavily in the stock market, you suddenly care about daily financial news. 

Your possessions dictate the focus of your mental energy. If your possessions are entirely earthly, your attention is consumed by the anxiety of decay and loss. 

**Attention captures Love**
Whatever chronically holds your attention will eventually capture your heart. In biblical terms, the "heart" is the center of human will, desire, and allegiance (love). You cannot love what you do not pay attention to. Because earthly possessions demand constant maintenance and protection, they monopolize your attention, and thereby tether your love to the temporary, physical world.

**The Heavenly Alternative**
By contrast, Jesus says that heavenly possessions are secure from moths, rust, and thieves. When you "possess" heavenly treasure—by giving to the poor, advancing justice, practicing radical generosity, or serving others—you no longer have to worry about protecting it. God guarantees the ROI (Return on Investment). 

Because your "possessions" are now in heaven, your **attention** turns toward heaven. You become deeply invested in the things of God. And as your attention focuses steadily on eternal things, your **love** (your heart) naturally takes root there.

### Summary
In these three verses, Jesus brilliantly intertwines economics, psychology, and spirituality. He recognizes that human beings are fundamentally shaped by what they possess. Therefore, He doesn't just say, "Try harder to love God." Instead, He offers a practical mechanism: move your assets. 

Your **possessions** dictate your **attention** (what you must protect and focus on), and your attention directs your **love** (your heart). By consciously choosing to "lay up" treasure in heaven, you are not merely doing a good deed; you are actively training your heart to love the eternal over the temporary.
