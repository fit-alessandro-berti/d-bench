 The problem in this verse is not the act of doing good deeds in public per se, but rather the motivation behind doing them publicly. The danger lies in the craving to be seen by others and the potential for self-righteousness or seeking recognition and praise from others, rather than doing good out of a pure heart and desire to help others or please God. The verse suggests that if one's primary goal is to be seen by others, then they may not be truly acting out of love and compassion for others or a desire to please God, and therefore may not receive the reward in heaven.
