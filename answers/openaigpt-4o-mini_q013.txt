The passage from Amos 5:21–24 serves as a powerful critique of religious practices that are disconnected from genuine moral and ethical living. The sharp rejection of worship, rather than a call for better balance, underscores the notion that rituals and ceremonies are meaningless if they are not accompanied by authentic justice and righteousness. 

In this context, the moral or spiritual fraud being exposed is the hypocrisy of those who perform religious acts while simultaneously engaging in injustice and oppression. The Israelites may have been diligently observing festivals and making offerings, but their actions did not reflect a heart committed to justice, compassion, or ethical behavior. This contradiction indicates that they believed mere ritualistic observance could substitute for a true relationship with God and accountability to one another.

The passage suggests that God prioritizes justice and righteousness over empty religious expressions. Worship without a commitment to social justice and ethical living is viewed as insincere and repugnant to God. The call for "judgment" to "run down as waters" and "righteousness" to flow "as a mighty stream" emphasizes the need for justice to be pervasive and crucial to the community, rather than a mere afterthought to worship.

This critique reveals that authentic faith is not just about religious observance but about embodying the values of justice and righteousness in everyday life. The rejection of the worship being offered indicates that rituals cannot absolve individuals or communities from their responsibility to act justly and uphold righteousness in their relationships with others.
