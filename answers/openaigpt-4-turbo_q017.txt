In John 3:19-21, the metaphor of light and darkness is used to discuss the nature of human actions and moral choices. The "light" in biblical terms often represents truth, purity, divine revelation, and the presence of God, specifically embodied in Jesus Christ. In contrast, "darkness" symbolizes ignorance, sin, and moral corruption.

### Why Would Light Be Hated?

1. **Exposure of Evil:**
   People who engage in morally wrong or evil deeds may fear the light because it represents exposure. Light illuminates what is hidden in darkness, and in this context, it reveals actions or intentions that are unrighteous or sinful. Just as a bright light can be harsh and reveal every flaw, the spiritual light exposes all imperfections and sins.

2. **Fear of Judgement:**
   Coming into the light not only exposes one's actions but also opens them up to judgment. Those who do evil are aware, at some level, of the wrongness of their actions and thus fear judgement. The light metaphorically represents divine judgment, where actions are not only made visible but are also assessed against moral and spiritual standards.

3. **Resistance to Change:**
   Often, individuals may hate the light because it necessitates a change in lifestyle, behavior, and perhaps even in fundamental beliefs. Acknowledging the truth about one’s actions can mean a need to abandon certain behaviors and make reparative actions, which can be uncomfortable or challenging.

### Relation to Truth, Exposure, Shame, and the Desire Not to Be Seen Clearly

- **Truth:** In this passage, truth is like light—it is illuminating, revealing, and unyielding. It forces honesty and clarity where there may be a desire to keep aspects of oneself hidden. For those engaged in unethical, immoral, or sinful actions, truth threatens to expose them.

- **Exposure:** Exposure is a direct consequence of coming into the light. It strips away the veils that one might hide behind. This exposure is crucial for accountability but can be intensely feared because it leaves one vulnerable to scrutiny and the consequences of their actions.

- **Shame:** Shame is closely connected to this process of exposure. It is a powerful emotion that encompasses feelings of disgrace and dishonor when one's shortcomings or misdeeds are revealed. For many, avoiding shame is a strong motivator to shun the light and continue hiding in the darkness.

- **Desire Not to Be Seen Clearly:** This desire stems from a fear of judgment, a reluctance to admit fault, and a resistance to the implications of what it means to be exposed. There’s comfort in obscurity where one's flaws or wrongdoings might go unnoticed or unaddressed.
  
Overall, in John 3:19-21, the aversion to light (truth) is portrayed as a natural reaction by those afraid of what it reveals and implies about them. In spiritual or moral contexts, embracing the light means a willingness to be honest and transparent, to undergo scrutiny, and to strive for betterment in alignment with divine expectations, which can be a formidable journey for many.
