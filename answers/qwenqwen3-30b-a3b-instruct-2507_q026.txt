Matthew 12:34–35 presents a powerful and enduring insight into the relationship between speech and the inner moral life. Jesus uses vivid language—"generation of vipers," "evil," "good treasure," and "abundance of the heart"—to emphasize that words are not isolated actions but deeply rooted expressions of the soul.

### Connection Between Speech and Hidden Moral Stockpiles in the Heart:
The passage connects speech directly to the moral condition of the heart. Jesus does not treat speech as arbitrary or merely surface-level; rather, He asserts that **what we say reveals what we truly are at the core**. The phrase *"out of the abundance of the heart the mouth speaketh"* (v. 34) means that our words flow from an internal reservoir—what we treasure, value, believe, and desire in our inner being. This "heart" is not just emotion but the center of moral, spiritual, and intellectual orientation (cf. Proverbs 4:23).

Thus, when Jesus says a person "being evil" cannot speak good things, He affirms that evil speech cannot spring from a truly good source. Likewise, a good person naturally produces good speech because their inner life—rooted in virtue, faith, and love—provides the necessary "treasure" to draw from.

### Is Speech a Symptom, Choice, Revelation, or All Three?
The passage suggests **all three**—speech is simultaneously a *symptom*, a *choice*, and a *revelation*:

1. **Symptom** – Speech reflects the inner state. Just as a tree is known by its fruit (Matt 7:17–18), words are symptoms of the heart's condition. When people speak maliciously or arrogantly, it's not accidental—it is *evidence* of a corrupted inner world. The words are the outward sign of an inward sickness or health.

2. **Revelation** – Words expose the heart. Jesus is not saying people are merely *influenced* by their hearts, but that their speech *reveals* what is hidden. The "treasure" in the heart is not always obvious, but when someone speaks, their true moral disposition is disclosed. Speech becomes a diagnostic tool: it shows whether the inner life is aligned with goodness or evil.

3. **Choice** – Although rooted in the heart, speech is not automatic; it is an expression of what is freely drawn from the inner store. A person chooses what to say, and those choices reflect their internal priorities. While the heart governs, the mouth *selects* from the "abundance" stored within. Hence, a good person *chooses* to speak well because their heart is full of good things; an evil person *chooses* to speak ill because that's what their heart provides.

### Conclusion:
Matthew 12:34–35 teaches that **words are not random or independent, but the fruit of the soul’s moral treasure**. They are symptoms of the inner condition, revelations of the hidden self, and expressions of conscious choice. This verse calls for self-examination: if we are constantly speaking bitterness, gossip, or dishonesty, we must ask—*What is in my heart?* And conversely, if our speech is kind, truthful, and life-giving, we can give thanks—*the good treasure within is flowing freely*.

In essence: **Your words are not just what you say—they are who you are.**
