This passage from Matthew 12:34–35 draws a direct connection between what is in a person’s heart—the "treasure" or moral stockpile—and what that person speaks. Let’s break down the elements to understand the connection and how speech relates to the heart:

1. **Connection Between Heart and Speech:**
   - Jesus says the mouth speaks from the "abundance of the heart," meaning whatever is stored deeply inside—whether good or evil—will overflow into speech.
   - The heart here represents the inner moral and spiritual condition, the core of a person’s character.
   - Speech is thus an **expression or product** emanating naturally from what the heart contains.

2. **Speech as a Revelation:**
   - Words reveal the true nature of a person.
   - Speech acts like a window into the hidden contents of one’s heart, making internal character visible externally.
   - Since people can’t always conceal what is truly inside them, their words often unintentionally disclose their real intentions, values, and moral condition.

3. **Speech as a Symptom:**
   - Just like symptoms indicate the presence of an illness, words indicate the moral state.
   - If the heart is evil, words will be evil; if good, words will be good.
   - Speech here is like an indicator or signpost of an underlying issue or virtue.

4. **Speech as a Choice:**
   - Though the passage emphasizes natural outflow, it does not exclude human responsibility.
   - People choose what they store in their hearts through their beliefs, thoughts, and actions.
   - Likewise, they choose how to express or withhold certain words.
   - Therefore, words are not random but chosen responses, based on the moral stockpile.

### Synthesizing these points:
The passage suggests that words are **all three**: symptoms, choices, and revelations.

- **Symptoms:** Speech acts as an outward sign of what is within.
- **Choices:** People have responsibility for the moral content of their hearts and their speech.
- **Revelations:** Speech exposes internal realities that might otherwise remain hidden.

**In summary:** The passage teaches that genuine goodness or evil in speech is rooted in the heart’s moral stockpiles. Words are both a natural product revealing inner character and a responsible choice that reflects that character to others.
