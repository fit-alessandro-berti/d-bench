The verse from 1 John 4:20 is a profound reflection on the nature of love and its relationship to God. The author of 1 John is emphasizing that genuine love for God is demonstrated through love for one's fellow human beings. Here's a breakdown of the reasoning behind this statement and the self-deception it exposes:

1. **Love as a Test of Authenticity**: The verse suggests that love for God is not merely an abstract belief or intellectual assent, but a lived experience that manifests in our actions towards others. The author uses the phrase "he that loveth not his brother" to highlight that if one claims to love God but harbors hatred or indifference towards their fellow human beings, this claim is false. This is because love for God and love for others are intrinsically linked in the Christian faith. The love for God is expressed through love for others, as it is through our interactions with others that we reflect the love of God to the world.

2. **Visible Neighbor as a Reflection of Invisible God**: The "visible neighbor" refers to fellow human beings, who are created in the image of God. By extension, loving one's neighbor is a reflection of loving God, since God is the source of all love. The verse implies that if one cannot love the visible neighbor, they cannot truly love the invisible God. This is because the love for God is not just a personal relationship but a relational one that extends to all of humanity. The visible neighbor is a tangible manifestation of the invisible God, and loving them is a way of loving God.

3. **Self-Deception**: The verse exposes a form of self-deception where individuals may claim to love God while simultaneously harboring negative feelings towards others. This is a form of self-deception because it involves a disconnect between one's professed beliefs and their actual behavior. The author is warning against this kind of hypocrisy, where one's actions do not align with their stated beliefs. The verse is a call to introspection and genuine self-evaluation, urging believers to ensure that their love for God is reflected in their love for others.

4. **The Nature of Love**: The verse also touches on the nature of love itself. True love is characterized by selflessness and a willingness to put the needs of others before one's own. It is not merely an emotion but an action. When one loves God, they are called to embody that love in their relationships with others. The verse suggests that if one cannot extend this love to their neighbor, they are not truly loving God. This is a profound statement about the transformative power of love and the importance of living out one's faith in concrete ways.

In summary, 1 John 4:20 treats love of the visible neighbor as a test of love of the invisible God because genuine love for God is expressed through love for others. The verse exposes the self-deception that occurs when individuals claim to love God but fail to love their fellow human beings. It calls believers to live out their faith authentically, ensuring that their love for God is reflected in their love for others.
