The wording pushes in a specific direction:

> “For **where your treasure is, there will your heart be also**.”

Grammatically and logically, Jesus puts *treasure first, heart second*. He does not say “where your heart is, there your treasure will be,” but the reverse. That suggests:

- Your heart **follows** your treasure.  
- What you invest in doesn’t only *reflect* what you care about; it also *shapes* what you come to care about.

So:

- **Yes, treasure reveals the heart** – you can look at your spending, saving, and sacrificing and “read” your priorities.
- But in this text, the stress falls on **treasure training the heart** – where you deliberately place your treasure becomes where your heart will migrate over time.

This fits the immediate context:

- 6:19–21: Treasures on earth vs. treasures in heaven.  
- 6:22–23: The “eye” is the lamp of the body – your *gaze/attention* determines inner light or darkness.  
- 6:24: You cannot serve God and mammon – you will end up *loving* one and *hating* the other.

Those three pieces line up:

1. **Possession (treasure)** – what you store, protect, and invest in.
2. **Attention (eye)** – what you watch, think about, plan around, and look toward.
3. **Love (service)** – what you finally trust, obey, and attach yourself to as master.

The movement is:

> What you *possess* and invest in draws your *attention*;  
> what dominates your attention captures your *love*;  
> what you love becomes your *master*.

So in practice:

- If you keep laying up treasure “on earth” (security in money, status, things), your attention is drawn there, your emotional life circles around it, and your love/loyalty shifts toward it.
- If you intentionally lay up “treasure in heaven” (acts of generosity, hidden obedience, sacrificial love that God values), your attention is pulled Godward, and your love follows.

In other words, Jesus is not only describing a diagnostic (“your treasure shows your heart”) but prescribing a discipline: **choose where to put your treasure, because that choice will aim and train your heart.**
