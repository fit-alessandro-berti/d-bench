The passage from Jeremiah 17:9-10 presents a complex view of human nature. It does not merely present a pessimistic view of human nature, but rather a realistic one. The verse acknowledges the inherent deceitfulness and wickedness of the human heart, which is a sobering reminder of our own limitations and the need for divine guidance.

The verse does not deny the existence of good in humans, but it does highlight the unreliability of the heart as a source of truth about one's own motives. This is not a pessimistic view, but a realistic one. It reminds us that we are all prone to self-deception and that we need divine help to understand and act according to our true selves.

As for how to talk about motives when the heart is an unreliable witness, the passage suggests a few things. First, it underscores the importance of seeking divine wisdom and guidance. Jeremiah 17:9-10 is a call to turn to God for understanding, not a condemnation of human nature. 

Second, it suggests that we should be cautious about judging others based on outward appearances or self-deceived motives. We should strive for humility and recognize our own limitations. 

Finally, it emphasizes the importance of repentance and transformation. Jeremiah 17:9-10 is a call to turn away from self-deception and towards a life that is aligned with God's will. This involves acknowledging our faults, seeking forgiveness, and striving to live according to God's principles. 

In conclusion, Jeremiah 17:9-10 presents a realistic view of human nature, acknowledging our propensity for self-deception. However, it also offers hope and guidance, reminding us of the need for divine wisdom, humility, and repentance.
