According to this verse, the problem is not visibility itself, but the craving to be seen. The phrase "to be seen of them" suggests that the motivation behind doing good deeds publicly is to gain recognition, approval, or admiration from others. This craving for external validation can lead to a focus on self-promotion rather than genuine concern for others or a desire to serve God.

In other words, the danger lies not in the fact that others can see our good deeds, but in the fact that we are doing them with the intention of being seen and praised by others. This can lead to a form of self-aggrandizement, where our actions are motivated by a desire for human recognition rather than a genuine desire to serve and honor God.

This interpretation is reinforced by the phrase "otherwise ye have no reward of your Father which is in heaven," which suggests that when our motivation is to be seen by others, we forfeit the true reward that comes from serving God with a humble and sincere heart.
