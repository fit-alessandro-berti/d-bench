Amos 5:21–24 rejects worship with such sharpness not because God opposes worship in principle, but because the people’s worship had become a **spiritual and moral fraud**—a hollow performance that masked systemic injustice.  

The prophet’s language—“I hate, I despise”—is meant to shock the hearers out of their complacency. They likely believed that carrying out the external rituals of feasts, offerings, and music was sufficient to maintain their covenant relationship with God. But Amos reveals that their worship was not just inadequate; it was **actively detestable** to God because it was disconnected from ethical living.

The moral fraud exposed is **using religious ritual as a cover for oppression**. In the broader context of Amos, the wealthy and powerful in Israel were exploiting the poor, perverting justice in the courts, and living in luxury gained through injustice (Amos 2:6–8; 4:1; 5:10–12). Their worship was a form of hypocrisy: they honored God with ceremonies while breaking covenant with their neighbors daily.

God does not call here for a “better balance” between ritual and justice because the situation was beyond balance. The people’s hearts were so hardened that their worship had become an **offensive substitute** for obedience, not a complement to it. They imagined God could be appeased by sacrifices while they ignored the core demands of covenant loyalty: justice (*mishpat*) and righteousness (*tsedaqah*).  

In verse 24, Amos provides the positive counterpart: what God truly desires is for justice and righteousness to flow ceaselessly and powerfully, like a perennial stream in a desert land. This metaphor implies that true worship is not just singing and sacrifices but living in a society where fairness and moral integrity are as abundant and life-giving as water.  

Thus, the passage confronts any religion that uses pious activities to avoid prophetic self-examination and social responsibility. It is a timeless warning against reducing faith to ritual while tolerating inequality, corruption, or indifference to human suffering.
