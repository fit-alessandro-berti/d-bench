This is an excellent and penetrating question that cuts to the heart of biblical theology. The contrast between "external compliance" and receiving a "new heart" is the difference between religion and redemption, between the Old Covenant and the New Covenant.

Let's break down the passage and your specific questions.

### 1. The Difference: External Compliance vs. A New Heart

The difference is fundamentally about **source, motive, and power.**

**External Compliance (The Old Covenant Problem)**
- **Source:** Human effort and willpower. It is an attempt to conform to a standard from the *outside in*.
- **Motive:** Often driven by fear of punishment, desire for reward, social pressure, or a sense of duty. It can be cold, calculated, and contractual ("I obey so God blesses me").
- **Power:** Relies on the "flesh"—human nature that is inherently weak and inclined toward sin (Romans 8:7-8). It is a constant battle against one's own desires. This leads to either:
    - **Hypocrisy:** Outwardly following rules while inwardly resenting them or finding loopholes.
    - **Despair:** Constant failure and the crushing weight of guilt.
    - **Self-Righteousness:** A prideful belief that one's own obedience is good enough to earn God's favor.
- **Result:** It is a form of godliness but denies its power (2 Timothy 3:5). It can control behavior in a limited way but cannot change the heart's core affections.

**Receiving a "New Heart" (The New Covenant Promise)**
- **Source:** A divine, sovereign act of God. It is a transformation from the *inside out*. God *gives* it; we do not achieve it.
- **Motive:** Love for God, delight in His goodness, and a genuine desire for holiness. Obedience flows from a relationship, not a contract. The person *wants* to walk in God's statutes.
- **Power:** Relies on the "Spirit"—God's own life and power dwelling within the believer (Ezekiel 36:27). The Spirit doesn't just help you try harder; He *causes you* to walk, giving you the desire (the new heart) and the ability (the new spirit/Spirit).
- **Result:** Genuine, lasting, and joyful obedience. It's not perfect in this life, but it is *real*. There is a new nature that loves the law of God (Psalm 119:97), and when sin occurs, there is genuine repentance, not just regret for getting caught.

**In short:** External compliance is *behavior modification*. A new heart is *nature transformation*.

### 2. Why is Hardness Described as a Material Condition?

This is a brilliant observation. The phrase "stony heart" is not just a poetic metaphor for stubbornness. The text presents it as a **literal, diagnosable condition of the human soul** that requires a radical, surgical intervention.

Here are the key reasons for this material language:

- **To Emphasize Total Inability:** A stone is inert, cold, unyielding, and lifeless. It cannot feel, respond, or change itself. This powerfully illustrates the biblical doctrine of total depravity—not that humans are as evil as they could be, but that sin has so corrupted our nature that we are spiritually *dead* and *unable* to respond to God on our own (Ephesians 2:1). You cannot scold, teach, or motivate a stone to feel. It must be *replaced*.

- **To Contrast with the New Creation:** God doesn't just "soften" the stone. He removes it and gives a heart of "flesh." This isn't repair; it's **re-creation**. The "heart of flesh" is alive, sensitive, tender, and responsive to God. It can be moved by His love, convicted by His truth, and filled with His joy. The material language ("stone" → "flesh") shows the radical, ontological change that must occur.

- **To Foreshadow the New Testament Teaching:**
    - **John 3:3:** "You must be born *again*." This is not an improvement of the old life; it is a new birth, a new origin.
    - **2 Corinthians 5:17:** "If anyone is in Christ, he is a *new creation*. The old has passed away; behold, the new has come."
    - **Ezekiel's vision of the Valley of Dry Bones (Ezekiel 37):** The bones are not revived; they are given new flesh, sinews, and breath. It's a resurrection from the dead.

- **To Show God as the Sole Agent of Salvation:** A stone cannot turn itself into flesh. This language leaves no room for human boasting. God is the one who acts: *"I will give... I will take away... I will put..."* Salvation is a monergistic work of God—He is the sole effective agent.

### Summary for Your Question

**The difference** is between a person who obeys God's rules because they have to (external compliance) and a person who obeys God's rules because they have a new, God-given nature that *wants* to (a new heart). The first is a slave; the second is a son or daughter.

**The hardness is material** because the problem is not just a lack of information or bad habits. It is a fundamental spiritual deadness. The stony heart is the condition of being "dead in trespasses and sins" (Ephesians 2:1). It cannot be reformed or educated; it must be *replaced* by the creative, resurrection power of God. This is the miracle of the New Covenant, fulfilled in Jesus Christ and applied by the Holy Spirit.
