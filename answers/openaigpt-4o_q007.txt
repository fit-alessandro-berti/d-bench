The verse from Luke 6:37 addresses the attitudes and behaviors of judgment and condemnation towards others. When it suggests "Judge not, and ye shall not be judged," and "condemn not, and ye shall not be condemned," it is not implying that moral judgments are irrelevant or that individuals should abandon discernment altogether. Instead, it speaks to the manner and spirit in which we make those judgments.

### Discernment vs. Condemnation:

1. **Discernment:**
   - **Definition:** Discernment involves the ability to perceive, understand, and make balanced decisions or judgments based on wisdom and insight.
   - **Purpose:** It serves as a tool for understanding right from wrong, making ethical decisions, and navigating complex moral and social situations.
   - **Approach:** Discernment is characterized by empathy, seeking understanding, and contemplating the greater context, motivations, and circumstances.

2. **Appetite to Condemn:**
   - **Definition:** Condemnation involves expressing strong disapproval or passing severe judgment, often without an effort to understand or empathize.
   - **Purpose:** Condemnation can stem from a desire to assert moral superiority, control, or retaliate, rather than to foster growth or reconciliation.
   - **Approach:** It is typically characterized by harshness, lack of compassion, and an unwillingness to forgive or offer grace.

### Difference from Moral Judgments Never Matter:

- **Role of Context:** This verse doesn’t deny the importance or need for moral judgment in guiding actions but calls for humility and caution to avoid a self-righteous or punitive mindset.
- **Empathy and Compassion:** Jesus' message encourages people to employ empathy, understanding, and mercy in their interactions, reflecting a compassionate approach rather than an absolute prohibition on moral evaluation.
- **Forgiveness:** The emphasis on forgiveness ("forgive, and ye shall be forgiven") suggests a focus on healing and restoring relationships rather than fostering division and resentment.
- **Self-Awareness:** It also highlights the need for self-awareness and introspection—acknowledging one's own flaws and limitations when assessing others to avoid hypocrisy.

Therefore, the verse advocates for a balanced use of discernment enveloped in love and understanding, avoiding the intolerance and harshness associated with condemnation.
