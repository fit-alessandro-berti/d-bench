Matthew 6:19–21 presents a profound teaching on the relationship between one’s treasures (whether material possessions, investments, time, energy, etc.) and the condition of the heart. The passage can be interpreted as speaking to both the revelatory and formative aspects of treasure in relation to the heart—treasure reveals **and** trains the heart. Here's how this unfolds, along with the interconnection between possession, attention, and love:

### 1. **Treasure Reveals the Heart**

The latter part of verse 21 directly states, *"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also."* This statement implies a reflective relationship: what you value most (your "treasure") serves as an indicator of where your affections, priorities, and ultimately, your spiritual or moral center lie. In this sense, the text suggests that one's treasures **reveal** the state of their heart because:

- **Indicators of Priority:** What a person chooses to accumulate or invest in—be it wealth, relationships, knowledge, or experiences—highlights what they consider most important.
- **Reflection of Values:** The nature of one’s treasure (heavenly vs. earthly) mirrors their values and ethical compass. Heavenly treasures suggest a focus on the eternal, altruistic, and spiritual; earthly treasures might indicate a preoccupation with temporal, material, or self-serving concerns.

### 2. **Treasure Trains the Heart**

While the explicit wording emphasizes revelation ("where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"), the broader context of Jesus' teaching on detachment from worldly wealth and investment in heavenly rewards implies a formative process as well. This training aspect can be understood through:

- **Focus and Attention:** By deliberately choosing where to invest (in terms of time, resources, or emotional energy), one trains their focus. Continuous attention to earthly treasures tends to entrench materialistic values; conversely, prioritizing heavenly treasures cultivates a heart oriented towards generosity, compassion, and eternal perspectives.
- **Habitual Orientation:** The act of laying up treasure in heaven (through acts of kindness, charity, worship, prayer) habituates the heart to seek joy and fulfillment beyond the material. Over time, this habitual orientation reshapes desires, fears, and hopes—essentially training the heart towards heavenly values.

### 3. **Interconnection of Possession, Attention, and Love**

The passage intricately ties together possession (treasure), attention (where one's focus lies), and love (the ultimate expression of where one’s heart is) in a cyclical relationship:

- **Possession Influences Attention:** What we possess or strive to accumulate naturally draws our attention. If our treasures are earthly, our daily thoughts, decisions, and anxieties gravitate towards securing, maintaining, or expanding these possessions.
  
- **Attention Shapes Love:** Our focus (attention) over time molds what we love. Continuous engagement with material pursuits can deepen attachment to those pursuits; similarly, dedicating attention to acts of service, generosity, and spiritual disciplines nurtures a deeper love for the values and people associated with them.
  
- **Love Directs Possession:** Ultimately, where our love lies determines our future possessions or investments. If our heart is set on heavenly treasures, we will seek actions and attitudes that align with gaining such treasures (e.g., righteousness, mercy, faithfulness). This not only reflects but also reinforces the initial choice of where to direct our treasure.

In summary, Matthew 6:19–21 articulates a dynamic interplay between what we treasure, how we focus our attention, and what we love—suggesting that while treasures reveal the current state of the heart (what it already values), they also have the power to train or reorient the heart towards new values through consistent engagement and investment. This dual aspect underscores a call to intentional living, urging believers to consciously choose where their treasure lies, understanding its profound impact on the spiritual trajectory of their hearts.
