The passage rejects worship so sharply because the outward acts of worship being performed by the people were not accompanied by genuine righteousness and justice. It condemns the hypocrisy of offering sacrifices and attending religious festivals while neglecting the weightier matters of justice, righteousness, and mercy. The people were engaging in moral and spiritual fraud by going through the motions of religious rituals without living out the true principles of their faith in their everyday lives. This passage exposes the facade of piety and calls for true justice and righteousness to flow through the actions and attitudes of the people.
