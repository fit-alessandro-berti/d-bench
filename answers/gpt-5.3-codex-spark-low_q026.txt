• Short answer: all three, with emphasis on revelation and symptom.

  - Revelation of inner moral storehouse: Jesus says the heart contains “treasures” (good or evil) that are the source
    of speech. So words disclose what has been stored there.
  - Symptom, not only isolated acts: Speech is treated as a visible output of an inner moral condition, like fruit that
    “comes from” the tree/root, not a random event.
  - Choice is still involved: The verse does not deny accountability in speaking (you still choose to speak, and the
    following context includes moral condemnation), but it says those choices are shaped by prior moral formation.

  So a good way to read it is: your words both arise from and announce what is already in your heart.
