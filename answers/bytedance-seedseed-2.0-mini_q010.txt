The text from Philippians 2:5–8 clearly praises a humble kind of power. Instead of using power to get an advantage over others, it advises believers to have the same mindset as Christ Jesus, who humbled himself and became obedient to the point of death on the cross.

As to why humility combined with greatness might feel more unsettling than open domination, it might be because open domination is more easily recognizable and predictable. It is clear who has power and who doesn't, and one knows what to expect. However, when someone with great power chooses humility, it goes against people's expectations and the usual way things are done. It's confusing because one may wonder why someone who seems to have it all would humiliate themselves in this way. This kind of power that humbles and serves goes against the intuitive understanding of power being about exertion and domination.
