Isaiah 55:6–7 presents a profound call to repentance that extends beyond mere outward behavior to encompass the heart’s deepest thoughts and motivations. Here’s what this passage implies about outward conduct, inward imagination, and the possibility of mercy:

### **1. Repentance Involves Both "Way" and "Thoughts"**
- **"Way" (or "path")** refers to **lifestyle, actions, and habits**—what a person does in practical, daily life. The wicked forsake their *way* (e.g., sinful habits, ungodly choices, rebellion against God).
- **"Thoughts" (or "thoughts of the heart")** refers to **internal attitudes, intentions, and motivations**—what a person *thinks* about God, others, and themselves. The unrighteous man’s "thoughts" may include pride, idolatry, self-deception, or a hardened heart.

Isaiah 55:7 suggests that **repentance is holistic**—it requires both **external obedience** (turning from sinful actions) and **internal transformation** (turning from sinful desires and false beliefs).

### **2. Outward Conduct and Inward Imagination Are Interconnected**
- A person’s **actions** (way) often reveal their **thoughts** (heart). For example:
  - A person who **steals** (outward sin) may also **despise God’s justice** (internal sin).
  - A person who **lives in pride** (way) may also **reject God’s truth** (thoughts).
- Conversely, **false thoughts** (e.g., self-righteousness, idolatry) can lead to **evil actions**. The heart’s corruption often manifests in outward behavior.

This passage implies that **God sees both the fruit of the soul and the root of the heart**. Repentance must address both because **sin is not just what we do but what we are in our innermost being** (Jeremiah 17:9).

### **3. Mercy Is Possible Only When Repentance Is Complete**
- The passage states that **God will have mercy** when the wicked **return to the Lord**—not just in name, but in **both their way and their thoughts**.
- If repentance stops at **external actions** (e.g., confessing sin but not changing habits or attitudes), mercy may be delayed or denied (see **Luke 15:7, 10**—the parable of the lost sheep and the lost coin).
- However, if repentance includes **turning from sinful thoughts** (e.g., unbelief, self-deception, hatred), God’s mercy is **fully extended** (see **1 John 1:9**—confession of sin leads to forgiveness).

### **4. What This Implies About Salvation**
- **Salvation is not just about changing behavior but about changing the heart** (Jeremiah 31:33; Ezekiel 36:26).
- **Mercy is conditional on genuine repentance**—not just lip service but a **real turning from sin** in all areas of life.
- **God’s pardon is abundant** when the heart is truly repentant, but **hardening of the heart** (Jeremiah 17:9) can block mercy if the sin remains unconfessed and changed.

### **Practical Application**
- **Repentance must include:**
  - **Turning from sinful actions** (way).
  - **Turning from sinful desires and false beliefs** (thoughts).
- **Mercy is not earned but received**—but it requires **genuine repentance**, not just superficial change.
- **God’s grace is sufficient** when we seek Him with both **heart and hands**.

This passage reminds us that **true repentance is not just about feeling sorry but about turning completely from sin to God**, in all aspects of life.
