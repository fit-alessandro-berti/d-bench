This verse from 1 John 4:20 presents a profound theological and ethical principle: genuine love for God must be demonstrated through tangible, visible love for others—especially fellow believers. The reason love of the visible neighbor serves as a test of love for the invisible God lies in the nature of authentic spiritual reality.

God, being spirit and invisible, cannot be directly observed or manipulated for personal gain. In contrast, human beings are present, tangible, and often inconvenient. It is easy to profess love for God in words or rituals while harboring indifference, resentment, or neglect toward those right in front of us. This creates a dangerous self-deception: one may believe they are spiritually devoted while their heart remains unchanged.

The verse exposes the hypocrisy of claiming divine love without embodying it in human relationships. If someone cannot extend compassion, patience, and care to a brother or sister they can see—someone with flaws, needs, and demands—then their claim to love a God they cannot see rings hollow. True love for God transforms the heart and overflows into concrete actions toward others. Thus, love of neighbor becomes the visible evidence—or lack thereof—of an invisible spiritual reality.
