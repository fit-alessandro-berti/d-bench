The passage from Amos rejects worship sharply rather than merely calling for balance because it highlights a profound moral and spiritual fraud: the substitution of outward religious ceremony for genuine ethical behavior. God’s sharp rejection suggests that worship without justice is not merely incomplete—it's fundamentally false.

This criticism targets a deceptive spirituality that allows worshippers to feel pious or justified without addressing deeper moral failings. In other words, the Israelites believed rituals, sacrifices, and songs could cover their neglect or oppression of others. Amos denounces this illusion explicitly, making clear that genuine worship cannot coexist with injustice or unrighteousness.

By demanding justice that runs "as waters" and righteousness like "a mighty stream," Amos emphasizes that authentic devotion is inseparable from ethical action—true spirituality always manifests itself in moral integrity and social justice.
