• Short answer: it gives both, with emphasis on disclosure and practical formation.

  In Matthew 6:19–21, “treasure” is the object you treat as your enduring value.

  - Reveals the heart: “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” reads as an observable diagnosis—your
    priorities show your true loves.
  - Forms/trains the heart: The previous commands (“do not store up…”) imply habits shape desire; repeatedly investing
    in what decays breeds attachment, fear, and self-protection, while investing in what is eternal trains trust,
    generosity, and hope in God.

  So in this passage, possession, attention, and love are linked through value orientation:

  - What you possess/seek is not neutral.
  - What you attend to (where your mind and energy go) tracks what you truly value.
  - What you truly love becomes the center of your life, and that love is seen in ongoing choices.

  In other words: possessions are signs of the heart, and repeated attachment to possessions (or to God’s kingdom
  values) forms the heart over time. The verse is a concise statement of that loop.
