The elder brother’s deepest problem is most fundamentally rooted in **envy**, though it is intertwined with feelings of resentment, disappointment, and a sense of being slighted. His reaction to his younger brother’s homecoming—hostility followed by passive aggression—is driven primarily by jealousy over the attention and reward given to his prodigal sibling rather than solely anger or wounded fairness.

### Explanation:

1. **Envy as the Core Issue**:
   - The elder son resents that he has been dutifully obedient while his younger brother squanders resources and ends up in a state of moral decline, only to return penniless with no respect from those around him.
   - He feels abandoned by his father’s apparent leniency toward the prodigal child, whom he perceives as unworthy of such treatment.

2. **Discomfort with Mercy**:
   - Underlying his anger and resentment is a profound discomfort with the notion that mercy should be extended to someone who has shown disrespect for their upbringing.
   - He feels that justice—especially in terms of social standing or respect—should have been served, not merely restored through love and forgiveness.

3. **Anger as Manifestation**:
   - Anger is a natural reaction to his father’s apparent favoritism toward the prodigal son after he has returned.
   - Yet this anger stems from deeper insecurities about being unworthy or undeserving of such treatment compared to his brother.

4. **Wounded Fairness (Beneath Surface)**:
   - His sense of betrayal by his father’s actions hints at a wounded notion of fairness, where he sees the reward given to his brother as unjust.
   - This perceived unfairness exacerbates the initial anger and leads to an attitude of resentment and reluctance to forgive.

5. **Pride in Obedience (Contrast)**:
   - While it might seem that pride in obedience is a factor, it is more a manifestation of resentment against being overlooked for his virtues.
   - His sense of personal righteousness and perceived honor could be another layer, but the ultimate driver remains the jealousy toward the younger brother’s undeserved reward.

In summary, while the elder son exhibits anger towards the situation, the **deepest problem** he faces is rooted in **envy**—a desire for recognition and validation that stems from feeling unjustly overlooked by his father over actions of his brother. His underlying discontent with mercy itself reflects a complex web of resentment, disappointment, and personal pride intertwined with an identity shaped by strict adherence to perceived justice.
