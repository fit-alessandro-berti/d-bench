 In Philippians 2:4–5, the Apostle Paul mentions "a humble mind." This isn't really a power in the conventional sense, but a mental or philosophical state of mind that focuses less on self and more on others. He draws from the example of Jesus Christ, whose nature and life were marked by humility, even to the extent of self-emptying, obedience to death on a cross. The kind of power being praised here can be seen as the divine power that underpins this quality of servant-ship, love, and humility.

This divine power is one that is not domineering, controlling, or seeking to impose itself on the world through force. Instead, it operates through love, grace, and an example of unassuming service, as embodied in Christ himself. It is a power that respects the integrity of others, sees injustice and suffering, and is moved to alleviate, heal, and transform.


Why might humility, especially when combined with greatness or authority, feel more unsettling or unusual than open domination?


The reasons for this are multifacde and can be interpreted through various lenses, from psychological to sociological to theological. Here are a few to consider:

1. **Mutual Respect**: A hierarchical society or organization has an expectation of power dynamics, in which the powerful is expected to lead, and the subordinate to follow. When roles are inverted, it often challenges the status quent hierarchy's comfort zone and may unsettle individuals who are accustomed to such a system. This can be both uncomfortable and unsettling because it requires a shift in mindset from self-importance towards mutual respect, breaking conventional norms, and potentially redefining one's understanding of leadership.

2. **Inspiration and Aspiration**: When someone in a position of greatness or power exhibits humility, it raises the bar for what is admired, desired, and aspired to within a community, inspiring people to mirror such behavior can lead to profound shifts in societal attitudes and values. This might challenge traditional ideals of power where domination and control are often glorified, making them seem unsettling because they represent an ideal that is not typically seen in the world.

3. **Authenticity and Integrity**: The unsettling feeling might arise when realizing the pretense and facades often associated with authority are removed, revealing a more authentic and humble self. This authenticity can disrupt conventional power structures based on deceit, corruption, or manipulation.

4. **Empathy and Vulnerability**: When those in positions of greatness, power, or authority show humility, they demonstrate vulnerability, which opens up to empathy and understanding from others. This openness can reveal a previously hidden human side, showing that even the great can experience common human experiences and emotions, which disrupts typical expectations and may make them seem vulnerable or less powerful.

5. **Reconciliation and Healing**: This type of power can be healing and reconciling, which is often underestimated in its potency. Domination often leaves wounds and scars because it is not based in compassionate and understanding principles. In contrast, humility is relational and empathetic, inviting healing, which can be unsettling to some because it challenges the narrative that strength is associated with fear and control.

6. **Inclusivity and Unity**: Humility invites others to participate and feel valued. In a world where hierarchies can lead to division and exclusivity, humility brings a sense of inclusivity that dismantles societal barriers, providing unsettling as it requires a collective effort and shared responsibility towards societal well-being.

7. **Accountability and Reflection**: Humility in greatness or power invites responsibility and reflection on the impact of one's actions, which can be unsettling because it requires the powerful to acknowledge and address their shortcomings and past misdeeds.

8. **Grace and Forgiveness**: Embracing humility brings the recognition that one has been forgiven and redeemed. This understanding can transform one's perspective on power, replacing the unsettling notion of merit-based authority with the grace-filled acknowledgment of being loved unconditionally and accepted.

9. **Ethical Responsibility**: Such humility shows ethical responsibility and a genuine concern for the greater good. It prioritizes principles over personal gain, which can be unsettling to those focused solely on ambition and self-interest, as it challenges their moral compass and forces them to confront internal and external societal failures.

10. **Self-Reflection and Growth**: Finally, this power fosters a continuous cycle of self-reflection and personal growth, constantly seeking to better oneself and elevate others, breaking the cycle of egoism and self-service, which can be unsettling for those who believe power is a means to control and maintain status.


In essence, divine or exemplary power, as embodied in humility, represents a transformative and redemptive influence. It invites healing, inclusivity, and empathy, which, though disruptive to established norms, offers a more profound and fulfilling connection with individuals and communities. Such shifts can reveal the true nature of power - not as control but as compassionate service. This vision can be unsettling while it offers a more authentic, grace-filled, and redemptive approach, aligning with divine principles where love always prevails. Hence, humility in greatness not only feels more honorable and compassionate but is ultimately more impactful for the wellbeing and unity in a society.
