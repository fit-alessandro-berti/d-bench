Short answer: both. Jesus’ saying functions as revelation and formation: what you treasure exposes what already rules your life, and what you deliberately treasure trains and relocates your heart.

How the verse says both
- Revelation: The statement “For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” reads naturally as diagnostic — the location of someone’s treasure shows where their inner center (kardia, “heart”) already is. In other words, possessions and investments are reliable indicators of what a person truly values.
- Formation: The surrounding imperatives (“Lay not up… Lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven”) point to intentional action. Jesus invites his hearers to redirect their investments. The act of storing up treasure (giving, serving, practising righteousness) shapes habits, attention, and affections so that the heart is increasingly fixed where those “treasures” are.

How possession, attention, and love are tied together here
- Possession: What you own or where you invest resources expresses priority. Earthly treasures are described as vulnerable to moth, rust, and thieves; heavenly treasures are secure. “Treasure” stands for money, goods, security, status, and whatever you bank on for well-being.
- Attention: Possessions and investments demand and attract attention (care, planning, guarding). What you protect and tend occupies mental space and practical activity.
- Love (affection/loyalty): Repeated attention and guarded investment cultivate love. Psychologically and spiritually, doing and guarding produce desire and attachment; conversely, existing attachments determine where you put your resources.

A way to put it: treasure points to the heart (diagnosis) and treasure-making practices form the heart (therapy). Jesus is both exposing a truth about people’s priorities and calling for practices that reorder those priorities toward God. Practically this means changing not merely beliefs but where you actually put your time, money, and energy — because changing those externals will redirect attention and, over time, reshape the heart.
