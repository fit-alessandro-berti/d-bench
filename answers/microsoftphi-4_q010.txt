The passage from Philippians 2:5–8 highlights the power of humility and self-sacrifice as exemplified by Christ. This "power" being praised is not one of domination or authority in the conventional sense, but rather the transformative and redemptive strength found in humility and servanthood. Christ, despite being equal with God ("in the form of God"), voluntarily relinquished His divine privileges and took on the role of a servant ("form of a servant"), ultimately embracing suffering and death on the cross.

Humility joined to greatness can feel more unsettling than open domination for several reasons:

1. **Subversion of Expectations**: Traditional power often manifests through superiority, control, and dominance. When greatness is paired with humility, it challenges conventional expectations of how power should operate. It defies the typical understanding that power and authority equate to self-promotion or exploitation.

2. **Moral and Ethical Imperative**: Humility coupled with greatness sets a higher standard of moral and ethical behavior. It calls for leaders and influential figures to prioritize service over self-interest, which can be difficult for individuals or societies accustomed to hierarchical models of power.

3. **Socratic Paradox**: This model of power resonates with the Socratic idea that true wisdom involves recognizing one's own limitations. By showing vulnerability and willingness to serve, individuals demonstrate a depth of character that can inspire respect and loyalty more effectively than fear or dominance.

4. **Transformative Influence**: The power of humility inspires change and growth in others. It encourages self-reflection and personal transformation, as individuals are moved to adopt similar values of humility and service in their own lives.

5. **Relational Over Transactional**: This kind of power fosters deep, meaningful relationships based on mutual respect and understanding rather than transactional interactions driven by power dynamics. It promotes community and cooperation over competition and division.

In essence, humility joined to greatness offers a model of leadership and influence based on love, sacrifice, and service, which can be more profound and long-lasting than the effects of domination and control.
