The text from Matthew 6:19-21 does not explicitly state whether treasure reveals the heart or trains the heart. However, it does provide a clear contrast between earthly and heavenly treasures, and the implications of where one's heart is placed.

The passage suggests that earthly treasures, which are subject to decay, theft, and loss, can lead to a heart that is focused on material gain and security. In contrast, heavenly treasures, which are eternal and secure, lead to a heart that is focused on spiritual values and eternal life.

The connection between possession, attention, and love in this context can be understood as follows:

1. **Possession**: The text suggests that what we possess can significantly influence our hearts. If we place our possessions in earthly, transient things, our hearts will likely be preoccupied with these things. Conversely, if we place our possessions in heavenly, eternal things, our hearts will be directed towards spiritual values and eternal life.

2. **Attention**: The attention we give to our possessions can also reflect the state of our hearts. If we are constantly focused on accumulating and protecting earthly treasures, our attention is likely to be on material things. If we are focused on spiritual treasures, our attention will be on spiritual matters.

3. **Love**: The text implies that where our treasure is, our heart will be as well. This suggests that our love and affection are directed towards what we treasure. If we love earthly treasures, our heart will be with them. If we love heavenly treasures, our heart will be with them. This ties into the broader Christian teaching about the centrality of love in our relationship with God and others.

In summary, while the text does not explicitly state whether treasure reveals or trains the heart, it does suggest a strong connection between what we treasure, how we attend to our possessions, and where our heart ultimately resides. This passage encourages Christians to prioritize spiritual treasures over earthly ones, as this aligns with the values of the Kingdom of Heaven.
