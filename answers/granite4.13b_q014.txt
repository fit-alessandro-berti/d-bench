The text from Matthew 6:19–21 does not explicitly state whether it says "treasure reveals the heart" or "treasure trains the heart." However, the principle conveyed in these verses is that where one places their focus and resources—i.e., their treasure—will also reflect their deeper values and priorities. This suggests a form of self-disclosure: what we accumulate (whether material wealth or other forms of value such as knowledge, relationships, or experiences) indicates what we truly value.

### Possession, Attention, and Love

1. **Possession**:
   - The verse warns against placing one’s treasures on earth, where physical possessions are prone to deterioration (mote and rust). This implies that worldly treasures—money, property, material goods—are transient and vulnerable to decay or loss.
   - By contrast, laying up treasures in heaven suggests a focus on immaterial values—spiritual growth, wisdom, virtue—that endure beyond the physical realm.

2. **Attention**:
   - The attention one directs towards accumulating earthly possessions (treasure upon earth) is a reflection of what they consider important or valuable.
   - Conversely, directing attention toward laying up treasures in heaven signals an intention to cultivate values that align with enduring spiritual well-being rather than fleeting material satisfaction.

3. **Love**:
   - Love for the source (heavenly treasure) and love for those we care about are intertwined. By choosing heavenly treasures, one is essentially choosing a form of love that prioritizes eternal value—love for God and others unchanging and selfless.
   - This aligns with broader spiritual teachings in Christianity, where divine love (agape) is unconditional and seeks the well-being of all, contrasting sharply with earthly pursuits driven by selfish or temporary desires.

### Tie Together

- **Self-Disclosure**: Treasure—be it wealth or anything else we pursue materially—acts as a mirror to our heart's desires. Just as a reflection shows what lies beneath, so too does our investment choices reveal where our hearts truly lie.
  
- **Moral Guidance**: By emphasizing the pursuit of heavenly treasures over earthly ones, Jesus advises followers to align their lives with spiritual values that transcend physical impermanence and are rooted in love for God and neighbor. This guidance helps maintain moral integrity by keeping materialism from dominating one’s inner life.

In summary, while the text doesn’t explicitly state "treasure reveals the heart" or "treasure trains the heart," it effectively communicates that both possessions (what we acquire) and attention (what we focus on) are indicators of our deeper values—values shaped by love for what is eternal rather than fleeting. This encourages a life oriented toward spiritual growth and genuine love, reflecting Christ’s teaching on stewardship of all things as ultimately serving God's kingdom.
