Matthew 6:19–21 does **both**, but in slightly different ways:

- **Treasure reveals the heart**: “Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”  
  This reads as an **exposure** statement: what you value most shows where your inner life already leans.

- **Treasure also trains the heart**: by telling people to *lay up treasures in heaven*, Jesus implies that repeated investment in a thing will **redirect and form** desire. What you store, protect, and pursue tends to shape what you care about.

So the passage is not merely observational; it is also **formative**.

## How possession, attention, and love are tied together

### 1) Possession directs attention
What you treat as “mine” naturally becomes the object of watchfulness.  
If a person owns treasure on earth, their mind tends to go there:
- How to keep it
- How to increase it
- How to avoid losing it

So **possession creates mental preoccupation**.

### 2) Attention reinforces desire
What you keep looking at, planning for, and guarding becomes more significant to you.  
Attention is not neutral: it **deepens attachment**. The more a person attends to earthly treasure, the more the heart settles there.

### 3) Desire becomes love
In biblical terms, “heart” includes will, desire, and allegiance—not just emotion.  
So the “treasure” you store up is not only what you have; it is what you **value as your good**. That is close to love.

Thus:
- **Treasure held**
- becomes **treasure attended to**
- becomes **treasure cherished**
- becomes **treasure loved**

## In short
The text says:
- **Your treasure reveals your heart**
- and **your treasure helps shape your heart**

Jesus is exposing a moral principle:  
**the things we invest in externally become the things we love internally.**
