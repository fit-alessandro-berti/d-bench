This is a profound question about the nature of prophetic critique. The sharpness of the rejection isn't just about "balance"—it's exposing a deep **spiritual and moral fraud** at the heart of institutionalized worship.

Here’s why the tone is so severe and what fraud is being exposed:

### 1. The Rejection is of *Empty Ritual as a Shield*
The fraud being exposed is the belief that **ritual performance itself—feasts, offerings, songs—secures divine favor or personal righteousness**, while one's economic and social life remains unjust. God isn't asking for "better worship" alongside their current behavior; He's declaring their worship **invalid** because it exists in profound contradiction to their actions. It's not a matter of balance; it's a matter of hypocrisy so deep it corrupts the worship itself.

### 2. Worship as a Transaction and a Distraction
The people are engaging in a **quid pro quo religion**: "We offer these sacrifices, so God blesses us and ignores our other deeds." This turns worship into a transaction and a **spiritual distraction**. The "noise" and "melody" are the sounds of a religious system functioning smoothly, but they are deafening God to the cries of the oppressed. The sharp rejection is meant to shatter the illusion that ritual can substitute for righteousness.

### 3. The Fraud of Institutionalized Piety
This isn't individual hypocrisy alone; it's a **systemic fraud**. The "feast days" and "solemn assemblies" are national, communal events. The fraud is that the entire community uses its religious calendar and institutions to affirm its own righteousness while perpetuating systemic injustice (as described in the surrounding verses of Amos 5, which condemn those who "trample on the needy and do away with the poor of the land"). The worship system has become a **prop for the unjust status quo**.

### 4. The Prophetic Inversion of Values
Amos performs a radical inversion: what the people see as their most sacred acts (offerings), God sees as offensive "noise." What they see as a minor moral flaw (imperfect justice), God sees as the only thing that matters. The sharpness is necessary to **overturn their entire value system**. Mere "balance" would validate their framework; the rejection dismantles it.

### 5. What is Exposed: The Idolatry of Order over Justice
The deeper fraud is **idolatry**. They have made an idol of their religious order, their national prosperity, and their social hierarchy. Their worship is ultimately directed at maintaining this order, not at the God who demands justice. God rejects their worship because it is, in essence, worship of a different god—one made in their own image, who is content with sacrifices while ignoring oppression.

### The "Mighty Stream" as Antithesis
The final verse isn't a balanced addition; it's the **replacement** for their entire religious edifice. "Let justice roll down..." means **justice is not a component of worship; it is the very essence of true worship.** The "mighty stream" is the overwhelming, cleansing force that must sweep away the fraudulent, superficial religion of the status quo.

In short, the sharp rejection exposes the fraud of **a religion that comforts the oppressor and alienates God**. It declares that worship untethered from justice is not merely incomplete—it is an abomination, a "noise" that pollutes the sacred. The prophetic demand is not for better ritual, but for a revolution in values where justice becomes the primary, non-negotiable act of worship.
