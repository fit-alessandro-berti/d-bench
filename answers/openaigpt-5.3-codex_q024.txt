Great question. In Ezekiel 36:26–27, God is describing **more than behavior change**—He is describing **personhood change**.

### External compliance vs. a “new heart”

- **External compliance** = doing the right actions from outside pressure:
  - fear of punishment
  - desire for approval
  - social/religious habit  
  You can obey outwardly while inwardly remaining resistant, proud, or cold.

- **A new heart** = inward renewal:
  - new desires
  - new loves
  - new responsiveness to God  
  Obedience now flows from within, not merely from constraint.  
  In v.27, God says, “I will put my Spirit within you, and **cause** you to walk…”—that is internal empowerment, not just external regulation.

### Why “hardness” sounds like a material condition

The “heart” in biblical language is the center of will, thought, and desire—not just emotion.  
So “heart of stone” is a vivid diagnosis:

- **stone** is hard, unresponsive, and resistant to shaping
- **flesh** (here) means living, soft, responsive tissue

So God is not saying people just need better rules; He says they need a **transplant**.  
It’s covenant language: the problem is not lack of information but lack of inward capacity. Hence the repeated “**I will**…”—God must act to replace what humans cannot fix themselves.

In short: external compliance can mimic obedience; a new heart makes real obedience possible.
