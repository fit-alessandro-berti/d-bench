Two things are going on.

Why so many negations
- Paul is correcting a concrete problem. Corinth was marked by rivalry, status-seeking, and spiritual one‑upmanship (cf. 1 Cor 1–4; 8; 11–14). So he defines love not only by its warmth (patient, kind) but by what it refuses to do—envy, boast, be inflated, be rude, insist on its own way, keep score. These are precisely the habits tearing their community apart.
- He’s carving away counterfeits. By an apophatic (via negativa) portrait, Paul strips love of the ego’s common disguises. Love is not mere passion, talent, or zeal; it is a will that refuses pride, resentment, and suspicion.
- He is training perception. Most of these “nots” target everyday relational reflexes we excuse as normal. Naming them exposes them as incompatible with love and retrains the church’s instincts.

What vision of strength this proposes
- Strength as self‑giving steadiness, not dominance. Love’s power is seen in durable patience and active kindness under pressure—absorbing provocation without becoming reactive.
- Strength as freedom from the honor game. “Not puffed up, not seeking its own” rejects the status economy of Corinth. Real power doesn’t need to win, impress, or have its own way to be secure.
- Strength as truth‑anchored hope. “Rejoices with the truth” keeps this from naivete: “believes all things” means a charitable trust that gives the benefit of the doubt, yet never at the expense of reality. It is tender without being gullible, hopeful without denying hard facts.
- Strength as resilient fidelity. “Bears, believes, hopes, endures all things” describes covenantal stamina—the capacity to carry burdens, keep short accounts, and persist in doing good over time.
- Ultimately, Christlike strength. It reflects the cruciform pattern of Jesus: power perfected in restraint, self‑emptying for others, and joy in the truth that sets people free.

In short, the negations clear away the ego’s distortions so that love’s positive power can appear: a courageous, truth‑loving, other‑seeking steadiness that actually holds a community together.
