


The passage connects **speech** directly to **hidden moral stockpiles in the heart** by framing words as the natural outflow of what resides within. It suggests that words are:  

1. **Symptoms** – They reveal the internal condition of the heart (like a diagnostic sign).  
2. **Choices** – The speaker actively draws from their inner "treasure" (implying intentionality).  
3. **Revelations** – The content of speech exposes what is truly stored in the heart (good or evil).  

Thus, words function as all three simultaneously: they *reveal* an internal state (symptom), *express* a deliberate act (choice), and *manifest* hidden reality (revelation). The "treasure" metaphor emphasizes that the heart is a reservoir whose contents inevitably surface through speech.
