The passage from Matthew 6:19-21 emphasizes how where one places their treasure reflects and influences where their heart is set, suggesting both the revelation and training of the heart through actions and decisions.

1. **Treasure Reveals the Heart:**
   - The statement "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" implies that the priorities and values evidenced by one's accumulation of treasures reveal the true focus and concerns of their heart. If one invests in earthly treasures, it suggests a heart more aligned with material wealth and worldly possessions. Conversely, if one invests in "treasures in heaven," it indicates a heart set on spiritual and eternal values.

2. **Treasure Trains the Heart:**
   - The act of deliberately choosing where to invest one's treasure can be seen as a process that trains the heart. Over time, continually prioritizing spiritual treasures over material ones can condition the heart to value spiritual growth, generosity, and reliance on God. This transformation is more about the intentional cultivation of one's values and priorities through deliberate choices.

3. **Possession, Attention, and Love:**
   - **Possession:** The type of treasures one accumulates reflects possessions chosen to hold value. These possessions, whether material or spiritual, become focal points where attention and resources are directed.
   - **Attention:** Where one invests their time and energy is a key indicator of what truly captures their attention. The choice of investing in earthly versus heavenly treasures demonstrates where one's attention lies.
   - **Love:** Ultimately, where one chooses to place their treasure is indicative of what they love most. A heart fully invested in earthly treasures suggests a greater love for self-sufficiency and temporary satisfaction. In contrast, a heart that invests in heavenly treasures shows a love for something greater, eternal, and not susceptible to decay.

In summary, Matthew 6:19-21 suggests that treasure both reveals where the heart lies and plays a role in cultivating and training the heart through what one values, attends to, and loves. This dynamic relationship encourages individuals to reflect on their priorities and make conscious decisions that align with spiritual growth and eternal values.
