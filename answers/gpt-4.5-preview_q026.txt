This passage from Matthew 12:34–35 connects speech directly with the hidden moral reservoirs within the heart, suggesting a deep relationship between inner character and outward expression.

Words here appear as:

1. **Symptoms:**
   Speech flows naturally from the heart's "abundance." Just as symptoms reflect underlying conditions, words expose the inner moral state. They are not accidental slips, but indicators of a person's true inner self.

2. **Choices:**
   Although speech arises naturally, there is also an implied responsibility or choice in maintaining either "good treasure" or "evil treasure." One intentionally cultivates the heart’s moral store, determining what eventually spills out.

3. **Revelations:**
   Words are also revealing, acting as windows into the hidden life. The phrase "bringeth forth" underscores the idea that speech uncovers internal realities that might otherwise remain hidden.

In summary, the passage frames speech as simultaneously symptomatic, volitional, and revelatory—words show openly the moral condition one has secretly nurtured within.
