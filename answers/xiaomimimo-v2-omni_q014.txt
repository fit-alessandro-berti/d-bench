This text beautifully captures **both ideas—treasure reveals the heart and treasure trains the heart**—and connects them in a profound way.

### 1. **Treasure Reveals the Heart**
Jesus says, *“Where your treasure is, there will your heart be also.”*  
This means your **focus and priorities show what you truly value**. If you invest your time, energy, and emotions in earthly things—wealth, status, possessions—it reveals that your heart is anchored there. It’s not just about having things, but about **what has you**.

### 2. **Treasure Trains the Heart**
The passage also implies a **directional relationship**:  
What you consistently pursue *shapes* your affections.  
If you “lay up treasures in heaven”—invest in love, justice, generosity, relationship with God—your heart gradually becomes aligned with eternal values.  
Your attention trains your love; your love then directs your attention.

### 3. **Possession, Attention, and Love Intertwined**
- **Possession** isn’t just about ownership, but about what you identify with and depend on.
- **Attention** follows what you cherish—you think about it, plan for it, protect it.
- **Love** is the heart’s deep attachment; where your treasure is, your love is drawn.

Jesus warns that earthly treasures are vulnerable to decay and theft—they cannot secure your heart. But heavenly treasures (like compassion, truth, service, worship) are permanent and instead **secure and elevate your heart’s deepest longings**.

In short:  
**What you own ends up owning you.**  
But if you “store up” in what is eternal, your heart will be free, fixed on what truly lasts.
