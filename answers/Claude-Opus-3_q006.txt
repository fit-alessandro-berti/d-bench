There are a few important aspects to Jesus' call to self-denial here that are worth exploring:

1. Jesus is calling for a denial of the self-centered, sinful nature within us that is opposed to God. He's not calling us to deny our true self as God created us to be, but rather the false self corrupted by sin. So in one sense, this self-denial is actually liberating because it frees us from slavery to sin and allows us to become our true, best selves in Christ.

2. However, to our human nature, giving up control and submitting ourselves fully to God can feel offensive, restrictive and scary. Our pride rebels against the idea of denying our own will and desires to follow Jesus unconditionally. Carrying our "cross" implies suffering and giving up what we want for God's sake. 

3. Ultimately, Jesus is calling us to completely reorient our lives around him as the center, rather than ourselves. This radical reordering of priorities from self to God is extremely costly. It requires humility, obedience, and a willingness to put God's will above our own. Many find this deeply unappealing.

4. At the same time, Jesus promises that what we give up pales in comparison to what we gain - true life and joy in him. Paradoxically, by releasing our tight grip on our own life, we actually find real life. But this remains offensive to human sensibilities.

5. Some are attracted to a gospel of self-fulfillment, not self-denial. They want a Christianity that exalts and gratifies the self. But that is antithetical to authentic discipleship. Following Jesus is not a path to self-actualization but self-crucifixion. Yet therein lies the path to resurrection life.

So in summary, I believe Jesus is calling us to deny our fallen, sinful, self-directed nature, not our God-given personality and humanness. But this remains a radical, offensive summons to the human heart. Discipleship is costly. Yet Jesus invites us to trust that by losing our life for his sake, we will actually find it. Therein lies the challenging but liberating paradox of the gospel. Submitting to Christ is the road to freedom and joy.
