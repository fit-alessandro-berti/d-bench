The passage from Matthew 12:34–35 offers a profound insight into the relationship between one's inner moral state and outward speech. Here's how it can be understood in terms of symptoms, choices, and revelations:

1. **Symptoms**: The passage suggests that speech can be seen as a symptom or reflection of what is stored in the heart. Just as physical health can manifest in visible ways, the heart's moral and ethical state expresses itself through words. Thus, the words one speaks are indicative of their inner moral condition, much like symptoms are indicative of one's health.

2. **Choices**: While the passage emphasizes the natural overflow of the heart through speech, it also implies an element of choice. A person's speech is partly a result of their conscious decisions about what to express. While one's "treasure" in the heart influences their speech, individuals still choose what to say, suggesting a level of moral responsibility and the possibility of choosing to speak differently.

3. **Revelations**: Speech can be seen as a revelation of the heart's contents. It exposes what is truly valued and prioritized within an individual. In this way, words reveal the moral and ethical priorities of a person, offering insight into their true character and values.

Overall, the passage connects speech with the hidden moral stockpiles in the heart by illustrating that words are both a natural overflow of one's inner state and a choice that can reveal the true condition of one's heart. It suggests a complex interplay where speech acts as a symptom of inner moral reality, a choice reflecting intentional expression, and a revelation that unveils one's true character.
