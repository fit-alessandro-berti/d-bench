 This passage from Matthew 12:34-35 connects speech with hidden moral stockpiles in the heart by suggesting that what comes out of a person's mouth is a reflection of what is in their heart. The words we speak are seen as a revelation of our inner moral character. The good and evil things that we bring forth from our hearts are compared to good and evil treasures.

The passage implies that words are both symptoms and choices. They are symptoms in the sense that they reveal what is in our hearts. They are choices in the sense that we have control over what we say. The passage does not explicitly mention that words are revelations, but the implication is there, as the words we speak give insight into our moral character.

Therefore, according to this passage, our speech is a reflection of our inner moral stockpiles, and it is both a symptom and a choice. It reveals what is in our hearts, and it is something that we have control over.
