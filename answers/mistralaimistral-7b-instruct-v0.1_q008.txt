 The verse in 1 John 4:20 treats love of the visible neighbor as a test of love for the invisible God because love for God and love for one's neighbor are intimately connected in the teachings of Christianity. This verse is emphasizing that if someone claims to love God but does not show love to their visible neighbor, then their claim to love God is a lie.

The self-deception that this verse is exposing is the idea that one can claim to love God while neglecting or even hating their fellow human beings. The verse is reminding us that our actions towards our neighbors reveal the truth of our hearts and our love for God. If we truly love God, we will also love those whom He has placed in our lives, including our neighbors. Therefore, the verse is emphasizing the importance of practicing love and compassion towards others as a reflection of our love for God.
