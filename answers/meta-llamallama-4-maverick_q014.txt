This passage from Matthew 6:19-21 presents a nuanced view of the relationship between treasure and the heart. To address the question, let's analyze the text step by step.

### Understanding the Text

1. **Treasure on Earth vs. Treasure in Heaven**: Jesus contrasts two types of treasures. Earthly treasures are perishable and vulnerable to loss (due to corruption, thieves, etc.), while heavenly treasures are imperishable and secure.

2. **The Heart's Location**: The key statement is, "For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" (Matthew 6:21). This indicates a direct relationship between the location or focus of one's treasure and the heart's orientation.

### Treasure Reveals the Heart or Trains the Heart?

The text suggests that **treasure reveals the heart**. The phrase "where your treasure is, there will your heart be also" implies that the heart follows the treasure. This indicates that what one values or treasures is a reflection or indicator of where their heart is. The heart is not necessarily trained by the treasure in this context but is instead revealed by it.

However, it's also possible to interpret the passage as suggesting that treasure has a formative influence on the heart over time. If one continually invests in or prioritizes a particular treasure, their heart becomes oriented towards it. In this sense, treasure could be seen as **influencing or shaping the heart**. Yet, the primary emphasis in Matthew 6:21 is on the heart's existing orientation being reflected by its treasure.

### Possession, Attention, and Love Tied Together

1. **Possession (Treasure)**: The concept of treasure implies possession or valuing of something. Jesus encourages his followers to reevaluate what they consider valuable or worth possessing.

2. **Attention**: The focus on either earthly or heavenly treasures indicates where one's attention is directed. The act of laying up treasures requires and reflects attention towards those treasures.

3. **Love**: The heart is often associated with love or affection. The orientation of the heart (where it "is") is tied to what one loves or values, which is represented by the treasure.

The passage ties these concepts together by suggesting that what one possesses or values (treasure) is a reflection of where their attention is focused and what they love. The heart's orientation is a result of or is reflected by these factors. In essence, the passage suggests that our values (as reflected by our treasures) and our love (as reflected by the heart's orientation) are intimately connected.

In summary, Matthew 6:19-21 primarily indicates that **treasure reveals the heart**. It highlights the intrinsic link between what we value (treasure), where our attention is focused, and what we love (heart's orientation), suggesting that these aspects are deeply intertwined.
