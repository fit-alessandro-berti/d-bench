This passage from James 3:9–10 is exposing the inner contradiction between blessing God and cursing others from the same mouth. In other words, it highlights how people can, in the very same breath or even with the same tongue, praise God and then immediately speak negatively or harmfully about others. The contradiction lies in claiming to honor the divine while at the same time dishonoring one's fellow human beings, who are said to be "made after the similitude of God" (created in God's image).

James calls this out as something that "ought not so to be" — it is morally inconsistent and even disordered. Speech, according to this passage, reveals moral disorder quickly because it is an immediate and visible expression of the heart. What comes out of our mouths reflects what is truly inside us. When someone alternates between blessing and cursing, it shows a lack of unity and integrity in their character. The ease and rapidity with which people can switch from praise to blame demonstrates a lack of self-control and moral consistency. James is pointing to the fact that our words are not neutral; they are an extension of our inner moral state, and contradictions in our speech expose inner moral chaos or spiritual immaturity.
