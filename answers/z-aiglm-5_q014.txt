Based on the text of Matthew 6:19–21, the answer to your first question involves a nuance that challenges modern assumptions. While the verse is often quoted as a diagnostic tool (revealing the heart), the grammar and context suggest it is primarily a formative tool (training the heart).

Here is an analysis of how the text answers your questions.

### 1. Does Treasure Reveal or Train the Heart?

The text indicates that treasure **trains** (or directs) the heart, though it implicitly reveals it as a consequence.

The key lies in the connector word **"For"** (Greek: *gar*) at the start of verse 21. This word links the command (verses 19–20) to the principle (verse 21). Jesus is providing the *reason* why the listener must command their treasures to move to heaven.

If the verse meant only to *reveal* the heart, the logic would be: *"You already love earthly things; therefore, your money is here."*

However, the logic of the passage is active and causal: *"Put your money in heaven, because wherever your money is, your heart will inevitably follow."*

*   **The "Magnet" Effect:** Jesus is teaching that the heart does not lead the treasure as much as the treasure leads the heart. The "heart" in biblical anthropology is the center of the will and allegiance. Jesus is describing a spiritual law of gravity: You attach your heart to whatever you invest in.
*   **The Implication:** If you want your heart to be in heaven (to care about eternal things), you must send your treasure there first. The act of giving, storing up, or investing in eternal things acts as a rudder that steers the heart away from earth and toward God.

Therefore, the text presents treasure as a **formative force**. It suggests that we have more control over where our heart goes (by moving our money) than we do over where our money goes (by trying to summon willpower out of a vacuum).

### 2. How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together

In this passage, Jesus creates a chain reaction linking what we own (possession), what we think about (attention), and what we value (love).

**A. Possession creates Attachment ("Where your treasure is...")**
"Treasuring" is an act of assigning value. When we lay up treasure on earth, we are mentally and physically anchoring ourselves to a temporary, decaying system. Possession is not just ownership; it is identification. We tend to defend what we own. Once we possess something, our security becomes tied to it.

**B. Attachment demands Attention ("...where moth and rust corrupt...")**
In verses 19 and 20, Jesus highlights the conditions of the storage locations.
*   If your treasure is on earth, your attention is consumed by **anxiety and maintenance**. You must worry about "moth and rust" (decay) and "thieves" (loss). The possession commands your mental energy. You cannot help but watch the stock market, worry about the housing market, or fear theft.
*   If your treasure is in heaven, your attention is freed from the fear of loss. The security of the treasure determines the state of the mind.

**C. Attention solidifies Love ("...there will your heart be also.")**
In the Bible, the "heart" represents the core of one's being—the seat of allegiance and love.
*   **The Tie:** You cannot love what you ignore, and you cannot ignore what you treasure.
*   **The Cycle:**
    1.  **Action:** You place your treasure in a specific location.
    2.  **Attention:** Your mind naturally migrates to that location to guard, monitor, or enjoy the treasure.
    3.  **Love:** As your attention fixes on that location, your allegiance shifts there. You eventually become emotionally and spiritually wedded to the object of your investment.

### Summary
The text says that **treasure trains the heart**. It functions like a leash. If you hold the leash of earthly treasure, your heart is dragged along with it into anxiety and corruption. If you cut that leash and send your treasure ahead to heaven, your heart is pulled upward with it.

Jesus is teaching that if you want to change your heart (love), you must change your treasure (possession), because your mind (attention) will always follow the money.
