This text does not merely say that treasure reveals the heart; it asserts that treasure **draws** the heart. While it functions diagnostically (it tells us where the heart is), it operates causally (it determines where the heart goes).

Here is an analysis of how the text functions as both a mirror and a magnet, and how possession, attention, and love become bound together in this logic.

### 1. Reveal or Train?
**Verdict: It trains (binds) the heart, thereby revealing it.**

If we view the text as purely revealing, it suggests a passive observation: *"If your heart is already in heaven, you will store up heavenly treasure."* While true, this misses the imperative force of the passage.

Jesus is commanding a specific action: **"Lay up."** This implies an active investment. In the logic of Matthew 6, the movement is not from Heart $\rightarrow$ Treasure, but from Treasure $\rightarrow$ Heart.
*   **The Causal Link:** Verse 21 begins with the Greek conjunction *gar* ("for"), which indicates that verse 21 is the *reason* for the commands in verses 19 and 20. The reason you should not store earthly treasure is not just because it rusts, but because storing it on earth *moves your heart* to earth.
*   **The Gravity of Desire:** To "lay up" treasure requires you to value that specific currency or asset. As you invest energy, resources, and anxiety into something, your heart attaches itself to it. Therefore, the accumulation of treasure **trains** the heart by habituation. It anchors the affection where the investment lies.

### 2. The Trinity of Devotion: Possession, Attention, Love
The text weaves three distinct psychological and spiritual activities into a single chain of custody regarding human devotion.

#### A. Possession (The Investment)
> *"Lay up for yourselves treasures..."*

In this context, possession is not merely "having," it is **storing for security**. "Moth and rust" and "thieves" represent the vulnerability of earthly security. When a human being "lays up" treasure, they are making a practical declaration that: *"This thing will provide my future safety."*
*   **The Tie:** Possession creates a dependency. You possess the treasure because you believe it holds the power to sustain you. By creating this dependency, you are voluntarily submitting your future well-being to the object of that possession.

#### B. Attention (The Location)
> *"Where your treasure is..."*

Attention is the geographical marker of the soul. The text assumes that a human mind cannot be invested in multiple ultimate things simultaneously. You cannot give your resources to a "storehouse" without directing your gaze toward it.
*   **The Tie:** If you possess earthly treasure, your attention is on the "earthly." You are calculating market trends, worrying about theft, or enjoying material luxury. Your mental bandwidth is consumed by the preservation and enjoyment of that specific possession. You cannot focus on heaven if you are anxiously tending to earth.

#### C. Love (The Heart)
> *"There will your heart be also."*

In Biblical Hebrew and Greek anthropology, the "heart" (*kardia*) is not just the seat of emotion, but the center of **will, intellect, and affection**. It is where the human being says "I love" and "I trust."
*   **The Tie:** Love is the energy that flows from attention. Because you possess the treasure, you pay attention to it; because you pay attention to it, you love it. The "heart" follows the "treasure" like a shadow. The love is not an abstract feeling; it is a **transfer of trust**.
    *   If the treasure is on earth, your trust is in rust.
    *   If the treasure is in heaven, your trust is in God’s kingdom.

### Summary
The text proposes an **economy of devotion**. Jesus argues that it is impossible to remain neutral. The human heart functions on a principle of attraction: it goes where its security is stored.

*   **Possession** is the act of buying the heart's allegiance.
*   **Attention** is the focus required to maintain that possession.
*   **Love** is the result of that sustained focus.

Therefore, to "lay up treasures in heaven" is a deliberate strategy to **train the heart** to love what is eternal rather than what is temporal. It forces the heart to seek a treasure that cannot be stolen, thereby freeing the believer from the anxiety that binds the heart to earthly security.
