Excellent and penetrating questions. This passage is less about *revealing* a pre-existing heart condition and more about *training* or *forming* the heart through the deliberate act of "laying up" treasure.

Here’s a breakdown of the mechanics at work:

### 1. Treasure Trains the Heart (The Primary Action)
The verb "lay up" (Greek: *thesaurizō*) implies a **deliberate, ongoing process of accumulation and prioritization**. It's not about a single choice, but about a habit of investing one's resources (money, time, energy, affection).

*   **The logic is formative:** When you consistently "lay up" treasure in a particular realm, you are **actively training your heart** to love, desire, and trust in that realm. Your heart becomes a slave to that treasure. The "where" you invest determines the **orientation of your love**.
*   **The heart follows the treasure:** Jesus says, "For where your treasure is, there your heart will be also." This is a statement of **inevitable consequence**, not mere observation. The heart doesn't just *discover* its love; it is **directed and bound** by what it pursues. To lay up treasure on earth is to train your heart to find its life, security, and joy in the perishable.

### 2. Treasure Reveals the Heart (The Secondary, Inseparable Truth)
While the primary dynamic is training, there is also a revelatory element. The passage implicitly argues:

*   **Your treasure exposes your ultimate allegiance.** What you "lay up" is the visible, behavioral expression of your deepest trust and love. The moth, rust, and thieves are the great equalizers—they expose what is truly valuable versus what is merely *claimed* to be valuable.
*   **The heart cannot help but follow.** Therefore, seeing where a person "lays up" their treasure is an infallible guide to the true state of their heart. The heart is already there.

But the warning is against **passive revelation**; it's a call to **active redirection**. Jesus is not saying, "Let me see your bank account and I'll tell you your heart." He is saying, "**You are actively forming your heart by where you invest. Stop and invest in the eternal.**"

---

### The Tying Together of Possession, Attention, and Love

Your three terms form a seamless chain of cause and effect, which is the engine of heart-formation:

1.  **Possession (What You Lay Up):** This is the *material* anchor. You cannot lay up what you do not have. It starts with resources—money, time, energy. But it extends to anything you "store up": achievements, reputation, relationships, intellectual pursuits.
2.  **Attention (Where Your Eye/Heart Is):** The process of possessing and laying up *demands* attention. You must monitor, protect, and tend to your treasure. The worries of verse 19-20 (moth, rust, thieves) are the enemies of your earthly treasure, and they command your mental energy. Conversely, **the way you spend your resources is the surest guide to where your mind and concern reside.**
3.  **Love (Where Your Heart Is):** This is the *final destination* and the root of the problem. Attention is the *means* by which possession leads to love. As you possess and attend to something, **your love (affection, loyalty, trust, identity) becomes wrapped up in it.**

**The Cycle:** You possess → you give it attention (to protect, increase, enjoy it) → your love for it grows → your heart is now *there* → you possess more of it to secure that love... and the cycle deepens.

### Conclusion
Jesus is not offering a diagnostic tool; He is issuing a **formative warning and invitation**. 

*   **The warning:** If you are training your heart on earth, you are busy making it a slave to the transient and corrupting. You are forging your own chains.
*   **The invitation:** The solution is not to abstractly "love heaven more," but to **actively redirect your laying up.** Lay up treasure in heaven—by investing in eternal things (justice, mercy, faithfulness, God's kingdom, the poor)—and you will *thereby* train your heart to love what is eternal. Your heart will be freed from the tyranny of the perishable and "laid up" with the incorruptible.

**In short: Treasure doesn't just *reveal* the heart; it *makes* the heart.** The passage calls us to be intentional architects of our own desire, using the powerful mechanism of where we invest our lives.
