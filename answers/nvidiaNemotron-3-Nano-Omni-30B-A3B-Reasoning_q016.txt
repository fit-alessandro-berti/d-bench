The elder brother’s deepest problem is **discomfort with mercy itself**.

While he voices anger and a sense of unfairness, these are symptoms of a deeper issue: he cannot accept the father’s gracious, unearned forgiveness. He equates service and obedience with entitlement to reward, and the father’s mercy—shown in the celebration for the repentant son—threatens his self‑image as the “good” son. Thus, the core difficulty lies in his inability to embrace the unconditional mercy extended to his brother.
