To address the question, we must analyze the given passage from Jeremiah 17:9-10 and understand its implications on human nature and self-awareness.

The passage describes the heart as "deceitful above all things, and desperately wicked." On the surface, this characterization can be interpreted as pessimistic about human nature. However, understanding the context and the role of the prophet Jeremiah is crucial. Jeremiah is not merely stating a fact about humanity but is conveying a spiritual and moral insight. The heart, in biblical terminology, is often associated with the core of a person's being, including their intentions, emotions, and decisions.

The assertion that "who can know it?" suggests a limitation in human ability to fully understand the heart's condition, implying that human self-assessment can be flawed. However, the subsequent verse clarifies that "I the Lord search the heart, I try the reins, even to give every man according to his ways, and according to the fruit of his doings." This indicates that while human self-understanding may be limited or biased, there is a divine perspective that accurately assesses an individual's true nature and actions.

Thus, the passage can be seen as realistic about the complexities of human nature and the issue of self-deception rather than being outright pessimistic. It acknowledges that humans may not always be aware of their true motives or the condition of their heart.

When discussing motives in light of this passage, several considerations come into play:

1. **Acknowledge the Potential for Self-Deception**: Recognize that one's own heart (or true intentions and feelings) can be deceitful. This awareness can foster a more nuanced and humble discussion about motives.

2. **Reflect on Actions Rather Than Just Intentions**: Since the passage mentions being judged according to "the fruit of his doings," examining the outcomes of one's actions can provide insight into true motives.

3. **Seek External Feedback and Reflection**: Engaging with trusted individuals or spiritual guides can offer perspectives that help in understanding one's motives and heart condition.

4. **Prayer and Spiritual Reflection**: For those with a religious belief, praying for insight into their heart and motives, as well as reflecting on spiritual teachings, can be a way to gain a deeper understanding of oneself.

5. **Cultivate Mindfulness and Self-Examination**: Regularly examining one's actions, feelings, and thoughts can help in identifying patterns or discrepancies that may indicate self-deception.

In summary, Jeremiah 17:9-10 presents a realistic view of human nature, highlighting the complexities and potential for self-deception. It encourages a thoughtful and humble approach to understanding one's motives, acknowledging the limitations of human self-knowledge, and seeking insight through various means.
