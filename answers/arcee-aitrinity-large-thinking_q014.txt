
Excellent question. This short passage from the Sermon on the Mount is dense with meaning. The answer is that **the text primarily teaches that *where your treasure is, your heart will follow* (it trains the heart), but this process inherently *reveals* the heart's existing orientation as well.** The two concepts are two sides of the same coin.

Let's break it down:

### 1. "Treasure Trains the Heart": The Directional Force
The key Greek phrase is: **"ὅπου γάρ ἐστιν ὁ θησαυρός σου, ἐκεῖ ἔσται καὶ ἡ καρδία σου."**
*   **"ἔσται" (estai)** is the future tense of "to be." It does not say "your heart *is* there," but "**your heart *will be* there**."
*   This is a **promise or a warning about a future state**. It indicates a **directional movement**. Your heart (your attention, your affections, your ultimate allegiance) is not static; it is being drawn, shaped, and trained *toward* whatever you have invested your treasure in.

**Why this is "training":**
*   **Possession → Attention:** When you "lay up treasure" (possess/accumulate), you must constantly **pay attention** to it. You think about it, protect it, maintain it, and plan how to use it. This focused attention is the primary mechanism of training.
*   **Attention → Love:** Sustained, deliberate attention to something **fosters affection for it**. You grow to love what you serve and protect. Psychologically and spiritually, we are formed by what we habitually focus on.
*   **Love → Heart Orientation:** This cultivated love re-orients your "heart" (the core of your will, desire, and personhood). Your values, your goals, and your sense of security begin to flow from that object of love.

**The negative example (earthly treasure)** proves the point: Moth, rust, and thieves *force* your attention. You must constantly guard, repair, and worry. This very process of defensive attention trains your heart to be anxious, possessive, and earth-bound.

### 2. "Treasure Reveals the Heart": The Diagnostic Function
While the primary thrust is directional ("will be"), the verse also **diagnostically reveals** where the heart *already is inclined*. You don't invest your treasure in something you are utterly indifferent to.
*   Your **use of possessions** is the most concrete, daily evidence of your true priorities. Where you *choose* to spend your money, time, and energy is a direct readout of what you currently value most.
*   Therefore, looking at your "treasure" (your financial statements, your schedule, your energy expenditure) **reveals** the existing trajectory of your heart. It shows where your attention and love are *already being trained*.

### How Possession, Attention, and Love Become Tied Together Here

The verse presents a **causal chain**:

1.  **Possession (Treasure):** The act of "laying up" or storing something for security and value. This is the initial, volitional choice of an object.
2.  **Attention:** The necessary, ongoing consequence of possession. You must monitor, maintain, and protect what you own. This is the **active, daily practice** that forms you.
3.  **Love:** The affective result of sustained attention. You grow to cherish and find identity in what you serve. Love here is not just a feeling but a **bond of loyalty and trust**.
4.  **Heart (καρδία):** The final, settled orientation of the whole person—will, desire, and allegiance. The "heart" is the **trained state** of being.

**The Crucial Contrast:**
*   **Earthly Treasure:** Trains the heart to be **anxious, temporary, and possessive** (because it decays and can be stolen).
*   **Heavenly Treasure:** Trains the heart to be **secure, eternal, and generous** (because it is imperishable and cannot be taken).

### Conclusion

**The text says: "Treasure trains the heart."**
Your deliberate choice of what to possess sets in motion a process (attention → love) that re-orients your entire inner life ("heart"). At the same time, your existing treasure **reveals** where that training process has already led you.

Therefore, the call is **radical and practical**: *Deliberately, intentionally, "lay up" for yourselves treasures in heaven (through acts of secret giving, prayer, and fasting—the very next topics in Matthew 6). This conscious act of heavenly investment will train your heart, over time, to be where your treasure is—fixed on God and His eternal kingdom.*
