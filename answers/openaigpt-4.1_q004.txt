Great question! The trust described in **Proverbs 3:5–6** is not a call to ignorance or the rejection of wisdom and understanding, but rather a call to **humble dependence on God**—even as we use our minds.

### 1. **Trust in the Lord with All Your Heart**
This is:
- **Wholehearted:** It calls for undivided reliance on God, not partial or conditional trust.
- **Relational:** Trust is rooted in a relationship with God, not abstract fate.
- **Active:** It demands that one’s decisions, plans, and perspectives are filtered through faith in God’s goodness, wisdom, and sovereignty.

### 2. **Lean Not on Your Own Understanding**
This is **not** advocacy for anti-intellectualism, but rather a corrective to **self-sufficiency** or pride in human reasoning:

- **Humble Self-distrust:**  
  - Recognizes the **limitations** of human understanding.  
  - Is aware that our perspective can be faulty, short-sighted, or skewed by self-interest.  
  - Accepts correction, seeks wisdom from God, and is teachable.
  - Examples: Praying for guidance, seeking godly counsel, being willing to change your mind in light of Scripture.

- **Mere Anti-intellectualism:**  
  - Rejects **all** use of reason or knowledge.
  - May oppose learning, reflection, or questioning.
  - Can foster superstition or blind credulity.

**The biblical position is not to turn off your brain, but to recognize its limits—and to submit your understanding to God’s greater wisdom.** In fact, the book of Proverbs repeatedly exhorts readers to pursue wisdom, understanding, and knowledge (e.g., Prov. 4:7). But even the wisest person must acknowledge that God’s perspective is higher.

### 3. **In All Your Ways Acknowledge Him**
This means:
- Bringing God into every area of life—decisions, relationships, plans, and uncertainties.
- Being mindful of His presence, authority, and guidance.

### 4. **He Shall Direct Your Paths**
The promise is that humble, God-centered trust leads to guidance—not confusion or aimlessness.

---
**Summary:**  
**Proverbs 3:5-6** calls for **humble self-distrust**: a posture that honors reason and understanding as God’s gifts, but does not idolize them or make them ultimate. We’re to use our minds *with* faith and *under* God’s authority, not *instead of* faith or *against* God’s authority. It’s a trust characterized by humility and teachability—not anti-intellectualism.
