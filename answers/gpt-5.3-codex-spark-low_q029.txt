• The required posture is humble, restorative, and self-aware grace.

  In Galatians 6:1, Paul is not inviting judgment or punishment; he is calling for redemptive correction:

  - Restoration says the goal is healing, not shame (“restore” = bring back to wholeness).
  - Meekness says the method must be gentle and patient, not domineering or self-righteous.
  - Self-suspicion (“considering thyself, lest thou also be tempted”) says correction starts with awareness of one’s own
    fragility before God.

  So these are kept together because correction done by believers is morally shaped by the same grace they’re offering:

  1. for the other’s good,
  2. with Christlike humility,
  3. while remaining aware that “the one correcting” is also morally vulnerable.

  In short: truth spoken in compassion, by someone who knows they also need mercy.
