This passage presents treasure as both a **revelation** of the heart's current orientation and a **training** of the heart toward that orientation. The concluding line—"For where your treasure is, there will your heart be also"—suggests that what you accumulate, cherish, and secure reveals where your deepest allegiances lie. Yet, the imperative preceding it—"lay up for yourselves treasures in heaven"—implies an active disciplining of desire, attention, and will toward eternal realities. In this way, treasure is both diagnostic and formative: it exposes the heart's state and, through intentional stewardship, shapes its trajectory.

The tying together of possession, attention, and love follows a tripartite logic:
- **Possession** (treasure secured) requires deliberate action—choosing what to acquire, save, and accumulate.
- **Attention** (treasure tracked) naturally flows toward what is valued—one tends, monitors, and worries over what one possesses.
- **Love** (treasure cherished) is the affective bond that forms around sustained attention and stewardship.

Thus, you cannot separate what you possess from what you pay attention to, nor what you give attention to from what you ultimately love. This interconnection warns against earthly accumulation (which forms fragile, corruptible attachments) and invites heavenly investment (which forms enduring, incorruptible affections). The heart follows the treasure—and so the wise steward wisely chooses where to store wealth, knowing that in doing so, they are also choosing where their heart will dwell.
