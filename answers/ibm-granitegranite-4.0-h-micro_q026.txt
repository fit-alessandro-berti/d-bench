The passage from Matthew 12:34-35 is a profound reflection on the relationship between speech and inner moral disposition. Jesus uses the metaphor of a "viper" (a symbol of venomous, poisonous creatures) to illustrate the inherent connection between a person's heart and their speech. 

**Connection between Speech and Hidden Moral Stockpiles:**

1. **Speech as a Reflection of Inner State:** The passage suggests that what we say is a direct reflection of what is within our hearts. This is a profound statement about the inseparability of our actions and our inner disposition. If our hearts are filled with evil, our words will also be filled with evil. Conversely, if our hearts are filled with good, our words will also be good. This implies that our speech is not merely a product of external influences or circumstances, but a manifestation of our inner moral state.

2. **Speech as a Symptom:** The passage suggests that speech can be seen as a symptom of our inner moral state. Just as a fever can indicate an underlying illness, our speech can indicate the state of our hearts. If we are speaking in ways that are harmful or deceitful, it may be a sign that our hearts are filled with similar qualities. This suggests that speech can be a diagnostic tool for understanding our inner moral state.

3. **Speech as a Choice:** The passage also implies that speech is a choice. We have the power to choose what we say, and this choice is influenced by the contents of our hearts. If we choose to speak good things, it suggests that our hearts are filled with good. If we choose to speak evil things, it suggests that our hearts are filled with evil. This implies that speech is not entirely determined by our inner state, but also involves a conscious choice.

4. **Speech as a Revelation:** The passage suggests that speech can also be a revelation of our inner moral state. Just as a person's actions can reveal their true character, their words can reveal their true moral disposition. This implies that speech can be a form of self-revelation, allowing others (and ourselves) to see what is truly in our hearts.

In conclusion, Matthew 12:34-35 suggests that speech is a complex interplay of our inner moral state, conscious choices, and self-revelation. It is not merely a symptom of our inner state, nor is it solely a choice, but a combination of both. This passage underscores the importance of cultivating a good heart, as our words will inevitably reflect what is within us.
