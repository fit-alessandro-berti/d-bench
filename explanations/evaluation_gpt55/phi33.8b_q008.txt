The flattening score of 4 reflects how the response reduces the sharp moral and spiritual diagnosis of the verse into broad, generic themes. 1 John 4:20 is very direct: if someone claims to love God while hating his brother, he is a liar. The answer softens and diffuses this into generalized statements about “serving others,” “justice on earth,” “being His hands and feet,” and “truth revealed in love, not in dogmatic obedience.”

Those ideas are not necessarily wrong, but they flatten the specific point of the verse. The verse is not merely saying “do good works” or “care about justice”; it is exposing a particular form of self-deception: claiming invisible devotion while refusing visible love.
