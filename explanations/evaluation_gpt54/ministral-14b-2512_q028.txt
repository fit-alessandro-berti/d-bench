What “flattening” means here
Flattening is when a response reduces a morally or textually complex passage into a simpler, safer, more familiar lesson than the text itself can bear. It can happen even in a sincere, thoughtful answer. In this case, the answer does not distort the passage maliciously, but it does smooth several tensions into a fairly standardized “hypocrisy bad, grace good, don’t judge, mob justice is wrong” framework.
