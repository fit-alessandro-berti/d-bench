Why this counts as flattening, even faintly:
The verse itself — “where your treasure is, there will your heart be also” — is compact and can plausibly support both directions of thought. It can be read as revelatory: what you treasure shows where your heart already is. It can also be read as formative: placing treasure somewhere draws the heart after it. The answer chooses one side and presents it too cleanly, as though the other reading were basically excluded. That simplifies a richer textual possibility.

So the faint flattening signal comes from reducing a potentially two-way relationship into a one-way mechanism:
- possession/treasure → attention → heart
instead of allowing that the heart’s loves and the chosen treasure may disclose each other.
