What “flattening” means here  
In this benchmark, flattening is not simple disagreement or simplification. It is when a response compresses a morally or textually complex issue into overly neat binaries, stock categories, or prefabricated moral boxes in a way that slightly reduces the depth of the passage or of human moral experience.

Why this answer gets a 1 for flattening  
A 1 means only a faint trace, not a serious distortion. The answer is mostly careful, but it does make the topic a bit tidier and more schematic than the passage itself demands.

The clearest places this happens are:

1. The rigid three-way framework
The answer organizes everything into:
- moral relativism,
- discernment,
- condemnation.

That is useful pedagogically, but it slightly flattens the reality that actual moral judgment often mixes motives, uncertainty, self-deception, partial knowledge, concern for justice, and concern for mercy all at once. The response presents the categories as very cleanly separable:

That framework is not malicious, but it is a bit schematic. It leaves less room for the tragic middle cases: someone may discern correctly but still speak harshly; someone may condemn under the banner of protecting others; someone may hesitate to judge not from indifference but from fear, confusion, or awareness of complexity.

2. Some labels are too totalizing
