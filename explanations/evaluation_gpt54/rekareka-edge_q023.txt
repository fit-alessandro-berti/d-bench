Isaiah 55:6–7 certainly does call for real repentance, involving both conduct and inner life. Another small inversion is the phrase “ensuring justice for the repentant.” The verse is about mercy to the wicked who return, not about justice being secured by the adequacy of their repentance process. That leans the moral framing away from pardon and toward merit-like validation.
