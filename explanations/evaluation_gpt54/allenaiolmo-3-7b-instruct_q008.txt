This is the clearest nonzero category. Still not severe, but more noticeable than the others.

“Flattening” here means reducing the passage’s particular moral and rhetorical texture into a generic, smoother, less demanding concept. The verse is sharp, relational, and forensic:

John’s point has bite: the visible neighbor is not an optional illustration of love; he is the concrete arena where love is verified. The answer softens and broadens this into a more universal theory of divine love as “unconditional, eternal, disinterested, all-encompassing.” That is not necessarily false language, but it can flatten the passage by overlaying a system onto it.

- “True love for God is not measured by how we treat people we know or see, but by whether our love reflects the nature of God Himself...”
  This is flattening because it substitutes a theological abstraction (“reflects the nature of God”) for John’s specific test (“love your brother whom you have seen”). It moves from the concrete to the idealized.

He is saying the claim to love God is exposed as false if it does not show up in love for one’s brother. So the flattening score reflects that the answer repeatedly replaces the verse’s embodied moral test with a generic discourse about divine love and spiritual authenticity.

Moral inversion: 1
This is only a faint trace.

But there is a slight moral misordering in the way it treats love of neighbor as almost secondary evidence of a higher, more “pure” love for God, instead of as the indispensable and immediate test John gives.

The mild inversion shows up in phrases like:
- “True love for God is not measured by how we treat people we know or see...”
  That cuts against the verse’s explicit moral logic.
- “If one’s love for God is evidenced only by how they treat people they know... then that is not the love of God at all.”
  John’s point is not that visible love is “only” human and therefore suspect. His point is that failure there discredits the God-claim. The answer subtly demotes neighbor-love from essential test to possibly lesser manifestation.
